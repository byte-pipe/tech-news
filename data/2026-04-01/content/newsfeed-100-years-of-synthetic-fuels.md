Few industrial chemical processes have a history as winding as that of the Fischer–Tropsch (FT) synthesis, which was reported by Franz Fischer and Hans Tropsch1100 years ago inBrennstoff-Chemie. This catalytic process converts syngas — a mixture of carbon monoxide and hydrogen gas — into hydrocarbons. Initially used to synthesize liquid fuels from coal, it now holds promise as a way to make hydrocarbons, including aviation fuel, from renewable sources of carbon.
