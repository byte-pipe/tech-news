# Apple confirms Mac Pro is discontinued after two decades

## What Apple said and what disappeared from its store
- Apple removed the Mac Pro from its online store; searches now redirect to the general Mac catalog.  
- The last model, updated in 2023 with the M2 Ultra chip, started at $6,999.  
- It retained PCIe slots for specialty cards but never supported discrete GPUs or user‑upgradable memory.  

## Why the tower lost its place in Apple’s lineup
- Apple’s shift to in‑house silicon makes RAM and GPU integrated on‑package, eliminating the benefits of a modular tower.  
- Integrated Media Engines and high‑bandwidth memory provide sufficient performance for most creative workflows (Final Cut Pro, DaVinci Resolve, Logic Pro).  
- Professionals dependent on Nvidia CUDA for 3D, ML, or scientific computing have already migrated to Windows or Linux workstations.  
- Industry analysts note a broader move toward compact desktops and high‑performance laptops, reducing demand for large towers.  

## Performance has moved to Mac Studio for pro desktops
- Mac Studio with M2 Ultra offers a 24‑core CPU, up to a 76‑core GPU, 32‑core Neural Engine, and up to 192 GB unified memory with 800 GB/s bandwidth.  
- Delivers near‑Mac Pro compute in a smaller, quieter, and cheaper enclosure.  
- Lacks internal PCIe slots, but Thunderbolt expansion supports external RAID, SDI capture, 10/25 Gb Ethernet, and multi‑display hubs.  

## The end of an era for Apple’s iconic Mac Pro tower
- Introduced in 2006 as the Intel‑based successor to the Power Mac G5; gained a loyal studio following.  
- The 2013 cylindrical redesign suffered thermal and upgrade limitations.  
- 2019 “cheese‑grater” chassis restored serviceability and PCIe flexibility.  
- 2023 silicon transition kept slots but removed discrete GPU and memory upgrades, eroding the tower’s core appeal.  

## What pros should do now after the Mac Pro’s sunset
- macOS‑first shops should adopt Mac Studio as the practical successor; it matches or exceeds typical Mac Pro workloads at lower cost.  
- Specialty PCIe needs (audio DSP, SDI, networking) can be met with Thunderbolt enclosures from established vendors.  
- Pipelines reliant on CUDA or multi‑GPU rendering should consider Windows or Linux workstations with Nvidia RTX cards.  
- Remaining Mac Pro inventory may appear at select resellers briefly; Apple typically provides several years of macOS and security updates for discontinued models.  

## What this signals for Apple’s pro strategy
- Apple believes performance leadership now stems from silicon integration rather than modularity.  
- Expect continued investment in Metal, ProRes/ProRes RAW, and hardware‑accelerated codecs.  
- Closer collaboration with developers such as Adobe and Blackmagic to maximize Apple‑silicon efficiency.  
- Future pro Macs will be compact, power‑efficient, and vertically optimized, moving away from tower form factors.
