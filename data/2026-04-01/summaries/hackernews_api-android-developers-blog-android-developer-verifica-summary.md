# Android developer verification: Rolling out to all developers on Play Console and Android Developer Console

## Overview
- Android aims to keep the platform open while improving safety against malware, especially from sideloaded sources.
- A new Android developer verification system is being introduced to prevent malicious actors from remaining anonymous.

## Verification rollout
- Verification is now available to all developers in both the Android Developer Console and Play Console.
- Developers distributing only outside Google Play should create an account in the Android Developer Console immediately.
- Play Console users should watch for updates in the coming weeks; those already verified are likely set.

## Impact on users
- The download and installation experience for most users will remain unchanged until later in the year.
- User‑facing protections will first launch in Brazil, Indonesia, Singapore, and Thailand in September 2026, with a global rollout planned for 2027.
- Only unregistered apps will require ADB or an advanced sideloading flow.

## Tailored developer experience
- **Android Studio integration**: Within two months, app registration status will appear when generating a signed App Bundle or APK.
- **Play Console**: Verified developers will have their eligible Play apps automatically registered; a manual claim process is available if needed. Registration status will be shown per app.
- **Students and hobbyists**: A free limited‑distribution account (no government ID, email only) will allow sharing with up to 20 devices. Early‑access invitations will be sent in June.
- **Power users**: The option to install from any source remains, using either the new advanced flow for unregistered apps or the existing ADB method.

## Timeline
- **April 2026**: Android Developer Verifier appears in Google System Services settings.
- **June 2026**: Early access to limited‑distribution accounts for students and hobbyists.
- **August 2026**: Global launch of limited‑distribution accounts and advanced flow for power users.
- **September 30 2026**: Apps must be registered by verified developers to be installed/updated on certified devices in the four pilot countries; unregistered apps can still be sideloaded via ADB or advanced flow.
- **2027 onward**: Global enforcement of the registration requirement.

## Next steps
- Developers are encouraged to start verification now and consult the provided developer guides.
