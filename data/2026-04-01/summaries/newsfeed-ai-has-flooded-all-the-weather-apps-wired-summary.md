# AI Has Flooded All the Weather Apps | WIRED

## New AI‑powered Storm Radar app
- The Weather Company released an updated Storm Radar app with an AI Weather Assistant.
- Users can toggle layers (radar, temperature, wind, lightning) and sync forecasts with calendars for personalized text notifications.
- Voice personas range from vintage radio weathermen to pop‑culture fans.
- Data still comes from NOAA and the National Weather Service.
- Subscription costs $4 / month, iOS‑only at launch; Android version promised later.

## Market landscape and competitors
- Google and Apple embed weather directly in their mobile OSes, now adding AI‑generated insights.
- Third‑party apps such as Carrot Weather, Rain Viewer, Acme Weather, and newer AI‑first apps like Rainbow Weather compete for niche audiences.
- AccuWeather has integrated its service into OpenAI’s ChatGPT.
- Dark Sky’s founder, Adam Grossman, launched Acme Weather to emphasize forecast uncertainty and transparent communication.

## How AI is changing forecasting
- Traditional forecasts rely on government‑sourced data (satellites, radar, balloons) processed by supercomputer models.
- Machine‑learning models reduce computation time, enabling quicker predictions, though sometimes at the cost of accuracy.
- Apps like Storm Radar and Acme Weather use AI to merge multiple models, create high‑resolution visual maps, and generate concise textual or spoken summaries.

## Challenges, skepticism, and transparency
- Government funding cuts to NOAA shift more data‑collection responsibilities to private firms.
- Extreme weather and climate‑related events remain difficult for AI models to predict reliably.
- Grossman warns against “AI for AI’s sake,” urging clear, transparent interfaces that let users see the underlying data rather than a black‑box chatbot.

## Outlook
- AI is viewed as the biggest recent shift in weather services, still in early stages.
- Personalization (customizable widgets, voice personas) is a key selling point for new apps.
- The industry balances the promise of faster, more user‑friendly forecasts with the need for accuracy, uncertainty communication, and openness about AI’s role.
