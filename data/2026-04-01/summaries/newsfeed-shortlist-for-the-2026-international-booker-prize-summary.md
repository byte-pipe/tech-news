# Shortlist for the 2026 International Booker Prize announced

## Overview
- Six titles have been named finalists for the 2026 International Booker Prize, formerly the Man Booker International Prize.  
- The prize honors works of fiction originally written in a language other than English, then translated into English and published in the U.K. and/or Ireland.  
- Announcement made on March 31, 2026; the winning author and translator will be revealed on May 19, 2026.  
- The shortlist highlights stories set in diverse historical moments, including 1930s Japanese‑controlled Taiwan, Nazi‑era Germany, and the 1979 Iranian Revolution.  
- Female authors and translators are prominently represented: five of the authors and four of the translators are women.

## Shortlisted Books
- **The Nights Are Quiet in Tehran** – *Shida Bazyar* (translated from German by Ruth Martin)  
  - Multigenerational tale spanning the 1979 Iranian Revolution and the family’s later life in West Germany; explores trauma, hope, and the enduring revolutionary spirit.

- **She Who Remains** – *Rene Karabash* (translated from Bulgarian by Izidora Angel)  
  - Follows Bekja, who flees an arranged marriage in Albania’s rural Accursed Mountains, lives as a man, and confronts a blood feud that reshapes her identity.

- **The Director** – *Daniel Kehlmann* (translated from German by Ross Benjamin)  
  - Fictionalized account of Austrian filmmaker G.W. Pabst’s exile from Nazi Germany to Hollywood, his return to Austria, and the moral pressures of making propaganda.

- **On Earth As It Is Beneath** – *Ana Paula Maia* (translated from Portuguese by Padma Viswanathan)  
  - Horror novella set on a remote penal colony where inmates are released each full moon to be hunted, blending poetic language with visceral violence.

- **The Witch** – *Marie NDiaye* (translated from French by Jordan Stump)  
  - Originally published in 1996; follows Lucie, a modest witch who passes her powers to her daughters, exploring family legacy and supernatural unease.

- **Taiwan Travelogue** – *Yáng Shuāng‑zǐ* (translated from Mandarin Chinese by Lin King)  
  - Award‑winning novel tracing a year‑long journey of a fictional Japanese novelist invited to Japanese‑controlled Taiwan, examining power dynamics and the complexities of travel.

## Prize Details
- The winning author and translator will share a prize of £50,000 (approximately $66,000).  
- The shortlist celebrates geographic diversity, with authors and translators hailing from four continents and varied professional backgrounds (e.g., manga and video‑game scriptwriting, acting).

## Jury
- Natasha Brown (author, chair of the jury)  
- Marcus du Sautoy (writer, broadcaster, professor)  
- Sophie Hughes (translator)  
- Troy Onyango (writer, editor, bookseller)  
- Nilanjana S. Roy (novelist, columnist)
