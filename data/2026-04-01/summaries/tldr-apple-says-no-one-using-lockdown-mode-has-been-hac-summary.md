# Apple says no one using Lockdown Mode has been hacked with spyware

## Overview
- Apple reports no known successful mercenary spyware attacks on devices with Lockdown Mode enabled.
- The claim follows a similar statement made a year after the feature’s launch in 2022.
- Lockdown Mode is an opt‑in security setting that disables features commonly exploited by spyware.

## Apple’s Statement
- Spokesperson Sarah O’Rourke told TechCrunch that Apple is “not aware of any successful mercenary spyware attacks against a Lockdown Mode‑enabled Apple device.”
- Apple has sent numerous notifications to users in over 150 countries about potential spyware infections, though exact numbers are undisclosed.

## Evidence from Researchers
- Amnesty International’s security lab head, Donncha Ó Cearbhaill, says no evidence of iPhone compromise with Lockdown Mode active has been found.
- Citizen Lab documented cases where Lockdown Mode blocked attacks, including NSO’s Pegasus and Intellexa’s Predator spyware.
- Google researchers observed that some spyware aborts infection attempts when Lockdown Mode is detected.

## Expert Opinions
- Apple security expert Patrick Wardle describes Lockdown Mode as “one of the most aggressive consumer‑facing hardening features ever shipped.”
- Wardle explains that the feature shrinks the attack surface by blocking many attachment types and restricting WebKit features, making zero‑click exploits much harder.
- He notes that while bypasses are theoretically possible, none have been publicly identified.

## User Experience
- Long‑time users report minimal impact, aside from occasional confusing notifications.
- Certain actions, such as opening links from messages, require extra steps (e.g., copying and pasting URLs).
