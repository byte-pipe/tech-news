# Dear Substack: You are missing out on a big revenue model opportunity

## The current landscape
- Reach used to be the primary goal; revenue has become equally important.
- Platforms such as Substack, Patreon, Medium, Twitch, YouTube, Reddit and X are experimenting with new monetization models.
- AI training on content brings consent, credit, and compensation (the “3 Cs”) to the forefront.
- Existing solutions offer a binary choice between maximizing reach or protecting content from AI use, which forces creators into trade‑offs.

## Why the existing trade‑offs are problematic
- Content quality suffers when low‑quality or spammy engagement is cheap.
- Attention is fragmented and distribution is increasingly mediated.
- Creators must sacrifice one of the following to gain another:
  - Reach vs. revenue
  - Engagement vs. algorithmic volume
  - Exposure vs. exclusivity

## Agentic payment infrastructure as a solution
- A payment system that can handle transactions from both humans and autonomous agents.
- The technology already exists (stablecoins, Pay‑per‑crawl, HTTP 402, Stripe’s MPP, Shopify’s UCP, ATXP, A2A, Anthropic’s MCP, etc.).
- Adoption, not technical capability, is the missing piece.

## New revenue models enabled by agentic payments
1. **Pay to unlock** – charge a one‑time fee for a single article or video instead of a subscription.
2. **Pay for preview** – require a small payment for bots or AI services to view a preview before deciding to purchase the full content.
3. **Pay to comment** – impose a micro‑fee on each comment, discouraging spam and rewarding genuine interaction.
4. **Tip jar** – allow both humans and agents to leave small tips when they find content useful; can coexist with the models above.

## Benefits of these models
- Simultaneously increase reach and revenue without compromising quality.
- Provide creators with more optionality and a larger overall revenue pie.
- Make low‑effort abuse more costly, improving moderation and community trust.

## Readiness of the ecosystem
- Stablecoin volumes in the billions.
- Cloudflare’s Pay‑per‑crawl service.
- Coinbase’s support for HTTP 402.
- Stripe’s Machine Payments Protocol.
- Shopify’s Universal Commerce Protocol.
- Various agent‑to‑agent transaction standards already in use.
