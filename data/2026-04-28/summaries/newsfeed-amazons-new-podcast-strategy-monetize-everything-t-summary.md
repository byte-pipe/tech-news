# Amazon’s new podcast strategy: Monetize everything

## Overview
- Amazon’s podcasting business has been overhauled in the last six months, according to The New York Times.  
- In August 2025 the company cut more than 100 jobs at its Wondery studio; the Wondery brand remains but the studio’s structure was dismantled.  
- Audio‑only podcasts are now managed under Audible, while a new division called **Creator Services** works with on‑camera personalities such as Dax Shepard, Keke Palmer, and the Kelce brothers.

## Monetization approach
- Creator Services’ goal is to “infuse both the content and the commerce together,” as stated by general manager Matt Sandler.  
- The Kelce brothers’ podcast **New Heights** illustrates the model:  
  - A dedicated “Kelce Clubhouse” section on Amazon sells related merchandise, streams the documentary “Kelce,” and offers product recommendations for a football‑watching party.  
  - Revenue streams extend far beyond traditional podcast advertising.

## Industry context
- While other online creators are also linking content to commerce, Amazon is unique in having “dismembered” an existing company (Wondery) to create this integrated ecosystem.
