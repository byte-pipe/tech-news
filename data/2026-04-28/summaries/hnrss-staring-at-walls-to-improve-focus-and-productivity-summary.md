# Staring at walls to improve focus and productivity

## The problem
- Modern life delivers massive information overload (≈87 GB per day in 2026), leading to frequent “brain fog” and loss of focus.  
- Typical cycle: poor sleep → heavy caffeine → reliance on media for dopamine → more scrolling → further fatigue.  
- Small, repeated dopamine hits from media create a feedback loop that deepens the focus deficit.

## Disconnecting
- Simply stopping scrolling does not fully resolve the midday “focus wall” that appears around 1‑2 pm.  
- The instinctive response is more coffee, which only sustains work at a reduced, painful pace.  
- A more effective break is needed to reset mental energy.

## Stare at a Wall!
- Inspired by Simple Lucas, the author tried staring at a wall for 5‑10 minutes when focus waned.  
- Technique details:  
  - Look at the wall “out‑of‑focus,” using peripheral vision to activate the parasympathetic nervous system.  
  - Practice “mind blanking” – deliberately trying to think of nothing.  
- Results: focus and motivation returned after each interval.  
- Difficulty: maintaining a blank mind for several minutes feels as challenging as a workout, but pushing through yields a strong sense of accomplishment.

## Outcomes and next steps
- Notable improvements in focus and productivity have been observed.  
- The author plans to keep the wall‑staring routine and explore additional focus‑enhancing strategies in future posts.
