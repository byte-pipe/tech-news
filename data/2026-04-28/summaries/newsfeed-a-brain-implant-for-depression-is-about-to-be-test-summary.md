# A Brain Implant for Depression Is About to Be Tested in Humans | WIRED

## Overview
- Motif Neurotech received FDA approval to begin a human trial of its small, skull‑mounted brain‑computer interface designed to treat major depressive disorder, especially cases resistant to medication.  
- The implant delivers patterned electrical stimulation to the central executive network, aiming to “turn on” underactive brain regions associated with depression.

## Device and Procedure
- Implanted just above the dura in a 20‑minute outpatient procedure, requiring no open brain surgery.  
- Powered and controlled wirelessly via a magnetoelectric system; patients wear a baseball‑cap‑like device that charges the implant and transmits the prescribed stimulation dose.  
- Initial treatment involves wearing the cap for 10–20 minutes several times a day; maintenance doses are expected to be less frequent after the first 10 days.

## Clinical Trial Details
- Primary goal: demonstrate safety of the implant and procedure over a 12‑month period.  
- Secondary goals: assess reductions in depression symptoms, quality of life, anxiety, and cognitive function.  
- Planned enrollment: about 10 participants.  
- Researchers will monitor participants for meaningful symptom improvement and potential remission within the first 10 days of therapy.

## Context within Depression Treatments
- Electrical stimulation has a long history: electroconvulsive therapy (since the 1930s), deep brain stimulation (experimental, not FDA‑approved), and transcranial magnetic stimulation (FDA‑approved in 2008, requiring intensive treatment schedules).  
- In 2021, nearly 9 million U.S. adults were treated for major depressive disorder; roughly 3 million had treatment‑resistant depression.

## Future Directions
- Motif plans future versions that can record brain activity, enabling personalized stimulation regimens.  
- Co‑founder Jacob Robinson emphasizes the need for objective, longitudinal brain measurements to improve psychiatric care, likening current practice to managing diabetes without measurable biomarkers.

## Company Background
- Houston‑based startup founded in 2022, part of a growing BCI industry that includes firms like Neuralink, Paradromics, and Synchron, which focus on communication and motor restoration for paralyzed individuals.  
- Motif’s niche is psychiatric therapy, targeting depression that does not respond to conventional medication.
