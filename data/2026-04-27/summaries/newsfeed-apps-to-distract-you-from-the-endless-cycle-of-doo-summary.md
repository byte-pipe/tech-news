# Apps to distract you from the endless cycle of doomscrolling – Summary  

## Overview of doomscrolling  
- Doomscrolling = excessive time spent consuming social‑media feeds, often negative.  
- 64 % of Americans admit to doing it (survey, 2025).  
- Reported impacts: mental‑health strain, brain fatigue, reduced focus, disrupted sleep, anxiety, and emotional drain.  
- Breaking the habit is difficult; the article suggests using short, purposeful phone apps as an alternative to endless scrolling.  

## Recommended apps  

- **Dudel Draw** (iOS, free)  
  - Daily random shape to turn into a drawing.  
  - Encourages creativity and can be shared with friends for light competition.  

- **Radio Garden** (iOS & Android, free with optional $2.99 / month ad‑free plan)  
  - Streams over 25,000 live radio stations worldwide via a map of green dots.  
  - Allows favorites, search by location, and offers a global listening experience without social feeds.  

- **Elevate** (iOS & Android, free tier limited to 3 games/day; $39.99 / year for unlimited)  
  - Brain‑training suite with >40 games targeting focus, memory, reading, math, etc.  
  - Tracks streaks and performance over time.  

- **Vocabulary** (iOS & Android, free trial; $4.99 / month or $59.99 / year)  
  - Daily word learning with selectable difficulty and categories (e.g., emotions, business).  
  - Includes definitions, example sentences, pronunciation guides, and mini‑games for review.  

- **Seterra** (iOS & Android, free)  
  - Geography‑focused games: flags, oceans, rivers, mountains, volcanoes, etc.  
  - Over 300 game types, progress tracking, and leaderboards.  

- **NYT Games** (iOS & Android, $5.99 / month for full access; some games free)  
  - Daily crossword, Wordle, Connections, Spelling Bee, and other logic/number puzzles.  

- **Drops** (iOS & Android, free limited to 5‑minute lessons/day; $11.99 / month or $79.99 / year)  
  - Language‑learning with visual mini‑games for 45+ languages.  
  - Designed for quick daily practice, suitable for beginners and advanced learners.
