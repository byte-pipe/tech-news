## Purpose & Methodology
- Goal: provide reference points on how high‑growth B2B startups structure and run their marketing functions.  
- Sample: 100 venture‑backed B2B companies, from seed to IPO, selected for recent funding, growth trajectory, and visibility.  
- Bias: heavy concentration in AI‑related firms; not a random or statistically significant sample.  
- Data collection: Claude Code skill scraped LinkedIn, company sites, ad libraries, and other sources; results visualized in Figma and stored on MCP servers.  
- Categorization: companies grouped into 8 audience‑based buckets and classified by GTM motion (self‑serve, sales‑led, hybrid).  

## Key Findings – Marketing Teams
- **Team size relative to headcount**: companies selling to marketers have the largest marketing teams (median 8.3 % of total headcount, mean 8.1 %). Sample size for this subgroup is 8.  
- **Hiring trends**: fast‑growing startups are actively expanding marketing headcount across all GTM motions.  
- **GTM motion impact**:  
  - Self‑serve (PLG‑like) firms tend to have smaller, product‑focused teams.  
  - Sales‑led firms maintain larger, revenue‑oriented marketing groups.  
  - Hybrid firms blend characteristics of both.  
- **Category‑motion alignment**: vertical‑focused startups (e.g., legal‑tech, health‑tech) are predominantly sales‑led; mismatching playbooks (PLG at sales‑led firms or vice‑versa) often leads to failure.  

## Tool & Role Insights (selected from graphs)
- **Commonly mentioned tools in job descriptions**: CRM platforms, marketing automation, analytics, and AI‑assisted content creation tools.  
- **Leadership titles**: prevalence of CMOs versus CROs varies by GTM motion; sales‑led companies more often have CROs, while self‑serve firms lean toward CMOs.
