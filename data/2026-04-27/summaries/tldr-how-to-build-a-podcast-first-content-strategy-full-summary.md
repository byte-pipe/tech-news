# How to Build a Podcast‑First Content Strategy – Full Guide  

## 1. Podcast‑first content strategy for demand generation  
- **95 % vs 5 % rule** – most buyers are passive (95 %) and need top‑of‑mind awareness rather than salesy emails.  
- **Podcast as ecosystem hub** – a recurring show with industry guests creates authority and feeds short videos, newsletters, case studies, reports, etc.  
- **Founder time bottleneck** – founders can share insights by speaking; the repurposing system handles all downstream content, removing the need for them to write constantly.  

## 2. How to build and run your podcast as a content program  

### 2.1 The three podcast formats that generate demand  
- **Expert Interview Show** – host respected industry guests; leverage their credibility and audience.  
- **Solo Thought‑Leadership Show** – founder or internal experts discuss frameworks, opinions, and market insights to position the brand as an authority.  
- **Customer & Case‑Study Show** – invite customers or partners to share real results, building trust and bottom‑of‑funnel demand.  

### 2.2 The six rules for a high‑performing podcast  
1. **Clear positioning** – own a specific topic that instantly signals relevance to the target buyer.  
2. **Reliable setup** – use a stable recording platform (e.g., Riverside), a good microphone, and an automated production workflow.  
3. **Guest selection** – prioritize respected guests and make onboarding frictionless.  
4. **Content structure** – repeatable episode format (intro, core segments, key question, CTA) for faster production and listener familiarity.  
5. **Sustainable cadence** – commit to a publishing frequency you can maintain for at least 12 months (e.g., weekly).  
6. **Analytics** – track listens, guest impact, and topic performance; let data guide future episodes.  

### 2.3 Tool recommendation  
- Riverside’s built‑in editor offers text‑based, timeline, and AI‑chat editing, simplifying production and repurposing.  

## 3. The repurposing workflow to start your content flywheel  

### 3.1 Core three‑step flywheel  
1. **Record & edit** podcast audio/video in 4K directly in Riverside.  
2. **Distribute** the full episode to podcast directories (Spotify, Apple) and video platforms (YouTube).  
3. **Repurpose** using Riverside’s AI to create clips, blog posts, social posts; design infographics in Figma.  

### 3.2 Three practical repurposing workflows  
- **LinkedIn infographics** – AI extracts key insights, generates a brief; design in Figma; publish on LinkedIn.  
- **YouTube clips** – AI selects highlights; edit as needed; post clips on YouTube, Instagram, TikTok; drive traffic to landing page.  
- **Substack newsletter** – AI creates a content brief; publish insights across Substack’s formats (newsletter, notes, video, podcast).  

## Conclusion  
- Most B2B brands stall because founders are forced to write.  
- A podcast‑first strategy lets the founder speak once; an automated system turns that recording into a full content calendar (shorts, newsletters, infographics, LinkedIn posts, YouTube clips).  
- The result is a sustainable content flywheel that fuels demand without overburdening the founder.
