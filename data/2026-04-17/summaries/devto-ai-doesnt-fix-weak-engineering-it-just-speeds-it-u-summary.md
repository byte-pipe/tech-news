# AI Doesn't Fix Weak Engineering. It Just Speeds It Up. - DEV Community

**The Trap of Speed without Foundation**

The article discusses the limitations and pitfalls of using AI tools for engineering teams. The author shares an anecdote with their friend, a CMS platform developer, who cut through the hype around AI adoption on his customer base.

**Key Points**

* AI doesn't fix coordination problems or unclear ownership.
* AI only produces output faster, without addressing underlying issues.
* Teams that struggle with architectural decisions before adoption won't find a shortcut by using AI.
* AI reveals how little structure was present in the first place, rather than directly improving engineering processes.

**The Judgment Gap**

A crucial aspect of successful AI tooling in engineering is judgment. Strongly judgmental teams can evaluate what AI generates and recognize when it's not a suitable solution. However, teams without this judgment may make bad decisions by outsourcing those choices to AI.

**Comparison to the Customer Base**

The author shares that their friend's customer base has seen similar patterns emerge across multiple companies: teams struggling with architectural decisions before adoption didn't find a shortcut; they only found ways to compress their gaps into a tighter window. The same issue persists, but this time it's being addressed faster due to AI.

**Speed Without Foundation vs. Reflection and Judgment**

The author emphasizes that the initial boost from AI speed is not a solution in itself. A truly successful AI tooling process requires strong judgment, reflection, and ability to evaluate what was generated by the AI model.

**Reflection and Evaluation Required for Success**

To truly benefit from an AI-powered engineering tool, it's essential to:

* Recognize when AI-generated output isn't suitable
* Understand underlying trade-offs and decision-making processes
* Learn how to critically evaluate AI-generated outputs based on their context
* Prioritize reflection and judgment over speed

By acknowledging these limitations, teams can avoid relying on AI as a shortcut for complex engineering problems.
