# A Giant Cell Tower is Going to Space This Weekend

Amazon's Blue Origin plans to launch a giant cell tower in space on Sunday, April 19th. The mission aims to compete with SpaceX's Starlink and AST SpaceMobile's network expansion efforts.

## What Will be Launched

* New Glenn rocket
* First-stage booster
* AST SpaceMobile's BlueBird 7 satellite

### Key Features of the Satellite

* Massively large phased-array antenna (2,400 sq ft)
* Can provide 4G/5G broadband speeds exceeding 120 Mbps
* Expected to launch by the end of 2026 with up to 45-60 satellites

### How it Will Compete

* Amazon's Starlink will continue competing for users in the US
* Globalstar (partially owned by Amazon) is launching its service, available on Apple devices
* Other smaller satellite networks are likely following suit

## Background and Purpose

Amazon initially launched Leo missions to provide 247 satellites last year. However, its plans have been overshadowed by SpaceX's success with their reusable launch vehicles.
Blue Origin aims to fill a gap in the market for a network of affordable, reliable cell towers in low Earth orbit (LEO).

### Space Industry Trends and Disruption

The launch marks an interesting development in the competitive space industry. The ability of companies like AST SpaceMobile to establish a global satellite network signals a shift towards increased competition and innovation.

## Takeaway: The weekend's launch is significant for Amazon as it seeks to expand its services and keep pace with competitors, while demonstrating its capabilities in LEO-based communication technologies.
