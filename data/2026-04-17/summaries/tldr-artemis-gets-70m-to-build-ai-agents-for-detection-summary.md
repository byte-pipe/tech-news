# Artemis Gets $70M to Build AI Agents for Detection, Response

## Overview

* A $70 million Series A funding round to build AI agents that can detect threats across cloud environments, identity systems, and endpoints.
* Artemis aims to overcome fragmentation in traditional security architectures where tools fail to provide a cohesive view of attacker behavior.

## Key Points

- Artemis, a new startup, is backed by Felicis with $70 million for its AI-based agent technology.
- The funding will enable the development of correlated telemetry data across multiple systems and environments.
- Co-founders Shachar Hirshberg and Dan Shiebler leverage experience in Amazon's GuardDuty threat detection service to build on existing technology.
- Artemis employs 30 people, has a $15 million seed round, and is led by its founder.

## Benefits

- **Improved Detection Accuracy**: Artemis' dynamically generated and tuned detections based on customer-specific environment characteristics.
- **Simplified Security Operations**: Natural language interfaces simplify security operations for analysts.
- **Reduced Technical Barrier to Entry**: Enables accelerations in workflows without requiring significant expertise or time.

### Conclusion

Artemis emerges as a promising technology that addresses traditional security fragmentation by providing AI-based agents with correlations and insights. With its $70 million funding, the company is poised to bring these capabilities to multiple industries.
