# Your Job Isn't Going Away. But Someone's Fundraise Depends on You Thinking It Is. - DEV Community

**AI Narrative and Capital Valuations**

The development and valuation of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies have been a topic of public discourse, often perpetuated by companies in the field. However, there are underlying logical patterns that contribute to these narratives.

### Jevons Paradox

A classic economic phenomenon known as Jevons Paradox states that if a technology becomes cheaper and more efficient, people typically use it less and instead adopt new forms of innovation. This can create an upward spiral where the emergence of new uses for AI leads to increased demand, driving further innovation.

The example provided in the passage highlights this scenario with two historical examples: ATMs and spreadsheets. In each case, a perceived replacement of human workers led to the evolution or expansion of their roles within industries.

### The Role of Capital

The narrative of AI replacing high-value white-collar workers is also fueled by capitalization and valuations. Large technology companies are willing to invest in research and development at significant costs, seeking higher returns on investment (ROI). By positioning themselves as a necessary tool for businesses, these companies aim to increase their market share and subsequently raise valuations.

This approach is exemplified by the tech firms mentioned in the passage, such as OpenAI, Anthropic, and Sand Hill Road. These companies are engaged in an active arms race with other large technology firms, seeking to outperform them at every possible cost – including resource allocation and talent acquisition.

### Conclusion

Understanding Jevons Paradox and how it influences AI capital valuations is essential. The math underpinning companies' fundraising efforts assumes an expectation of replacement, which may not necessarily hold true given the complex interplay between technology adoption rates, economic cycles, and market dynamics. Companies that have successfully adapted these concepts in a contextually relevant manner often achieve significant valuation growth while remaining committed to innovation-driven product development.
