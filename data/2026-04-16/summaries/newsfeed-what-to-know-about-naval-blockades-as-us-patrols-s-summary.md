# What to know about naval blockades as U.S. patrols Strait of Hormuz : NPR

**Understanding Naval Blockades in the Strait of Hormuz**

• **The US wants to blockade Iran**: The White House aims to strangle Iran's economy by cutting off its main source of revenue: oil exports.

• **Historical context**: Naval blockades have been used in the past, such as during the French Revolution and the Napoleonic Wars, but enforcing them is often challenging.

• **Challenges with current strategy**: Key questions remain unanswered about how to sustain a large-scale naval blockade in the Strait of Hormuz, where Iranian vessels operate freely nearby.

• **Iran's response**: Despite US efforts to stop shipping to and from Iran, President Trump argues that it would effectively strangle Iran's ability to export oil and labeled the tactic as a blockade instead of a quarantine.

• **The economics of blockades**: The US is allegedly targeting an essential good (oil) to weaken the enemy's economy, a classic strategy used by countries to exert pressure on others.

• **Lessons learned from history**: Naval blockades have not always been effective in the past. There are limitations to how effectively they can block or interrupt shipping traffic.

**Blockades in History and Reality**

• **Coordinated patrols are more common in modern naval warfare**: Effective enforcement relies on coordinated military efforts rather than relying solely on individual vessels.

• **Naval blockades often fail due to logistics and operational challenges**: Successful blockade operations require significant logistical support, including naval forces, ground troops, communication networks, and precise targeting systems.

• **A well-planned blockade can be an effective strategy in theory; its success is uncertain in practice**.
