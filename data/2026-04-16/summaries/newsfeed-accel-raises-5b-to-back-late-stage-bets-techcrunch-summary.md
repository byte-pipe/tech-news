# Accel raises $5B to back late-stage bets | TechCrunch

**Accel Raises $5B to Back Late-Stage Companies**

Accel, a venture capital firm focused on AI-powered technology, has raised $5 billion in fresh capital. The investment will be made through its Leaders Fund. Accel is targeting late-stage companies that have developed software and hardware solutions for various industries.

The fund aims to cut at least 20 checks worth $200 million each. Accel is investing in companies such as Anthropic, Perplexity, and Lovable. This move comes as technology continues to boom, with increased investment in AI-focused startups.

Accel limited partners have also contributed $650 million, which will be split between the Leaders Fund and a sidecar fund to increase investments in certain companies.

This latest raise falls in line with Accel's efforts to keep up with the competition in the late-stage funding market. The firm has backed over 800 companies since its inception, indicating a strong interest in AI-driven innovation.

### Key Points:

* $5 billion raised through the Leaders Fund
* Targets late-stage companies developing software and hardware for various industries
* Investing in companies such as Anthropic, Perplexity, Lovable
* Increased investments allowed by split allocation between Leaders Fund and sidecar fund
