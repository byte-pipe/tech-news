# BTC, ETH, XRP fall as U.S., Iran negotiators fail to reach war resolution

# Markets
## US & Iran Negotiators Fail to Reach War Resolution

### Key Points:

* U.S. Vice President J.D. Vance announced that negotiations with Iran failed after a daylong session in Pakistan, resulting in Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies falling by 2% overnight.
* The parties met to discuss an extended ceasefire for the ongoing conflict but were unable to reach an agreement on key issues.
* Vice President Vance stated that the U.S. has "made very clear what our red lines are" and had not reached a deal.

## How the Failure Impacted Cryptocurrency Prices

* Bitcoin (BTC) traded at $71,600 as of press time after falling around 2% in response to news of the failed negotiations.
* Ethereum (ETH) fell to $2,200, while XRP (XRP) dropped to $1.33 and the CoinDesk 20 index slipped to 1,188.52.

## Sticking Points in the Negotiations

* The U.S. insisted on Iranian non-nuclear ambitions, but Iran countered by suggesting limiting war reparations and lifting sanctions.
* Other sticking points included tensions over the Strait of Hormuz and nuclear issues.

### Reaction from Iran

* Emphasized that discussions were ongoing between Iranian officials and U.S. negotiators, but pointed out progress in establishing relationships with key parties.
* Indicated preparation for further action if necessary.
