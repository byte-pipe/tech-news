## # Anna's Archive Loses $322 Million Spotify Piracy Case Without a Fight /$150 Million in Damages Awarded Against Unknown Operators

The website Anna's Archive, previously known for its meta-search engine functions, has faced intense legal scrutiny after announcing it had backpedaled on integrating metadata from other platforms like Spotify. This decision sparked substantial repercussions across the music industry.

### **Background**

- Initially, Anna's Archive provided basic information about books available through its library due to a lack of action from the music industry.
- Following concerns and lawsuits, it began working with major players in the music sector by integrating Spotify metadata into a shadow network for discovery and retrieval of relevant material.

### **The Fallout: A $322 Million Default Judgment**

On January 17th, 2024, Judge Jed Rakoff of the Southern District of New York entered a default judgment against the unknown operators of Anna's Archive in its favor. The court has awarded Spotify and major music labels the estimated amount of $150 million each as statutory damages for willful copyright infringement.

The total verdict stands at over $322 million for the entire organization.

### **Damages Request**

Although Spotify initially sought an extremely conservative $300 million in damages, Judge Rakoff's decision indicates that they were more lenient due to a lower number of works and files affected. The operator of Anna's Archive allegedly requested this lesser amount due to concerns over defending their entity without revealing identities or clear actions taken against clients.

### **Other Consequences**

- Spotify has removed the listing for its torrents page.
- Removals of the music files have also ceased as ordered by the court after initially prompting an unwanted increase in the company's assets with payments sought under domain names previously registered for domains associated with the site.
