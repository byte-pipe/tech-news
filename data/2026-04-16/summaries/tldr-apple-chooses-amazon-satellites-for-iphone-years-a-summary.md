# Apple chooses Amazon satellites for iPhone, years after rejecting Starlink offer - Ars Technica

# Amazon Joins Forces with Apple for Mobile-Satellite Connectivity

In a significant move, Amazon announced two new satellite deals that will enhance its competitive position against SpaceX's Starlink. The company signed an agreement to purchase satellite operator Globalstar and entered into another deal with Apple to provide satellite service for iPhones and Apple Watches.

**Key Points:**

* Amazon acquired Globalstar, a satellite network operator currently offering messaging services on the iPhone.
* Globalstar has partnered with Apple previously, providing satellite connectivity features such as Emergency SOS and Messages on the apple device.
* By acquiring Globalstar, Amazon hopes to expand its Direct-to-Device (D2D) market, where satellites provide connectivity to mobile phones.

**Satellite Service Features:**

* With the new deal, Apple will benefit from improved satellite service features, including:
	+ Emergency SOS
	+ Messages
	+ Find My
	+ Roadside Assistance via satellite

**Amazon's Expansion Plans:**

* Amazon plans to utilize Globalstar's expanded satellite network for additional satellite connectivity features.
* Globalstar stockholders will vote on whether to accept the deal, offering $90 per share in cash or 0.3210 shares of Amazon common stock.

## Satellite Market Competition

* Globalstar operates at a smaller scale than SpaceX's Starlink, currently with over 10,000 satellites in orbit and plans for many more.
* The acquisition by Apple adds significant value to Samsung's mobile satellite technology platform
