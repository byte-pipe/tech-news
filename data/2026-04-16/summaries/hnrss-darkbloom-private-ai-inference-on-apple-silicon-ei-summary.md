# Eggshell: Decentralizing AI Inference on Apple Silicon
=====================================================

## Private inference network for Apple Silicon idle machines

We propose Darkbloom, a decentralized inference network that connects direct suppliers of idle Apple Silicon machines with buyers who demand AI compute services.

## Key Points
### 1. Avoid hyperscaling middlemen and concentrates wealth and access

Darkbloom bypasses third-party hyperscalers, API providers, and end-users in the supply chain, reducing costs for hardware owners to a mere 70% of centralized alternatives.

### 2. OpenAI-compatible API compatible with other networks

Our network enables AI compute services directly through open APIs, allowing seamless interoperability with existing systems.

## Motivation
### 1. Existing tech problems: market and marginal cost
The current system is dominated by a small number of companies controlling the supply, resulting in high prices and lack of access for most users.

### 2. Market pattern recognition
Similar to Airbnb, Uber, or rooftop solar, Darkbloom takes idle capacities and uncovers centralized incumbents on price due to near-zero marginal costs.

## Problem Statement
Decentralizing AI compute services requires addressing trust issues between buyers and sellers in the network.

## The Challenge
### 1. Trust: Establishing intermediary credibility

Darkbloom must overcome difficulties in establishing trust between hardware owners, who often operate with proprietary data, and buyers who typically do not have a vested interest in the integrity of their AI compute resources.

This is where decentralization comes in. By providing an open API and leveraging existing infrastructure (Apple Silicon machines), we create a decentralized network that empowers operators to directly interact with each other without relying on intermediaries.
