# 6 Proven Tips to Find Cheaper Airfare

## Go, Go, Go!
- Book early: domestic flights 4‑6 weeks out, international about 3 months out; now aim 3‑7 months (domestic) or 4‑10 months (international) for summer trips.  
- Act quickly because airlines are cutting routes and raising prices as fuel costs rise.  
- Airline consolidation may reduce competition, leading to higher fares.

## Go Where Everyone Else Is Going
- Choose high‑competition routes (e.g., NY‑LA, LA‑SF, ATL‑Orlando, LV‑LA) where multiple carriers keep prices down.  
- If you’re far from a major hub, consider driving or taking a train to a nearby hub to catch cheaper flights.

## Get Flexible
- Use flexible‑date search tools like Google Explore: enter departure airport and a time window, then compare destination prices.  
- This helps spot low‑cost options such as a weekend trip from Oklahoma City to Las Vegas or Miami to New York in mid‑May.

## Pack Strategically
- Airlines are raising baggage fees, especially for the third checked bag (e.g., $150 → $200).  
- Reduce checked luggage, roll clothes instead of folding, and consider a more efficient carry‑on bag (WIRED lists top picks).

## Check Your Rewards
- Review credit‑card benefits: free or discounted checked bags, lounge access, trip insurance, and waived foreign‑transaction fees can lower overall travel costs.

## Don’t Expect Holiday Discounts
- Airlines rarely slash fares around holidays; waiting often results in higher prices.  
- Book summer travel now rather than hoping for a seasonal sale.

## Don’t Fly at All
- Consider alternative modes such as train or bus, which can be cheaper and avoid the discomfort of flying.
