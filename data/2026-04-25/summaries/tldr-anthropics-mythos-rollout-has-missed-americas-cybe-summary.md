# Anthropic’s Mythos rollout has missed America’s cybersecurity agency  

## Key developments  
- **CISA’s exclusion**: The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) does not have access to Anthropic’s “Mythos Preview,” a model marketed for discovering and patching security vulnerabilities.  
- **Other agencies have access**: The U.S. Commerce Department and the National Security Agency (NSA) are reported to be using the model, and the Trump administration has been negotiating broader governmental access.  
- **Anthropic’s statements**: In a blog post, Anthropic said it is in “ongoing discussions with US government officials” about Mythos’s offensive and defensive cyber capabilities. An Anthropic spokesperson told Axios that CISA was among the agencies briefed, though the company declined further comment.  

## Context of CISA’s marginalisation  
- **Political pressure**: Since the 2020 election, CISA has faced attacks from the Trump administration and congressional Republicans, especially after labeling the election “the most secure in American history.”  
- **Leadership changes**: Trump’s first administration removed the agency’s head.  
- **Resource cuts**: CISA has lost staff due to Department of Government Efficiency cost‑cutting and has had personnel reassigned to immigration work within DHS. The acting director told Congress that detection resources are limited amid a DHS shutdown, and the administration seeks to trim hundreds of millions more from its budget.  

## Implications for cybersecurity  
- **Risk to critical infrastructure**: CISA is the central coordinator for cybersecurity information, helping state and local officials protect elections, utilities, and other essential services. Its lack of access to a tool that “has found security issues in every major operating system and web browser,” according to Anthropic, raises concerns about the nation’s defensive posture.  
- **Limited rollout strategy**: Anthropic is providing restricted access to Mythos to give “key institutions a head start” on cyber defenses, as explained by Newton Cheng, Anthropic’s frontier red‑team cyber lead. CISA’s current exclusion suggests it will not benefit from this early advantage.
