# Australian banks warned frontier AI could create larger, faster cyber attacks

## Summary
- The Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA) told banks that their information‑security practices are lagging behind the rapid development of frontier AI models such as Anthropic’s Claude Mythos.  
- APRA warned that such models could help malicious actors discover vulnerabilities more quickly, increasing the probability, speed and scale of cyber attacks on the financial sector.  
- Banks are said to rely heavily on AI model presentations from vendors without fully assessing associated risks, and many board members lack the technical literacy needed for effective AI oversight.  
- While banks claim they have strict security procedures and invest billions of dollars annually in cyber defence, APRA notes that some of these controls are not designed to keep pace with evolving AI capabilities.  
- The Australian government is collaborating with software providers, including Anthropic, to address potential cybersecurity vulnerabilities.  
- Separately, S&P Global cautioned that AI will influence the credit standing of Asia‑Pacific financial institutions over the next one to five years, though large technology budgets may mitigate negative effects.

## Key Details
- **APRA’s assessment**: A recent review found most banks’ security practices struggling to match AI’s rapid evolution; boards are still building the technical understanding required for AI risk oversight.  
- **Frontier AI risk**: Claude Mythos, with high‑level coding abilities, could enable attackers to identify and exploit system weaknesses faster than before.  
- **Industry response**: Australian Banking Association chief executive Simon Birmingham said banks continuously assess cyber‑risk settings and are well‑positioned to respond to emerging AI technologies.  
- **Government involvement**: A Home Affairs spokesperson confirmed ongoing work with Anthropic and other tech firms on cybersecurity concerns.  
- **Regional outlook**: S&P Global noted AI’s impact on credit ratings for Asia‑Pacific banks will vary, but substantial technology spending should help offset potential downsides.
