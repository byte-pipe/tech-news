# Antigua and Barbuda prime minister set to win fourth term in election

## Overview
- Snap general election held in Antigua and Barbuda.
- Prime Minister Gaston Browne is on track to secure a fourth term.
- Preliminary results indicate his Antigua and Barbuda Labour Party (ABLP) will win 15 of the 17 parliamentary seats.

## Election Results
- **ABLP:** Projected to hold 15 seats.
- **United Progressive Party (UPP):** Only leader Jamale Pringle retained his seat; the party lost all other constituencies.
- **Barbuda People’s Movement:** Leader Trevor Walker won the Barbuda constituency.
- Five former UPP members defected to the ABLP before the vote; one, Lamin Newton, was defeated by Pringle.

## Political Context
- Campaign took place amid shifting party loyalties and concerns over cost‑of‑living pressures.
- Global issues such as US visa restrictions and rising fuel prices due to Middle‑East conflict were cited as influencing voter sentiment.
- Three by‑elections occurred since the 2023 general election, including one triggered by the death of independent MP Asot Michael.

## Opposition Issues
- The opposition highlighted the Trump administration’s suspension of US visa processing for Antiguan and Barbudan nationals, linking it to concerns over the citizenship‑by‑investment programme.
- The government responded that reforms had been introduced to make the investment scheme more transparent and that it was cooperating with Washington.

## Analyst Commentary
- Caribbean political analyst Peter Wickham described Browne’s victory as “impressive,” attributing it to disarray and defections within the opposition.
- Wickham noted that the single seat retained by Pringle reflects limited public support for the UPP.
- He also observed that voters likely viewed US visa restrictions as beyond the Antiguan government’s control, diminishing their electoral impact.
