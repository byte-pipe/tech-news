# Spotify adds 'Verified' badges to distinguish human artists from AI

## Key points
- Spotify will display a “Verified by Spotify” label with a green checkmark next to artist names that meet authenticity standards (e.g., linked social accounts, merchandise, concert dates, consistent listener activity).  
- The company says over 99 % of artists that users actively search for will receive the badge, covering “hundreds of thousands of artists” and prioritising those with significant cultural contributions.  
- The rollout will occur over the coming weeks and aims to separate human creators from AI‑generated personas.

## Reactions and concerns
- Critics note the badge only confirms a human creator, not whether the music itself is AI‑free.  
- Creator‑rights advocate Ed Newton‑Rex warns the criteria could disadvantage genuine artists lacking touring or merch, and suggests automatic labeling of AI‑generated tracks instead.  
- Music professor Nick Collins calls the move unsurprising given the AI debate, but highlights the difficulty of labeling music that may be partially AI‑assisted and worries the system may favor established, commercial artists over independents.

## Context of Spotify’s AI challenges
- Spotify has faced criticism for handling AI‑generated content, with users requesting clear AI labels and developers building tools to block such music.  
- In 2023, CEO Daniel Ek said there were no plans to ban AI content.  
- A 2025 incident involved the band The Velvet Sundown, whose verified profile was later re‑identified as a “synthetic music project” after accusations of AI‑generated music.  

## Additional notes
- The verification badge does not guarantee AI‑free music; it merely signals a verified human presence behind the artist profile.
