# A Dark‑Money Campaign Is Paying Influencers to Frame Chinese AI as a Threat | WIRED  

## Campaign overview  
- Build American AI, a dark‑money group linked to the $100 million super‑PAC **Leading the Future**, is running a two‑phase influencer marketing operation.  
- Phase 1 (April 2024) used lifestyle influencers to promote “American‑made AI” and US innovation.  
- Phase 2 shifts focus to portraying China’s AI development as a national security and economic threat.  

## Key players and funding  
- **Leading the Future**: PAC backed by tech figures associated with OpenAI, Palantir, Andreessen Horowitz, and Perplexity; reports $140 million in contributions, $51 million earmarked for pro‑AI lobbying.  
- **Build American AI**: The operational arm that contracts influencer agency **SM4** to recruit creators.  
- Individual influencers mentioned: Melissa Strahle (1.4 M IG followers), Megan Linke, Uche Madson, Josh Murphy, among others.  
- Payments reported at roughly $5,000 per TikTok video; contracts labeled as advertisements but without disclosure of the sponsoring organization.  

## Messaging supplied to creators  
- Sample script: “China is trying hard to beat the US in AI. If they succeed, they could get personal data from me and my kids and take jobs that should be here. I’m Team USA!”  
- Influencers are instructed to blend the anti‑China narrative with everyday activities (e.g., making breakfast) to appear authentic.  
- Briefing documents call for “left‑leaning” and “right‑leaning” creators to discuss the need to protect Americans in the AI race.  

## Influencer participation and response  
- Many influencers posted AI‑positive videos while labeling them as ads, yet omitted who paid them.  
- Some, like Josh Murphy, declined offers, noting discomfort with “aggressive anti‑China messaging.”  
- Most contacted creators did not respond to WIRED’s request for comment.  

## Political context and strategy  
- AI is emerging as a central issue for the 2026 midterm elections; industry groups aim to counter public concerns about data privacy, energy use, and job displacement.  
- Polling (Pew Research) shows 53 % of U.S. adults get news from social media; 38 % of 18‑29‑year‑olds rely on influencers for news.  
- The campaign seeks to shape public debate without journalistic disclosure requirements, exploiting the lack of ethical standards for influencer content.  

## Reactions and denials  
- OpenAI and Palantir publicly state they have no financial ties to Build American AI or Leading the Future.  
- Perplexity declined comment; Andreessen Horowitz did not respond.  
- Academic expert Jamie Cohen (Queens College) warns that undisclosed paid political messaging “is extremely corrosive to democracy.”  

## Implications  
- The operation illustrates how dark‑money groups can weaponize influencer marketing to push industry‑friendly policy positions and frame geopolitical rivals as threats.  
- Lack of transparency means many social‑media users are exposed to political propaganda presented as personal endorsement.  
- The campaign’s success could influence future regulatory debates on AI by amplifying a pro‑U.S., anti‑China narrative among broad, non‑traditional news audiences.
