# AI agents are forcing enterprises to overhaul their operations

## Dive Brief
- A Gartner report (survey of 469 CEOs and executives, late 2025) finds 80% of CEOs expect AI automation to drive major changes as companies move from digital to autonomous models.  
- Some CEOs warn AI agents could disrupt profit models by bypassing intermediaries and price negotiations, pushing firms toward recurring, outcome‑based revenue streams.  
- Gartner’s senior principal analyst Jennifer Carter says executives must rethink how work is performed and how human value is delivered, focusing on the specialized, intrinsic contributions employees can make.

## Dive Insight
- Enterprises are in early‑stage, large‑scale deployment of autonomous AI but plan to expand usage markedly by 2028.  
- Over 50% of CEOs see automation now limited to specific tasks; only 13% expect that to remain the case by end‑2028.  
- One‑third anticipate AI tools that learn and adapt will assist human decision‑making, while more than a quarter aim for AI‑driven processes with minimal human involvement.  
- Tech leaders feel pressure: 61% fear job loss if they cannot successfully lead AI adoption, and some worry their own skills may become obsolete.  
- Executives believe AI can deepen relationships with both human and machine customers; Gartner predicts growth in dedicated business units serving machine customers.  
- Increased reliance on AI agents heightens the need for trust, accuracy, and data integrity; poor “AI hygiene” could have lasting negative effects.  
- CIOs will need to balance internal operational reviews with external market shifts, ensuring their autonomous ecosystems align with customer needs.  
- Roles across the organization will evolve: agent management will integrate into workflows, freeing teams to focus on uniquely human, high‑value activities and eliminating routine drudgery.
