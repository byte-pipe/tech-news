# BYD’s Fastest-Charging Car in the World Is Astonishing—in Good and Bad Ways | WIRED

# Summary of “BYD’s Fastest-Charging Car in the World Is Astonishing—in Good and Bad Ways | WIRED”

## Main Highlights
- The Denza Z9 GT, BYD’s premium EV, can charge from 10 % to 100 % in just over nine minutes, far outpacing any current competitor.
- The car combines extreme fast‑charging capability with high performance (1,140 bhp, 0‑62 mph in 2.7 s) and advanced driver‑assistance features.
- BYD is positioning the Z9 GT as a European challenger to Porsche and Polestar, but the €115,000 price tag raises serious questions about brand perception and value.

## Charging Technology
- **Flash charging** pushes up to 1.5 MW (1,500 kW) into the new Blade Battery 2.0, compared with Tesla’s 325 kW Superchargers.
- Battery design uses multi‑sized particle cathodes and reduced internal resistance, halving heat generation.
- Upgraded thermal‑management and self‑repairing systems keep the lithium‑iron‑phosphate (LFP) chemistry stable over many cycles.
- Planned network: 6,000 global Flash‑charging sites outside China, with 600 in the UK initially; 10‑70 % charge could drop from 30 min to 5 min, full charge in nine minutes.
- Current EU deployments operate at roughly two‑thirds capacity, with potential for a further 30 % increase.

## Performance & Features
- Powertrain: 309 bhp front motor + two 416 bhp rear motors = 1,140 bhp total.
- Top speed 168 mph; 0‑62 mph in 2.7 s.
- Unique capabilities: crab‑walk, on‑spot U‑turn, “performance park” (front‑first driving with rear following).
- Range: about 372 miles (≈3 mi/kWh on test drive).
- L2+ autonomous driving with roof‑mounted lidar for future upgrades.
- Interior luxuries: massaging seats for driver and rear passengers, 1,100‑watt 20‑speaker Devialet audio system.
- Ride: confident but slightly soft; heavy steering due to all‑wheel setup; vehicle weight 2.9 tons.

## Pricing & Market Strategy
- European price: €115,000 (~US $134,000), far higher than the same model in Australia (£55,000) or China (£45,000).
- BYD cites “market contextualization” as the reason for the premium pricing, aiming to compete directly with high‑end German marques.
- The strategy abandons BYD’s traditional “more for less” value proposition, risking brand trust among six‑figure buyers.
- Potential resale‑value concerns: premium EVs (e.g., Porsche Taycan) can lose up to 50 % in a year; BYD hopes the “wow factor” of flash charging will mitigate depreciation.

## Criticisms & Verdict
- Design: exterior described as confusing and not particularly attractive; interior feels over‑engineered for the price.
- Value: despite the groundbreaking charging speed and many tech bells, the overall experience does not justify the €115,000 price tag.
- The reviewer concludes the Z9 GT is a remarkable technical showcase but not a compelling purchase at its current cost, especially when comparable BYD models are far cheaper in other markets.
