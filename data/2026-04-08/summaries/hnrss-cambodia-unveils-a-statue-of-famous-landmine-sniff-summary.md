# Cambodia Unveils Statue to Honour Famous Landmine-Sniffing Rat

Cambodia has unveiled a statue of Magawa, a famous African giant pouched rat who was trained by the Belgian charity Apopo and cleared over 20 football pitches before his death in 2022. The monument is part of a larger effort to make Cambodia mine-free by 2030.

## Key Points:

- Magawa was a hero landmine-sniffing rat who cleared over 100 landmines during his five-year career (2016-2021).
- He was awarded the PDSA Gold Medal in 2020 for his life-saving devotion to duty.
- After retiring due to old age and "slowing down", Magawa died peacefully at the age of eight.
- Apopo's Cambodia Programme Manager, Michael Raine, said that Magawa's statue is a reminder of the importance of mine-clearing efforts.
