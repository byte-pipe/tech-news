# A Truck Driver Spent 20 Years Making This Astonishing Scale Model of Every Single Building in New York City

# 20-Year Model: A Marvelous Scale Replica of New York City's Infrastructure


## Overview
The article tells the story of Joseph Macken, a truck driver from Clifton Park, New York. He created an astonishing scale model of New York City, covering 1,350 square feet, over two decades.


## Key Facts

*   Made by Joseph Macken: Created the miniature replica as a way to stay connected to New York City.
*   Material and Scale: Used balsa wood, wooden buildings, painted parks, and artificial trees to create each section.
*   Length of Build Project
The Model
Macken began building his first model out of 300 square feet, which was used for 15 years.


## **Viral Success**
Last summer, Macken's work went viral on social media platforms like TikTok. Many people commented that the model could be put in a museum, indicating its value and recognition.

The finished product
The completed miniature replica took over more than two decades to build.
After completing the larger structure, there were nearly 100 other miniatures created.
