Project Glasswing: Anthropic Debuts New AI Model for Cybersecurity Partnership with Multiple Companies

Anthropic, an artificial intelligence company, has announced the launch of Project Glasswing, a novel AI technology that flags vulnerabilities in various operating systems and web browsers. The new model is part of a partnership with Nvidia, Google, Amazon Web Services, Apple, Microsoft, and other major companies.

* Key Points:
  - Project Glasswing enables large companies to identify high-stakes vulnerabilities virtually without human intervention.
  - The model uses general-purpose AI that can analyze systems to spot weak points.
  - Claude Mythos Preview, the new AI model, has flagged "thousands of high-severity vulnerabilities" across various operating systems and web browsers, including Google's Android and Amazon Web Services' AWS.
* What makes Project Glasswing distinct:
  - Not specifically trained for cybersecurity purposes; instead, it identifies weaknesses independently using its "strong agentic coding and reasoning skills".
  - No human involvement is required to analyze vulnerability risks or develop fixes.

Anthropic has offered their launch partners access to Claude Mythos Preview, a partially released AI model, but notes that individuals are not yet allowed to use it for its intended purpose of identifying cybersecurity threats.
