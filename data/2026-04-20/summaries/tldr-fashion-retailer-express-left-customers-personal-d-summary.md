# Fashion retailer Express left customers' personal data and order details exposed to the internet | TechCrunch

**Fashion Giant Express Fixes Security Flaw Exposing Customer Order Details**

The fashion retailer Express has patched a significant security flaw that allowed individuals to view customer order details and personal information, including names, phone numbers, email addresses, and mailing addresses. The issue was revealed on the online store website after an accidental discovery by a security and privacy advocate who found an unsecured web page containing exposed data.

**Affected Information Includes:**

* Customer names
* Phone numbers
* Email addresses
* Postal and billing addresses
* Order details, including item information and partial payment card information (e.g., card type and last four digits)

The exposure occurred because the website's order confirmation pages contained sequential order numbers that could be used to access other customers' orders. The issue is consistent with security lapses seen recently in similar instances where customer data was compromised due to misconfigurations or inadvertent security breaches.

**Express Takes Steps to Address Security Flaw:**

The company has fixed the flaw and informed customers of the incident, but its decision not to notify all affected individuals about the vulnerability leaves ongoing concerns.
