**Jeremy Hansen Puts French Language Row to Rest: Canadian Astronaut Makes History in Space**

In a significant moment of linguistic innovation, Canadian astronaut Jeremy Hansen surprised everyone on July 20, 2023, by speaking two words of French during his mission to the moon with NASA's Artemis I spacecraft. This historic event marked the first time French has been spoken from nearly 125 million miles away.

Hansen's decision came after a linguistic row and public relations nightmare emerged for Canada's flagship airline when Air Canada chief Michael Rousseau was forced to resign last month for speaking only two words of French (hello) in a video tribute to two pilots killed in a crash. Although Rousseau had a francophone mother and wife, there was no French language training or proficiency mentioned.

Hansen's statement, broadcast on a NASA press conference as the spacecraft hurtled towards the moon, brought widespread recognition and praise, including from Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney who welcomed Hansen to space with words in fluent French. "It's incredibly proud of what you're doing," Carney said.

The incident highlighted the importance of language and its connection to a nation's culture and history. With this momentous achievement, Hansen has made history as Canada's first astronaut to speak multiple languages in deep space.
