# AI, Confluence Docs, and READMEs: Why AI Written Docs End Up Unread - DEV Community

## The Impact of AI on Technical Documentation in Confluence

AI has made significant contributions to technical documentation in Confluence, including writing code, generating tests, summarizing pull requests, and producing READMEs. However, its use should be approached with caution, as it can sometimes produce content that is not truly consumable.

### Benefits of AI in Documentation

*   AI can generate structure from nothing, creating consistent templates and translations.
*   It can fill in obvious sections to provide a starting point for documentation effort.
*   Speed can be rapid, especially when compared to manual writing.

### Limitations of AI in Documentation

*   When AI-generated content lacks articulation or clarity, it fails the test of long-term retention.
*   The output can feel overly verbose, abstract, and dense with concepts that do not anchor themselves in memory.
*   AI often equates length with usefulness, leading to excessive verbosity without adding value.

## Common Pitfalls of AI-Generated Documentation

*   **Lack of context**: AI-generated content may lack the nuance and depth needed for technical explanation.
*   **Information density**: Overly dense, repetitive ideas can make documentation feel dry or cumbersome.
*   **Lack of human judgment**: AI may not always grasp complex situations or nuanced contexts.

## Best Practices for Using AI in Documentation

To maximize the benefits of AI-generated documentation without compromising its effectiveness:

1.  **Integrate with existing content management systems**: Embed AI-powered documentation tools within Confluence to create a seamless workflow.
2.  **Use feedback loops**: Regularly update and refine AI-generated documents through user input, providing clearer guidance when needed.
3.  **Prioritize readability**: Strive for concise, clear language that aligns with the needs of various users.

By recognizing both the benefits and limitations of AI in documentation and adopting a balanced approach, teams can maximize the value of these tools while minimizing potential drawbacks.
