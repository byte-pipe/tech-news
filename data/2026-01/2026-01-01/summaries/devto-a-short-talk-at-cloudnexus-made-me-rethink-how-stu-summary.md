# A short talk at CloudNexus made me rethink how students are using AI - DEV Community

**Summary**

The author, speaking at an AWS Cloud Club QAU event, discussed the challenges of teaching Agentic AI to students. The attendees' confidence in using AI tools was surprising, as most were not utilizing them for understanding and learning concepts. However, they relied heavily on code generation rather than productive use.

The author emphasized that while chat-based tools like ChatGPT are great for quick answers, coding copilot tools (like Amazon Q) are more valuable for development work due to their context-aware nature and integration with AWS environments.

Agentic AI is not just about prompts but delegation of tasks. It requires infrastructure, scalability, and security. The author suggested using cloud AI as a platform that enables real-world applications while leveraging agents when specific outcomes are needed.

**Key Points**

* Agentic AI requires more than simple chat-based tools
* Students rely heavily on code generation rather than productive use of AI

* Cloud AI should be used as a platform to enable real-world applications, while leveraging agents when specific outcomes are needed
* Agentic AI requires infrastructure, scalability, and security
