# AI, Confluence Docs, and READMEs: Why AI Written Docs End Up Unread - DEV Community

**The Promise and Pitfalls of AI in Documentation**

AI has become increasingly integrated into our developer workflows, automating tasks such as writing code, generating tests, summarizing pull requests, and creating documentation. While faster content is often touted as an advantage, the reality is more complex.

    **The Benefits of AI-Assisted Documentation**

For non-technical teams under time pressure, AI-assisted tools can save significant time and effort, particularly when it comes to structuring unstructured material or translating code into text. Additionally, AI can fill in obvious sections to provide immediate clarity.

    **Disappointing Experience with AI Documentation**

However, many of these speed gains come at the cost of usability. When teams revisit their AI-generated documentation after a few days, they often find it difficult to understand the content's meaning. The output lacks explicit explanations and fails to anchor concepts in their memory.

**Key Takeaways from Experience**

    - AI-generated documentation is not effective when trying to articulate complex ideas.
    - Verbosity does not necessarily equate to usability or understanding; clear mental models, concise guidance, and simple explanations are needed instead.
    - While speed gains can be beneficial, they must be balanced against the need for ease of understanding.

**Conclusion**

The integration of AI into documentation has raised important questions about its effectiveness for conveying complex information. To truly make AI-assisted tools shine, developers must prioritize clear explanation, concise guidance, and simple concepts that stick in users' minds.
