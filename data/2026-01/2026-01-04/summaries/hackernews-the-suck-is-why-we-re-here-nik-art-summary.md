### Catching Up and Reflections on Writing with AI

I started the conversation by showcasing my blog, sharing my approach as a writer and researcher. Nick Wignall expressed interest in using an AI model to mimic my style. The idea seemed like a research exercise, training the tool with past work, then providing it with headlines and paragraphs from future pieces. While I compared some initial output to their originals, I found several issues.

### The Uncanny Valley Effect

As more comparisons were made, I noticed that writing generated by AI started to exhibit characteristics similar to those of AI-generated images. It would seem fine at first glance but often veer off in an unexpected direction or present a contrary argument just as one was questioning it. This pattern felt like something familiar – the uncanny valley effect.

### The Need for Unique Content

To counter this perception, I emphasized that my writing is more than simply generating output with AI; it requires effort and dedication to understand and convey ideas effectively. My daily blog serves as a creative ritual strengthening my writing muscles and commitment to my readers. Nick pointed out that he himself relies on human research instead of AI to achieve his goals.

### The Significance of Human Research

According to Ezra Klein, writer and founder of Vox, while AI can assist with specific tasks like research or data structure, the quality of output generated by these models depends heavily on the researcher doing the original work. This makes reading books in their entirety more valuable for gaining insightful connections that an AI summary would not capture.

### Staying Engaged Through Writing

Nick stressed the importance of writing to stay engaged and maintain creative momentum. For him, using AI is not about outsourcing tasks but rather about providing enough opportunities for inspiration that might then be realized through his original work. While I did compare some initial outputs to their originals, my continued use of writing as a means of personal enrichment seems increasingly advantageous.

### Conclusion

While it's clear that using AI can transform the way we create content and facilitate learning, the results so far do not meet expectations. For me, this suggests that relying solely on human research for creative output may be more fulfilling than merely outsourcing tasks to artificial intelligence software. Ultimately, understanding and embracing these findings could empower readers like you to further invest in the craft of writing as a way to engage and challenge the world around us.
