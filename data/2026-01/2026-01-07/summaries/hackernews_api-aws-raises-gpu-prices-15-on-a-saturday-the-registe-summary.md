# AWS Raises GPU Prices 15% on a Saturday

AWS has increased its GPU prices for EC2 Capacity Blocks by approximately 15%, effective immediately. This change affects most regions and customers worldwide.

## Key Details

* Pricing hike: up to 15% increase on specific instances (p5e, p5en)
* Prices:
    * **m5**: $34.61-$39.80 per hour
    * **p5e**: $43.26-$49.75 in US West (N California)
    * **p5en**: $36.18-$41.61 in US West (N California)

## Impact

* The pricing changes are due to supply and demand patterns, which AWS expects will occur this quarter.
* AWS prefers to decrease prices instead of increasing them, often citing regulatory action as a revenue driver.

## Market Implications

* This raise is likely beneficial for enterprise sales teams targeting AWS customers, providing ammunition for promotions or discounts.
* The company faces competition from Azure and Google Cloud Platform (GCP), which may try to capitalize on the increase in GPU demand
