# Senior Developer Myth-Busting: The Truth Behind the Title

## Introduction

The concept of becoming a senior developer without extensive experience has been perpetuated in tech culture as a badge of honor. However, numerous anecdotes highlight that this reality is vastly different from what it seems.

## Year 1-2: The Early Struggles

* Being a good developer means knowing frameworks, libraries, and shortcuts.
* Shifting into " Senior Developer" requires accumulating damage to one's ego and expertise.

## Year 3-5: The Shift Towards Regret

* The experience leads to regret as the individual realizes the need for feedback, questioning, and re-evaluation.
* Recognizing readable code over clever tricks is key; documentation becomes an absolute requirement.
* Learning to say "no" to requests becomes essential.

## Year 6+: A New Perception of Seniority

* At this point, something significant shifts: one becomes calm and aware that they don't need to know everything.
* The experience teaches the individual how things usually fail; one stops rushing into coding.
* This newfound calm enables developers to take a step back and re-evaluate their approach.

## AI vs. Human Developers

* An unlikely collaboration has occurred, with AI serving as an "explayer" - exposing (one's own) frailties rather than replacing human capabilities.
* The focus shifts from coding speed and quantity (represented by AIs) to actual value creation: decision-making, architecture, understanding costs, and justifying existence.

## Advice for Early Careers

* If being a senior developer is not merely mastering everything but the restraint of effort and the wisdom gained at the expense of mistakes.
* Remembering that your purpose goes beyond showcasing code; it includes helping others understand trade-offs and explaining where necessary.

## A True Senior Developer Acknowledgment

* One cannot simply be "good enough." That only serves to highlight their own shortcomings, not demonstrating value in an industry often dominated by high-speed prototyping. The ultimate truth for senior developers is the retention of wisdom in a time when AI may become increasingly persuasive to speed up coding progress and diminishing attention paid to fundamental flaws or consequences of projects initiated early on.
