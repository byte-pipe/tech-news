# I Am 38, I Am a Nurse, and I Have Always Wanted to Learn Coding - DEV Community

# Summary

The author, a 38-year-old nurse, has long been fascinated by coding without taking the initiative to learn. Despite her daily work as a nurse, which requires experience, confidence, and trust from patients, she struggled with the idea of starting to code due to fear of being a beginner again.

## Key Points

* The author's desire to learn coding stems from her fascination with how things are built from nothing, solving real problems through software, and understanding the technology behind screens used every day.
* Fears about feeling like a beginner, wanting vs. doing, and taking on new challenges drove her inaction.
* Age is not seen as an obstacle, but rather as an opportunity for growth and change.
* The journey to learn coding involves overcoming self-doubt and patience.

## Recent Reflection

The author realized that starting to code does not mean quitting nursing or committing to a new path. It's about allowing curiosity to take over without fear of breaking identities.
The decision to start coding has shifted from "Is it too late?" to "How much longer am I willing to wait?"

## Takeaways

* Code can be learned at any age and doesn't require prior experience in most fields.
* The comfort and security of being a nurse will still be present, regardless of the outcome.

## Uncertainty and Progress

The author understands that uncertainty about learning coding remains, but it has become more manageable than before. The wanting has not disappeared; it has simply changed from feeling like an obstacle to understanding coding as a challenging yet achievable goal.
The future is uncertain, but progress can be made without fear of breaking identities or losing the comfort of being a nurse.
