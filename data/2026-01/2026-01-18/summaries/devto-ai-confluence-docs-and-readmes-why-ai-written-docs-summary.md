# AI, Confluence Docs, and READMEs: Why AI Written Docs End Up Unread - DEV Community

**AI-Generated Documentation: Is it Faster Than Ever or More Distracting Than Ever?**

AI has increasingly slipped into the daily development workflow, automating tasks such as code generation, test summarization, and documentation writing. The proliferation of Confluence pages and READMEs created by AI has made technical documentation faster than ever before.

**The Promise of AI in Documentation**

In its early days, using AI-assisted tools for creating automation and productivity was beneficial. However, as it took over specific tasks like creating structure from nothing and generating text translations, users started to realize that while the solutions were speedy, they often lacked clear meaning.

**Why Can't AI Write Better Documentation?**

It turns out that AI-generated documentation rarely achieves its true goal: being digestible and easy to retain. Instead of providing concise explanations or mental models, it relies on dense, abstract language that fails to anchor itself in memory. This results in lengthy documents with little stickiness.

**The Real Purpose of Documentation Survives Time**

Good documentation must be more than just a product of the AI process; it also needs to withstand time like Confluence pages do. It should provide enough guidance and clarity for readers to remember its purpose, content, and warnings without needing additional context.

**AI-Generated Docs Fail When Asked Why and How**

When users ask why or how an AI-generated doc is written, many AI output is either still overly verbose or abstract; it's unclear about the context, practical significance, and potential risks involved. While prompt guidance helps, more emphasis should be put on refining AI outputs instead of relying solely on verbosity.

**Conclusion**

While AI can accelerate documentation tasks, its benefits are diminished when it comes to producing truly consumable content like explanations and conceptual knowledge. To create better documentation, developers need to take responsibility for asking why and how such AI-generated docs should work in the first place, not just accept their verbosity as a viable attribute of quality.
