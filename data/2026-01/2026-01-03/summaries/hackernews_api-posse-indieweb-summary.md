# POSSE - IndieWeb

**POSSE (Publish On Your Site, Syndicate Elsewhere) - An Overview**

• **Definition**: Publish (on your) Own Site, Syndicate Elsewhere, a practice of posting content on your own site first and then publishing copies or sharing links to third-party platforms like social media silos.

**Why POSSE is Important**

* Focus on relationships that matter
* Prioritize staying in touch with current friends over potential friendships in the future.
* Key benefits:
 + Reduce dependence on 3rd party services for syndication.
 + Create direct ownership and canonical URLs of your content.
 + Enable better search results using public domain content (e.g., searching your own domain).
 + Leverage other social media platforms' layers and aggregation features.

**The Indigenous View**

POSSE is a robust and preferable syndication model due to:

* Minimizes 3rd party dependence
* Provides direct ownership and control of the content chain
* Ensures canonical URLs for easy discovery and citation

By publishing content first on your own site, you can ensure that it remains accessible and authoritative. The practice also enables you to take advantage of other services' social layers and aggregation features while storing a canonical copy of your content on your own site.

**Perms and Notes**

* Permalinks enable direct citations or linking from multiple platforms.
* Canonical URLs guarantee the consistency of the original content across platforms.
* Backfeed allows for the reverse syndication (reverse-linking) of responses from other services.
