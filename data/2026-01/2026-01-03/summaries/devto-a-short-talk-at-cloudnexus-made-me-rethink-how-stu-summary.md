# A short talk at CloudNexus made me rethink how students are using AI - DEV Community

# The Misconceptions Around Agentic AI and its Role in Student Learning

## Introduction to Agentic AI

Agentic AI is a three-phase approach: Chatbots understand context, Copilots understand user context, Agent provides goals. It's less about prompts and more about delegation.

## Surprising Findings

* Most students believed they were using AI for correct purposes (code generation vs. concept understanding), but not enough time was spent on real-world tasks.
* The main issues arose during debugging, project design conversations, and interviews when relying too heavily on non-AI tools.

## Key Takeaways from the Presentation

1. **Tools are different from AI in purpose**: Not all AI tools are created equal; focus on those designed for development work.
2. **Cloud-based platforms can help**: Like AWS Bedrockstart, these platforms provide infrastructure, scale, and security needed for real-world AI applications.

## Importance of Understanding Agentic AI

AI should complement human thinking, not replace it entirely. Use it strategically to:

* Learn from ChatGPT
* Build with coding copilots
* Scale with cloud-based AI capabilities

## Final Thoughts on Student Success

Recognize that students may rely too heavily on non-AI tools for real-world tasks, hindering their ability to learn and adapt.

By teaching and guiding them in productive ways, you can empower students to become effective agents of change in the field of Agentic AI.
