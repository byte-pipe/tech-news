# Try to Take My Position: The Best Promotion Advice I Ever Got - AGY

## Taking Charge: What Promotions Require from You

Promotions require more than just preparation or skills; they demand commitment, responsibility, and a willingness to take charge of your responsibilities. Here are key points that "taking my position" actually looks like.

### 1. **Doing the Job Before the Title**

Before receiving a formal promotion title, it's essential to demonstrate expertise by taking on more significant responsibilities. This doesn't mean accepting every task with enthusiasm but rather recognizing areas where you can improve and contribute meaningfully.

The process works when someone takes ownership of complex engineering issues on their team. They:

*   Identify problems
*   Develop solutions
*   Estimate costs
*   Come up with a clear path forward

### 2. **Sustained Performance, Not Just One-Off Wins**

Promotions are not handed out randomly; qualified candidates demonstrate consistently excellent performance over time. Research has shown that managers typically select candidates who can perform at a high level for six months before formal reviews.

**Key Takeaways:**

*   Sustained performance is what truly matters in career milestones.
*   Taking charge of complex projects or tasks not only shows willingness to take responsibility but also demonstrates expertise.
*   Demonstrated ability to handle ongoing challenges and operate reliably.

### 3. **The Responsibility-First Mindset**

Career growth requires more than just individual competence; it demands a mindset shift toward taking ownership of your responsibilities. This involves recognizing areas where you can improve, developing solutions, and coming up with a clear path forward. It's essential to take on leadership roles to gain experience and build confidence in your abilities.

### Example Scenario: The Junior Engineer

*   **Initial Task:** Addressing an issue to reduce incidents
*   **Responsibility Shifted:** Taking ownership of the problem, proposing solutions, estimating costs, and developing a clear path forward
*   **Sustained Performance:** Demonstrating expertise by consistently improving the solution over time
