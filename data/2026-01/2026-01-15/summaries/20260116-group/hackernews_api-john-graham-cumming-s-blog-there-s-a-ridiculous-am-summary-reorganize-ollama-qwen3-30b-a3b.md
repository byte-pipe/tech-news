# John Graham-Cumming's Blog: The Ridiculous Amount of Tech in a Disposable Vape

## Summary
The blog details a "Fizzy Max III 60K" rechargeable disposable vape found discarded in a park. Despite being empty and discarded, the device retains significant technological components, including USB-C connectivity, a rechargeable battery, small display, and circuit boards. The author emphasizes the surprising sophistication of what is otherwise marketed as a simple disposable product.

## Design & Purpose
* The device features a cyberpunk-inspired aesthetic with integrated electronics.
* It appears designed to incorporate multiple technologies within a compact, disposable form factor.
* Despite being marketed as disposables, the device includes components typically associated with reusable products (e.g., rechargeable battery).

## Recycling Consideration
* The blog highlights that such devices are **not designed for recycling** despite containing components that could potentially be reused.
* This creates a contradiction: while the technology suggests part recyclability, the disposal model ignores this possibility.
* The presence of sophisticated components in disposable items raises questions about the environmental impact and design ethics of such products.
