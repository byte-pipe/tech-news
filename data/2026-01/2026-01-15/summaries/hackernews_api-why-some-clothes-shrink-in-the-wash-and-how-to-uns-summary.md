# Why some clothes shrink in the wash - and how to 'unshrink' them | Swinburne

**Why Clothing Shrinks and How to 'Unshrink' it**

Washing fabrics that shrink can be upsetting for those who love their favorite pieces. But what causes this phenomenon? And how can we prevent or "unshrink" shrunk garments?

**Understanding Textile Fibres**

Textiles are made from plants, consisting of irregular fibers like cotton and linen. These fibers have thousands of long-chain cellulose molecules that are coiled or convoluted inside the plant. During manufacturing, these fibers are pulled, stretched, and twisted to create smooth threads.

On a chemical level, hydrogen bonds between the chains hold the fibers together, making them more cohesive. However, fabrics can develop "memory," retaining crinkled shapes when subjected to heat, moisture, or mechanical action like agitation in the washing machine.

**Mechanics of Shrinkage**

When water is added during washing, hot temperatures increase energy levels, disrupting hydrogen bonds and shaking fibers rapidly. The fabric's openness in knitting or weaving can also lead to shrinkage due to excess space for swelling under tension.

Additionally, cellulose molecules attract water, causing fabrics to swell inside. In the washing machine, this moisture disrupts fibers' natural alignment, making them unwind and contract back to their original shape, resulting in shrinkage.

**Not Just Hot Water**

Rayon, a fabric made from wood pulp, was prone to shrinkage due to its hydrophilic properties (attracts water). The "unshrinkability" of these fabrics can arise when the washing machine doesn't effectively remove excess moisture or if the fiber structure is designed to retain moisture.

**Restoring Shrinkage**

While it's impossible to completely reverse fabric shrinkage, there are techniques to mitigate its effects:

*   **Cold water**: Washing in cold water can help minimize shrinkage.
*   **Reducing agitation**: Minimizing mechanical action during washing can reduce fiber tension and prevent further contraction.
*   **Washing with a small amount of surfactants**: Using mild detergents containing surfactants can improve fabric care without excessive shrinkage.

**Preventing Shrinkage**

To avoid or minimize fabric shrinkage, follow these best practices:

1.  **Use cold water**: Washing in cold water can significantly reduce shrinkage.
2.  **Check labels**: Before washing, always reference the manufacturer's recommended temperature and detergent.
3.  **Minimize agitation**: Try to eliminate excessive mechanical action during washing.
4.  **Separate delicate items**: Pair fragile fabrics with similar-care items to avoid damaging them.

By understanding why fabrics shrink and taking steps to minimize its effects, individuals can enjoy their beloved garments for a longer time.
