# Bluetooth Headphone Jacking: A Key to Your Phone - media.ccc.de

## Bluetooth Headphone Jacking Vulnerabilities

**Introduction**

Bluetooth headphones and earbuds are ubiquitous, but security risks exist that attackers can exploit them for malicious purposes. A new vulnerability was discovered in popular Bluetooth audio chips developed by Airoha, which could enable complete device compromise.

**Identified Vulnerabilities**

Three vulnerabilities (CVE-2025-20700, CVE-2025-20701, CVE-2025-20702) were identified in numerous Airoha-branded devices from manufacturers such as Sony, Marshall, Beyerdynamic, and Jabra. These chips are widely used in popular headphones and earbuds.

**Demonstration**

A demo was created using current-generation Bluetooth headphones, demonstrating the immediate impact of compromised Airoha audio chips. It also shown how a pair of devices can be abused to attack paired smartphones due to their trust relationship with the peripheral.

**Impact on Device Owners**

Affected devices include:

* Sony (e.g., WH1000-XM5, WH1000-XM6, WF-1000XM5)
* Marshall (e.g. Major V, Minor IV)
* Beyerdynamic (e.g. AMIRON 300)
* Jabra (e.g. Elite 8 Active)

**General Consensus**

The presentation aimed to inform users about the identified vulnerabilities and provide technical details to understand the issues. While some device manufacturers have done a good job of informing their users, others may feel misled.

**Disclosure and Patching Efforts**

Tooling has been released for checking affected devices' security status, and research tools are available for other parties to explore Airoha-based devices further.

## Vulnerabilities and Device Impact

- **CVE-2025-20700**: Allows attackers to exploit a power function vulnerability in some chipsets.
- ** CVE-2025-20701**: Affects chipsets used in headsets and earbuds from Sony, Marshall, Beyerdynamic, and Jabra.
- **CVE-2025-20702**: Impact on all audio devices that use these Airoha-branded chips.
