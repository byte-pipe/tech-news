# AI is a business model stress test | Dries Buytaert

## AI is a Business Model Stress Test

AI commodityizes any task you can define. It cannot commoditize unoperational tasks.

Tailwind Labs, an engineering firm, recently laid off 75% of its team due to the impact of AI on their business. CEO Adam Wathan spent time running revenue forecasts and writing in his GitHub comment about what happened.

**The Reality vs. Expectation Misunderstanding**

Wathan argues that 75% of Tailwind's engineering team lost their jobs but believes that AI is responsible, while others suggest it's not due to the technology itself but rather a failure in understanding its impact on the business model.

**AI and Development**: The majority of development tasks do not require specifying how to accomplish them or running them. Operations focus on deployment, testing, rollbacks, and observability, which do not make 90.5% uptime possible in just one day.

**Value Shift**

The value shift from "what you can specify" to "showing up" for operational tasks is evident worldwide. Even in the digital age with cloud services like Vercel creating and hosting Next.js without selling explicit products.

Open Source does not commoditize, but rather provides a conduit for delivering operations-focused solutions. Acquia's business lies in providing hosting, search, CI/CD pipelines, and other digital assets under Drupal, which offer operational value regardless of product sales.

In summary, AI commoditizes anything that can be prescribed, whereas the true focus should be on showing up to operational tasks.
