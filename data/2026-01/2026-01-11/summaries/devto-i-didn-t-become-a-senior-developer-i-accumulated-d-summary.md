# I Didn’t “Become” a Senior Developer. I Accumulated Damage. - DEV Community

# The Myth-Breaker: What Actually Happens to Senior Developers

## Not the Glamourous Title You Expect

Senior developers are often portrayed as having every button mashed, every technical problem solved effortlessly. We get titles. Responsibilities are bestowed upon us. No agenda is set. It's a myth.

## Year 1-2 & Beyond: Boring, Yet Essential Work

* Mastery of frameworks and libraries is nice, but not what defines you.
* Shipment code may be fast, but it won't solve long-term problems.
* "Abstractions" can mask ignorance. Documentation is important. Naming things is a nightmare.

## Seniority Revealed

* Reading code beats clever writing; documentation matters.
* Avoid over-engineering; focus on simple solutions.
* Be calm not because you know everything, but because familiarity soothes.

## What's Left

We're left with more valuable skills – judgment, architecture, tradeoff analysis, and explaining why certain things work.

AI writes code while developers decide what code should exist at all.

## The Boring Truth of Senior Development

Most days, my job is not coding but turning ideas into reality. It's translating complexities into actionable solutions that benefit humans. And when I do write code, it may be boring, but I'm learning by doing so much more effectively than through demonstrations.
