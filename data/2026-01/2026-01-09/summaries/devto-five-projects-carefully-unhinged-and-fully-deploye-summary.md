## The Vercel Dashboard Debacle: A Side Project Saga
Despite appearances of a professional pursuit, I appear to have been speedrunning a series of side projects with an ulterior motive. These experiments, though technically impressive, serve as exercises in user experience, mobile layout design, and code optimization.

### 1. **The Guilt Trap**: Minimizing emotional engagement through buttons
• A minimalist experiment aiming to apply light emotional pressure
• Ensures a better user experience but may overlook deeper psychological aspects

### 2. **The Checker**: Analyzing personality traits and providing feedback
• Polite analysis with no emotional consequences
• Offers insight into human behavior while neglecting nuance

### 3. **Quantum Weather**: Developing accurate forecasts under emotional challenge
• Technically precise weather forecasts but emotionally questionable results
• Illustrates the importance of considering multiple perspectives in weather modeling

### 4. **The Waste Project**: A deliberately useless website requiring bug fixes
• Uninspired design, but still necessary for development and debugging purposes
• Highlights the role of imperfection in software production environments

### Lessons Learned from Each Project:
- Prioritize projects with a clear goal over theoretical perfectionism.
- Focus on shipping, deploying, and refining each project before moving forward.
- Templates can help generate quick FAQs or reusable snippets.
