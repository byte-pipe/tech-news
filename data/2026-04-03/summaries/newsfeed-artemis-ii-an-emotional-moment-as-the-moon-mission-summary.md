- Artemis II lifted off from Kennedy Space Center, carrying four astronauts on a lunar‑orbit mission.

- After the maximum‑dynamic‑pressure point (1 min 10 s), the rocket accelerated smoothly over the Atlantic, leaving a white‑smoke trail.

- Engineers and launch personnel celebrated with laughter, clapping, and a sense of triumph.  
- The launch director, Charlie Blackwell‑Thompson, declared “You are go for launch,” to which Commander Reid Wiseman replied, “We go for all humanity.”

- Minutes before liftoff, a problem with the launch‑abort system halted the countdown at T‑10 min.

- Artemis II is the first crewed mission to travel beyond low‑Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in 1972, marking the return of the Kennedy Space Center to its original lunar purpose.  
- The mission is portrayed as a revival of the human‑space‑exploration dream, echoing Neil Armstrong’s belief that the dream would return.
