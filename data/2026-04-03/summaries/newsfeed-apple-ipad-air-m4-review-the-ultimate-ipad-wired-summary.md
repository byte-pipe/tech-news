# Apple iPad Air (M4) Review: The Ultimate iPad | WIRED

## Overview
- 2026 iPad Air refresh introduces the M4 chip and 12 GB unified memory.  
- Available in 11‑inch and 13‑inch models; pricing ranges from $599 (Apple) to $799 (Best Buy, 13‑inch).  
- Rated 7/10 by WIRED.

## Performance
- M4 provides smooth operation for everyday tasks: web browsing, FaceTime, Apple Arcade, and Adobe Illustrator.  
- 12 GB RAM eliminates bottlenecks and offers longevity for school or work use.  
- Windowing and cursor improvements in iPadOS 18 make the experience feel more like a Mac.

## GPU & Gaming
- Noticeable GPU uplift over M3, especially in graphics‑intensive apps.  
- Tested with Oceanhorn 3: balanced graphics run smoothly; higher scaling shows frame‑rate drops.  
- Benchmarks show up to ~13 % gain in 3DMark compared to M3‑based devices.  
- Real‑world impact is most evident in gaming; other GPU‑heavy tasks (video rendering, 3D modeling, AI) also benefit.

## Accessories
- Magic Keyboard (larger, MacBook‑like key spacing) available, but palm rests are small.  
- Apple Pencil Pro adds haptic feedback, barrel roll, squeeze, and Find My integration; appreciated by artists.  
- Base iPad accessories are less capable; the Air’s accessories enhance productivity.

## Comparison with iPad Pro
- iPad Pro targets professionals needing M5 performance, OLED screen with HDR, and stronger USB‑C port.  
- For most users, the Air’s LCD screen, M4 chip, and price point are sufficient; Pro’s extra brightness and 120 Hz refresh are not essential.

## Comparison with MacBook Air
- When paired with the Magic Keyboard, the 13‑inch Air costs more than a 13‑inch M5 MacBook Air but offers less storage and memory.  
- Advantages over MacBook: higher screen position, ergonomic tilt, touchscreen, world‑facing camera, and detachable tablet form factor.  
- MacBook still wins on USB‑C speed, headphone jack, and superior trackpad.

## Pros
- Powerful M4 chip with ample 12 GB RAM.  
- High‑resolution LCD screen (2732 × 2048) – sharper than MacBook Air.  
- Versatile form factor: tablet plus optional keyboard.  
- Improved accessories (Magic Keyboard, Apple Pencil Pro).  
- Longevity for everyday and light‑professional use.

## Cons
- Magic Keyboard is pricey; palm rests are too small for comfort.  
- No Face ID and keyboard lacks Touch ID, reducing convenience.  
- Screen unchanged since 2024; lacks OLED and 120 Hz refresh of iPad Pro.  
- Overall performance may be overkill for typical tablet tasks.

## Verdict
The 2026 iPad Air with M4 is a well‑rounded tablet that sits comfortably between the budget iPad and the high‑end iPad Pro. Its strong performance, excellent display, and upgraded accessories make it the most capable “everyday” iPad, though the price approaches that of a MacBook Air and some ergonomic shortcomings remain. For users who value a detachable tablet with solid productivity features, the iPad Air (M4) is the optimal choice.
