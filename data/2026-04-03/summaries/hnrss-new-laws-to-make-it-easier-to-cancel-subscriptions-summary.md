# New laws to make it easier to cancel subscriptions - BBC News

## Main provisions
- Laws to take effect in spring 2027 will require companies to allow cancellations “with the click of a button”.
- A 14‑day cooling‑off period will apply after a free trial ends or an automatic renewal occurs, giving consumers the right to a full or proportionate refund.
- Firms must provide clear upfront information to avoid “silently rolled” contracts and must send reminder notices before a trial ends or a yearly contract renews.
- The government expects the measures to save the average person nearly £170 a year and £400 million in total annually.

## Expected impact
- The Department for Business and Trade (DBT) estimates there are about 10 million unwanted active subscriptions in the UK.
- More than 3.5 million people are thought to be “quietly rolled” from free or discounted trials into full‑price contracts, while another 1.3 million are caught out by unexpected auto‑renewals.

## Reactions
- Sue Davies, head of consumer‑rights policy at Which?, says the new rules will give consumers “proper transparency and protection”.
- Timo Boldt, founder and CEO of Gousto, welcomes the rules, noting they align with the company’s existing focus on customer control; Gousto’s practices will not change.
- Charitable, cultural and heritage organisation memberships are excluded from the new requirements.

## Example case
- Kim Biggs from Lincolnshire described a difficult experience cancelling an AVG anti‑virus free trial: she struggled with confusing online steps, delayed refund, and unhelpful phone support.
- AVG responded that customers can cancel trials or subscriptions via their account and that reminder emails are sent before auto‑renewals, outlining renewal date, price and cancellation options.
