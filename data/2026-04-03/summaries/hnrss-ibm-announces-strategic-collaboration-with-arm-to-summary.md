# IBM Announces Strategic Collaboration with Arm to Shape the Future of Enterprise Computing

## Overview
- Announcement date: April 2 2026, ARMONK, N.Y.
- IBM and Arm will co‑develop dual‑architecture hardware for enterprise AI and data‑intensive workloads.
- Goal: increase flexibility, reliability, and security while expanding infrastructure choices for mission‑critical environments.

## Objectives
- Combine IBM’s expertise in system reliability, security, and scalability with Arm’s power‑efficient architecture and extensive software ecosystem.
- Deliver computing platforms that support modern AI workloads without compromising enterprise‑grade performance or data‑sovereignty requirements.
- Broaden software choice and improve system performance for existing IBM Z and LinuxONE customers.

## Key Statements
- **Mohamed Awad (Arm)**: Collaboration extends Arm’s ecosystem into mission‑critical enterprise environments, offering greater deployment flexibility.
- **Tina Tarquinio (IBM)**: Reinforces IBM’s pattern of anticipating enterprise needs, expanding software options while maintaining reliability and security.
- **Patrick Moorhead (Moor Insights & Strategy)**: Highlights the partnership as a significant step toward flexible, portable enterprise platforms for AI and data‑intensive applications.
- **Christian Jacobi (IBM)**: Emphasizes IBM’s long‑standing role in shaping enterprise infrastructure and the innovation path for future IBM Z and LinuxONE systems.

## Collaboration Focus Areas
1. **Virtualization & Compatibility**
   - Expand virtualization technologies to run Arm‑based software within IBM enterprise platforms.
   - Streamline developer and enterprise adoption of Arm applications in mission‑critical settings.
2. **Performance, Security & Data Sovereignty**
   - Explore solutions that meet high‑availability, security, and local data‑sovereignty demands.
   - Enable enterprise systems to recognize and execute Arm workloads efficiently.
3. **Ecosystem Growth**
   - Create shared technology layers to open broader software ecosystems.
   - Provide enterprises with more deployment and management flexibility while protecting existing investments.

## Expected Impact
- Greater workload portability across heterogeneous hardware environments.
- Enhanced choice of software ecosystems for enterprise customers.
- Strengthened ability for organizations to adopt emerging AI and data‑intensive applications without disruptive trade‑offs.
