# BrowserGate

# BrowserGate Summary

## Executive Summary
- LinkedIn, operated by Microsoft, covertly scans every visitor’s browser for installed software and extensions, transmitting the data to LinkedIn and third‑party firms without user consent or disclosure.
- The scan captures sensitive personal information (religion, political views, disabilities, hidden job‑search activity) and details about competing products used by companies.
- Under EU GDPR, the Digital Markets Act, and other privacy regulations, these practices are likely illegal and may constitute criminal offenses.
- Fairlinked e.V.’s BrowserGate investigation documents the breach, gathers evidence, and seeks funding to pursue legal action.

## Who We Are
- **Fairlinked e.V.** – an association of commercial LinkedIn users representing professionals, businesses, and toolmakers that rely on the platform.
- **BrowserGate** – the investigative campaign aimed at exposing the alleged corporate espionage, informing the public and regulators, and financing litigation.

## What We Found

### Mass Breach of Personal Data
- Scans reveal users’ religious beliefs, political opinions, disabilities, and covert job‑search activity.
- No consent, disclosure, or legal basis is provided; LinkedIn’s privacy policy does not mention these practices.

### Corporate Espionage and Trade‑Secret Theft
- Over 200 competing sales‑tool products (e.g., Apollo, Lusha, ZoomInfo) are detected, allowing LinkedIn to map which companies use which rivals.
- Extracted customer lists are used to issue enforcement threats to users of competing tools.

### Deceiving EU Regulators
- After being designated a gatekeeper under the Digital Markets Act, LinkedIn presented two restricted APIs handling ~0.07 calls/second, while its internal “Voyager” API processes ~163,000 calls/second for LinkedIn’s own services.
- The surveillance list expanded from ~461 products in 2024 to >6,000 by February 2026, targeting tools the regulation intended to protect.

### Shipping Data to Third Parties
- An invisible tracking pixel from HUMAN Security (formerly PerimeterX) sets cookies on browsers without notice.
- Additional fingerprinting scripts run from LinkedIn’s servers and Google, all encrypted and undisclosed.

## Why Funding Is Needed
- Microsoft’s resources (33 000 employees, $15 billion legal budget) far exceed those of Fairlinked.
- Funding will support evidence‑based legal proceedings to hold Microsoft/LinkedIn accountable and halt the surveillance operation.
