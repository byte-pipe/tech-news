# Apple releases security fix for older iPhones and iPads to protect against DarkSword attacks

## Key updates
- Apple issued iOS 18.7.7 and iPadOS 18.7.7 to patch vulnerabilities exploited by the DarkSword hacking toolkit.
- The update extends protection to devices running iOS 18.4 – 18.7 that cannot upgrade to iOS 26.

## DarkSword threat
- DarkSword can compromise a device simply by a user visiting a compromised website, even if the site appears legitimate.
- Exploits steal messages, browser history, location data, cryptocurrency wallets, and upload the information to hacker‑controlled servers.
- Attacks have been observed in China, Malaysia, Turkey, Saudi Arabia, and Ukraine; publication of the tools means any attacker could use them.

## Apple’s broader response
- Users on iOS 26 received protection weeks earlier.
- The new 18.7.7 update protects millions who could upgrade to iOS 26 but have delayed the move, often due to complaints about the new “liquid glass” UI.
- Devices with automatic updates enabled will receive the patch automatically.
- Apple’s optional Lockdown Mode also mitigates DarkSword attacks; the company reports no known successful government spyware incidents on devices using Lockdown Mode.

## Context and implications
- The fix addresses a critical web‑based attack vector that could affect any older iPhone or iPad still on iOS 18.x.
- Security researchers warn that the publicly released tools increase the risk for unpatched devices worldwide.
