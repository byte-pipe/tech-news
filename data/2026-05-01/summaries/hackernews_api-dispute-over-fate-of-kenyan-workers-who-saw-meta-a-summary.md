# Dispute over fate of Kenyan workers who saw Meta AI glasses films

## Background
- Meta uses AI‑powered smart glasses (in partnership with Ray‑Ban and Oakley) that record video and audio.
- The company hired the outsourcing firm Sama to provide data annotators who review content captured by the glasses to train Meta’s AI.

## Allegations by workers
- In February, unnamed workers at Sama told Swedish newspapers Svenska Dagbladet and Göteborgs‑Posten that they had to view graphic footage, including people using the toilet and having sex.
- One worker reported seeing “everything – from living rooms to naked bodies.”
- A specific case described a man’s glasses recording in a bedroom where his wife undressed.

## Meta’s initial response
- Meta acknowledged that subcontracted workers sometimes review content from the glasses, stating it is done with clear user consent to improve product performance.
- The company said it paused work with Sama to investigate the claims.

## Termination of the contract
- Two months after the allegations, Meta ended its contract with Sama, citing that Sama “did not meet our standards.”
- Sama disputes this, asserting it consistently met operational, security and quality standards and was never notified of any failure.
- The termination is expected to make 1,108 Sama workers redundant.

## Workers’ and unions’ perspective
- Naftali Wambalo of the Africa Tech Workers Movement believes Meta ended the contract to silence workers speaking about human review of intimate footage, referring to “standards of secrecy.”
- Mercy Mutemi, lawyer for petitioners and director of the Oversight Lab, warned that the situation shows a fragile foundation for Kenya’s AI industry.

## Regulatory reactions
- The UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) wrote to Meta after the Swedish investigation, calling the report “concerning.”
- Kenya’s Office of the Data Protection Commissioner launched an investigation into privacy concerns linked to the glasses.

## Earlier controversies with Sama
- Sama previously faced criticism and legal action over a Facebook‑post moderation contract, where workers were exposed to graphic and traumatizing content.
- Sama later expressed regret for taking that work.

## Ongoing issues
- Misuse of the glasses has also been linked to non‑consensual recordings of women in Kenya.
- The BBC has requested further comment from Meta regarding the alleged motive behind ending the contract.
