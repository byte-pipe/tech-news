# Summary of Press Release: J. Craig Venter, genomics pioneer and founder of JCVI and Diploid Genomics, Inc., dies at 79

## Announcement
- J. Craig Venter, Ph.D., founder, board chair, and CEO of the J. Craig Venter Institute (JCVI), died on April 29, 2026 in San Diego.
- Death followed a brief hospitalization for unexpected side effects of treatment for recently diagnosed cancer.
- The Institute requests privacy for the family; memorial details will be shared later.

## Career Highlights
- Recognized as a visionary leader who shaped modern genomics and launched synthetic biology.
- Built interdisciplinary teams, promoted bold ideas, and emphasized rapid translation of discoveries.
- Advocated for strong federal science funding and collaborative partnerships across government, academia, and industry.

## Contributions to Genomics
- At the NIH, pioneered gene discovery using expressed sequence tags (ESTs), accelerating identification of human genes.
- Led efforts that produced the first draft human genome sequence, ushering biology into the digital age.
- Co‑authored the first high‑quality diploid human genome, highlighting the importance of parental genetic variation.

## Synthetic Biology Achievements
- Directed the creation of the first self‑replicating bacterial cell controlled by a chemically synthesized genome, proving that genomes can be designed digitally, built chemically, and “booted up” to run a living cell.

## Global Ocean Sampling
- Led the Sorcerer II Global Ocean Sampling Expedition, using metagenomics to uncover millions of new genes and expand known protein families.
- Revealed extensive microbial diversity and deepened understanding of the ocean microbiome’s role in planetary systems.

## Entrepreneurial Ventures
- Founded JCVI and co‑founded several companies: Synthetic Genomics, Inc.; Human Longevity, Inc.; and most recently Diploid Genomics, Inc.
- Aimed to translate genomics and synthetic biology into practical tools for health and society.

## About the J. Craig Venter Institute
- Non‑profit research institute with locations in Rockville, MD, and La Jolla, CA.
- Focuses on advancing genomics science, its societal implications, and communication to scientists, the public, and policymakers.
- Employs roughly 120 scientists and staff across synthetic biology, genetics, bioinformatics, high‑throughput sequencing, policy research, and science education.
- Designated as a 501(c)(3) organization; website: www.jcvi.org.
