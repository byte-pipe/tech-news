# Africa and Asia back Infantino for unique fourth term as FIFA president

## Support from CAF and AFC
- The Confederation of African Football (CAF) announced it “unanimously agreed” to back Gianni Infantino for the 2027‑2031 FIFA presidential term.
- The Asian Football Confederation (AFC) also pledged its “continued and full support” for Infantino’s candidacy.
- Both statements were made before the FIFA Congress in Vancouver on 30 April 2026.

## Voting weight
- CAF and AFC together control 101 of the 211 votes in the FIFA presidential election.
- Infantino already enjoys the backing of CONMEBOL, which adds another 10 votes.

## Infantino’s tenure and eligibility
- First elected in 2016 after the Sepp Blatter corruption scandal; re‑elected in 2019 and 2023.
- FIFA statutes limit presidents to three terms, but a ruling clarified that his initial partial term (2016‑2019) does not count, allowing a fourth term in 2027.

## Controversies
- Close ties to former U.S. President Donald Trump, who received the inaugural FIFA Peace Prize at the 2022 World Cup draw, prompted a formal ethics complaint by FairSquare alleging breach of political neutrality.
- Criticism over expanding the World Cup to 48 teams and launching a revamped 32‑team FIFA Club World Cup.

## Financial and development achievements
- Under Infantino, FIFA has recorded record revenues; the 2026 World Cup is projected to generate about $13 billion.
- The FIFA Forward Program will allocate $2.7 billion to member associations for the 2027‑2030 cycle, an eight‑fold increase compared with a decade earlier.
