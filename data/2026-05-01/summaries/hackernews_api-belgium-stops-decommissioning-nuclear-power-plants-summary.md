# Belgium stops decommissioning nuclear power plants

## Main announcement
- Prime Minister Bart De Wever announced that Belgium will halt the decommissioning of its nuclear power plants.
- The government intends to negotiate the nationalisation of the nuclear fleet with operator ENGIE.

## Negotiations with ENGIE
- ENGIE signed a letter of intent for exclusive negotiations with the Belgian government.
- The talks cover possible acquisition of the entire nuclear fleet (seven reactors), related personnel, subsidiaries, assets, and liabilities, including decommissioning and dismantling obligations.
- A basic agreement is targeted for October.

## Policy background
- In 2003 Belgium planned to phase out nuclear power by 2025, but the schedule has been repeatedly delayed due to political debate and energy‑security concerns.
- In the previous year the Belgian parliament voted by a large majority to end the nuclear phase‑out.
- The current government also plans to build new nuclear power plants.

## Current nuclear situation
- Belgium operates seven reactors at two sites; three reactors have already been taken off the grid.
- The country remains heavily dependent on gas imports for electricity, as expansion of renewable generation has been limited.
