# 15 ideas to help you build a better personal network

# Summary of “15 ideas to help you build a better personal network”

## 1. Stop asking to “pick people’s brains”
- Be specific about what you need and why you’re reaching out.
- Avoid vague requests that waste the other person’s time.

## 2. Make it easy for people to help you
- Reduce friction by giving clear, concise asks.
- Provide any necessary context or drafts so the helper can act quickly.

## 3. Send thank‑you emails
- Keep them short but mention a concrete detail from the conversation.
- Consistent gratitude builds goodwill over time.

## 4. Don’t talk bad about other people
- Negativity erodes trust and can backfire because you never know who’s connected to whom.
- Stay away from gossip.

## 5. Aim to actually be helpful
- Focus on providing value (feedback, introductions, shout‑outs) rather than extracting benefits.
- Creativity in helping strengthens relationships.

## 6. Practice active listening
- Pay close attention, ask clarifying questions, and show you understand the speaker’s goals.
- Demonstrates genuine interest and care.

## 7. Beware of interrupting
- Let others, especially strangers, finish their thoughts.
- Interruptions hinder conversation flow and rapport.

## 8. Remember small details
- Note personal tidbits (hometown, interests) and reference them later.
- Helps create deeper connections and future talking points.

## 9. Ask unique questions
- Move beyond generic small talk; probe deeper with specific, thought‑provoking queries.
- People remember distinctive conversations.

## 10. Hold judgments
- Resist quick categorization; give people space to reveal themselves.
- Leads to better understanding and stronger bonds.

## 11. Keep people top of mind
- Maintain a list of contacts and share relevant articles, events, or resources with them.
- Positions you as a connector.

## 12. Beware of overpromising and underdelivering
- Be honest about the strength of your relationships; add qualifiers when needed.
- Protects your reputation.

## 13. Be consistent
- Build a recognizable personal brand through regular, repeatable actions.
- Consistency stands out in a trend‑driven world.

## 14. Find optimism in other people’s work
- Highlight potential success rather than focusing on flaws.
- Encourages a supportive environment, especially in startups.

## 15. Remember where you started
- Recall your own early‑career uncertainties and extend the same patience you received.
- Cultivate optimism and humility in new connections.
