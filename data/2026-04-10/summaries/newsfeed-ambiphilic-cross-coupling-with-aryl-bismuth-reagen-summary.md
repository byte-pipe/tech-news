# Ambiphilic cross‑coupling with aryl‑bismuth reagents

## Abstract summary
- Traditional cross‑coupling reactions rely on a clear distinction between aryl nucleophiles and aryl electrophiles, each undergoing specific catalytic steps.  
- The authors present aryl‑bismuth reagents that are **ambiphilic**, capable of acting either as nucleophiles or electrophiles in transition‑metal‑catalysed cross‑couplings.  
- This dual reactivity originates from the ability of the Bi–C bond to engage in both oxidative addition and transmetalation with metal complexes, as demonstrated by stoichiometric and mechanistic studies.  
- By showing that a single aryl reagent can participate in both canonical elementary steps, the work challenges the long‑standing assumption that intrinsic bond polarity rigidly dictates mechanistic roles in cross‑coupling chemistry.

## Key findings
- Development of a new class of aryl‑bismuth reagents with ambiphilic behavior.  
- Mechanistic evidence confirming participation in both oxidative addition and transmetalation processes.  
- Potential to simplify cross‑coupling design by eliminating the need for separate nucleophilic and electrophilic partners.  
- Opens new avenues for synthetic methodology in homogeneous catalysis.
