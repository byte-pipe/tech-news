# Poke makes using AI agents as easy as sending a text | TechCrunch

## Overview
- Poke is an AI‑powered personal assistant that operates through text messaging (iMessage, SMS, Telegram, limited WhatsApp).
- Launched publicly in March 2026, it lets users automate everyday tasks such as planning, calendar management, health tracking, smart‑home control, photo editing, and more.
- The service is positioned as a “do‑something‑quickly” alternative to general‑purpose chatbots like ChatGPT or Claude.

## How it works
- Users start by visiting Poke.com, entering a phone number, and receiving messages—no app download or signup required.
- Poke selects the most suitable AI model for each request, pulling from major providers or open‑source options.
- Messaging integration is powered by Linq, enabling the assistant to live inside iMessage and other platforms.
- Users can create “recipes” (plain‑text automations) and share them with friends.

## Funding and valuation
- Backed by Spark Capital, General Catalyst, and angel investors.
- Raised an additional $10 million after a $15 million seed round last year.
- Post‑money valuation stands at $300 million.

## Features and recipes
- Pre‑built recipes cover categories like health & wellness, productivity, finance, travel, smart home, education, and developer tools.
- Integrations include Gmail, Google Calendar, Outlook, Notion, Strava, Withings, Oura, Fitbit, Philips Hue, Sonos, PostHog, Webflow, Supabase, Vercel, Sentry, GitHub, and more.
- Developers can earn between $0.10 and $1 per new user who signs up via their shared recipe.

## Security model
- Multi‑layered approach with regular penetration testing and permission limits for both agents and staff.
- Tokens are opaque to the team unless a user explicitly opts in to share logs or analytics.
- TechCrunch has not performed an independent security audit.

## Pricing
- Free to start; pricing is usage‑based.
- Real‑time inference (e.g., email monitoring, flight check‑ins) incurs cost, while static queries may remain free.
- During beta, users negotiated monthly fees ranging from $10 to $30; current model uses personalized pricing guided by the AI’s cost estimates.
- Monetization is secondary to growth; the goal is to reach a billion users.

## Market position and future outlook
- Poke aims to make agentic AI accessible to non‑technical users, contrasting with more complex systems like OpenClaw that require terminal setup and raise security concerns.
- The company plans to expand recipe discovery, improve WhatsApp support (currently limited by Meta’s policies), and continue scaling the product globally.
- Growth focus outweighs immediate profitability, with the next months dedicated to broader adoption and feature expansion.
