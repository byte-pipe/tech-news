# Amazon CEO takes aim at Nvidia, Intel, Starlink, more in annual shareholder letter | TechCrunch

# Amazon CEO Andy Jassy’s annual shareholder letter – key takeaways

## Competitive positioning
- Jassy frames AWS as a challenger to Nvidia, acknowledging Nvidia’s current AI dominance while announcing a shift toward Amazon‑built Trainium AI chips.  
- Demand for Trainium 3 is “nearly sold out,” and pre‑orders for Trainium 4 (expected in 18 months) are also almost full.  
- The chip business is reported at a $20 billion annual revenue run rate; Jassy suggests it could reach $50 billion ARR if sold to external customers.  
- Intel is critiqued by highlighting Graviton CPU adoption: 98 % of the top 1,000 EC2 customers use Graviton, with two firms requesting all 2026 Graviton capacity.

## New product initiatives
- Amazon Leo, a satellite‑internet service slated for mid‑2026 launch, has already secured contracts with Delta Airlines, AT&T, Vodafone, Australia’s National Broadband Network, and NASA.  
- Jassy hints at future robotics offerings, proposing to turn data from roughly 1 million warehouse robots into “robotics solutions” for industrial and consumer markets.

## Financial commitments
- Amazon plans to spend $200 billion on capital expenditures in 2026, primarily for expanding AWS data‑center infrastructure—the largest capex commitment among major tech firms.  
- The investment is justified by an OpenAI agreement to spend $100 billion on AWS, along with several other disclosed and undisclosed customer deals.  
- Jassy stresses that the spending is not speculative, despite Amazon’s stock falling below $200 per share.

## Additional business highlights
- Ongoing emphasis on same‑day delivery, grocery services, and drone initiatives.  
- Personal anecdotes about Jassy’s unrealized dream of becoming a sportscaster and memories of watching hockey with his father.

## Context and outlook
- Acknowledges public debate over AI hype and “bubble” concerns, asserting that Amazon does not view the technology as over‑hyped.  
- The letter aims to reassure shareholders of growth potential across AI chips, cloud services, satellite internet, and emerging robotics.
