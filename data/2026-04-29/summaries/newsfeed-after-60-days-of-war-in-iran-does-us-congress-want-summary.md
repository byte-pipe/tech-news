- The 1973 War Powers Act requires the president to cease military action after 60 days unless Congress authorises continuation.  
- Presidents have historically stretched or ignored the 60‑day limit; courts usually avoid intervening in armed‑conflict matters.

- If Congress does not act and the courts stay out, the war could continue on uncertain legal grounds.  
- Legal challenges could arise if Trump persists beyond the deadline without congressional authorisation.

- Past presidents (e.g., Bill Clinton) have re‑interpreted “hostilities” under the War Powers Act to sidestep congressional approval, conducting limited operations in Iraq, Somalia, and the former Yugoslavia without explicit authorisation.
