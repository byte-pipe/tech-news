# A billion miles in less than a decade: GM's Super Cruise reaches a milestone

## Introduction
- Super Cruise launched in the 2017 Cadillac CT6 as a hands‑free driver‑assist system.
- Unlike Tesla, GM limited it to pre‑mapped, restricted‑access highways and added a driver‑facing infrared camera to monitor eye gaze.

## Expansion and Adoption
- The system has been added to many GM models, now covering almost 750,000 vehicles in the US and Canada.
- It has accumulated more than one billion miles (1.6 billion km) of driven distance.
- Renewal rate is close to 40 % for owners; the feature is free for the first three years and then requires an active OnStar subscription.
- GM describes the usage as passing the “toothbrush test,” meaning users keep using it regularly after the first experience.

## Mapping and Usage Statistics
- Mapped highway network grew from about 160,000 miles (258,000 km) in 2018 to roughly 700,000 miles (1.1 million km) today.
- Average trip using Super Cruise: 17 miles (27 km) and 24 minutes.
- More than half of Super Cruise users drive with the feature weekly or daily.
- Year‑over‑year usage is doubling, reaching 7.1 million hours of active use, 485.9 million miles (782 million km) across 28.7 million trips in 2025.

## Comparison with Tesla FSD
- Tesla’s Full Self‑Driving (FSD) works on all roads and reported about 1.3 million active subscriptions.
- Tesla surpassed 8.4 billion miles (13.5 billion km) earlier this year, with roughly half of that mileage logged in 2025.

## Future Development
- GM’s next‑generation system, originally called Ultra Cruise, will retain the Super Cruise name.
- Goal: add “eyes‑off” capability on highways, moving from a Level 2+ assist to a partially automated Level 3 system.
- Planned debut in the Cadillac Escalade IQ around 2028, with supervised testing already underway in multiple states.
