# A Terrible, Terrible Week in Software Stocks. But Not Necessarily a Terrible 12 Months

## What Actually Happened This Week
- ServiceNow posted Q1 revenue of $3.77 B (slightly above consensus) but opened down 15‑17%; drivers were a 75 bp subscription headwind from Middle‑East on‑prem deal slippage, a cut in full‑year adjusted operating margin guidance to 31.5 %, missed billings expectations, and no mention of its “generative AI book of business.”
- IBM’s revenue growth slowed to 9 % (software segment to 11.3 %); the stock fell about 10 % on top of a 13 % single‑day drop in February after Anthropic’s Claude Code demo.
- Analysts responded with tighter price targets (e.g., Truist lowered ServiceNow’s target to $120) and emphasized that even a beat with cautious guidance now incurs a steep penalty.

## Year‑to‑Date (YTD) Picture
- Out of 25 major B2B names, most are deep in the red; biggest losers include:
  - Monday (‑74.58 %)
  - Atlassian (‑68.11 %)
  - HubSpot (‑63.09 %)
  - Others range from ‑50 % to ‑20 %.
- DigitalOcean is the only notable green performer (+96 %).
- The iShares Expanded Tech‑Software ETF (IGV) fell from an all‑time high of $117 to $85, putting the sector in bear‑market territory since early February.

## 12‑Month View Shows a Different Story
- Several names are up strongly on a trailing‑12‑month basis:
  - DocuSign (+233.88 %)
  - Cloudflare (+78.93 %)
  - Twilio (+61.70 %)
  - MongoDB (+55.77 %)
  - Palantir (+44.06 %)
  - Shopify (+36.29 %)
  - Datadog (+32.48 %)
  - CrowdStrike (+15.57 %)
- Snowflake is near breakeven (‑1.89 %).
- Winners tend to be infrastructure, security, data, and AI‑native platforms; losers are seat‑based workflow SaaS that AI can replicate.

## What the Market Is Signaling
1. **Seat‑based pricing on human workflows is exposed.** Companies whose ARR scales with per‑seat usage (e.g., Monday, Atlassian, HubSpot, Workday, DocuSign, ServiceNow) are seeing multiple compression.
2. **Infrastructure that powers AI is protected.** Demand for edge compute, databases, observability, and security rises as AI agents proliferate, lifting firms like Cloudflare, MongoDB, Datadog, DigitalOcean, and CrowdStrike.

- ServiceNow’s response: introduction of an “Agentic ACV” tier that charges for tasks completed by AI agents rather than per seat.

## AI Is Killing One Business Model, Creating Another
- AI is not killing software overall; it is eroding the seat‑based SaaS model that charges $50‑150 per seat per month for functions AI can perform.
- Simultaneously, AI is driving a usage‑based model for infrastructure, security, and data platforms, expanding their TAM and multiples.

## Guidance for Investors Holding B2B Stocks
- YTD performance is painful but less informative than the 12‑month trend.
- Key questions to monitor:
  - Is the company shifting from seat‑based to outcome or consumption pricing?
  - Is its “AI book of business” growing, shrinking, or disappearing from earnings commentary?
  - Is consumption rising even if seat count is flat?
  - Are they acquiring into AI‑native categories or defending a shrinking moat?
- The sector is not dying; it is being rebuilt with new pricing rules, different multiples, and a reshuffled set of winners.
