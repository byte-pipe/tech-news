# Google and Pentagon reportedly agree on deal for ‘any lawful’ use of AI

## Overview
- Google has signed a classified agreement permitting the U.S. Department of Defense to employ its AI models for “any lawful government purpose.”
- The deal was reported by *The Information* shortly after Google staff urged CEO Sundar Pichai to block Pentagon use of the company’s AI over ethical concerns.

## Companies Involved
- Google joins other AI firms—OpenAI, xAI, and previously Anthropic—in holding classified contracts with the U.S. government.
- Anthropic was later blacklisted after refusing to remove weapon‑ and surveillance‑related guardrails from its models.

## Terms of the Agreement
- Both parties agree that Google’s AI should not be used for domestic mass surveillance or autonomous weapons without appropriate human oversight.
- The contract does **not** grant Google the right to control or veto lawful government operational decisions, making the restrictions largely informal.
- Google must assist the Pentagon in adjusting AI safety settings and filters upon request.

## Official Statements
- A Google spokesperson said the company is “proud to be part of a broad consortium of leading AI labs and technology and cloud companies providing AI services and infrastructure in support of national security.”
- The spokesperson reaffirmed Google’s commitment to the consensus that AI should not be used for domestic mass surveillance or autonomous weaponry without proper human oversight.

## Context
- The announcement follows internal employee protests demanding that Google block Pentagon access to its AI tools, citing fears of “inhumane or extremely harmful ways” of use.
- The deal is described as an amendment to Google’s existing government contract.
