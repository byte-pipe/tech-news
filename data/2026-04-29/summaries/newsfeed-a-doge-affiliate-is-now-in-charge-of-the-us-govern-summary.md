# A DOGE Affiliate Is Now in Charge of the US Government’s ID Platform | WIRED

## Main Appointment
- Greg Hogan, an affiliate of the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE), has been named acting assistant commissioner of Technology Transformation Services (TTS) within the General Services Administration (GSA).  
- He will oversee Login.gov, the federal secure login and identity service.  
- According to an internal email, Hogan’s mandate is to grow Login.gov’s user base and turn it into a “world‑class identity platform recognized beyond the federal government.”

## Hogan’s Background
- Joined the government in January 2025 after working at Comma.ai, a self‑automation startup for cars.  
- Served as chief information officer at the Office of Personnel Management (OPM), where DOGE operatives were embedded.  
- Approved a privacy impact assessment that enabled a new OPM email server used by DOGE to send controversial mass emails (e.g., the “Fork in the Road” message encouraging “deferred resignations”).  
- The email server later became the subject of a lawsuit alleging failure to publish the required privacy impact assessment.  
- Remained VP of Infrastructure at Comma.ai until October 2025, overlapping with his OPM role.  
- Left OPM in September 2025 to join the National Design Service, led by Airbnb co‑founder Joe Gebbia, who is also linked to DOGE.

## Login.gov Overview & Future Plans
- Launched in 2017 to provide a single, secure account for accessing multiple government services; originally co‑developed by the US Digital Service (now the US DOGE Service).  
- December 2025 roadmap announced plans to:  
  - Integrate mobile driver’s licenses.  
  - Use passports for identity confirmation.  
  - Emphasize fraud‑prevention, a priority of the current administration.  
- An anonymous TTS employee described a push to make Login.gov a national ID repository containing income, citizenship status, dependents, and other personal data, raising concerns about surveillance under DOGE leadership.  
- Another anonymous employee expressed optimism, noting Hogan’s emphasis on listening to career federal staff and his long‑term attitude.

## DOGE’s Continuing Influence
- Although the original DOGE organization dissolved in June 2025 after Elon Musk’s departure, many former operatives retain influential positions across the government, including at the State Department, Department of Defense, and Social Security Administration, or have moved to private‑sector roles and contractors.  
- Hogan’s appointment illustrates the ongoing presence of DOGE affiliates within federal technology infrastructure.

## Official Comment
- A GSA spokesperson told WIRED that Hogan will focus on expanding agency adoption, improving user experience, and advancing Login.gov while maintaining the highest security and privacy standards, reinforcing the agency’s commitment to secure, scalable technology solutions for the American people.
