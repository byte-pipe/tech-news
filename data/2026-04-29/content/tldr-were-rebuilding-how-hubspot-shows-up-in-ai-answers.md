description: 'We''re rebuilding how HubSpot shows up in AI answers. Our data shows 57% of marketers are doing the same. Aja Frost and I dug into this on Field Notes a while back. She runs our SEO and AEO strategy and has been deep in this problem longer than most. The biggest thing I took away: AEO pages need to be way more specific than SEO pages. You''re not writing "The ultimate guide to content marketing." You''re writing "The ultimate guide to content marketing for logistics companies in New Jersey." Answer engines surface highly relevant, contextualized answers. Aja''s team developed and implemented these 7 best practices at HubSpot. I''ve been sharing them ever since. 1) Put the answer first. Don''t bury the lede. Answer the primary question in the first sentence. 2) Give more context. Add 2-3 paragraphs with examples and definitions. Help the engine understand the response is credible and complete. 3) Add structure throughout. LLMs are lazy readers. Headers, bullet points, tables.
  Every passage should make sense on its own. 4) Add an FAQ. Answer engines work conversationally. FAQs let you hit the specific sub-questions your audience is actually asking. 5) Include original insights. Data, quotes, research no one else has. Net new information is what gets pulled into citations. 6) Connect every point to the product. If you don''t make the connection, the LLM won''t. 7) Embed schema markup. It''s the code layer that helps answer engines understand exactly what''s on your page. The one thing that really stuck with me: LLMs don''t pull the entire page at once. They grab a few paragraphs. You don''t know which ones. So every section needs to hold up on its own. The content form is just as important as the function.'
