# Girl, 6, has sight restored by life-changing gene therapy

## Girl's Sight Restored through Gene Therapy
A 6-year-old girl from Bedfordshire has been treated for an inherited condition Leber's Congenital Amaurosis (LCA), a rare genetic disorder that prevents cells in the eye from making a specific protein needed for normal vision.

**Background**

The child, Saffie Sandford, had the LCA gene therapy since age 2. Research at Moorfields Eye Hospital in London was conducted to find an effective treatment. After testing, Luxturna therapy was administered as one-off treatments on each eye in April and September 2025, resulting in significant improvements.

**Treatment Outcome**

Saffie's mother reported that the treatment had been life-changing for her daughter's vision, with Saffie now able to see colors and perform everyday tasks without any assistance. The results have demonstrated an improved peripheral sight in daylight.

The therapy was administered at Great Ormond Street Hospital (GOSH) in conjunction with ongoing eye care at Moorfields Eye Hospital, making it one-of-a-kind treatment for LCA on the NHS. **Key Points**
