## Dynomight

The article discusses the potential risks associated with taking certain medications, specifically acetaminophen (Tylenol/Panadol) and ibuprofen (Advil/Nurofen/Motrin/Brufen). According to estimates, 56,000 people die every year in the US due to acetaminophen overdose.

Despite the known risks, acetaminophen's safety profile is often unclear or underestimated due to its narrow therapeutic window. For example, taking more than eight grams can cause liver failure and death.

On the other hand, ibuprofen has a different set of complications, with 10 documented cases found in medical literature. The authors attribute this disparity to varying factors, including individual tolerance levels and underlying health conditions.

## How does ibuprofen work?

Ibuprofen inhibits the Cyclooxygenase (COX) enzyme, which contributes to inflammation by regulating messenger molecules involved in pain signaling. This mechanism is shared among over-the-counter painkillers like acetaminophen and NSAIDs, although acetaminophen itself lacks this cyclooxygenase inhibition.

## How does acetaminophen work?

Acetaminophen's primary mechanism involves inhibiting COX enzymes, similar to ibuprofen. However, its effects are distinct due to the lack of cyclooxygenase inhibition and the presence of other metabolites and byproducts.
