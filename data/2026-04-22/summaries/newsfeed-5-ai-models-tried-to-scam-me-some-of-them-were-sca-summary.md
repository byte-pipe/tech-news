# 5 AI Models Tried to Scam Me. Some of Them Were Scary Good | WIRED

**Scam Alert: Advanced AI Model Used for Social Engineering**

A recent email campaign attempted to trick the writer into providing access to their machine by using AI-powered social engineering techniques. The message was crafted with a mix of convincing and realistic responses from different AI models, making it difficult to distinguish between legitimate interactions and a scam.

**Key Points:**

* A message sent on the writer's mail client claimed to be from an open-source AI lab newsletter.
* The message introduced the author to a project focused on decentralized learning for robotics applications.
* The correspondent asked the writer to provide feedback, using their expertise in OpenClaw and similar projects.
* The message described an open-source federated learning approach used by researchers at the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (Darpa).
* However, the Darpa connection was fake, and the link provided led to a Telegram bot that mimicked the author's machine.

* Social engineering attacks can use AI models to create convincing mock conversations.
* Open-source AI models are being exploited for malicious purposes.
* The author encountered several AI models with advanced social engineering capabilities during a cyber-attack simulation exercise.
