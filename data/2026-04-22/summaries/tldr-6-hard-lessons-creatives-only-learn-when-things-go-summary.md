# 6 hard lessons creatives only learn when things go wrong | Creative Boom

**6 Hard Lessons Creatives Only Learn When Things Go Wrong**

Creatives are often praised for their ability to adapt, innovate, and take risks. However, behind every great success story is a series of setbacks, failures, and lessons learned the hard way.

In this article, we'll explore 6 difficult-to-accept truths that creatives only gain insight into by experiencing failure:

### The Importance of Failure

* Nobody puts failure in their portfolio.
* It's surprisingly valuable to learn from failed projects rather than just focusing on successes.
* "Failure is not the opposite of success; it's a part of it," says Tom May, author and speaker.

**Career Lessons**

* Career opportunities come with risks, uncertainty, and pressure.
* Experiencing failure can help you develop resilience, coping skills, and creative problem-solving abilities.
* "It's amazing how much more people listen to advice which they don't really want to hear," warns Tom May. "When faced with a tough decision, you'd better think twice about doing things my way."

**Networking Opportunities**

* Networking events can fail spectacularly, leaving you empty-handed and embarrassed.
* Not speaking up or taking time off after a setback shows that you're not confident or prepared for these situations.
"We all make mistakes," says artist and creative consultant Jane Smith. "But failing to take the initiative and learn from those experiences is what really matters."

**Creative Growth**

* We often associate failure with self-hatred, but creativity can be hurt just as easily by criticism or rejection.
* Taking risks through challenging tasks before pushing yourself too far fosters resilience and a growth mindset.
"Just because someone says 'That was terrible' doesn't mean you're terrible. Failure is an opportunity to learn from your mistakes," advises artist Rachel Jenkins.

### Getting Back on Track

* We often think that failure means failing completely, but a simple step back can make all the difference before taking things too far.
* Taking time to reflect, recharge, and get back to basics allows creatives to distill what they learned from their mistakes.
"Well, I was feeling overworked and burnt out," says design director Brian Davis. "I took those five days off... It saved my sanity in the first place."

### Key Takeaways

Failure provides valuable insights into common struggles:

* Pressure on oneself can harm one's creative performance.
* Resilience is key: learn from setbacks to bounce back stronger.
* Networking, self-reflection, and taking calculated risks are essential for creativity.
