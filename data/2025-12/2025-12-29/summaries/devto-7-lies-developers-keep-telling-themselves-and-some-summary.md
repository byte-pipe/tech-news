# 7 Lies Developers Keep Telling Themselves (And Somehow Still Believe) - DEV Community

** Developer "Lies" I Keep Telling Myself **
================================================================================

This collection of lies highlights the common challenges faced by many developers. They struggle with similar problems, share similar thoughts, and often make similar mistakes.

### 1. Assumptions about Code Efficiency

Many developers make this assumption without evidence: "I know this will only take a moment." Research shows that some tasks can consume hours or days of work due to hidden complexities.

### 2. Refactoring Inevitability

Refactoring is rarely done, despite being essential for maintainable code: "I'll refactor this later." Pro tip: create refactoring tasks and make them a priority to avoid delaying the sprint process.

### 3. Sprint Estimates are Enough

Estimating time for sprints doesn't always translate to real-world behavior: "We'll definitely finish this by the end of the sprint." Using buffers can improve estimated time by incorporating variability from other teams or unexpected requirements.

### 4. Code Testing is Overlooked

Writing tests is often skipped in favor of immediate implementation: either write them now, create a task for later, or skip altogether: "I'll write the tests later."

### 5. Bugs are Exceptions

Commonly thought of as rare issues, bugs actually occur frequently enough to cause significant problems: "If it works on my machine, it'll work everywhere." Recognizing and addressing these edge cases is crucial.

### 6. Cross-Platform Consistency is Impossible

Assuming differences in frontend and backend components based solely on technical factors (e.g., assuming they are not cross-reactive): "This is definitely not on my side."

### 7. Assumptions about Problem Severity

Holding onto the mistaken assumption that most problems won't affect your machine: "If it works on my machine, it'll work everywhere." It's often better to account for varying compatibility across devices.

**Conclusion **
---------------

Dealing with these common developer lies requires a recognition of our shared challenges. By keeping track of and addressing these myths, we can improve efficiency, increase confidence in our codebase, and foster a culture of responsible development practices.
