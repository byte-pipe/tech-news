# Accepting US car standards would risk European lives, warn cities and civil society - ETSC

**Revisiting Trade Deal with US - A Threat to European Life and Economy**

EU officials must reconsider a hastily agreed trade deal with the US, where they have stated that they will accept lower US vehicle standards, according to cities across Europe.

The current agreement allows for mutual recognition of each other's standards, but some argue it would undermine EU efforts in road safety, public health (e.g., air quality and climate), and competitiveness.

**European Concerns**

1.  **Reduced Road Safety**: Accepting lower US standards would leave European cities vulnerable to increased road deaths, as a36% reduction in European road deaths since 2010 is compared to US increases of 30%.
2.  **Mandatory Life-Saving Technologies**: Most EU regulations require life-saving technologies like pedestrian protection and automated emergency braking, which have improved significantly over time. Accepting lower standards would undo this progress.
3.  **Health Risks**: Increased exposure to pollutants from brake and tyre wear can lead to conditions such as asthma, cancer, cardiovascular disease, and neurological disorders in Europeans.
4.  **Job Losses**: Lowered safety and environmental standards could result in larger-scale job losses in European car plants and across the automotive supply chain.
5.  **Open Loopholes**: The EU has been using Individual Vehicle Approval (IVA), which can be abused to allow foreign vehicles without meeting necessary safety, air pollution, and climate standards. Accepting lower US standards would create loopholes that could further increase imports of non-compliant vehicles.

**Recommendations**

The signatories urge European lawmakers to reconsider the trade deal and instead strengthen EU regulations to match those in the US. This can be achieved by:

*   Strengthening life-saving technologies
*   Implementing stricter environmental regulations
*   Ensuring that all new cars meet high safety standards
*   Enhancing the use of Individual Vehicle Approval (IVA) to prevent abuse
