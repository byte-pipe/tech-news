# Coding Without Pressure: How Slowing Down Helped Me Learn Faster - DEV Community

### Summary:

The author shares their personal journey of learning to code, where they initially pushed hard and felt like they weren't making progress. However, as they learned to let go of pressure and perfectionism, they discovered that slowing down actually helped them learn faster.

Their experiences revolved around the concept of "fast learning" vs "shallow learning," where superficial understanding gave way to deep comprehension. They realized that focusing too much on speed led to shallow knowledge rather than actual mastery.

The author highlights several strategies they adopted to slow down and focus on practical skills:

* Breaking down complex concepts into smaller, manageable pieces
* Reading and analyzing own code instead of trying to tackle everything at once
* Allowing themselves time to struggle without feeling guilty or comparing progress to others' timelines

Through these efforts, the author gained confidence in their learning pace and noticed significant improvements in understanding and retention of material.

### Key Points:

*   Learning doesn't require intense focus or high levels of speed.
*   Practicing "good enough" is often more valuable than striving for perfection.
*   The line between shallow learning and actual mastery can be blurred, especially when focusing too much on speed.
*   Reflecting on past experiences allows individuals to identify where they went wrong in their approach and learn from those mistakes.
