# Nvidia Acquires AI Chip Startup Groq for Approximately $20 Billion

Nvidia has agreed to purchase assets from the nine-year-old chip startup Groq for about $20 billion. This is its largest acquisition ever and represents a significant increase in Nvidia's capabilities in artificial intelligence (AI) workloads.

* **Acquisition Details**: Nvidia acquired Groq's inference technology, which competes with itself for AI-related projects.
* **Deal Structure**: The deal is a non-exclusive licensing agreement, with Netflix CEO Reed Hastings and Disney executive Michael Eisner also invested in Groq through their respective companies.
* **Founding Team**: The acquisition brings key personnel from the founding team, including Jonathan Ross, Sunny Madra (CEO), and Simon Edwards (finance chief).

The newly unified company will continue to operate independently and pursue its cloud-based computing service, known as GroqCloud. The deal does not include Groq's nascent cloud business.

"This represents a significant strategic shift for Groq," said one of the investment firms leading the financing round. "Groq founder Jonathan Ross is joining us to explore AI-related projects."

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang framed the acquisition as an opportunity to expand its capabilities in AI inference and real-time workloads.

"We plan to leverage Groq's expertise to deliver innovative AI solutions that drive greater performance, efficiency, and competitiveness," Huang wrote. "At the same time, we are committed to continuing our efforts to enhance our customers' experiences through AI-driven innovation."
