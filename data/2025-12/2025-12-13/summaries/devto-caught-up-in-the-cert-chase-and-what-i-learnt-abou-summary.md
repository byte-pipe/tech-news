# Caught Up in the Cert Chase (And What I Learnt About Coding) - DEV Community

## The Value of Certificates in the Context of Learning and Development

- **Key Points:**
  - The pursuit of certificates can be misleading as they don't directly correlate with personal growth or real-world application.
  - Building projects demonstrates problem-solving skills, coding knowledge, and creativity more than accumulating certifications.
  - A strong skill portfolio is far more valuable for career advancement.

## Importance of Practical Experience

- **Rationalizing the Purpose of Certificates:**
  - The value of certificates lies in their ability to provide industry-recognized credentials that demonstrate specific technical or professional expertise.
  - In contrast, certificates lack practical relevance as they don't directly address real-world problems and challenges.

## Finding Effective Resource Materials

- **Seeking Practical Learning Resources:**
  - There's a need for reliable source materials that focus on theoretical aspects of programming, such as:
    * Free coding tutorials or courses.
    * Books that cover fundamental concepts in an engaging manner.
    * Online communities or forums where experienced developers share knowledge and insights.

## Conclusion

- **Real Skills, Real Impact:**
  - Ultimately, the true value of certifications lies in their ability to validate one's technical skills and promote personal growth through hands-on experiences.
  - A strong focus on building projects that demonstrate real-world capabilities is far more significant than collecting certificates.
