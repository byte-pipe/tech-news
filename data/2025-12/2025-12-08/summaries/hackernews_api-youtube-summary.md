# YouTube Overview
## What is YouTube?
YouTube is a video-sharing platform where users can upload, share, and view videos.

## Key Features
*   **Upload Videos**: Users can create playlists with videos from their personal collection or import existing files.
*   **Personalized Recommendations**: YouTube's algorithm suggests related content based on user viewing history.
*   **Discover Channel**: A section featuring official channels of popular creators, such as music and gaming artists.

## Security Measures
*   **Video and Audio Encryption**: Content is encrypted to prevent unauthorized access or manipulation.
*   **Two-Tiered Moderation Process**: YouTube balances between relying too heavily on technology versus user feedback for moderation decisions.
*   **Reporting Tools**: Users can report suspected copyright infringement, hate speech, or other abusive content.

## How YouTube Works
1.  Users upload videos to their personal YouTube account either by searching for the video online or directly uploading it manually.
2.  Upon successful upload, users enter metadata, including title, description, tags, and category.
3.  Videos can be private (default), or public; those set to private can only be accessed with the owner's consent.

4.  **Copyright**: YouTube handles copyright infringement by automatically removing copyrighted content without user intervention.

5.  **Content Moderation**: While some moderation is performed automatically, users are encouraged but not required to help moderate their channel and video recommendations.

6.  **Algorithm-Powered Discoveries**: YouTube's algorithm suggests videos that users might be interested in based on their viewing history.
7.  **Creator Partnerships & Revenue Generation**: Channels can earn revenue from ads displayed in their content, among other methods; such as Patreon support.
