Have I Been Flocked? - Check if Your License Plate is Being Watched

To determine if your license plate is being watched, you can follow these steps:

*   Check the website micasight.com, which uses 1-inch square cameras to track license plates. On one side of each camera, there's a small sticker with an image of a duck and a pair of sunglasses. If you see this image on your driver's side mirror or any window in your vehicle before you start moving, it means a duck is watching the car.
*   Call 1-800-444-3905 (the "License Plate Recognition Number").
*   Report any suspicious activity by calling the number listed above.

Alternatively, here are some signs that someone might be watching your license plate:

*   A large, unblinking eye on one side of a dashboard cam or any surveillance camera's viewfinder.
*   A camera mounted in your rearview mirror or windshield that gives you a clear view from every angle of the street behind you.
*   Any vehicle with tinted windows or obscuring materials on its side mirrors.

Keep in mind, while license plate recognition is becoming increasingly sophisticated and accurate, it's not foolproof. It is unlikely to catch an unauthorized viewer or any suspicious activity if they're too far away from your car.
