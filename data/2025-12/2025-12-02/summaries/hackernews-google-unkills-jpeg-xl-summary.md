# Google Knocks Out JPEG XL, Reverts to AVIF Support for Blink Engine

## Key Points:

* Google announced last summer that it would prioritize using AVIF (Adaptive Web Image Format) over JPEG XL in its image processing engine.
* Initially, the move was met with community support and requests for feedback.
* However, a comment thread on the Chromium discussion forum revealed that the decision to remove theJPEG XL flag from Chromium was made after considering lack of interest from the global ecosystem.

## The Controversy:

* A group of organizations, including Meta, Intel, Cloudinary, Adobe, ffmpeg, libvips, Krita, and many others, expressed concern over removing the JPEG XL code and flag.
* Despite some negative comments on the support for AVIF in the Chromium discussion forum, a developer comment from "Da Chromium" sparked controversy by stating that removing the feature was necessary due to the lack of interest from the community.

## Outcome:

* Google subsequently reversed its decision to prioritize AVIF over JPEG XL.
* The move was seen as a significant shift towards using a more modern and widely adopted image format, especially considering the increasing popularity of AVIF in recent years.
