# Don't get scammed on an interview. - DEV Community

### Don't Get Scammed on an Interview

**Identifying Red Flags: How To Spot These Schemes**

Two recent "interviews" turned out to be scams. Both followed a similar playbook:

1. Giving a legitimate-looking repository link, sharing the screen, and asking you to install/run the project during the call.
2. Providing a friendly introduction, showcasing a demonstration project, and gathering feedback on your past work.

**The Scammers' Goal: Getting Their Hands Dirty**

The scammers use psychological tactics to distract you from finding red flags, trying to convince you that it was all just a routine interview. However, not everyone can overlook these warning signs:

* **Hidden scripts**: Discovering a hidden script or fake MetaMask popup could indicate a malicious extension.
* **Fake company branding**: If the URL's domain looks suspiciously similar to your own company's website.

**What Happened Before We Could Stop These Scams**

Two former interviewees shared their experiences, detailing how they had already noticed red flags but couldn't avoid getting scammed. They also revealed that another scammer in the background was attempting to replicate their method using Figma. The excitement and alarm were palpable as they witnessed other devs being duped into installing malicious extensions.

**Protect Yourself: Take Action**

To avoid these scams, keep your guard up:

* **GitHub Codespaces or codesandbox with Copilot**: Utilize this feature for quick repository analysis.
* **Full VSCode IDE in a Docker**: Consider hosting and running code locally to prevent sensitive data from being accessed remotely.
* **Dev Containers for free**: Try using Dev Containers for reliable, local setups.

**What's the Difference Between GitHub Codespace and VSCode Dev?**

Finally, clarifying the difference between GitHub Codespace (VSCode in the browser) and its VSCode Dev counterpart highlights a subtle distinction: where exactly does your code run?

**Why Listen to Us?**

Some key red flags that caught the attention of this author:

*   **Hidden scripts**: Identifying these malicious extensions can make all the difference.
