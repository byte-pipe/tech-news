# Background

The author is a Senior Staff Engineer at Google. After reading Goedecke's essays on being a staff engineer (particularly "Software engineering under the spotlight", and "It's Not Your Codebase"), they felt uneasy about being described as "forgiving".

## The Author's Experience

* Their job operates in large teams behind the scenes.
* Their team is composed of thousands of engineers across various product lines (Android, Chrome).
* Leadership relationships are different from those described by Goedecke. Executives prioritize revenue and MAU over engineer input.

### Stewardship vs. Fungibility

The author explains how their job prioritizes systems over spots and stewardship over inventing something entirely new.
The approach deviates from Goedecke's suggestions on dealing with quarterly priorities and experimental features.

## Conclusion

The author shares insights into why they prefer to operate in a different way, one that balances systems thinking with engineer-centric work.
