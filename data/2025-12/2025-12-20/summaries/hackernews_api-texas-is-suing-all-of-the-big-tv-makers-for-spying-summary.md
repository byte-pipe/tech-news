# Texas Files Lawsuit Against Big TV Makers for "Mass Surveillance"
Sony, Samsung, LG, Hisense, and TCL Accused of Spying on Consumers
by



Emma Roth
Dec 16, 2025 (3:38 PM UTC)
* **News Source:** The Verge

Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton has filed lawsuits against five major TV makers accusing them of secretly monitoring what consumers watch in their homes. In separate cases filed over the past few days, Texas alleges that these TVs use Automatic Content Recognition (ACR) to collect personal data used for targeted advertising.

* **Key Points:**

  • Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton claims the TVs made by Sony, Samsung, LG, Hisense, and TCL part of a "mass surveillance system" using ACR.
  • The lawsuits target five major TV makers accused of deceiving consumers about how their data is collected and used for targeted advertising.
  • ACRM uses visual and audio data to identify what users watch on TV, including movies, shows, and YouTube videos.
* **Paxton's Allegations:**
  • Victims of the alleged surveillance include people who stream content from services like Netflix, Hulu, or Amazon Prime.
  • The lawsuit claims that TV manufacturers siphon viewing data back to each company without consent.
  • ACRM is accused of capturing security and doorbell camera streams along with media sent using Apple AirPlay or Google Cast.
* **Paxton's Concerns:**
  • Texas wants the court to impose a civil penalty on the offending companies for violating the state's Deceptive Trade Practices Act, which aims to protect consumers from false advertising practices.
  • A CRM-based investigation found concerns about TCL and Hisense's ties to China due to their shared country of origin.
