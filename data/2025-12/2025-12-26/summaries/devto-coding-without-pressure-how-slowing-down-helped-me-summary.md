# Learning to Code Without Rushing: The Art of Slowing Down

## Overcoming Intense Pressure and Finding Patience in Coding

For a long time, the pursuit of learning to code was fueled by intense pressure to become proficient quickly. Daily goals and long hours were common strategies for progressing faster, despite feeling exhausted and uncertain about progress.

However, it took realizing that this mindset is ultimately flawed: "I wasn’t failing because I was slow; I was failing because I wouldn’t let myself slow down." This shift in perspective led to a new understanding of what it means to learn to code without the feeling of intense pressure.

## The Invisible Pressure and Code's Relational State

There's an unseen timer embedded within one's mind when learning to code, fueling expectations of rapid progress. To clarify this mindset, examine common feelings: "I should understand by now," "I'm behind everyone else," and "Why does [the project] take me so long?" These thoughts can quickly become overwhelming.

## A Shift in Perspective When Fast Learning Becomes Shallow Learning

Prior to embracing the art of slowing down, it was a pattern to hastily cover various topics without diving deeply into their explanation or nuances. The result? Consuming vast amounts of information but failing to achieve meaningful progress.

A pivotal moment arrived when focusing on specific topics became less about consuming and more about absorbing them accurately. Attributing success to merely covering the surface level versus attempting to genuinely understand each subject demonstrates a change.

## Slowing Down: A Method for Effective Learning

Slow learning involves deliberately focusing on one aspect or concept at a time, rather than tackling everything with immediate speed. Examples of effective strategies include:

• **One idea per task**: Prioritize specific concepts and focus on their details
• Simple solutions instead of innovative ideas
• Taking breaks without guilt or stress caused by the pressure to complete tasks quickly
• Stopping when cognitive fatigue sets in

This approach helps bridge the gap between learning theory and real-world application by providing a deeper comprehension. Noticing how taking slower, more deliberate strides leads to calmer, deeper learning experiences is essential.

## Confidence and Learning

When pressure subsides and understanding evolves at a sustainable pace, confidence typically follows closely in its wake. This shift allows for better communication within tasks and projects as questions "Am I good enough?" gradually give way to seeking improvement ("What can I understand better?").

## Lessons Learned from the Experience

Learning faster while keeping up with pressures has been achieved through this approach: It's not about covering more topics, but rather focusing on retaining information. Recognizing that speed without depth holds no value is crucial.

In conclusion, embracing a mindset of allowing learning to progress gradually offers numerous benefits such as reduced stress related to pressure and time loss due to inadequate understanding.
