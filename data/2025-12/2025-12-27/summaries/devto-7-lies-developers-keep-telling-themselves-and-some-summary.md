# 7 Lies Developers Keep Telling Themselves (And Somehow Still Believe) - DEV Community

## Lies Developers Tell Themselves

### Key Points
- Many developers tell themselves "This will only take a moment" when working on tasks.
- They often fall for refactoring tasks that never come to fruition due to forgotten priorities or unexpected issues.
- When estimating work, they may underestimate the complexity of tasks if not well-planned.
- They frequently promise to write tests but fail to do so.
- Development involves various edge cases and assumptions, leading to unrealistic claims about other developers' abilities.

## Summary

The article discusses common lies that developers tell themselves. These include:

* Making optimistic promises over past performance or potential failures ("This will only take a moment" )
* Underestimating task complexity by assuming it can be refactored later without effort
* Disregarding testing by neglecting to write necessary code ("I'll refactor this later")
* Downplaying the likelihood of problems with assumptions about other developers' work ("It's definitely not on my side")
* Assuming functionality will work everywhere without evidence ("This is definitely not on my site")
