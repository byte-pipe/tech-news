# Caught Up in the Cert Chase (And What I Learnt About Coding) - DEV Community

## Cultivating Skills Rather Than Certificates: A Focus on Practical Experience Over Paperwork

The pursuit of certificates has led many to prioritize them over tangible projects that demonstrate skills in coding. However, this approach overlooks the essence of programming - building something from scratch.

### The Limitation of Certificates

- **They don't guarantee employability**: Employers and clients are more interested in a portfolio of real projects than certificates.
- **Certifications can be outdated quickly**: New developments and updates often render old certifications useless.
- **Skills learned through experience matter more**: A programmer's ability to solve problems, debug code, and think creatively is invaluable.

### Seeking Practical Guidance

- **Free resources are available**: Utilize online platforms such as freeCodeCamp, Coursera, edX, and Udemy for introductory coding courses.
- **Tutorials can be effective**: Websites like Codecademy, FreeCodeCamp, and W3Schools offer interactive coding lessons and exercises.
- **Books provide in-depth knowledge**: There are numerous books on programming that cater to different levels of expertise.

### The Importance of Portfolios

- **Project-based learning is the best approach**: Instead of focusing on individual projects, concentrate on building a portfolio of meaningful projects.
- **Showcase your work online**: Utilize platforms like GitHub or GitLab to host and promote your projects publicly.

## Creating a Sustainable Solution

By shifting from certificates to practical skills development, individuals can focus on creating something real and demonstrating their programming abilities.
