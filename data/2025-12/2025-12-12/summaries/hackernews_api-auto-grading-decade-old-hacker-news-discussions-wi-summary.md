# Auto-grading decade-old Hacker News discussions with hindsight | karpathy

## Auto-Grading Decade-Old Hacker News Discussions with Hindsight

Yesterday, I stumbled upon an interesting HN thread from 10 years ago titled "Gemini Pro 3 hallucinates the HN front page." One comment caught my attention: Bjartr linked to the HN front page exactly 10 years prior. While thinking through discussions from the past, I realized that Large Language Models (LLMs) could excel at this task.

I created a code script using Opus 4.5 and ChatGPT 5.1 to analyze hundreds of front pages from December 2015 to 2025. This exercise is not only entertaining but also fascinating, considering the possibilities for future prediction and analysis.

## Key Findings

*   **Most prescient comments:** I awarded one comment with "Most prescient" as it accurately predicted the topic after 6 years of discussions.
*   **Incorrect predictions:** Another comment on its list was deemed "Most wrong," despite being correct about 2 other recent articles and topics.

## Interesting Insights

This task highlights how LLMs can assist in evaluating historic discussions, understanding potential outcomes, and making predictions. While it's true that future advancements could potentially enable perfect reconstruction, the act of using LLMs for this exercise is just as fascinating as predicting a 10-year future scenario.
