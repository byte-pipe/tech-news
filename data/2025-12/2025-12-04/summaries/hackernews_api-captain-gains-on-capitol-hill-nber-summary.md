# "Captain Gains" on Capitol Hill

## Key Takeaways

The article reviews research on a phenomenon known as "Captain Gains," where lawmakers who ascend to leadership positions perform similarly to their matched peers beforehand but outperform them by 47% annually after taking office. The study analyzed usage of transaction-level data from US congressional stock trades.

## Mechanisms Behind Captain Gains

Two primary mechanisms contribute to the superior performance of Congressional leaders:

### Political Influence Channel (Higher Returns When Party Controls Chamber)

* Increased returns when party controls or participates actively in regulatory decisions
* Sales of stocks preceding formal action taken by the party
* Purchase of stocks of companies with more government contracts and increased favorable support from senators/family ties

### Corporate Access Channel (Stock Trades Predicting Corporate News)

* Use of stocks expected to trade positively after company announcements, news, or changes
   + Greater positivity in stock prices when a company has received government attention and made improvements
