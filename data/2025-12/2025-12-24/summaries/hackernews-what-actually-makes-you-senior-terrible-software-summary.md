# What Actually Makes You Senior – Terrible Software

## Reducing Ambiguity – The Key to Senior Engineer Status

While junior engineers excel in architecture, communication, leadership, and technical expertise, it is their ability to reduce ambiguity that truly separates them from the rest. This concept is often overlooked, but its importance cannot be overstated.

### Key Points:

* Reducing ambiguity requires asking questions, separating noise from substance, and identifying what should be done now vs. what to punt.
* This process demands expertise in identifying underlying problems, understanding user pain points, and making assumptions clear.
* Senior engineers can turn vague product requirements into shippable plans by first clearly defining the problem they are trying to solve.

### Essential Details:

* Junior engineers excel at specific tasks like architecture and communication but struggle with ambiguity.
* Good senior engineers have a deep understanding of what's being solved, who is affected by the outcome, assumptions being made, and potential risk.
* Reducing ambiguity allows for successful project delivery, reducing surprises, and minimizing production fires.

### Test to Determine Senior Engineer Status:

One way to assess whether an engineer has reached senior+ status is to evaluate their ability to reduce technical ambiguity. Consider the following characteristics:
- Able to ask complex questions that drive clear understanding of the problem.
- Effectively separates what is necessary from unnecessary noise in technical requirements or designs.
- Identifies and prioritizes critical tasks, avoiding suboptimal decisions due to incomplete information.

By focusing on reducing ambiguity, senior engineers demonstrate their value by delivering high-quality work with minimal risk and more productive teams.
