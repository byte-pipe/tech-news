# Are We Losing Our Manners in Software Development? - DEV Community

## The Manners of Modern Software Development

The article explores how software development has evolved over time, particularly in terms of the approach to building systems and writing code.

### Key Points:

* Modern technology, including operating systems like Windows 95, lacks the discipline and "impressive" factor that drove its creators.
* Compared to older systems, modern codebases are often much larger due to new features and libraries that increase complexity without providing a clear benefit.
* This has led to a focus on convenience over craftsmanship, resulting in systems that require more resources (hardware and money) to maintain parity.

### Maintenance of Abstractions

The article critiques the design of modern software development environments:

* The abundance of abstraction layers and middleware can hide complex underlying issues.
* These systems often rely on performance optimizations at the expense of maintainability and flexibility.

### Developer Discipline

However, the article also touches upon the lost discipline that characterized older systems:

* Unlike modern systems where code is often long-running or resource-intensive, older systems were designed to be efficient.
* This shift away from craftsmanship has led to problems like:

* Environmental impact due to inefficient software usage
* Accessibility issues for users with outdated hardware
* Security vulnerabilities that exploit the fragility of layered dependencies

### Key Takeaways

1. **Modern Software Development is Losing Its "Impressive" Factor**: The shift from older systems to more complex and feature-rich codebases has lost some of the discipline that contributed to their success.

2. **Maintaining Abstraction Layers is a Challenge**: Despite the importance of these layers, modern software environments often prioritize performance over maintainability, leading to maintenance issues.

3. **Designing for Success is More Important Than Just Efficiency**: Old systems were designed with a clear goal in mind: to be efficient and effective. Modern systems must balance this with proper design to avoid the pitfalls mentioned above.
