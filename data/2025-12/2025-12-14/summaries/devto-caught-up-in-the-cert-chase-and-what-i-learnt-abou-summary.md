# The Illusion of Certificates in the World of Coding

## A Reality Check on Collecting Certificates

*   When scrolling through LinkedIn and seeing everyone with their shiny badges, it's easy to think that collecting certificates is the fastest way to prove one knows their stuff.
*   However, this perspective ignores the importance of practical skills over mere certifications.

### The Case for Practical Skills Over Certificates

*   Employers, clients, or anyone looking at your skills don't care about certificates; they care about what you've made and can show.
*   A portfolio of real projects is far more valuable than a list of badges.
*   Building something – even if it's small – teaches problem-solving, debugging, and creative thinking.

### Finding Free Resources for Practical Learning

*   If you know any courses, tutorials, or books that have helped you learn by heart, share them with others who might find them helpful.
*   Consider the following:

    *   [FreeCodeCamp](https://www freecodecamp.org/): Offers comprehensive coding bootcamps in programming languages and skills.
    *   [W3Schools](https://www.w3schools.com/): A popular platform for learning web development, covering JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and more.
    *   [EDUCRAFECTIVE](https://edcrfective.org/) (on GitHub): An open-source project that uses Python as the primary language to provide free and practical examples of various programming tasks.
