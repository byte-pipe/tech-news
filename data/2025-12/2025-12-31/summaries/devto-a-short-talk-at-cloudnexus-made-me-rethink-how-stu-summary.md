# A short talk at CloudNexus made me rethink how students are using AI - DEV Community

**Summary**

The author spoke at CloudNexus, an AWS Cloud Club event focused on cloud technologies and AI. The speaker discussed how students are currently using AI tools, particularly in the context of Agentic AI. However, most students did not realize the limitations of their skills when using these tools effectively.

The speaker shared that ChatGPT is a great tool for understanding concepts, breaking down ideas, writing content, and answering "why" questions. On the other hand, using it as one's main coding engine can lead to context and structural issues in real-world development.

The author emphasized the importance of mastering different AI tools tailored to their specific needs: ChatGPT for learning, coding copilots for building, cloud AI for scaling, and agents for automation when ready. The speaker recommended that students focus on growing with AI rather than replacing their thinking entirely.

**Main Points**

* Most students don't realize the limitations of their skills while using AI tools effectively.
* Agentic AI is less about prompts and more about delegation.
* Cloud-based AI systems require infrastructure, scale, and security to function effectively.
* Platforms like AWS Bedrockstart can help build agent-based systems that run in real-world environments.
* Students should use different AI tools tailored to their needs: ChatGPT for learning, coding copilots for building, cloud AI for scaling, and agents when ready.

**Key Takeaways**

* Focus on growing with AI rather than replacing your thinking.
* UseChatGPT to learn, coding copilots to build, cloud AI to scale, and agents when ready.
* Understand the limitations of your skills when using AI tools effectively.
