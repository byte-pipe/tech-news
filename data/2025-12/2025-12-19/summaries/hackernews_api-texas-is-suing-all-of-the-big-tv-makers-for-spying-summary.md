# Texas is suing all of the big TV makers for spying on what you watch | The Verge

* **Texas Sues Major TV Makers for Mass Surveillance of Consumers**:
  - The Texas Attorney General has filed lawsuits against five major TV manufacturers, alleging that they are secretly recording what consumers watch on their TVs.
  - It claims the TVs in question use Automatic Content Recognition (ACR) technology to collect personal data for targeted advertising.

# Key Points

* Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton is suing five big TV makers: Sony, Samsung, LG, Hisense, and TCL
* These TVs allegedly use ACR to record what consumers watch on their TVs
* The suits come under the Deceptive Trade Practices Act in Texas
* The accused companies are accused of "secretly recording" personal data from consumers without consent

# Case Breakdown by Manufacturer

* **Sony**: alleges that Sony devices capture screen shares within 500 milliseconds
* **Samsung**: claims Samsung captures screenshots every 500 milliseconds and discloses ACR usage "vague, misleading"
* **LG**: accused of collecting viewing data for targeted advertising using ACR
* **Hisense (TCL in China)**: based out of China, it allegedly uses "Chinese-sponsored surveillance devices" to record Texas residents' viewing habits
* **Vizio**: paid $2.2 million in 2017 to the FTC and NJ as part of a similar case

# Additional Concerns and Recommendations

- These accusations expose vulnerable consumers whose personal data may be misused by these companies
- The lawsuit urges the court to impose fines and ban the collection, sharing, or sale of collected data
