Caught Up in the Cert Chase (And What I Learned About Coding)
=================================================================

**The Pursuit of Certificates vs. Real-World Skills**

You're one step ahead in your pursuit of credentials. You sign up for free courses, complete modules on LinkedIn, and feel good about yourself knowing a language or following tutorials.

But as you focus more on certifications, you start to realize that they don't measure the value of skills like building projects. Employers, clients, and others care about what you've created, not just what you know.

**Deeper Learning: Theory vs. Practice**

When it comes to theory, there are some gems out there that can help solidify your programming knowledge. However, as a beginner, you want to focus on practical, real-world skills rather than theoretical ones.

**Resources for Practical Programming**

If you're looking for free resources to dive deeper into coding theory and practice, here are some recommendations:

*   GitHub: A platform where developers share their code and collaborate.
    * [Real World Algorithms](https://edabit.com/challenge/4PcG3k7tE)
    * [FreeCodeCamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org/)
    * [CS50](https://cs50.harvard.edu/)
*   Books: Some popular books on programming and code, including "Introduction to Algorithms" by Thomas H. Cormen.
*   Online Courses: Websites like Coursera, edX, and Udemy offer affordable courses from top universities.

**Temple of Real-World Experience**

When building projects isn't enough; what matters is the impact you make. Focus on making real-world projects that demonstrate problem-solving skills, debugging techniques, and creative thinking.

**Conclusion**
Real-world experience and a portfolio of practical projects will lead to better career prospects than a collection of certifications. By putting theory into practice, you'll become a more efficient programmer and develop valuable skills that employers want to see in their employees.
