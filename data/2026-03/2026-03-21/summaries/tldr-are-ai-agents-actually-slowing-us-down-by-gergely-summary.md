# Are AI agents actually slowing us down? - by Gergely Orosz

**Are AI Agents Actually Slowing Us Down?**

As more software engineers use AI agents daily, they inadvertently cause complications such as sloppy code, outages, quality issues, and a slowdown in shipping velocity.

Several companies are now adopting AI, including Uber and Big Tech, but this has led to a negative impact on product quality. Key concerns include degraded flagship websites (Anthropic) and increased SEVs (server error messages).

The use of AI is linked to the following problems:

*   **Slowdowns in long-term development**: Founders have reported that while AI tools can speed up startup velocity, they also introduce errors that take time to fix.
*   **Bloating code**: AI algorithms often produce code that is difficult to maintain and customize, leading to long-term development issues.

To address these concerns, potential solutions include:

1.  **Formal validation methods**: Implementing rigorous testing processes can help ensure the quality of AI-generated tools.
2.  **Reviving QA practices**: Establishing formal checklists and automated tests can assist in detecting bugs before release.
3.  **Reviving old-school QA ideas**: Manual review checks can identify inconsistencies in codebases that may cause problems down the line.

By acknowledging these challenges, developers can focus on creating more robust AI tools while maintaining the high-quality standards they strive for.
