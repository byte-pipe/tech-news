# Jim Himes Urges Support for Continued Spy Program in House Intelligence Committee Letter

## Key Points:
- Congressman Jim Himes is secretly lobbying colleagues on the House Intelligence Committee to preserve the FBI's warrantless search capability.
- He argues that the program targets foreign nations but also sweeps private communications of US citizens.
- Himes relies on internal FBI protocols as a substitute for constitutional warrants, citing improved compliance with recent changes within the agency.

## Summary:
Rep. Jim Himes is urging fellow Democrats to support the renewal of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA) surveillance program authorized under Section 702. However, his claims have been met with skepticism due to a lack of concrete evidence on how effectively the program prevents domestic misuse. Recent changes within the FBI undermine these arguments, and recent court opinions exposed the agency's improper search practices.

## Additional Information:

- However, this compliance metric relies on a unit that was recently shut down by the FBI, which might have affected its accuracy.
