# Competitive Differentiation is Poison

By Ravi Mehta
March 19, 2026 | 28 minutes read | Share
--------------------------------

The article argues that competitive differentiation is a destructive strategy for clear strategic thinking. Instead of focusing on the unique value proposition, teams prioritize differentiation over what truly sets them apart.

## Key Points

* The concept of strategic differentiation originated from the idea of pricing power.
* Commodity products (identical in all regards) can be matched by their competitors. Monopolies, with complete control over price and supply, can lower prices to increase demand or raise profits.
* Most companies don't exist at either extreme; they're driven by a desire for profit instead of maximizing demand.
* "Differentiation" is often used as a means to highlight the unique selling proposition (USP), but it only matters if customers perceive the USP as valuable.

## Problem

The problem with competitive differentiation is that:

1. It's incomplete: Customers may not care about what makes one company more different than another.
2. It prioritizes internal advantages over external opportunities: Companies focus on developing unique features or products instead of exploring new markets or offering better performance.

## Conclusion

The article critiques the idea that competitive differentiation is a valuable strategy, suggesting that it can lead to misguided priorities and a lack of understanding about what truly sets companies apart in the market. Instead, the authors propose refocusing on external advantages and exploration of new opportunities to drive innovation.
