# Nigeria's herder-farmer conflict: Deadly mix of religion, cattle and land in Plateau state

# 'Peace is a gradual thing': How land, cattle and identity fuel a deadly Nigerian conflict
## Background
Nigerian Plateau state has experienced recurring conflicts between herders (Christian) and farmers (Muslim), fueled by competition over resource space, including land. This clash has led to numerous incidents of violence and claimed thousands of lives.

### Key Points

* Multiple violent clashes occur annually in central Nigeria amidst growing tensions over land rights and cattle grazing
* Christian minority groups are widely perceived as the enemy
* Muslim communities have paid a disproportionately high toll due to Islamist militant attacks and gang activity
* US intervention aims to combat Boko Haram, Islamist insurgents operating in northern Nigeria
* Nigerian government disputes claims of persecution, citing all faiths as vulnerable

### Dynamics
- Historically, Plateau state has been linked to American missionaries
- State shares over 50 ethnic groups but Christians form the majority and are frequently targeted despite their peaceful nature

#### Key Facts Summary:

• Over 52 people were killed in a clash between Christian herders (Fulani) from the Irigwe ethnic group and Muslim farmers around midnight.
• The victims' homes were razed, causing hundreds of families to flee for their lives
• Conflict remains ongoing despite efforts by Nigeria's security forces to quell violence
