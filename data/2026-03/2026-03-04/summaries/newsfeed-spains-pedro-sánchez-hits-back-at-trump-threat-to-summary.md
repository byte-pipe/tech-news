### Text Summary

Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez has delivered a strong rebuttal to US President Donald Trump's threat to end trade with Spain, reaffirming his government's opposition to war. Sánchez criticized Trump's comments, describing them as "terrible" and accusing him of failing to increase Spain's defense budget. Trump had previously threatened to cut off all trade with Spain, calling it a "breakdown of international law" unless Spain agreed to let the US use its jointly-run bases for strikes against Iran. Sánchez responded by saying the Spanish government was not willing to engage in war and emphasized the need for economic measures to counter its impact. The meeting came as Sánchez faced criticism from some government coalition partners for his stance on Gaza and Iran, and as Trump accused Spain of being a "terrible partner" in NATO for failing to increase its defense budget.
