# TikTok Will Not Use End-to-End Encryption as it Claims It Enhances User Safety

TikTok has announced that it will not implement end-to-end encryption (E2EE) in its direct messaging (DM) platform. This decision was made in response to growing concerns that E2EE will make users more vulnerable to online harassment, cyber threats, and data breaches.

TikTok's US operations were separated from its global business in the US due to accusations that Chinese state ties may compromise user data. In light of this, the company believes E2EE will prevent authorities and law enforcement from accessing its direct messages if necessary.

This stance is part of TikTok's efforts to distance itself from its competitors, who have adopted E2EE as a standard practice for protecting user conversations. TikTok's approach is seen as a deliberate decision to prioritize the safety of its users, particularly young people.

However, this move may raise concerns about the company's compliance with global privacy expectations. Experts have criticized TikTok's decision, arguing that it allows for potentially exploitative and coercive interactions between users. They emphasize the importance of transparent accountability and the need for more stringent encryption and moderation controls.

The current status of other social media platforms' use of E2EE stands as follows:

* Apple's iMessage uses E2EE and is not available in a default setting.
* Google Messages users can also access secure conversations using end-to-end encryption.
* Snapchat has previously stated that it plans to offer E2EE as an option but is currently not defaulting to it.
* Instagram has recently announced that it is aiming to make E2EE a default feature for Direct Messages (DMs) in the near future.
* Facebook's Messenger uses E2EE but has not made it a default setting.

Overall, TikTok's decision to deny use of E2EE in its direct messaging platform has sparked a nuanced debate about the trade-offs between enhancing user security and prioritizing user experience.
