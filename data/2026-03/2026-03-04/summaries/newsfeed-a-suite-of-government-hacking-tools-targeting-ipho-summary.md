# A suite of government hacking tools targeting iPhones is now being used by cybercriminals | TechCrunch

**Hacking Tools Linked to Government Exploit Kit Coruna, Risking iPhone Compromise**

A suite of hacking tools targeting iPhones, dubbed Coruna, has been identified by security researchers as having leaked into the hands of cybercriminals from government customers. The exploit kit was first discovered in February 2025 by Google's surveillance vendor during an attempt to hack into someone's phone with spyware, but was later found being used by Russian espionage groups and financially motivated hackers in China.

Google security researchers warn of an emerging market for "secondhand" exploits, which are sold to hackers for money. The Coruna kit can bypass an iPhone's defenses by visiting a malicious website containing exploit code, such as being sent a malicious link, and has been linked to the U.S. government, but its origins are unclear.

The toolkit can hack an iPhone five separate ways by chaining together 23 vulnerabilities in its digital arsenal. Affected devices range from iOS 13 to 17.2.1, which was released in December 2023. The Coruna kit contains components previously used in a hacking campaign called Operation Triangulation, where the US government attempted to hack iPhones belonging to its employees in 2023.

**Porous Security and Exploits Risks iPhone Compromise**

The discovery highlights the risks of using outdated software and the importance of upgrading iPhone security. Google and other mobile security companies are warning of the potential for these tools to be used by cybercriminals with malicious intent, who may prioritize financial gain over protecting the security of iPhone users.

This development serves as a reminder of the need for continued security updates, patches, and awareness among iPhone users to prevent potential cybersecurity breaches.
