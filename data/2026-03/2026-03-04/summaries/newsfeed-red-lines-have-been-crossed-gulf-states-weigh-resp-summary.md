# 'All red lines have been crossed': Gulf states weigh response to Iranian strikes

Gulf states have reached a breaking point amid the ongoing conflict between Iran and the US-Israeli alliance, with multiple attacks on their territory and infrastructure.

*   Iranian state TV announced that the country had fired 100 drones and 400 missiles at several Arab states in response to their refusal to cede the right to strike Iranian oil and gas facilities. These attacks were reportedly based on intercepts in the Persian Gulf, but some also targeted key energy infrastructure in the region.
*   Qatar's foreign ministry spokesman told journalists that the country's sovereignty had been "crossed" by these attacks, and that all options were being considered to defend its territory and interests.
*   In Bahrain, the US-run naval base was damaged due to debris from an intercepted missile, and smoke was seen rising into the air.
*   The Financial Times reported that Iran had also fired missiles at the UAE, the key trade and tourism hub of the region.
*   Iran's strategy of targeting the global economy is also evident in calls by Tehran to US President Joe Biden to end the war. If the US were to continue its military campaign, it could push the Gulf states closer to Washington and increase their potential role in the conflict. However, this could also lead to unintended consequences, such as Iranian intervention on behalf of the US in the war.
