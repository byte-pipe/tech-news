# Anthropic's Claude Can Now Control Your Computer

## Key Announcement
- Anthropic introduced a new feature that lets Claude take control of a user’s computer to perform tasks such as locating and sending files.  
- The capability is available to subscribers on qualifying Claude Pro or Claude Max plans.  

## How It Works
- Claude selects appropriate tools via connectors (e.g., Google Calendar, Slack) to complete a request.  
- If no connector exists, Claude can manually interact with the OS by typing, moving the cursor, scrolling, and clicking, just like a human user.  
- The AI always asks for permission before acting and can be stopped at any time.  

## Safety Measures & Concerns
- Anthropic added safeguards against prompt‑injection attacks and other vulnerabilities; the system scans for known risks automatically.  
- Users are warned that the feature is experimental, may contain bugs, and should not be used with apps that handle sensitive data (many such apps are disabled by default).  
- Experts note that granting an AI direct computer access could expose users to security threats if the AI or its “claws” are hijacked.  

## Availability
- The research preview runs on macOS computers only.  
- It is limited to Claude Pro and Claude Max subscribers.  

## Integration with Dispatch
- Claude’s computer‑use feature works with Anthropic’s Dispatch, allowing tasks to be assigned from a phone (e.g., morning email check, opening Claude Cowork or Claude Code sessions).  
- Combined, they can generate briefings, run tests, or perform other automated workflows even when the user is away.  

## Outlook
- Anthropic released the preview to collect early feedback and identify areas needing improvement for more complex or reliable task execution.
