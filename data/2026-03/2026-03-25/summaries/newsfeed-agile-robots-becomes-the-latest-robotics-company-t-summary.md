# Agile Robots partners with Google DeepMind

## Partnership overview
- Agile Robots, based in Munich, announced a strategic research partnership with Google DeepMind.  
- The collaboration will integrate DeepMind’s Gemini Robotics foundation models into Agile Robots’ bots.  
- Data collected by the robots will be used to refine and improve the Gemini AI models.  
- Joint effort will focus on testing, fine‑tuning, and deploying robots in industrial sectors such as electronics manufacturing, automotive, data centers, and logistics.  
- The agreement is described as long‑term; specific duration and pricing were not disclosed.

## Statements from leadership
- Zhaopeng Chen, co‑founder and CEO of Agile Robots, highlighted the company’s existing footprint of over 20,000 robotic solutions worldwide and emphasized the opportunity to create autonomous, intelligent production systems.  
- He positioned the integration of Gemini models as a move that places Agile Robots at the forefront of the rapidly expanding market for intelligent automation.

## Agile Robots background
- Founded in 2018.  
- Has raised more than $270 million in venture capital.  
- Key investors include SoftBank Vision Fund, Xiaomi, and Midas Group.

## Industry context
- This is the latest in a series of robotics hardware companies partnering with Google DeepMind.  
- Earlier in the year, Boston Dynamics (owned by Hyundai) announced a similar partnership to use DeepMind’s AI models for its Atlas humanoid robot.  
- German startup Neura Robotics partnered with Qualcomm to use its IQ10 processor series for future robots.  
- Experts, including Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang, view “physical AI” as the next major frontier, suggesting such collaborations will continue to grow.
