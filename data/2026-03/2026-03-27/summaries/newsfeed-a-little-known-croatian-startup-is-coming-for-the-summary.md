# A little‑known Croatian startup is coming for the robotaxi market with help from Uber

## Partnership and launch plan
- Uber, Chinese autonomous‑driving firm Pony.ai, and Rimac‑born robotaxi startup **Verne** announced a joint plan to start a commercial robotaxi service in Europe, beginning in Zagreb, Croatia.
- Pony.ai will provide the autonomous‑driving system and the **Arcfox Alpha T5** robotaxi (developed with BAIC).
- Verne will own and operate the fleet; Uber will supply its ride‑hailing network and will invest an undisclosed amount in Verne as a strategic partner.
- On‑road testing is already underway in Zagreb, though no exact commercial launch date was given.

## Verne’s background
- Founded in 2019 as **Project 3 Mobility (P3)** within the Rimac Group ecosystem (which also includes Rimac Bugatti, Rimac Energy, and Rimac Technology).
- Mate Rimac, founder of Rimic Group, holds a 23 % stake in the group.
- Verve publicly launched in July 2024 after raising **€100 million** in funding.
- The company’s aim is an urban robotaxi service using purpose‑built two‑seater electric vehicles, rather than mass‑market EVs for human drivers.

## Vehicle and operations strategy
- Verne does **not** develop its own self‑driving stack; it focuses on the electric vehicle, the ride‑hailing app, and fleet‑management back‑end (including cleaning and maintenance).
- Production of Verne’s own robotaxi EVs is planned at a new factory in **Lučko, Croatia**, slated to start later this year.
- To date, Verne has built and tested **60 verification prototypes** but has not yet launched its two‑seater model.
- The initial service will use Pony.ai‑BAIC’s Arcfox Alpha T5, bookable via Uber and Verne’s own app.

## Future ambitions
- Verne targets a **fleet of thousands of robotaxis** within the next few years, beginning with Zagreb and expanding to other European markets.
- CEO **Marko Pejkovic** emphasized the need for autonomous mobility that moves from testing to real‑world service across Europe.
