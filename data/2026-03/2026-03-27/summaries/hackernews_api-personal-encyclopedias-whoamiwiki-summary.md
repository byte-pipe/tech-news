## Overview
- The author discovered a large collection of family photos and turned the material into a personal encyclopedia using MediaWiki and language‑model assistance.  
- The project evolved from organizing physical prints to automatically generating wiki pages from digital media and assorted data exports.

## Initial Photo Organization
- Counted 1,351 printed photos spanning three generations.  
- Grouped images by physical traits (aspect ratio, film stock).  
- Conducted an oral interview with the grandmother to order wedding photos and capture stories, creating detailed notes and name identifications.

## Building the First Wiki Pages
- Set up a local MediaWiki instance, modeled pages after Wikipedia (infobox, lead, sections).  
- Added scanned photos with descriptive captions.  
- Linked every person to stub pages, later connecting to real Wikipedia articles for broader context (venues, legal amendments, etc.).  
- Recognized the power of encyclopedia software for preserving otherwise lost media and knowledge.

## Expanding the Workflow
- Followed guidance from r/genealogy on oral‑history methods; used audio transcription and language models to streamline editing.  
- Created numerous pages linking individuals to life events, uncovering unexpected connections (e.g., a wedding singer later became the author’s birth nurse).

## Automating with Digital Media
- Leveraged EXIF metadata in digital photos and videos for dates, times, and coordinates.  
- Prompted Claude Code to browse a folder of 625 Coorg‑trip photos; the model generated a detailed wiki draft, identified locations, transportation, and people without explicit metadata.  
- Enriched drafts with personal anecdotes supplied by the author.

## Multi‑Source Data Integration
- For a 2022 Mexico City trip, combined 291 photos, 343 videos, Google Maps timeline, Uber receipts, bank transactions, and Shazam history.  
- Claude Code performed cross‑referencing:
  - Matched bank purchases to restaurant visits.  
  - Linked ticket invoices to sports events shown in media.  
  - Mapped Uber trips to exact pickup/drop‑off points.  
  - Used Shazam tracks to describe background music at venues.  
  - Extracted a specific video frame of a guitarist after a user prompt.  

## MediaWiki Features Utilized
- Talk pages for clarifying gaps.  
- Categories for thematic grouping.  
- Revision history to track incremental updates as new data arrived.  

## Scaling to Communication Archives
- Imported Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp archives (≈100 k messages, thousands of voice notes).  
- The model traced friendship arcs, extracted life episodes, and authored multiple pages that read as collaborative biographies.  
- Friends eagerly consumed the resulting narratives.

## Insight
