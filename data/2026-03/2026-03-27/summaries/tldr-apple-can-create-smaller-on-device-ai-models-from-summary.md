# Apple Can Create Smaller On-Device AI Models From Google's Gemini

## Main Points
- Apple has been granted “complete access” to Google’s Gemini model hosted in Google’s data centers, allowing the company to use the model for distillation and create smaller, task‑specific AI models that run locally on Apple devices.  
- The distillation process involves prompting Gemini to perform high‑quality tasks, capturing its reasoning steps, and feeding that data into cheaper models so they can emulate Gemini’s internal computations with far less compute power.  
- Apple can also modify Gemini’s behavior to align with its own response standards, though it faces challenges because Gemini is primarily tuned for chatbot and coding use cases, which do not always match Apple’s requirements.  
- The Apple Foundation Models team continues to develop proprietary AI models, but Gemini will serve as the backbone for the upcoming “smarter” chatbot version of Siri planned for iOS 27.  
- The enhanced Siri is expected to handle a range of functions similar to other chatbots: answering questions, summarizing content, analyzing uploaded documents, storytelling, offering emotional support, and completing tasks such as travel booking.

## Implications for Siri
- Siri will gain capabilities comparable to leading chatbots, including document scanning and understanding, narrative generation, and task automation.  
- On‑device execution reduces reliance on internet connectivity, improving privacy and responsiveness.  
- Apple’s ability to tailor Gemini’s responses ensures the assistant aligns with Apple’s brand voice and user experience standards.
