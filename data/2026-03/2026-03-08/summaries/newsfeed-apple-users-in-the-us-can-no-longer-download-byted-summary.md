# Apple users in the US can no longer download ByteDance's Chinese apps - Ars Technica

# Apple blocks ByteDance apps for US iPhone users  

## Background  
- TikTok now operates in the United States under new ownership, but Apple has added technical blocks that prevent iOS users in the US from downloading other ByteDance‑owned apps.  
- ByteDance’s portfolio includes Douyin (Chinese TikTok), the AI chatbot Doubao, and the reading platform Fanqie Novel, among many others.  
- Historically, iPhone owners could download these apps from any location with a valid Chinese App Store account, without a VPN.  

## New restrictions (effective January 2025)  
- Starting in late January, US iPhone users with Chinese App Store accounts receive a pop‑up stating “This app is unavailable in the country or region you’re in” when trying to download or update any ByteDance app.  
- The block applies only to ByteDance‑owned apps; other Chinese developers are not affected.  
- Apple’s “Learn More” link in the pop‑up, which once pointed to an explanatory Apple Support page, was removed after inquiries.  

## Legal context  
- The restrictions align with the Protecting Americans from Foreign Adversary Controlled Applications Act, which prohibits distribution, maintenance, or updates of apps majority‑controlled by ByteDance within US borders.  
- The law, originally aimed at TikTok, also covers other ByteDance apps such as CapCut and Lemon8.  
- An executive order extended the TikTok divestiture deadline to January 23 2026; the deal transferred TikTok’s US operations to a consortium led by Silver Lake, Oracle, and MGX, but did not explicitly include all other ByteDance apps.  

## Technical geoblocking mechanism  
- Apple has moved from account‑based regional restrictions to on‑device location checks (the “countryd” system) that use GPS, Wi‑Fi country codes, and SIM data.  
- This system mirrors EU‑specific geoblocking introduced after the Digital Markets Act, which limited alternative app store access to users physically inside the EU.  
- App Store terms added a clause in January 2025 stating Apple may use the IP address to approximate location for legal compliance.  

## Impact and workarounds  
- Users report being unable to download or update Douyin, Doubao, Fanqie Novel, and other ByteDance apps while in the US.  
- Some have temporarily bypassed the block with VPNs that spoof location, but these methods are not consistently reliable.  
- TikTok, CapCut, and Lemon8 remain available on the US App Store because they are covered by the divestiture agreement.  

## Summary  
- Apple now enforces US‑based geoblocking for all ByteDance‑owned apps, citing federal legislation that targets apps controlled by a foreign adversary.  
- The move reflects Apple’s broader shift toward device‑level location enforcement, a practice first seen in EU compliance efforts.  
- While the primary TikTok app is exempt due to the recent ownership transfer, dozens of other ByteDance applications are effectively removed from US iPhone users’ reach.
