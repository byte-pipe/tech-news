# Apple’s 512 GB Mac Studio vanishes, a quiet acknowledgment of the RAM shortage – Ars Technica

## Key Points
- Apple removed the 512 GB RAM option from the top‑tier M3 Ultra Mac Studio between March 4 and the present, and raised the price of the 256 GB model from $1,600 to $2,000.
- The Tech Specs page still lists the 512 GB configuration, but the Apple Store and configuration list no longer show it.
- This is an unusual move for Apple; it typically only drops older iPhone storage tiers or adjusts shipping estimates.

## Context & Impact
- The 512 GB Mac Studio was a niche, high‑end product costing $9,499, aimed at workloads needing massive graphics memory (e.g., large language models).
- macOS Tahoe 26.2 introduced Thunderbolt 5 clustering, allowing multiple Macs to pool memory; now a 512 GB cluster requires two Mac Studios instead of one.
- Apple’s Ultra chips are the only ones supporting >128 GB RAM; all other Apple silicon (M4/M5 series) max out at 128 GB, 64 GB, or 32 GB.

## Supply‑Chain Explanation
- DRAM shortages stem from manufacturers shifting capacity to high‑bandwidth memory (HBM) for AI accelerators like Nvidia’s H200, reducing traditional DRAM availability.
- Smaller companies have faced price hikes and launch delays; Apple’s scale gives it better negotiating power, but CEO Tim Cook warned that rising memory costs could pressure margins later in the year.
