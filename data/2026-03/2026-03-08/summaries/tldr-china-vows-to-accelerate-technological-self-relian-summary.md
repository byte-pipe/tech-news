# China vows to accelerate technological self‑reliance, AI push

## Main commitments
- China pledged to speed up the development of domestic high‑tech industries, aiming for greater technological self‑reliance.
- The government will nurture “unicorn” firms in emerging fields and promote large‑scale commercial use of artificial intelligence (AI).

## Target sectors
- Semiconductors, aerospace, and AI (including embodied AI for humanoid robots).
- Future industries such as quantum technology, 6G communications, and other advanced technologies.

## Implementation measures
- Launch new data‑centre projects and coordinate computing capacity distribution nationwide.
- Establish a system for AI security risk prevention and control.
- Encourage rapid R&D cycles, leveraging China’s world‑leading supply chains and low‑cost manufacturing.

## International and economic context
- U.S. tech firms have deeper financial resources, but China relies on its manufacturing base and supply‑chain advantages.
- A Rhodium report (January) warned that emerging industries may not generate sufficient investment to sustain a 5 % GDP growth rate in coming years.

## Outlook and challenges
- Beijing’s focus on AI and robotics aims to boost productivity across manufacturing, logistics, education, and healthcare.
- Potential limits to growth stem from investment shortfalls and competition with better‑funded foreign rivals.
