# Honda is killing its EVs — and any chance of competing in the future | TechCrunch

**Honda Axes Its Electric Vehicle (EV) Programs**

Honda is halting its development of several ground-up electric vehicles (EVs), including the Acura RDX and Prologue, due to dwindling motivation and opportunities in the EV market.

The company has been trying to compete with Chinese automakers and has kicked some EV projects to the curb. It points out US tariffs and Chinese competition as factors contributing to its departure from the EV arena.

**Factors Contributing to Honda's Exit**

*   **Lack of viable EV strategy**: Honda never had a complete EV plan, leaving it vulnerable to disruption.
*   **Production halt**: The company will not produce some non-renewable (P2D) and existing designs, which may harm its position in the market.

**Missed Opportunities for Legacy Automakers**

    *   **Drop batteries into an original design**: Dropping fuel systems into vehicles designed for EVs often results in a heavier, less efficient, and more expensive product.
*   **Disregarding Chinese competition**: Honda has not developed any fully electric products like the Ma XEV which would have shown competitiveness as compared to other major Chinese automakers.

**Impact of Honda's Shift**

    *   It may fall behind other automakers in shifts towards electric drivetrain and software-defined vehicles, particularly in 2026.
*   Honda is taking a risk by not being in the EV production cycle during this pivotal time.
