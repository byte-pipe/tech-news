# America risks a nuclear-arms race with China

## Blame an expired treaty with Russia

Share
Photograph: Reuters

Feb 3rd 2026
|
WASHINGTON, DC
|
7 min read

ROBERT OPPENHEIMER, the father of America’s nuclear bomb, described his country’s atomic rivalry with the Soviet Union as “two scorpions in a bottle”. The risks of this stand-off have been contained over the years by various arms-control agreements, most recently NewSTART. But that treaty expired this week, with no replacement. To make matters more dangerous, these days there is a third scorpion in the bottle: China. Its nuclear build-up, the world’s fastest since the height of the cold war, is likely to prompt America to expand its arsenal, too. A new arms race is in the offing.
