# Don’t Love Your Job? I Hear You. But Don’t Quit. At Least, 99% of You Shouldn’t.

## The Problem
- Many tech jobs that once felt rewarding now feel tedious or meaningless.
- Examples: endless deployments for engineers, repetitive feature updates for PMs, sales on shrinking products, CS tickets routed to bots, marketing campaigns forced into “AI”.
- The rise of AI makes the dissatisfaction feel more existential.

## Why Not Quit Yet
- Quitting without a solid plan can be risky in the current market.
- CEOs are reshaping organizations around AI, which changes the value of many roles.
- Leaving now may mean your position is filled by AI rather than a new human hire.

## CEO Trends in the Age of AI
- **Leaner organizations:** CEOs want to right‑size, often cutting headcount dramatically.
- **Team suitability:** Many CEOs doubt that half (or more) of their current staff can work effectively alongside AI.
- **Back‑filling with AI:** Departures are often replaced by AI agents; some companies have shifted from dozens of employees to a handful of humans plus many AI tools, seeing revenue improvements.

## The Market Reality
- The tech job market of 2025‑2026 is tighter than the 2021 boom.
- Senior roles are scarcer; mid‑level positions are being compressed or eliminated.
- Entry‑level jobs increasingly require AI‑first workflows and minimal human oversight.
- Employers are selective, preferring candidates who already build with AI rather than those who are merely “open to learning.”
- Before resigning, ask: Do I have a concrete offer or a pipeline of opportunities that match today’s demand?

## What To Do Instead of Quitting
- Treat a boring but stable job as a platform for skill development.
- **Learn to build with AI:** Go beyond prompt engineering; create and ship side projects, automate your own workflow, and demonstrate results to your boss.
- Become the team member who can multiply output with AI tools; such people are the last to be cut.
- Position yourself as someone the CEO sees as “AI‑adapted” and indispensable.

## Final Takeaway
- Loving your job is rare, both before and after AI’s rise.
- In 2021 you could job‑hop for big raises; that arbitrage has largely vanished except for elite AI talent.
- Success in the coming years belongs to those who stay, adapt, become leaner, and make themselves irreplaceable—whether by humans or AI.
- If you’re dissatisfied, ensure you have a better, concrete opportunity before walking away from what you have now.
