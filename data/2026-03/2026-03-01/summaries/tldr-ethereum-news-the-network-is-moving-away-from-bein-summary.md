# Ethereum news: The network is moving away from being a slow giant to become a high-speed 'internet of value' by 2029

# Here is why Ethereum's bold new plan could make the blockchain giant high‑speed 'internet of value' by 2029

## Key points
- Researchers from the Ethereum Foundation released a draft “Strawmap” roadmap that sketches possible upgrades through 2029; it is advisory, not binding.  
- The roadmap focuses on five ambitions: near‑instant finality, much higher throughput, built‑in privacy, quantum‑resistant security, and tighter integration with layer‑2 solutions.  
- The plan signals a dual‑track scaling approach where the base layer continues to strengthen while layer‑2 networks take on more specialized roles.  

## Strawmap overview
- Presented as a technical, diagram‑heavy document, the Strawmap is intended to guide research and development well before formal governance decisions.  
- Justin Drake describes it as largely independent of Ethereum governance, serving as an early‑stage R&D tool.  
- The roadmap is meant to give developers and businesses directional clarity about the network’s long‑term evolution.  

## Layer‑2 strategy shift
- Vitalik Buterin noted that earlier assumptions about layer‑2s handling most scaling are being reconsidered as base‑layer improvements progress.  
- The new vision envisions a balanced ecosystem: a stronger layer‑1 that can achieve finality in seconds, complemented by layer‑2s specialized for privacy, specific applications, or enhanced security.  
- Faster finality is expected to improve bridging between layer‑2s and overall user experience.  

## Privacy and quantum security
- The draft proposes native “shielded” transfers at the base layer, allowing ETH movements without full public disclosure, expanding use cases for individuals and businesses.  
- Post‑quantum cryptography research is being intensified to protect the network against future quantum threats, acknowledging the need for long‑term security of trillions of dollars in value.  

## Implications for the ecosystem
- By publishing a multi‑year sketch, the Foundation aims to move beyond the perception of slow, incremental upgrades.  
- The roadmap’s flexibility reflects the decentralized governance model, allowing debate and revision as the network evolves.  
- Success of the plan would position Ethereum as the “internet of value,” with ether serving as money for that internet.
