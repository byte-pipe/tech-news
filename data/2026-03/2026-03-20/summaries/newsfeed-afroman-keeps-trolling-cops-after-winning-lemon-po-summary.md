# Afroman keeps trolling cops after winning “Lemon Pound Cake” defamation case - Ars Technica

**Afroman Win Defamation Case Against Cops**

• Afroman, the rapper, won a defamation lawsuit against seven cops after they made music videos mocking him for a 2022 raid of his home.
• The rap video used real footage from the police raid and included security camera and police-recorded footage.
• The cops claimed to have been humiliated by the music video and received death threats.
• Afroman's lawyer argued that the lawsuit was a clear-cut freedom of speech case, as he had discussed the events on his social media channels without intending to defame the cops.

**Key Takeaways:**

* Afroman used public footage from the police raid in defense videos for "Lemon Pound Cake," "Why You Disconnecting My Video Camera," and other songs.
* The rap video was seen as a flippant commentary on events, not an attempt to defame the cops.
* Afroman's lawyer argued that the lawsuit was based on a misunderstanding of freedom of speech rules.

**Key Facts:**

* Afroman was granted $1.5 million in damages for the lawsuit.
* The trial featured testimony from Afroman and the police, as well as footage and statements from various parties involved in the case.
* The jury ultimately sided with Afroman on all claims.
* Local 12 reported that tears streamed down Afroman's face when he won the trial.
