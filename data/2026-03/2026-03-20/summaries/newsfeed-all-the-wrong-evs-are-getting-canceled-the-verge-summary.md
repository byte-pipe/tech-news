# The Misguided Focus on Affordable EVs in a Slowing Market

The electric vehicle (EV) industry has been experiencing a decline in recent months, with automakers aggressively promoting expensive models. While the focus has shifted to affordable EVs that attract new customers, it seems like an oversight.

* A look at past model year releases reveals that big, expensive EVs remain on the market, taking advantage of delayed demand and changing policies.
* Examples include:
	+ Volvos Ex30: no 2027 US release
	+ Chevys Bolt (only a limited 18-month run)
	+ Nissans Leaf S trim: cancelled in the US and Canada

These models have clear drawbacks, making them less desirable. Meanwhile, affordable EVs that had potential to draw new customers are being phased out.

* The Volvo Ex30, for instance, was supposed to start at $35,000 but won't feature a 2027 release.
* The Chevy Bolt's limited US production run will end after only 18 months.

Despite the decline of affordable EVs, it appears that expensive models continue to draw customers. This trend highlights an apparent mismatch between the perceived benefits of EVs and their actual performance.
