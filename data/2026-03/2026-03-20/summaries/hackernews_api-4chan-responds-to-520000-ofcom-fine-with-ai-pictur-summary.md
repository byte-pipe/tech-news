## US Messaging Platform 4Chan Responds to £520,000 Fine with AI-Generated Hamster Cartoon Image

The UK online safety regulator Ofcom has imposed a total fine of £520,000 on the American-based messaging platform 4Chan for failing to comply with various aspects of the Online Safety Act. In response to the fine, 4Chan's lawyer Preston Byrne used an AI-generated cartoon image of a hamster to respond.

### Key Points

* The US tech company is responding to Ofcom's £520,000 fine and using a £50,000 AI-generated image.
* 4Chan has claimed that it is complying with the regulations and has not paid the fines.
* Although it is refusing to pay all previous fines, Byrne cited age verification and risk assessments as cornerstones of UK online safety laws.

### Ofcom's Response

* Director Suzanne Cater expressed frustration at Ofcom's efforts to establish new standards for online safety in the digital world.
* She noted that companies like 4Chan are not allowed to promote unhealthy activities, especially those related to children in the UK, such as pornography and gambling.
