# A Mexican teen migrant dies in a Florida jail holding ICE detainees : NPR

**A Mexican Teen Migrant Dies in Florida Jail Holding ICE Detainees**

• A 19-year-old Mexican migrant, Royer Perez-Jimenez, has died in a Florida jail holding immigrants while in Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) custody.
• This is the second reported death of an immigrant at the facility since President Donald Trump's administration began.

**Key Points:**

* Perez-Jimenez was a U.S. citizen and had been living in Mexico before being detained by ICE.
* The cause of death remains under investigation, with an official classification as "presumed suicide" initially announced by authorities.
* This is the 46th reported death of an immigrant held at the Glades County Detention Center (Moore Haven) since January 2025.

**Immigration and Detention:**

* Since Trump took office in 2017, 13 immigrants have died while being detained or held at ICE facilities across the country.
* The Office of the District 21 Medical Examiner released a report indicating that no further autopsy details are available.
* Prosecutors expressed outrage over Perez-Jimenez's death and requested information from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security and Attorney General's offices.

**Social Reactions:**

* Detention Watch Network described the incident as a "tragic reminder of the unacceptable risks faced by immigrants in ICE detention facilities."
* Immigrant communities have condemned Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) for its policies, citing them as responsible for deaths.
