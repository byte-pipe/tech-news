# Apple’s new emoji has a controversial past but fans love it anyway

## Main news
- Apple’s iOS 26.4 update adds eight new emojis, including a trombone, treasure chest, fight cloud, orca, landslide, ballet dancer, “hairy creature” (Bigfoot) and the “distorted face”.
- The distorted face emoji is the same yellow character that appeared in Apple’s 2025 “Crush” advertisement, which sparked widespread criticism and an official apology from the company.
- The Unicode Consortium approved the designs last summer, but their inclusion in the iOS update surprised many users.

## Fan and critic reactions
- Some fans find the addition humorous and predict the distorted face will become a frequently used emoji.
- Critics view it as a reminder of the “horrible” Crush ad, questioning why Apple would revive that imagery.
- Comments on X (formerly Twitter) range from jokes about “mementos from that horrible advertisement” to anticipation of the emoji’s popularity.

## Context and branding shift
- Apple’s recent marketing, such as playful ads and the Gen Z‑focused MacBook Neo, signals a move toward a lighter, more whimsical brand image.
- The emoji rollout is presented as part of this broader strategy to embrace sillier, self‑referential content.
