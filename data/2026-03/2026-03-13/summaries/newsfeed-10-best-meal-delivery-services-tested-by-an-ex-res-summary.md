# 10 Best Meal Delivery Services, Tested by an Ex‑Restaurant Critic | WIRED  

## Overview  
- WIRED’s Reviews team has evaluated more than two dozen meal‑kit services since 2017.  
- The author, a former restaurant critic, judged each plan on recipe quality, menu breadth, ingredient sourcing, preparation ease, packaging, price transparency, and suitability for dietary restrictions.  
- Updates (March 2026) added new pricing, re‑tested Marley Spoon, and incorporated feedback on gluten‑free and vegan options for EveryPlate and Factor.  

## Top Picks by Category  

### Best Meal Kit Overall – Marley Spoon  
- Emphasises classic culinary techniques (e.g., European‑inspired dishes, Martha Stewart‑endorsed recipes).  
- Recent rebrand adds more convenient 15‑minute meals and ready‑to‑heat options, plus a broader international menu (Moroccan, Indian, etc.).  
- Pros: strong technique, large weekly menu (100‑140 choices), good protein sourcing (e.g., Cento tomatoes).  
- Cons: ingredients are packed loosely, making it harder to locate items; meat sourcing lacks full transparency; limited flexibility for advance ordering; ready‑to‑heat meals are less impressive.  
- Cost: $9‑$13 per serving plus $11 shipping; $74 per week for two meals serving two people.  

### Meal Kit With the Best Selection – HelloFresh  
- Largest market share and longest history; consistently refreshed recipes and sourcing.  
- Ingredients are pre‑portioned in clearly labeled bags, simplifying prep.  
- Offers a wide variety of modern, cosmopolitan dishes.  
- Known for reliable organization and steady improvements in quality.  

### Best Family‑Friendly Meal Kit – Home Chef *(mentioned in sidebar)*  
- Tailors meals to families with kid‑approved flavors and flexible serving sizes.  

### Most Flexible (No Subscription) – Blue Apron *(mentioned in sidebar)*  
- Allows ordering without a recurring commitment, appealing to occasional users.  

### Notable Alternatives  
- **Green Chef** and **Purple Carrot** – focus on organic, gluten‑free, and plant‑based options.  
- **EveryPlate** – budget‑friendly, tested for gluten‑free and vegan meals.  
- **Factor** – prepared‑meal service, also evaluated for dietary restrictions.  

## Key Findings  
- The best kits combine solid culinary technique with a broad, rotating menu.  
- Packaging waste varies: Marley Spoon’s loose packing reduces material but can be less user‑friendly.  
- Dietary accommodations are improving; many services now list “gluten‑free friendly” or vegetarian/vegan meals, though cross‑contamination guarantees are rare.  
- Price transparency remains a challenge; WIRED’s guide breaks down per‑meal costs and shipping fees.  
- Competitors like HelloFresh and Blue Apron have narrowed the gap with Marley Spoon by enhancing convenience and expanding flavor profiles.
