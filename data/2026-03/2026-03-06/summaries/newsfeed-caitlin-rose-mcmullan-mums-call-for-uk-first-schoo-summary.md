# Caitlin-Rose McMullan: Mum's call for UK-first school bus law

# How many more children must die? – Mum's call for UK‑first school bus law

## Background
- Caitlin‑Rose McMullan, 11, was killed in March 2025 in Castledawson, County Londonderry, after being hit by a car while exiting a school bus just metres from her home.
- The UK has no legal requirement for other vehicles to stop when a school bus is picking up or dropping off pupils; the Highway Code contains eight overtaking rules but none specifically address school buses.

## Mother's Call for Change
- Stella McGinn, Caitlin‑Rose’s mother, describes the incident as a “living nightmare” and says families have already endured “unbearable pain” because of inadequate bus safety.
- She questions how many more children must be injured or killed before legislation is introduced, noting that a year after her daughter’s death no law changes are “set in stone.”
- McGinn wants her daughter’s legacy to be safer school journeys and urges the government to act quickly.

## Legislative Response
- Northern Ireland Infrastructure Minister Liz Kimmins announced plans to bring forward legislation that would prohibit overtaking school buses at bus stops and promised further announcements in the coming weeks.
- The Department for Infrastructure is reviewing international examples, such as the United States and Canada, where overtaking a stopped school bus is a motoring offence and buses are equipped with flashing stop signs.

## Statistics & Safety Context
- Since 2020, the Police Service of Northern Ireland reports almost 700 young people (aged 4‑18) have been killed or seriously injured on roads during school term time.
- The Royal Hospital for Sick Children in Belfast notes an average of one child per year has been admitted to intensive care after being struck while exiting a school bus over the past 15 years; the true figure is likely higher due to unrecorded injuries.
- Translink, a major school‑bus operator, recorded 13 injuries and one death (Caitlin‑Rose) from 2020‑2025 when passengers were struck after alighting.
- The Education Authority (EA) holds no specific data on the issue.

## Current Safety Measures
- EA buses are fitted with lighting and signage that flash when children board or alight.
- The EA has increased social‑media safety messaging and produced a safe‑travel video for primary‑aged children.

## Outlook
- Stella McGinn stresses that without new legislation families will continue to suffer similar tragedies.
- The push for a UK‑first school‑bus law has intensified since Caitlin‑Rose’s death, with officials acknowledging the need for dedicated legislation and a “dedicated team” to address the problem.
