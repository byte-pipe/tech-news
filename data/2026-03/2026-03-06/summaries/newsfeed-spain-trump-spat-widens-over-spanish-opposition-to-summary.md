# Spain-Trump spat widens over Spanish opposition to Iran war

## Main points
- Spain’s defence minister Margarita Robles reiterated that Spain will not permit U.S. forces to use the joint bases at Rota and Morón for operations linked to the U.S.–Israeli war in Iran.
- White House press secretary Karoline Leavitt had said Spain had changed its position and would cooperate; Robles called the claim inaccurate and affirmed the original stance.
- President Donald Trump threatened to cut off all trade with Spain if the bases are not made available, widening the diplomatic rift.
- Over the weekend the U.S. relocated more than a dozen refuelling aircraft out of the Spanish bases after being denied launch permission.
- Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez defended Spain’s position, stressing respect for international law, calling for a diplomatic exit, and refusing to mention Trump by name.
- Sánchez’s centre‑left coalition faces criticism from the opposition Popular Party, which supports the U.S./Israeli campaign and attacks Sánchez’s stance.
- Spain’s defence ministry dispatched its most advanced warship, Cristóbal Colón, to the Mediterranean to operate with French and Greek forces.
- EU foreign‑policy chief Kaja Kallas warned that further escalation could have unpredictable consequences for Europe and pledged steps to protect EU citizens.

## Background
- The Rota naval base and Morón air base have been used by the United States since 1953 and serve as strategic stop‑over points for Middle‑East operations.
- The dispute arises amid an expanding U.S.–Israeli conflict with Iran.

## International reactions
