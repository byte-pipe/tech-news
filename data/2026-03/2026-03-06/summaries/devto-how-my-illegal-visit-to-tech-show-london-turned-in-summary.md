# How My "Illegal" Visit to Tech Show London Turned Into a Summer Internship Win

## Part 1 – The “Accidental” Infiltration
- Attended Day One of Tech Show London by scanning my badge and entering the ExCeL.
- Took notes during Cloud and DevOps keynotes and identified three “light‑bulb” insights:
  - Cloud‑native architecture is now essential, not just storage.
  - DevOps is a cultural shift; tooling alone won’t fix broken communication.
  - Every vendor is solving a specific developer “headache” (security, speed, bugs, etc.).
- Approached a keynote speaker, received career advice, and earned a CPD certificate for the sessions.

## Part 2 – The “Bathroom” Blunder
- While looking for the restroom, a security guard informed me that students are only allowed from Day Two, 2 PM onward.
- Was escorted out of the venue but left with notes, advice, and the certificate.
- Decided to attend a forgotten DevOps meetup later that day despite fatigue.

## Part 3 – The Pivot to “Data’s Revenge”
- Joined the “DevOps Next Generation: Data’s Revenge” meetup hosted by Fivium, Tarmac, and CODER.
- Heard industry leaders discuss massive cloud‑service spending.
- As the only student, I asked the audience if anyone needed an eager DevOps intern.
- Speakers directed me to several hiring managers present; after the keynote, informal conversations over pizza led to:
  - Two summer internship offers to pair‑program and learn DevOps.
  - A start‑up opportunity to grow with their infrastructure team.
  - Mentorship from a veteran platform engineer with 30 + years of experience.

## Takeaways
- Speaking up and showing up in tech events can create unexpected opportunities.
