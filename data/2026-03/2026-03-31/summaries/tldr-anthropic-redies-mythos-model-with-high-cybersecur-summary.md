# Anthropic redies Mythos model with high cybersecurity risk

# Summary of Anthropic “Mythos” Model Leak  

## Leak details  
- A configuration error exposed ~3,000 unpublished assets in Anthropic’s content management system.  
- Among the assets were draft blog posts about a new model called **Claude Mythos** and an alternate name **Capybara**.  
- Two versions were noted in the drafts: “v1 Mythos” and “v2 Capybara,” indicating the name was still under consideration.  

## Model description  
- Positioned as a new tier above the current Opus models (the top of the Haiku / Sonnet / Opus hierarchy).  
- Described as “larger and more intelligent” than Opus, with significant advances in reasoning, coding, and cybersecurity.  
- Characterized as compute‑intensive and expensive to serve; Anthropic plans to improve efficiency before any general release.  

## Cybersecurity risks  
- The draft warns that Mythos presents “unprecedented cybersecurity risks,” potentially enabling attacks that outpace defenders.  
- Anthropic intends to restrict early access to organizations focused on cybersecurity defense to give them a head start.  
- The concern is heightened by a prior incident where a Chinese state‑sponsored group exploited Anthropic’s Claude Code model.  

## Release strategy and pricing  
- Early access will be limited to security‑focused customers; broader availability is not yet scheduled.  
- Anthropic acknowledges the model will be costly for both the company and its customers, raising questions about its market viability.  
- Comparison is drawn to OpenAI’s GPT‑4.5, which was also expensive and saw limited adoption.  

## Market implications  
- The leak caused a drop in cybersecurity stocks.  
- Developers and enterprise users are evaluating whether the capability jump justifies a premium price or if the model will remain a niche research tool for well‑funded organizations.  
- No public timeline has been provided; Anthropic calls the documents “early drafts.”
