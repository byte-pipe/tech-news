# U.S.-Israeli strikes in Iran continue into 2nd day

## Operation “Epic Fury” overview
- Launched Saturday by the United States and Israel against Iran.
- CENTCOM reported more than 1,000 Iranian targets hit, including a Jamaran‑class corvette that began sinking at a Gulf of Oman pier.

## U.S. military casualties
- Three U.S. service members killed; they were ground‑based forces stationed in Kuwait.
- At least five other U.S. personnel suffered “minor shrapnel injuries and concussions.”

- Israeli officials said the strikes aimed to establish air superiority and clear a path to Tehran.
- State media in Iran announced the death of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei in an air strike on his office; Israel later described the operation as a “precise, large‑scale” attack on a central leadership compound.

## Changes in Iranian leadership

- A senior White House official indicated that the new Iranian leadership expressed a willingness to talk, though no timeline was given.

## Civilian casualties and investigations
- Iran’s foreign ministry reported 158 elementary‑school students killed in Minab, southern Iran, and additional attacks on hospitals in Tehran.
- Iran blamed Israel for the school strike; Israel denied involvement.
- U.S. Central Command said it was aware of civilian‑harm reports and was “looking into them,” emphasizing the importance of protecting civilians.
