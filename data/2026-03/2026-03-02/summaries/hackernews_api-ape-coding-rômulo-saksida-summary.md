# Summary of Ape coding

## Definition
- Ape coding is the practice of manually writing source code by human developers using text editors.
- Emphasizes direct, continuous human involvement and craftsmanship.

## History
- Term gained popularity as AI‑agent coding became dominant.
- Initially used derogatorily for developers who could not use AI agents.
- Critics of AI coding reclaimed the term to highlight manual development.
- Early adoption of AI coding faced reliability, comprehension, and labor‑market concerns, leading to software regressions.
- Ironic positive usage grew, portraying ape coding as deliberate manual work.

## Rationale
- Proponents argued AI‑generated software lacked reliability and comprehensibility.
- Large AI‑produced codebases were hard for humans to understand, test, and assure quality.
- Skepticism about AI’s true understanding and technical limitations (context length, memory, continual learning).
- Concerns about AI replacing human developers and shifting remaining roles to management, QA, or assistance.

## Advocacy for human‑written software
- Advocates called for restrictions on AI coding, subsidies for AI‑free firms, and quotas for human developers.
- Movement gained brief popular support but achieved none of its objectives.

## Decline
- Rapid AI advances rendered ape coding obsolete; arguments lost relevance.
- Political opposition labeled as neo‑Luddism.
- With most software engineering handed to AIs, ape coding faded from mainstream discussion.

## Revival and modern practice
- Hobbyist resurgence created communities where ape coders discuss CS topics and collaborate.
- Large clubs with hundreds of thousands of members host virtual and in‑person gatherings.
- Primary value is recreational: high engagement, relaxation, and competitive coding.
- Educational benefits include deeper understanding of computing concepts; now viewed positively as a sign of curiosity.
