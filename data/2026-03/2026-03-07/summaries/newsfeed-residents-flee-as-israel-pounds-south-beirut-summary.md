# Residents flee as Israel pounds south Beirut

## Background
- Israel launched a “broad‑scale wave” of air strikes on the Dahieh suburb of Beirut, a Hezbollah stronghold, targeting an executive council command centre and a UAV storage facility.  
- The strikes follow near‑daily Israeli attacks on southern Lebanon since the November 2024 ceasefire that ended the previous Israel‑Hezbollah war.

## Israeli evacuation orders
- Israel issued unprecedented evacuation orders covering the southern suburbs of Beirut and large parts of southern Lebanon, affecting hundreds of thousands of residents.  
- The orders do not specify individual targets; entire areas are told to leave.

## Human impact
- Many families could not find shelter and spent the night on streets or in cars.  
- Approximately 96,000 people are officially registered in displacement centres across the country.  
- Residents report fear, sleeplessness, and uncertainty about when they can return home.

## Personal testimonies
- **Monira Hassan** (central Beirut park): “I got used to the sounds of bombing… I didn’t want to leave… I just wanted to stay at home.”  
- **Mohamed Baydoun** (73, staying at the National Theatre balcony after fleeing Tyre): “They’re not giving a specific target… there is no mercy.”  
- **Dunia Hassaballaa** (14, from Dahieh): “We didn’t sleep last night. We couldn’t sleep because of fear.”  
- A mother with a two‑month‑old baby worried about lacking milk and keeping the child warm.

## Humanitarian response
- Shelters are reaching capacity; volunteers warn of dwindling food supplies and donations.  
- The Barzakh bookshop café prepared carrot soup for displaced families during Ramadan, but staff fear they cannot sustain assistance.  
- Lebanese Prime Minister Nawaf Salam warned of an imminent humanitarian disaster due to mass displacement.

## International reactions
- French President Emmanuel Macron called the situation a “moment of great danger” and urged Hezbollah to cease fire and Israel to avoid ground operations in Lebanon.  
- Concerns expressed about Lebanon being drawn into a broader conflict.

## Outlook
- Displaced residents continue to wait on the streets of Beirut, uncertain whether bombing will intensify.  
- The consensus among locals, including the 14‑year‑old, is that it is “too early for Lebanon to have another war.”
