## Overview  
- Laptop docking stations turn a portable laptop into a full‑desktop workstation by providing a single connection for monitors, keyboard, mouse, and other peripherals.  
- Modern laptops are thin and often lack the variety of ports needed for a multi‑monitor, high‑speed setup.  
- The article tests a range of docks and recommends the best option for several use‑cases, from high‑end Thunderbolt 5 to budget‑friendly USB‑C solutions.

## Buying Guide Highlights  
- **Determine your primary connection needs:** If you need HDMI, choose a dock that includes it (e.g., Plugable). If you prefer Thunderbolt 5 for future‑proofing, be prepared to use adapters for HDMI‑only monitors.  
- **Port speed matters:** 10 Gbps USB‑A and 40 Gbps Thunderbolt 5 are ideal for fast data transfer and multiple 4K displays.  
- **Power delivery:** High‑wattage power bricks (e.g., 330 W for the TS5 Plus) can charge power‑hungry laptops but add bulk.  
- **Ethernet speed:** 10 GbE is overkill for most home broadband but useful in office environments; 2.5 GbE is a solid middle ground.  
- **Form factor vs. durability:** Aluminum cases feel premium and last longer, while plastic docks are lighter and cheaper but may not survive heavy daily handling.
