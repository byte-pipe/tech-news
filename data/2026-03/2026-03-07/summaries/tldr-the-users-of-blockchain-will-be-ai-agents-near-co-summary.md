# AI agents will be primary users of blockchain, NEAR co‑founder says

## Main insights
- Illia Polosukhin, co‑founder of NEAR, predicts that AI agents—not humans—will become the primary users of blockchain, acting as the front‑end interface while the blockchain serves as the back‑end infrastructure.
- He argues that current crypto tools (wallets, explorers, transaction hashes) should be hidden behind AI, turning blockchain into an invisible settlement layer similar to an operating system.
- In this model, AI agents will directly interact with protocols to execute payments, manage assets, coordinate services, and vote in governance systems; humans will only interact with the AI.
- Polosukhin critiques the industry’s focus on speculative AI‑related tokens and memecoins, saying they damage reputation and distract from solving real problems.
- He stresses that blockchain’s value lies in providing neutral, programmable financial rails—settlement, ownership, verifiability, and incentives—for AI platforms.
- Governance tools like DAOs have failed because they lack clear economic or coordination goals; AI‑assisted voting only makes sense when tied to specific problems.
- The convergence of AI and crypto is expected to be infrastructure‑focused, with AI systems needing trusted execution, privacy, and programmable financial coordination that blockchain can supply.
- If AI becomes the internet’s operating system, crypto’s role will shift from being a visible application layer to an invisible, trusted settlement layer supporting AI agents.
