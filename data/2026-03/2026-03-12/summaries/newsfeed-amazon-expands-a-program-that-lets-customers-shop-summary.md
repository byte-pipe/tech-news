# Amazon expands Shop Direct program

## Overview
- Amazon is widening its Shop Direct program for U.S. shoppers, letting them discover and buy products that aren’t sold on Amazon’s own marketplace.
- The expansion adds support for third‑party product feeds, giving Amazon real‑time access to merchants’ inventory, pricing, and catalog data.

## New Feed Partnerships
- Integrated feed providers: Feedonomics, Salsify, and CedCommerce.
- Additional feed providers will be added later, and a merchant portal with a direct‑feed option is slated to launch soon.

## How Shop Direct Works
- When Amazon’s search results don’t include a requested product, the AI assistant Rufus or search results can direct shoppers to the retailer’s website.
- Product information is displayed on Amazon, with a clear notice that the user is leaving Amazon’s site.
- The “Buy for Me” AI agent can complete purchases on third‑party sites; customers only need to confirm order details (address, taxes, shipping, payment).

## Customer Experience
- Orders placed via Shop Direct appear in the regular “Your Orders” tab or a dedicated “Buy for Me Orders” tab.
- The service is live on Amazon.com, the Amazon mobile app, and through the Rufus AI assistant.

## Business Implications
- Brands gain exposure and potential sales, while Amazon collects data on competing products, pricing, and trends.
- The move strengthens Amazon’s position as the primary starting point for product searches.

## Timeline
- Beta testing began in February 2025; the expanded program is now live for U.S. customers.
