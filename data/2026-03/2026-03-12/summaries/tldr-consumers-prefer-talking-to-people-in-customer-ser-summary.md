# Consumers prefer talking to people in customer service

## Dive Brief
- 46% of consumers say AI‑powered customer service “rarely” or “never” leads to successful outcomes (YouGov survey for Pegasystems).
- Nearly two‑thirds of consumers are “not very confident” or “not at all confident” in how companies use generative AI for customer interactions (survey of >4,700 North American and U.K. adults); just over half doubt businesses can use the technology responsibly.
- Over 75% believe dealing with a human “always” or “often” produces better service outcomes; only 2% want exclusive interaction with generative AI chatbots.

## Dive Insight
- Business leaders tout AI benefits in customer service, but consumer sentiment remains skeptical.
- Examples of AI rollout:
  - Airbnb’s AI agent (launched ahead of schedule) praised by CEO Brian Chesky for “incredible” service capability.
  - Williams‑Sonoma introduced AI agents across its brands to boost efficiency, cut costs, and provide 24/7 support.
- Consumer attitudes:
  - Two‑thirds prefer human‑led support; 48% do not trust companies to let AI fully handle queries.
  - Consumers are more receptive to AI in a supporting role rather than as the sole point of contact.
- Expert view (Julie Geller, Info‑Tech Research Group):
  - AI helps live agents follow a measured plan, delivering consistent “next‑best actions” without losing the human touch.
  - Removing agent‑to‑agent variability creates a reliable, polished journey reflecting brand intent at every touchpoint.
- People‑led implementation example:
  - Lowe’s equipped associates with the “Mylow” AI assistant, leading to “dramatic improvements in customer service” (CEO Marvin Ellison, earnings call).
- Consumer self‑perception of AI use:
  - 48% do not actively choose generative AI for everyday tasks; 25% suspect they use it daily without noticing.
  - Consumers feel more comfortable using AI for research than for purchasing, citing concerns about payment security, errors, and loss of control (Riskified research).
