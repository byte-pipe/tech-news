# Alternative to HRT for menopausal hot flushes now on NHS

## Key points
- Veoza (fezolinetant) is a non‑hormonal daily pill now recommended by NICE for treating moderate to severe hot flushes and night sweats in England when hormone replacement therapy (HRT) is unsuitable.
- The drug works by blocking nerve pathways in the brain that trigger these symptoms.
- Up to 500,000 women could benefit, particularly those with conditions that make HRT risky, such as deep‑vein thrombosis, pulmonary embolism, certain cases of diabetes or heart disease.
- Veoza is **not** recommended for women with breast cancer, other estrogen‑dependent cancers, or liver disease; past breast‑cancer patients may be considered on an individual basis.
- NICE judged the medication effective and cost‑effective, but HRT remains the first‑line treatment because of its broader benefits.
- The recommendation applies to NHS England; Wales and Northern Ireland usually follow NICE guidance, while Scotland’s health authority has not adopted it.
- Experts quoted: Helen Knight (NICE) highlighted “much‑needed relief” for patients who cannot use HRT; Dr Sue Mann (NHS England) called the news “very welcome” for women unable or unwilling to take HRT.

## Context and impact
- Menopausal estrogen decline affects the brain, skin, muscles, emotions, and can cause sleep disturbance, reduced concentration, strained relationships, and work difficulties.
- Prior to Veoza, non‑hormonal options for managing hot flushes and night sweats were limited.

## Implementation details
- Veoza was approved in 2023; NICE’s recommendation for NHS prescribing was issued in 2024.
- It is prescribed as a daily oral tablet for women experiencing moderate to severe menopausal vasomotor symptoms.
