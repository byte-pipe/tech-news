description: 'After 8 years managing email for 500+ DTC brands, here''s every retention flow tip I could come up with: 1. If you don''t have a pop-up offer on your site, you''re leaving the cheapest leads on the table. 2. Aim for a 4-9% pop-up submission rate. If you''re under that, your offer or timing is off. 3. 70% of visitors won''t scroll past your hero section. If your best offer isn''t there, it doesn''t exist. 4. Your abandoned checkout flow should convert at 7-10%. If it''s not, there''s room to tighten up. 5. Max ONE SMS in your abandoned checkout flow. They''re still cold. Don''t overwhelm them. 6. Browse abandonment is not the same as cart abandonment. The psychology is completely different. Treat it that way. 7. Your browse abandonment email has one job: show them the product they viewed is the solution they''re looking for. 8. Abandoned cart visitors are blocked by something. Shipping cost, reviews, comparisons. Answer those objections before they have to ask. 9. Site abandonment
  visitors aren''t ready to buy yet. Don''t sell. Just guide them back and let your brand do the work. 10. Failure-to-Launch Flow targets people who signed up in the last 30 days and never engaged. Take one strong swing before they go cold. 11. Your highest open and click rates will always be right after someone buys. Use that window. 12. Most second purchases happen within 30 days of the first. If you''re not pushing for that, you''re leaving easy revenue behind. 13. Ask new customers "why did you buy?" Offer a small incentive for the reply. It boosts deliverability and tells you exactly what''s working. 14. A win-back flow is part reminder, part nudge, part proof. Lead with value, not just a discount. 15. Your sunset flow isn''t a failure. End the relationship well. Ask for a referral or review. Leave the door open. 16. A second purchase is a satisfaction signal. That''s your best moment to ask for a review and actually get one. 17. VIPs should feel like VIPs. First access to drops, sales,
  and launches. If they don''t feel special, they won''t stay loyal. 18. Cross-sell recommendations only work when they''re relevant. If it doesn''t feel helpful, it feels pushy. 19. Replenishment flows exist for one reason: to catch one-time buyers before their product runs out and they forget about you. 20. Subscriptions aren''t just good for retention. They''re good for customers too. Lower cost, more convenience. Lead with that. 21. Running all of these at once will usually add 20-30% more revenue from the same list. 22. The list you already have is worth more than you think. You just have to work it properly. Save this and implement it all. | 11 comments on LinkedIn'
