# After pivoting, Y Combinator grad Glimpse raises $35M led by a16z | TechCrunch

## Funding round
- Raised $35 million Series A led by Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) with participation from 8VC and Y Combinator.  
- Previously raised $10 million in 2023 (originally called Series A, now re‑classified as a seed round).  
- Total capital to date: $52 million, including pre‑pivot funding.  

## Company background & pivot
- Founded by Purdue alumni Akash Raju, Anuj Mehta, and Kushal Negi.  
- Initial venture (2020) focused on Airbnb product placements; lacked product‑market fit.  
- In 2024 the team pivoted to “Glimpse,” a fintech platform that automates retailer‑brand deduction and dispute processes.  

## Problem being solved
- Retailers deduct amounts from brand invoices for reasons such as damaged goods or short shipments.  
- Many deductions are invalid, leading to tedious manual tracking, fragmented data, siloed workflows, and revenue leakage for brands.  

## Solution & technology
- AI‑driven agents log into retailer portals, gather and centralize documents, classify each deduction, and validate against internal data (supply‑chain records, promotion calendars).  
- Invalid deductions are automatically disputed; outcomes are synced back to the brand’s ERP and related systems.  
- Human oversight remains for dispute follow‑up, quality assurance, and critical classification steps.  
- System improves over time, creating a compounding data advantage across customers.  

## Customers & competition
- Serves over 200 retail brands, including Suave and Chapstick.  
- Competing solutions include Revya and Confido.  

## Vision & use of new capital
- Aims to become the AI infrastructure layer for CPG and retail brands.  
- Funding will accelerate product development, expand integrations, and scale the platform to more customers.
