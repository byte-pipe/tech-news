# ‘There’s no safety anymore’: Palestinians warn of expanding West Bank settler violence  

## Overview  
- A surge of extremist Israeli‑settler attacks has hit Palestinian villages across the occupied West Bank.  
- The violence follows the funeral of Israeli teen Yehuda Sherman, whose death sparked anger among settlers.  

## Attack on Deir al‑Hatab (near Nablus)  
- Settlers stormed the village, shot at homes, set a bank manager’s villa on fire and injured at least ten people, one of them shot in the foot.  
- Residents escaped by hiding on rooftops; Barhan Omar describes the raid as “organized terrorism.”  
- The attack is the first large‑scale assault on Deir al‑Hatab, a village that had not previously faced such violence.  

## Wider pattern of settler aggression  
- Since the start of the Iran‑Israel war, six Palestinians have been killed by settlers, according to the UN.  
- Earlier incidents include alleged sexual assault and beatings in Khirbet Humsa, with seven arrests made by Israeli police.  
- Settler violence accelerated after the October 2023 Gaza war, coinciding with rapid settlement expansion and record‑high planning approvals.  

## Legal and territorial context  
- All Israeli settlements are deemed illegal under international law; many outposts lack official Israeli approval.  
- The focus has shifted from Area C (full Israeli control) to Area B villages, where Israel retains security authority while the Palestinian Authority handles civil matters.  
- Between Jan 2023 and Feb 2026, UN OCHA reports 4,765 Palestinians from 97 locations displaced, mainly Bedouin communities in Area C.  

## Government policies and statements  
- Far‑right finance minister Bezalel Smotrich, a settler, claims to have approved or retroactively authorized 69 new settlements and vows to “bury the idea of a Palestinian state.”  
- At Sherman’s funeral, Smotrich called for the dismantling of the Palestinian Authority and pledged to settle “all our land.”  
- Israel’s chief of staff Lt Gen Eyal Zamir condemned settler violence as “morally and ethically unacceptable,” yet the IDF faces criticism for standing by or failing to prosecute perpetrators.  

## International reaction  
- The EU and UK have demanded Israel halt the surge in West Bank settler violence.  
- UN human‑rights officials warned that forced transfers aimed at permanent displacement raise concerns of ethnic cleansing.  

## Local Palestinian response  
- Residents of Deir al‑Hatab fear further attacks; Samer Omar speaks of encirclement and loss of safety.  
- Barhan Omar warns Prime Minister Netanyahu that Palestinians will not remain passive and may rise against the pressure.  

## Implications  
- The coordinated settler attacks, backed by political support and expansionist policies, aim to empty Palestinian areas to enable annexation.  
- Continued displacement and intimidation threaten any prospect of security for West Bank Palestinians and heighten the risk of broader conflict.
