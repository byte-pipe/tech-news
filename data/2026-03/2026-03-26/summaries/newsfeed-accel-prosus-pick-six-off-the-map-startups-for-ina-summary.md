# Accel, Prosus pick six “off‑the‑map” startups for inaugural India cohort

## Overview
- Accel and Prosus have launched their first joint Indian cohort, targeting startups with “off‑the‑map” ideas where markets are undefined and progress is hard to measure.  
- The cohort covers health‑care, climate, space and longevity, emphasizing science‑led themes with long development timelines and uncertain commercial paths.  
- Selections were made from over 2,000 applications.

## Selected startups
- **Praanis** (Mumbai) – Air‑infrastructure system that improves indoor air quality through purification, sensing and automated controls. Prior investors include Social Impact Capital, Aera VC and Avaana Capital.  
- **QOSMIC** (Bengaluru) – Optical communication technology for higher‑bandwidth, lower‑latency data transfer between satellites and Earth.  
- **Ethereal Exploration Guild / EtherealX** (Bengaluru) – Reusable orbital launch vehicles aimed at reducing space‑access costs; recently raised a $20.5 M Series A at an $80.5 M valuation.  
- **Dognosis** – Cancer‑detection platform that combines dogs’ sense of smell with robotics and AI; users breathe into a mask, and samples are analyzed for cancer markers (product name: BreatheEasy).  
- **Ferrais** – Home‑based strength‑training system that automatically adjusts resistance to help older adults maintain mobility.  
- **Stealth startup** – Developing brain‑computer interfaces for direct communication between the human brain and external systems.

## Investment model
- Accel and Prosus co‑invest in each startup, with Prosus matching Accel’s contribution.  
- Check sizes range from $500 k to $2 M.  
- A portion of the capital is deferred to reduce early dilution for founders; equity is granted at a later stage.  
- The structure is designed for companies with long R&D cycles that need time more than immediate cash.

## Partner perspectives
- **Pratik Agarwal**, Accel partner: Emphasized that “more than capital, they require time to make those breakthroughs.”  
- **Ashutosh Sharma**, head of India ecosystem at Prosus: Noted that progress for these firms is non‑linear, driven by key technical breakthroughs rather than steady growth.
