description: 'Here are 10 ways to ask about budget without making it weird. ("Asking a hard thing in a soft way": budget edition.) 1.) “Before we go too far… is there a number already attached to this initiative?” (Simple. Neutral. No pressure.) 2.) “When you say ‘approved’… approved for what range?” (Forces clarity without sounding like you’re pushing.) 3.) “Do you have budget set aside, or do we need to build a business case to unlock it?” (This respects reality. And it tells you what motion you’re in.) 4.) “What have you typically invested in solving problems like this?” (Asks for precedent, not their wallet.) 5.) “If we land on the right solution, is budget the constraint… or is it prioritization?” (This is the nicest way to ask: do you want it?) 6.) “What would make this an ‘easy yes’ financially… and what would make it a ‘hard no’?” (Gives you boundaries fast.) 7.) “Would it be fair to bracket this?” Then give 2 ranges: “Are we talking closer to $25–50k… or $150–300k?” (They can correct
  you without feeling pinned.) 8.) “Who else has to sign off on spend like this?” (Not budget, but it’s budget. And it surfaces procurement early.) 9.) “What’s competing for the same dollars right now?” (Shows you’re thinking like them, not like a seller.) 10.) “If we solve this, where does the money come from?” Budget bucket, headcount, tool consolidation, churn reduction… you learn what they believe. If you want the even softer opener, steal this line: “Ok if I ask an uncomfortable question so we don’t waste each other’s time…” What’s your go-to way to ask budget without spooking the room? | 41 comments on LinkedIn'
