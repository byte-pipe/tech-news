# Federal cyber experts called Microsoft's cloud a "pile of shit," approved it anyway - Ars Technica

# Federal Cyber Experts Weigh In on Microsoft's Government Cloud High
### Summary

In 2024, federal cybersecurity evaluators at the US government's technology agency gave Microsoft's Government Community Cloud High (GCC) a "lack of confidence in assessing overall security posture" because they found inadequate detailed security documentation.

This verdict was surprising given that Microsoft had been trying to explain how it protects sensitive information in the cloud. However, reviewers agreed that Microsoft provided a "pile of shit," according to one team member's assessment.

As a result, federal policymakers and regulatory agencies considered granting FedRAMP approval for GCC High despite these findings. This decision has significant implications for government use of commercial cloud services, given the program is intended to ensure cybersecurity.

Despite receiving criticism from experts, Microsoft expanded its business empire worth billions through this unusual step.
