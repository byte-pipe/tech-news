# AI Drives Global Fraud Surge

A report from Nasdaq's Verafin software unit, Verafin, has found that losses to fraud and scams increased 9.2% in 2025 compared to two years ago, attributed largely to bad actors leveraging artificial intelligence (AI). The surge is expected to continue with much of the increase driven by criminals using AI programs to identify patterns in common scams.

Key Findings:

* Verafin recorded $579.4 billion lost to bank fraud and scams in 2025, a $53.3 billion increase from 2023.
* $62 billion of these losses ($62.4 billion) were attributed to losses related to scams, resulting in a 19.3% increase compared to 2023.
* Cyber-assisted or AI-enabled scams were responsible for $14.3 billion in losses (19.6%) and are believed to be driving the surge.
* The report's survey of cybersecurity professionals found that 90% saw an increase in AI-driven attacks over the past two years.

Technological Advancements:

* "We see AI in everything the day-to-day fraudsters are doing," said Colin Parsons, head of fraud product strategy at Verafin. "It's hard to put a number on it."
* AI programs can identify patterns and raise red flags, such as spelling mistakes or scripts used by scammers.
* These tools prevent payments from going through to victims.

Impact on Businesses:

* Unfortunately, security professionals are also leveraging some of the same tools that bad actors use, including AI, to increase efficiency and scale. 
* This overlap can worsen check fraud and other types of checks as well.

Overall, Verafin's findings suggest that AI is becoming increasingly difficult for businesses to distinguish from legitimate traffic and transactions, making it harder to prevent and detect fraud.
