**Overview**
The American Healthcare Conundrum project aims to identify fixable problems in the US healthcare system, quantify waste from primary federal data, and recommend policy fixes to reduce costs.

**Key Findings**

* The US spends ~$14,570 per person on healthcare, while Japan's average expenditure is ~$5,790.
* This gap of $3 trillion per year is roughly due to a 254% price disparity in commercial insurer payments compared to Medicare rates for identical hospital procedures.
* Specific issues addressed include:
	+ OTC drug overspending by CMS Part D 2023: estimated $0.6B in annual savings
	+ The Same Pill, A Different Price: estimated $25.0B in annual savings
	+ The 254% Problem: commercial insurers pay 4x Medicare rates for identical hospital procedures

**Savings and Recommendations**
Approximately $73 billion annually is saved when capping commercial hospital payments at 200% of Medicare rates.
