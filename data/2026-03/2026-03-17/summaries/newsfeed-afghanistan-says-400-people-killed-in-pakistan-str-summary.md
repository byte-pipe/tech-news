# Afghanistan Says 400 Killed in Pakistan Strike on Kabul Hospital

Afghanistan has accused Pakistan of targeting a hospital for drug users in the capital city with an airstrike, marking a dramatic escalation of a conflict that began late last month. Pakistan has dismissed the accusation.

**Causes and Effects of the Conflict**

*   The strike damaged a 2,000-bed facility at the hospital, which has reportedly taken over 400 lives.
*   International calls for a ceasefire have gone unheeded, marking a third week in a cycle of deadliest fighting between the countries.
*   Cross-border clashes have increased as Pakistan's defense forces operate alongside local security teams.

**Key Players and Their Positions**

*   Deputy Afghan government spokesman Hamdullah Fitrat: accuses Pakistan of targeting civilians at the hospital with an airstrike
*   Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif's spokesman: dismisses allegations, stating no hospital was targeted
*   Afghanistan government official Zabihullah Mujahid condemns the strike and considers it a crime against humanity

**International Response to the Conflict**

The international community has called for a ceasefire, but so far, no significant action has been taken. The United States, along with other countries, is maintaining firm stance on holding Pakistan leaders liable for such violent crimes.
