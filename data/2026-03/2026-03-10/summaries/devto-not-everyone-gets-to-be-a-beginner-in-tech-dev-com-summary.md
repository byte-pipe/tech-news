# Not Everyone Gets to Be a Beginner in Tech

## Main Argument
- The tech industry claims meritocracy, but beginners are not treated equally.
- Some people can learn publicly, ask awkward questions, and make mistakes without judgment; others are immediately judged and deemed “not belonging.”

## Effects of Unequal Treatment
- Creates pressure that makes individuals overthink, rewrite communications, and hide imperfections.
- Leads to self‑censorship, reduced participation, and higher dropout rates among those who feel their mistakes are costly.
- The fear of being “unfinished” pushes many to conceal their learning process until they feel “safe.”

## Beyond Access
- Access to courses, tools, and tutorials is only the first step.
- What matters is feeling *allowed* to stay and grow after entering the field.
- Small acts of patience, generosity, and mentorship can make a huge difference.

## Call to Action for Gender Equity and Inclusion
- Opening doors is insufficient; we must change the environment after someone walks through.
- Provide patient mentorship, safe spaces, and acceptance of unfinished work.
- Recognize that many talented people leave not because of lack of skill, but because the cost of being a beginner is too high.
