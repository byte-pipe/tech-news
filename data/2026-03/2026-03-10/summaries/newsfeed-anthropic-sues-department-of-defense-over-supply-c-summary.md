# Anthropic Sues Department of Defense Over Supply‑Chain‑Risk Designation

## Lawsuit Overview
- Anthropic filed a federal lawsuit in California challenging the Pentagon’s “supply‑chain risk” designation.
- The company seeks a reversal of the designation and a temporary restraining order to continue government sales.
- Anthropic argues the designation violates the Constitution by punishing protected speech and constitutes unlawful retaliation.

## Immediate Stakes
- Potential loss of hundreds of millions of dollars in annual revenue from the Pentagon and other U.S. agencies.
- Customers using Anthropic’s Claude model in federal contracts may seek alternative providers.
- Anthropic claims most customers will be unaffected, as the designation applies only to direct military contracts.

## Government and White House Response
- The Department of Defense declined comment on the lawsuit.
- White House spokesperson Liz Huston emphasized that the military will follow the Constitution, not “woke AI company’s terms of service,” and will ensure warfighters have needed tools.

## Legal Context
- Existing rules give the DoD broad authority to label technology as a supply‑chain risk, limiting avenues for appeal.
- Legal experts note the government’s prerogative to set contract parameters and protect mission effectiveness.
- Anthropic’s best argument may be that it was singled out, especially after OpenAI secured a Pentagon contract with safeguards.

## Industry and Advocacy Reactions
- Tech coalitions (TechNet, BSA, SIIA) urged the administration to reconsider, warning of chilling effects on U.S. innovation.
- A separate group of technologists and former national‑security officials sent a letter to Senate Armed Services Committee members, calling the action a “profound departure” from its original purpose.
- Signatories included former CIA director Michael Hayden, Harvard professor Lawrence Lessig, and several retired military admirals.

## Military Use of Claude
- U.S. military personnel access Claude mainly through third‑party tools such as Palantir’s Maven Smart System for tasks ranging from document drafting to high‑stakes analysis.
- If the designation remains, contractors would need to replace Claude, likely at higher cost.
- Other AI startups (e.g., Vannevar Labs) are pitching their models as replacements.

## Broader Government Impact
- Agencies outside the DoD have indicated they will stop using Claude to comply with a directive from President Donald Trump.
- Microsoft said it will continue offering Claude to U.S. agencies and businesses, except the DoD.
- OpenAI has not specified a timeline for replacing Claude in military applications; the DoD estimates a phase‑out could take up to six months.

## Current Status and Outlook
- Anthropic reports “productive conversations” with the Pentagon to resolve the dispute.
- The company pledges to support the department “for as long as we are permitted to do so.”
- A hearing on the temporary restraining order request is slated for Friday, with a response deadline set for 9 pm Pacific on Wednesday.
