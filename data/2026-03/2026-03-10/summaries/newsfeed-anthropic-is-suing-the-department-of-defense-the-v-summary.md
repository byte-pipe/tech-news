# Anthropic is suing the Department of Defense

## Background
- The Trump administration designated Anthropic’s AI as a “supply‑chain risk,” a label usually applied to foreign firms posing cybersecurity or national‑security threats.  
- President Donald Trump ordered all U.S. government agencies to stop using Anthropic’s technology within six months.  
- The designation sparked bipartisan controversy over whether a company could be penalized for refusing certain military uses of its AI.

## Lawsuit details
- Anthropic filed a suit in a California district court accusing the administration of violating the First and Fifth Amendments.  
- The complaint claims the government retaliated against Anthropic for maintaining “red lines” against mass domestic surveillance and fully autonomous weapons.  
- Anthropic alleges the executive branch exceeded its authority by demanding all agencies drop the company’s services.

## Government actions
- The Pentagon formally labeled Anthropic a supply‑chain risk but declined to comment on the lawsuit.  
- The General Services Administration terminated Anthropic’s OneGov contract, ending access for all three federal branches.  
- Other agencies, including the Treasury and State Departments, announced or reportedly plan to cease using Anthropic’s services.

## Company response and industry impact
- Anthropic announced it would challenge the supply‑chain risk designation in court.  
- Major customers such as Microsoft continue to work with Anthropic, but are establishing safeguards to separate their Pentagon‑related work from Anthropic collaborations.  
- Despite the blacklist, Anthropic’s usage and revenue continue to grow.

## Current status
- The lawsuit is pending; no court ruling has been issued.  
- The Pentagon’s official stance remains unchanged, and the broader debate over AI safety, surveillance, and autonomous weapons continues within the U.S. government and the tech industry.
