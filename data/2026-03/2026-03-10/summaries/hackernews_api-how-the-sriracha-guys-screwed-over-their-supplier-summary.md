# Summary of “How the Sriracha guys screwed over their supplier” (Kitchen Confidential)

## Background
- Huy Fong, founded by David Tran in the 1980s, sourced its peppers almost exclusively from Underwood Ranches starting in 1988.
- The two companies relied on informal “handshake” agreements; Huy Fong paid Underwood by the acres of pepper plants cultivated rather than by actual harvest.
- Underwood specialized in peppers, leased large tracts of land, and invested heavily in machinery and techniques to maximize yields, with Huy Fong assuring they would buy the entire crop.

## Escalation
- By 2015 the partnership had lasted over 25 years; Huy Fong created a spin‑off, Chilico, to source peppers.
- Tran attempted to poach Underwood’s COO; the offer was rejected.
- In 2016 Huy Fong requested drone footage of Underwood’s harvest (allowed for personal use only) and simultaneously signed a contract with Chilico to buy all its peppers.
- On 9 Nov 2016 Huy Fong demanded Underwood sell chilies to Chilico at $500/ton, far below Underwood’s cost of $610/ton, threatening to withhold an $18 million pre‑payment.

## Breakup
- Underwood, burdened by long‑term leases and high fixed costs, could not meet the new terms and faced imminent financial collapse.
- In Jan 2017 Underwood warned Huy Fong they could not supply peppers; Huy Fong turned to other farmers, using the confidential drone video to replicate Underwood’s harvest methods.
- Underwood incurred massive losses: ~ $8.5 million in 2017 and $6 million in 2018, fired 40 employees, and was unable to exit many leases.

## Legal Outcome
- Huy Fong sued Underwood for repayment of earlier payments; Underwood counter‑claimed for breach of contract and fraud.
- A unanimous jury found in favor of Underwood, awarding:
  - $13 million in compensatory damages
  - $10 million in punitive damages (for conduct deemed “quasi‑criminal”).

## Aftermath
- Underwood now produces its own Sriracha sauce using its peppers.
- Huy Fong continues sourcing cheaper, lower‑quality pepper mash, which many claim has degraded the taste of its original sauce.
