# Ben Horowitz on What Makes the Best Fundraising Pitch

## 1. Your Story Is Your Strategy. Write It Down.
- The company’s story and its strategy are the same thing, not separate.
- Founders should continuously rewrite the story as they learn; otherwise the strategy drifts unnoticed.
- Writing the story in long form forces discipline and clarifies the “why” (purpose, recruitment, investment).
- Knowing the why eliminates the need to explain the what.

## 2. In the AI Era, Hire for Creativity and Relationships
- Creativity and relationship‑building are undervalued traits that AI cannot replicate.
- AI excels at grind‑the‑out tasks; it cannot replace original ideas or high‑fidelity networking.
- Example: a16z’s marketing shift succeeded only after hiring people who were genuinely creative about new media.

## 3. “Right Product, Right Time” Is Still the Only Job
- The core product manager role remains delivering the right product at the right time.
- Landscape changes (e.g., AI) do not alter this fundamental responsibility.
- If a new tool or process does not achieve that outcome, it is irrelevant; automation can handle the ancillary tasks.

## 4. Build Where There Are Shortages
- Opportunities exist in solving supply‑side bottlenecks (e.g., electricity, chips, tokens, cooling).
- Investing in companies that alleviate these shortages can be highly valuable (e.g., Heron Power’s power transformers).
- Long‑standing human problems (e.g., cancer) become tractable when new technologies reduce other constraints.

## 5. The Best Fundraising Pitch Convince You First
- Founders should first persuade themselves of the greatness of their idea before trying to guess what investors want.
- A pitch rooted in genuine belief is resistant to being knocked down by skeptical questions.
- Trying to “pretend” to be someone else leads to a fragile relationship, both in fundraising and in broader partnerships.
