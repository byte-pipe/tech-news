# Apple cuts China App Store commission fees after government pressure

## Key changes
- Commission for in‑app purchases and paid transactions reduced from 30% to 25% in mainland China, effective 15 March 2026.
- For developers in Apple’s small‑business and mini‑app partner programmes, the rate falls from 15% to 12%.

## Expected impact
- Chinese developers could save more than 6 billion yuan (≈ $873 million) per year.
- Consumers may see price drops in subscriptions, game credits, live‑broadcast tips and other digital goods, saving up to nearly 1 billion yuan annually.
- Major “super‑app” platforms such as Tencent’s WeChat and ByteDance’s TikTok stand to benefit from lower costs for third‑party mini‑apps.

## Context and regulatory pressure
- The fee cut follows discussions with Chinese regulators and follows earlier antitrust scrutiny worldwide.
- The EU forced Apple to lower its rates to 10‑17% in 2024; the US now permits alternative payment links after the Epic Games case.
- China’s antitrust watchdog investigated Apple’s App Store pricing last year but had not filed formal charges before this change.

## Future considerations
- Chinese authorities may later require Apple to route App Store revenue through domestic channels and tighten oversight of foreign apps.
- Apple has previously removed VPN apps from its China store at regulator request.

## International relevance
- The reduction also applies to foreign developers with apps on the China App Store, e.g., Duolingo, which could save a significant portion of its roughly $50 million annual revenue from the market.
