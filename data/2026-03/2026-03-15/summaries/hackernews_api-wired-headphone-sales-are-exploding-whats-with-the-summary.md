# Wired headphone sales are exploding. What's with the Bluetooth backlash?

## Overview
- Wired headphones, once thought obsolete after the removal of headphone jacks, are experiencing a strong comeback.
- Sales surged in the second half of 2025 and revenue rose 20 % in the first six weeks of 2026 (Circana data).

## Reasons for the resurgence
- **Sound quality and value**
  - Wired models generally deliver better audio for the price than mainstream Bluetooth options.
  - Audiophile‑grade Bluetooth headphones exist, but they are niche and can suffer from connection or compatibility problems.
- **Cultural and anti‑tech sentiment**
  - Users describe wired listening as “comforting” and a statement that they are truly engaged with what they hear.
  - The trend is linked to a broader backlash against pervasive, AI‑driven technology and a desire for analog‑feeling experiences.
- **Fashion and status symbol**
  - Instagram accounts like “Wired It Girls” showcase wired earbuds as chic accessories.
  - Social media posts label wired headphones as a “class thing,” contrasting with the perception that constant wireless use signals a lack of “land ownership.”
- **Practical frustrations with Bluetooth**
  - Connectivity glitches, battery depletion, and the extra step required to pair devices are cited as annoyances.
  - Users report losing Bluetooth earbuds less often than wired ones, but the bulkier cases and charging needs are still seen as drawbacks.

## Market observations
- Apple continues to sell wired headphones despite removing the 3.5 mm jack in 2016; store staff note increased sales of wired earbuds.
- Specialty retailers (e.g., Audio 46 in New York) report a growing number of customers specifically requesting wired models.
- Adaptors (“dongles”) and headphones with built‑in USB or Lightning cables mitigate the lack of a traditional jack, making wired use more convenient.

## Broader retro trend
- Wired headphones join other once‑obsolete technologies—DVDs, cassettes, tube TVs, typewriters—returning to popularity as consumers seek tangible, low‑tech experiences.

## Personal anecdotes
- The author’s own switch from Bluetooth to wired highlighted both the comfort of a physical connection and the ease of losing lightweight wired earbuds.
- Industry voices (e.g., Chris Thomas of SoundGuys, Aryn Grusin, Delaney Czernikowski) echo the mix of audio, cultural, and practical motivations behind the trend.
