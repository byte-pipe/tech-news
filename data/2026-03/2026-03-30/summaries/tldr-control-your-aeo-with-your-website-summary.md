# Control your AEO with your website

# Summary of “Control your AEO with your website”

## Main Argument
- The panic over falling organic traffic is misleading; websites are more critical than ever in the AI‑driven Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) era.
- AI answer engines still rely on URLs from real websites; without a solid site, brands are invisible to LLMs.

## How AI Changes Traffic and Conversion
- AI results reshape search traffic, but the underlying source remains the website.
- LLMs cite URLs; if a brand’s content is scraped and cited, it drives brand awareness and direct traffic (often “Dark SEO” that bypasses traditional referral analytics).
- Declining traditional traffic often reflects a shift in distribution, not a broken site.

## Risks of Weak or Missing Websites
- Poorly designed sites can nullify AI citations, turning a potential recommendation into a lost conversion.
- Vague, adjective‑heavy pages are ignored by models; specific, plain‑language content that explains product purpose, audience, and differentiation gets cited.
- For local service businesses, a bad website can create a negative first impression, whereas AI now looks for detailed owned content rather than just directory listings.

## Recommendations for Brands
- Treat the website as an asset to be maintained and optimized for LLM friendliness.
- Create clear, specific pages that:
  - Define what the product/service does.
  - Identify the target audience.
  - Highlight unique value propositions.
- Develop robust help/documentation sections; these often outrank marketing pages in AI citations.
- Use the provided checklist (available to paid subscribers) to audit content for LLM compatibility.
- Shift SEO workflows toward autonomous, agent‑driven processes and measure AEO performance beyond traditional keyword rankings.

## Local Business Imperatives
- Google Business Profiles and Yelp listings provide limited information for LLMs; detailed owned website content is essential for AI recommendations.
- A website is the only digital property fully under the business’s control, protecting against platform suspensions, hacks, or policy changes.
- Investing in a well‑structured, informative site safeguards visibility in AI‑generated answers and ensures continuity of customer acquisition.

## Key Takeaways
- Websites remain the foundational data source for AI answer engines; neglecting them harms brand visibility.
- Specific, well‑written content is rewarded by LLMs, while vague marketing copy is ignored.
- Direct traffic may rise even as traditional referral metrics fall, reflecting AI‑driven discovery (“Dark SEO”).
- For both national brands and local service providers, owning and optimizing a website is now a non‑negotiable component of AEO strategy.
