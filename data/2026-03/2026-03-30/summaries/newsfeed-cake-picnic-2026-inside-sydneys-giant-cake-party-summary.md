# Cake Picnic 2026: Inside Sydney's giant cake party

## Overview
- Viral “Cake Picnic” event arrives in Sydney, following sold‑out shows in San Francisco, New York, Los Angeles, Dubai, London and Mexico City.  
- Over 500 attendees gathered at the Royal Botanic Garden for a day of cake sharing, photography and community.

## Origin & Founder
- Created by Elisa Sunga, a part‑time hobby baker and full‑time Google employee, originally from Baguio City, Philippines.  
- Two years ago she posted an online invite asking everyone to bring a cake; 200 people turned up with 180+ cakes, sparking the global phenomenon.  
- Sunga sees the tour as “whimsical and delightful,” contrasting it with typical music tours.

## Sydney Event Highlights
- Cakes must be at least 20 cm (8 in) wide and 7.5 cm (3 in) tall; they are inspected on entry.  
- Participants line up on the grassy knoll with the Harbour Bridge as backdrop.  
- After display, groups have five minutes to cut, carve and pack as many pieces as possible into large pizza boxes.  
- The event is non‑competitive and emphasizes inclusivity and fun.

## Participants & Atmosphere
- Attendees ranged from home bakers, professional chefs, influencers, and non‑bakers to families and seniors.  
- Notable moments: Hilary Lindgren (54) baked a last‑minute carrot cake with her daughter; influencer Eunice Lim took selfies with Sunga; Marcus Ehrlich (42) and wife Kelly (48) enjoyed pandan and ube cakes.  
- Children helped decorate store‑bought cakes and enjoyed the communal tasting.

## Cakes & Flavors
