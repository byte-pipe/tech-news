# The first 40 months of the AI era

## 40 months of ChatGPT
- OpenAI released ChatGPT in late November 2022; it felt dramatically better than older bots like Cleverbot.
- I experimented with creative prompts (poems, D&D worlds) and found the output coherent but stylistically bland and overly safe.
- After hearing about code generation on the Linus Tech Tips WAN Show, I tested simple “hello world” programs, which worked flawlessly and replaced many quick‑look‑ups on Stack Overflow.
- My first “vibe‑coded” project was a placeholder‑card generator for a MTG collection. The initial AI‑generated code was impressive but required extensive manual tweaking; the final version contained almost no AI code.
- This experience left me questioning how much real time‑saving AI provides for coding.

## Claude Code, my Review
- Two months ago I subscribed to Claude Pro and began using Claude Code, which lets me speak natural language commands to my computer.
- The interface removes copy‑paste friction; the model can edit code directly, maintain context, and even spot bugs I missed.
- For small projects, one‑shot results were strong and iterative prompting felt more productive than with ChatGPT.
- I also used Claude Code as a pseudo‑executive assistant to draft a pre‑launch plan for an IT services business after losing my technician job. The plan boosted my confidence and helped me launch, though the business still runs mostly without AI help.
- I label this “glazing”: AI spurs action for a procrastinator, but I remain wary of over‑reliance and possible rate‑limiting, especially as local LLMs improve.

## How useful is AI really?
- I notice genuine productivity gains, yet I still replace a lot of AI‑generated work manually.
- The presence of AI may tempt me to expand project scope with “nice‑to‑have” features, making it hard to assess true efficiency.
- Time saved is not the sole metric; added polish within a fixed deadline can also be valuable.
- I plan to keep Claude Pro for now, but may cancel if limitations persist or local models become competitive.

## Generating AI Content
- No content on this blog has been copied verbatim from an LLM.
- Although AI can produce perfectly spelled, grammatically correct, and lengthy drafts in seconds, the prose feels boring or unappealing to me.
- Using AI‑generated sentences would undermine the personal voice that the blog is meant to showcase.

## Consuming AI Content
- As a reader/listener, I find AI‑generated material uncomfortable, akin to the “uncanny valley” effect: it is almost human‑like but off enough to create dissonance.
- This discomfort reduces my willingness to engage with AI‑produced articles, videos, or audio.
