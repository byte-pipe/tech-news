# Are high gas prices good news for EVs? It’s complicated

## Main observations
- Gasoline in the US has risen to about **$3.98 / gal** after the Iran conflict, sparking discussion about its impact on electric‑vehicle (EV) adoption.
- Social media and some op‑eds celebrate the volatility as a “I told you so” moment for EVs, but the situation is nuanced.

## Drivers of increased EV interest
- **Historical parallel:** The 1970s oil crisis shifted consumers toward smaller, fuel‑efficient cars, benefiting Japanese manufacturers.
- **Current demand spikes:**
  - A US online car marketplace reports a **20 % rise** in EV search traffic after the Iran attack; searches for the Tesla Model Y nearly doubled.
  - International dealers (London, Manila) are seeing rapid order growth and are scrambling for inventory.
- **Supply side:** About **300,000 leased EVs** from the Inflation Reduction Act program will return to the used‑car market this year, expanding affordable options.

## Price thresholds for consumer switching
- At roughly **$4 / gal**, total cost of ownership for an EV already undercuts a gasoline car, even with higher electricity rates (BloombergNEF data).
- A **Cox Automotive** survey finds most U.S. consumers would consider an EV or hybrid only if gas reaches **$6 / gal**.
- Repeated fuel‑price shocks (2022 Ukraine war, 2024 Iran conflict) may lower the psychological barrier over time (Harvard senior fellow Elaine Buckberg).

## Broader economic implications of sustained high fuel prices
- **Shipping & logistics:** Fuel accounts for 50‑60 % of overseas freight costs; higher prices raise goods prices globally.
- **Fertilizer production:** Relies on natural gas, so agricultural input costs climb, especially in Europe.
- **Air travel:** Jet fuel has doubled in a month, representing ~25 % of airline operating costs, likely raising ticket prices.
- **Financing climate projects:** An economic slowdown could restrict capital for large‑scale wind, solar, and even home or EV purchases.

## Reporter’s perspective
- As a climate and energy reporter without a personal car, the author welcomes EV adoption but warns that **decarbonizing only transportation** won’t offset the wider damage of prolonged high fossil‑fuel prices.
- High gas prices remain a concern for everyone, not just drivers.

## Takeaway
- **Short‑term:** Elevated gas prices are boosting curiosity and demand for EVs, aided by an upcoming influx of affordable used models.
- **Long‑term:** Without broader decarbonization, persistent fuel cost spikes could strain the economy, hinder financing for clean‑energy projects, and offset some of the climate benefits that EV adoption alone would bring.
