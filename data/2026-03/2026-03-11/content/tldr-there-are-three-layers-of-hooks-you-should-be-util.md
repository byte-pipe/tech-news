description: 'There are three layers of hooks you should be utilizing in the videos you post to any social media platform, and yet I *still* see so many accounts fumbling through the first 3-7 seconds of their content. Both the product leaders at LinkedIn and Adam Mosseri have confirmed that this trio will help content perform better (i.e. they are all present in the most engaged videos), and I’m breaking it down with clear examples so you have no choice but to adopt them! [I’m also going to do that self-promotional thing and tell you to send this video to your manager or your team, because I don’t want these tactics to be gatekept secrets.] The three layers of hooks are: Text On Screen Hook ➡️ include this right at the beginning, and use it to give context to viewers about what you’re going to cover. Cliffhangers work! Questions work! Spoken/Audio Hook ➡️ these are the first words you say and they must count! Try to be succinct, pack in a few keywords that you want to rank in the algo,
  and remember that attention spans are short. Visual Hook ➡️ This is what occurs on the video layer, in your movement (shaky camera set ups, slow zooms, sliding into view, etc.), and acts to entice or even confuse the viewer. Slate makes these three layers stupid easy, especially now that you can animate text hooks (a lil’ extra intrigue), speed up or slow down key moments (I love a timelapse), and add quick transitions between clips (like a classic swipe!). And if you’re like, “But do I need all three?” the answer is an emphatic YES. More is better here! And every new layer increases the chances that viewers stick around and watch/listen/absorb the whole thing. On now! Go hook ‘em! 🪝🪝🪝 | 73 comments on LinkedIn'
