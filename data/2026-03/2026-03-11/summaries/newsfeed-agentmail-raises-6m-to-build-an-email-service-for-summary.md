# AgentMail raises $6M to build an email service for AI agents

## Background
- Early AI agents were limited to chatbots with basic tools, constrained by reliability, security, and cost concerns.
- Coding agents such as Claude Code, Codex, and Cursor drove early adoption among programmers.
- The launch of OpenClaw’s localized, personalized agents accelerated broader use cases, from debugging to calendar management.
- Industry predictions suggest AI agents will become as numerous as human internet users, handling software interactions, shopping, and work automation.

## AgentMail Overview
- San‑Francisco startup providing an email service built specifically for AI agents.
- Offers an API platform that gives each agent its own inbox with full email capabilities (threading, labeling, searching, replying, etc.).
- Includes a human‑usable interface for managing agent inboxes.

## Funding
- Raised **$6 million** in a seed round.
- Lead investor: General Catalyst.
- Participants: Y Combinator, Phosphor Capital, and angels Paul Graham, Dharmesh Shah (HubSpot CTO), Paul Copplestone (Supabase CEO), Karim Atiyeh (Ramp CTO).

## Product Features
- **Onboarding API**: agents can self‑sign up and create inboxes via API calls.
- Manual controls for inbox setup, permissions, allowlists, and API keys.
- Designed for API‑only interaction; no UI clicks required for agents.
- Generous free tier plus paid and enterprise plans; avoids Gmail’s rate and volume limits.

## Traction & Growth
- Launched in Y Combinator Summer 2025 batch.
- Tens of thousands of human users and hundreds of thousands of “agent users.”
- Over 500 B2B customers.
- User count tripled after OpenClaw’s debut in January and quadrupled in February.

## Abuse Prevention
- Default limit: agents can send only 10 emails per day unless authenticated by a human.
- Rate‑limiting for unusually high activity.
- Monitoring of bounce rates.
- Random sampling of new accounts to filter sensitive keywords.

## Vision & Identity Layer
- Positions email as an identity protocol for AI agents, leveraging the existing, deeply integrated human email ecosystem.
- By giving agents an email address, they can interact with any software service that already supports email.
