# After complaints, Google will make it easier to disable gen AI search in Photos

## Background
- Google has been embedding its Gemini AI models across many products, including Google Photos.
- The “Ask Photos” feature, launched as a beta in 2024, lets users search photos using natural‑language queries powered by Gemini.

## Problems with Ask Photos
- User feedback has been largely negative: the AI search is slower, returns less accurate results, and groups images poorly.
- Because of these issues, Google paused the full rollout of Ask Photos in summer 2025, though the feature remains imperfect.

## New toggle for search mode
- Google will add a visible toggle in the Photos search tab.
- When the toggle is on, Gemini‑powered Ask Photos (with summaries and grouping) is used.
- When the toggle is off, the classic “fast” search returns, matching the experience before Ask Photos.
- Previously, disabling Ask Photos required navigating three levels deep in settings.

## Ongoing improvements
- The Photos team continues to re‑tune the Gemini model and has recently improved results for the most popular searches.
- Google still aims to transition fully to Gemini‑based search, but the toggle lets users compare both versions side by side.
