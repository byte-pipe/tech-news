# Yann LeCun Raises $1 Billion to Build AI That Understands the Physical World

## Overview
- Advanced Machine Intelligence (AMI) is a Paris‑based startup co‑founded by former Meta chief AI scientist Yann LeCun.  
- The company aims to create AI world models that can understand the physical world, retain memory, reason, plan, and remain safe and controllable.  
- LeCun argues that human‑level intelligence requires grounding in the physical world, not just large language models (LLMs).

## Funding
- AMI raised more than $1 billion, valuing the startup at $3.5 billion.  
- Lead investors include Cathay Innovation, Greycroft, Hiro Capital, HV Capital, and Bezos Expeditions.  
- Additional backers: Mark Cuban, former Google CEO Eric Schmidt, and French billionaire Xavier Niel.

## Vision & Approach
- Build “a new breed of AI systems” that can be applied across industries such as manufacturing, biomedical, and robotics.  
- Example use case: a realistic world model of an aircraft engine to optimize efficiency, reduce emissions, and improve reliability.  
- Plans to release the first models quickly, initially partnering with companies like Toyota and Samsung before expanding.

## Team
- Co‑founders: Yann LeCun, former Meta research director Michael Rabbat, former Meta VP for Europe Laurent Solly, former senior director of AI research Pascale Fung.  
- Alexandre LeBrun (ex‑CEO of health‑care AI startup Nabla) serves as AMI CEO.  
- Saining Xie (former Google DeepMind researcher) is chief science officer.

## World Models vs. LLMs
- LeCun acknowledges LLMs’ usefulness for code generation and many applications but maintains they cannot achieve human‑level intelligence.  
- He views LLMs as a “delusion” when considered the sole path to general AI.  
- World models have been developed within Meta’s FAIR lab (e.g., Joint‑Embedding Predictive Architecture) but LeCun believes they are better pursued outside a consumer‑focused company.

## Relationship with Meta
- Meta is not an investor, but LeCun is in talks about potential collaborations, such as using AMI’s models for smart‑glass assistants.  
- LeCun left Meta in November 2025 to focus on AMI, citing faster, cheaper development outside the social‑media giant.

## Open Source & Governance
- AMI intends to build open‑source technology, arguing that AI’s power should not be monopolized by a single private entity.  
- LeCun stresses that decisions about AI’s societal impact belong to democratic processes, not individual CEOs or governments.  
- He references past concerns about AI misuse (e.g., surveillance, autonomous weapons) and the need for responsible deployment.

## Future Outlook
- Goal: develop a “universal world model” that serves as a foundation for generally intelligent systems across any industry.  
- LeCun describes the ambition as “very ambitious” but believes it is achievable with the planned funding and partnerships.
