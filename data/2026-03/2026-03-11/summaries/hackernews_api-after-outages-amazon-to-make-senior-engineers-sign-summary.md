# After outages, Amazon to make senior engineers sign off on AI-assisted changes

## Summary
- Amazon convened a “deep dive” meeting to examine a series of recent outages, many tied to the use of generative AI coding tools.
- An internal briefing note described a “trend of incidents” with a high blast radius and “Gen‑AI assisted changes,” noting that best practices and safeguards for novel Gen‑AI usage are not yet fully established.
- Senior Vice President Dave Treadwell highlighted the poor recent availability of the site and its infrastructure, making the weekly “This Week in Stores Tech” (TWiST) meeting mandatory for staff.
- New policy: junior and mid‑level engineers must obtain sign‑off from senior engineers for any AI‑assisted code changes.
- Recent outages included a six‑hour website and app outage caused by an erroneous software deployment, and an AWS cost‑calculator interruption after the Kiro AI tool deleted and recreated an environment.
- AWS incidents were limited in scope, affecting only a single service in parts of mainland China and not impacting broader customer‑facing services.
- The company attributes the outages to multiple factors and disputes claims that recent layoffs are the primary cause of increased incident rates.
- TWiST remains a regular weekly operations meeting where retail technology leaders review operational performance across Amazon’s stores.
