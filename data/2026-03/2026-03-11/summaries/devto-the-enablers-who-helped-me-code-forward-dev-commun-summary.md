# The Enablers Who Helped Me Code Forward

## Early Beginnings
- Started coding at age nine with LOGO’s turtle graphics, discovering the magic of making a computer draw ideas.
- Family, especially my dad, encouraged my interest without questioning gender norms, giving me early confidence.

## The Myth of the Solo Journey
- Popular tech narratives focus on individual perseverance, but my path was equally shaped by enablers who offered small, timely nudges.

## The First Catalyst
- A tech lead recognized my love for clean code, experimentation, and hackathons.
- He organized a team hackathon, turning a fun activity into validation of my curiosity and boosting my confidence.

## Finding My Voice
- Faced fear of public speaking despite technical confidence.
- An office head gently pushed me to present, offering simple advice: start with a morning “hi” and smile.
- Gradual familiarity turned intimidating meetings into comfortable spaces, leading me to speak on identity and authentication.

## The Reality of Being Heard
- Women’s voices can be ignored, ideas overlooked, and credit misattributed.
- Conversely, allies amplify contributions by highlighting good points and ensuring ideas receive attention.

## Recognising the Enablers
- Current workplace includes mentors, leaders, colleagues, and allies who create supportive environments.
- My growth has always involved recognizing those who saw potential and helped unlock it.

## Paying It Forward
- The key lesson: become an enabler for others—encourage junior developers, support quiet voices, and create experimental spaces.
- For girls and marginalized individuals in tech: look for quiet supporters, recognize them, and remember you are never coding alone.
- One enabler can change a trajectory; someday you may be that enabler for someone else.
