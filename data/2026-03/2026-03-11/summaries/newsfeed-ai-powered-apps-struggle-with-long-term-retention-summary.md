# AI‑powered apps struggle with long‑term retention, new report shows

## Overview
- RevenueCat’s 2026 State of Subscription Apps Report analyses subscription data from iOS, Android and web apps that use its management tools.  
- The study covers more than 1 billion in‑app transactions, generating over $11 billion in annual developer revenue.  
- It compares AI‑powered apps (27.1% of the sample) with non‑AI apps (72.9%) on retention, churn, refunds and monetisation metrics.

## Key Findings  

- **Retention**
  - Annual retention: 21.1 % for AI apps vs. 30.7 % for non‑AI apps.  
  - Monthly retention: 6.1 % for AI apps vs. 9.5 % for non‑AI apps.  
  - Weekly retention (the only area where AI leads): 2.5 % for AI apps vs. 1.7 % for non‑AI apps.  
  - AI apps lose subscribers about 30 % faster than non‑AI apps (median churn).

- **Refunds**
  - Median refund rate: 4.2 % for AI apps vs. 3.5 % for non‑AI apps (≈20 % higher).  
  - Upper‑bound refund rates: 15.6 % for AI apps vs. 12.5 % for non‑AI apps, indicating greater revenue volatility.

- **Monetisation advantages**
  - Trial‑to‑paid conversion: 8.5 % for AI apps vs. 5.6 % for non‑AI apps (52 % better).  
  - Monetisation of downloads: 2.4 % for AI apps vs. 2.0 % for non‑AI apps (≈20 % better).  
  - Median monthly realised lifetime value (RLTV): $18.92 for AI apps vs. $13.59 for non‑AI apps.  
  - Median annual RLTV: $30.16 for AI apps vs. $21.37 for non‑AI apps.

- **Category distribution**
  - Photo & Video apps hold the largest share of AI‑powered apps (61.4 %).  
  - Gaming has the smallest share (6.2 %).  
  - Travel (12.3 %) and Business (19.1 %) also show low AI adoption.

## Interpretation
- AI integration boosts early‑stage monetisation (higher conversion and RLTV) but does not sustain long‑term subscriber value.  
- Rapid evolution of AI capabilities may drive users to switch between competing AI apps, contributing to higher churn and refund rates.  

## Takeaway
- While AI can be a strong acquisition tool, developers should not rely on it for lasting retention; additional strategies are needed to keep users engaged beyond the initial months.
