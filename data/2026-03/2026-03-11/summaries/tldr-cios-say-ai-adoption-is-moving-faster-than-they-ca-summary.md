# CIOs say AI adoption is moving faster than they can manage

## Key findings from the Logicalis Global CIO Report 2026
- 51 % of surveyed CIOs think AI adoption is progressing too quickly.
- Fewer than half say their AI strategy aligns with overall business plans or KPIs.
- Only 36 % feel they have solid guidance and best‑practice frameworks for AI projects.
- 65 % lack confidence in scaling AI beyond pilots to enterprise‑wide rollouts.

## Main drivers and concerns
- **Innovation pressure** is the top reason organisations accelerate AI investments.
- Risk‑management, compliance and continuity planning are lagging; many have no plan if a key AI provider disappears.
- Sustainability is under‑addressed: 39 % measure environmental impact and 41 % prioritize energy efficiency, despite forecasts that datacenter electricity use could double by 2030.
- 67 % worry about an “AI bubble” and market stability.

## Skills and resource gaps
- Almost 90 % cite a shortage of internal technical capability as the biggest barrier, not funding.
- Only a third feel equipped with the necessary skills and governance structures.

## Areas where AI shows value
- Predictive analytics and forecasting where data, ownership and processes are well defined.
- Specific functional use cases succeed more often than organization‑wide deployments.

## Investment outlook
- 72 % of enterprise customers plan additional AI spending in the next 12 months.
- 60 % intend to invest in agentic AI technologies.

## Quotes
- CEO Bob Bailkoski: “Organizations are not short of ambition or appetite for AI, they are short of the frameworks, skills and confidence to deploy it at scale.”
