# Summary of “Alibaba’s Qwen tech lead steps down after major AI push”

## Key Events
- Junyang Lin, a central technical leader of Alibaba’s Qwen team, announced on X that he is “stepping down” from the project, without giving reasons.  
- Lin joined Alibaba in July 2019 and moved to the Qwen team in April 2023.  
- The announcement came one day after Alibaba released the Qwen 3.5 Small Model series (0.8B, 2B, 4B, 9B parameters).  

## Qwen 3.5 Small Model Launch
- The new models are native multimodal, targeting on‑device AI deployment and lightweight agents.  
- Elon Musk praised the models on X, noting their “impressive intelligence density.”  
- The launch reinforced Qwen’s position as one of China’s leading open‑weight AI efforts, often matching benchmark results of U.S. rivals.  

## Reactions from Colleagues and Industry Partners
- Wenting Zhao (research scientist, Qwen) called Lin’s exit “the end of an era” and thanked him for advancing open‑source AI.  
- Yuchen Jin (CTO, Hyperbolic) highlighted Lin’s role in connecting Qwen with the global developer community.  
- Tiezhen Wang (Head of APAC Ecosystem, Hugging Face) described the departure as “an immense loss.”  
- Chen Cheng, a Qwen contributor, expressed heartbreak and suggested Lin’s leaving was not his choice.  

## Unclear Circumstances
- No official comment from Lin or Alibaba regarding the reasons for the departure.  
- Binyuan Hui updated his X profile to “formerly MTS @Alibaba_Qwen,” but it is unclear if he has also left the company.  
- Alibaba did not respond to inquiries about leadership changes or the future structure of the Qwen team.  

## Broader Context
- The departure occurs amid intensifying global competition among AI developers (OpenAI, Google, Anthropic).  
- Qwen’s rapid model releases aim to position Alibaba as a major player in the open‑weight AI landscape.
