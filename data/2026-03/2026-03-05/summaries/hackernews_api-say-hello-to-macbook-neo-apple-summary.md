# Say hello to MacBook Neo

## Overview
- Apple introduced the MacBook Neo, a 13‑inch laptop priced at $599 (or $499 for education).  
- Combines a durable aluminum chassis, Liquid Retina display, Apple A18 Pro silicon, and up to 16 hours of battery life.  
- Available for pre‑order now; ships beginning March 11, 2026.  

## Beautiful and Durable Aluminum Design
- Lightweight (2.7 lb) aluminum enclosure with soft, rounded corners.  
- Offered in four colors: blush, indigo, silver, and citrus, with matching keyboard accents and wallpapers.  

## Stunning 13‑Inch Liquid Retina Display
- Resolution: 2408 × 1506, 500 nits brightness, 1 billion‑color support.  
- Anti‑reflective coating for comfortable viewing in various lighting conditions.  

## Apple Silicon‑Powered Performance
- Powered by A18 Pro chip with 5‑core GPU and 16‑core Neural Engine.  
- Up to 50 % faster than the best‑selling PC with Intel Core Ultra 5 for everyday tasks.  
- Up to 3× faster for on‑device AI workloads and up to 2× faster for photo‑editing tasks.  
- Fanless design ensures silent operation.  

## All‑Day Battery Life
- Up to 16 hours of usage on a single charge thanks to the efficiency of Apple silicon.  

## Magic Keyboard and New Multi‑Touch Trackpad
- Apple’s Magic Keyboard provides comfortable, precise typing; color‑matched to the chassis.  
- Large Multi‑Touch trackpad supports click, scroll, swipe, and pinch gestures.  
- Touch ID model enables secure login and Apple Pay authentication.  

## Camera, Audio, and Connectivity
- 1080p FaceTime HD camera with dual microphones.  
- Dual side‑firing speakers with Spatial Audio for immersive sound.  

## Software Experience
- Runs macOS Tahoe with built‑in apps (Messages, Pages, Calendar, Safari) and seamless iPhone integration.  
- Supports Apple Intelligence and a wide range of third‑party applications.  

## Pricing and Availability
- Starting price: $599 for consumers, $499 for education.  
- Pre‑orders begin today; shipping starts March 11, 2026.  

## Executive Quote
- John Ternus, SVP of Hardware Engineering, highlighted the MacBook Neo as “the magic of the Mac at a breakthrough price,” emphasizing its design, performance, battery life, and unique Apple ecosystem integration.
