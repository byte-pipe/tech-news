# A metabolic alarmin from keratinocytes potentiates systemic humoral immunity | Nature

## Abstract summary
- Identifies farnesyl pyrophosphate (FPP), a mevalonate‑pathway intermediate, as an endogenous alarmin that boosts IgG responses.
- Keratinocyte‑derived IL‑6 and CCL20 mediate the effect, enhancing T follicular helper (Tfh) cell differentiation and migratory dendritic cell (miDC) activity.
- Infection or UV irradiation activates the unfolded protein response–SREBF pathway, increasing FPP production in keratinocytes and amplifying germinal‑centre (GC) reactions in draining lymph nodes.

## Core mechanistic insights
- Cytosolic FPP binds intracellular domains of the TRPV3 channel, triggering Ca²⁺ influx.
- Ca²⁺ influx activates two signaling cascades:
  1. Calmodulin–calcineurin–NFAT pathway.
  2. PYK2–RAS–ERK pathway.
- Both pathways drive elevated IL‑6 and CCL20 secretion from keratinocytes.
- The FPP–TRPV3–IL‑6/CCL20 axis promotes GC formation, leading to stronger pathogen‑specific antibody production.

## Experimental evidence
- Wild‑type mice show enhanced antibody responses and protection after FPP stimulation; TRPV3‑deficient mice do not.
- Single‑cell RNA‑seq of SLE skin lesions and infected mouse skin reveal hyperactivation of the FPP–TRPV3 axis, especially in TRPV3‑high keratinocytes.
- In mouse models of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), heightened axis activity correlates with worsened disease pathology.

## Implications and potential applications
- The identified signaling axis offers a target for novel vaccine adjuvants that could amplify humoral immunity.
- Modulating FPP–TRPV3 signaling may provide therapeutic avenues for autoimmune conditions such as SLE.
