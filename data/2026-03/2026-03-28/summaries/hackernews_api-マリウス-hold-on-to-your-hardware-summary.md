## The “RAM‑pocalypse”
- “RAM‑pocalypse” describes a sharp, sustained increase in RAM prices caused by massive demand from AI‑focused data centers.  
- Major memory makers (e.g., Micron) are exiting the consumer market, leaving Samsung and SK Hynix as a duopoly with little incentive to keep prices low.  
- Forecasts predict component shortages lasting beyond 2028.

## Why It’s Happening
- AI workloads require huge amounts of DRAM, SSDs, and GPUs.  
- Example: OpenAI’s “Stargate” project alone may consume ~40 % of global DRAM output each month.  
- Companies such as Google, Amazon, Microsoft, and Meta have placed open‑ended orders, driving enterprise demand to an estimated 70 % of all memory chips produced by 2026.

## First Domino: Memory and Storage
- RAM and SSD price hikes are the most visible symptom, but the same pressure spreads to LPDDR memory used in phones, laptops, tablets, consoles, routers, and low‑power PCs.  
- Fab capacity is being reallocated to HBM, server DRAM, and GPU wafers, making consumer‑grade chips a low priority.  
- Resulting effects:
  - Higher retail prices or reduced base configurations.  
  - Features locked behind premium tiers.  
  - Devices become non‑serviceable because components (e.g., LPDDR) are solder‑on.  
  - Small supply disruptions cause disproportionate price spikes.

## Recommendations for Consumers
- Preserve existing hardware as long as possible; avoid premature upgrades.  
- Prioritize durable, repairable, and high‑quality products that can be maintained over many years.  
- Anticipate higher costs for any future purchases and limited availability of upgrade parts.
