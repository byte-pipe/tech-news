# Afghan asylum seeker jailed for raping girl, 12

## Crime and conviction
- Ahmad Mulakhil, a 23‑year‑old Afghan national, abducted and raped a 12‑year‑old girl in Nuneaton on 22 July last year.  
- He was found guilty by a jury of rape, child abduction, sexual assault and taking an indecent video.  
- He also pleaded guilty to a separate count of rape of a child under 13 before the February hearing at Warwick Crown Court.

## Sentencing details
- The court sentenced Mulakhil to 15 years in prison followed by 12 months on licence, totalling a 16‑year term.  
- Judge Kristina Montgomery KC noted the severe psychological harm suffered by the victim and described the crime as “the highest level of culpability.”  
- An indefinite Sexual Harm Prevention Order and an indefinite restraining order were imposed, preventing any contact with the victim.  
- The sentence meets the threshold for deportation after it is served.  
- Concurrent sentences included four years for child abduction and two years for taking an indecent video.  
- Mitigating factors considered were Mulakhil’s age, lack of prior convictions and his guilty plea to one offence.

## Defendant background
- Mulakhil arrived in the UK on a small boat four months before the offences, at age 22, after fleeing Afghanistan and fearing Taliban persecution.  
- His defence lawyer, Marcus Harry, said Mulakhil had intended to study economics at university before the crimes.  
- During the hearing Mulakhil showed little emotion, listening through an interpreter.  
- A co‑defendant, Mohammad Kabir, also an Afghan asylum seeker, was acquitted of all charges.

## Legal and public reactions
- The case prompted a review of guidance on reporting the ethnicity and immigration status of suspects, after claims of a “cover‑up” by politicians Nigel Farage and George Finch.  
- Police were later encouraged to disclose such information in high‑profile cases.  
- The judge highlighted the “very significant uplift” to the sentence due to the abduction and the victim’s trauma.

## Investigation and police comments
- Detective Chief Inspector Collette O’Keefe, who led the investigation, praised the victim’s courage and said her testimony was crucial to securing a conviction.  
- She stated that the attack violated fundamental community values.
