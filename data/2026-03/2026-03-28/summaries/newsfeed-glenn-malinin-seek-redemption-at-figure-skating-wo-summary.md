# Glenn, Malinin seek redemption at figure skating worlds

## Overview
- The 2026 ISU Figure Skating World Championships are being held in Prague from March 25‑28, following the Milan‑Cortina Winter Olympics.
- Americans Amber Glenn and Ilia Malinin are targeting medals after disappointing Olympic performances.

## Amber Glenn
- Performed a “Like a Prayer” short program, landing the triple jump that cost her an Olympic medal.
- Ranked third after the short program and will skate the free program on Friday, where the final medals are decided.
- Said she feels “not clawing my way up” and that the competition feels like it happened yesterday despite being a month after the Olympics.

## Ilia Malinin
- Finished eighth at the Olympics after several uncharacteristic mistakes.
- Leads the men’s short program with an international personal‑best score, more than nine points ahead of the field.
- Stated his goal is to prove the Olympic slip‑up was a one‑time event and to enjoy the remainder of the season without high expectations.

## Other notable American skaters
- **Women:** Isabeau Levito (fourth after short program) and Sarah Everhardt (first World Championship appearance).
- **Men:** Andrew Torgashev (personal best in short, seventh overall) and Jacob Sanchez (career‑best debut after stepping in for Jason Brown).
- **Ice dance:** Emilea Zingas/Vadym Kolesnik (building on fifth‑place Olympic finish), Christina Carreira/Anthony Ponomarenko, Caroline Green/Michael Parsons.
- **Pairs:** Alisa Efimova/Misha Mitrofanov (sixth), Emily Chan/Spencer Howe (sixteenth after a fall‑ridden free skate), Katie McBeath/Daniil Parkman (twelfth).

## International highlights
- French ice‑dance champions Laurence Fournier Beaudry & Guillaume Cizeron are competing after forming a new partnership.
- Japan’s Kaori Sakamoto, Olympic silver medalist, aims for a different color medal before retiring.
- First world champions crowned in pairs: Germany’s Minerva Fabienne Hase/Nikita Volodin (gold), Georgia’s Anastasiia Metelkina/Luka Berulava (silver), Canada’s Lia Pereira/Trennt Michaud (bronze).
