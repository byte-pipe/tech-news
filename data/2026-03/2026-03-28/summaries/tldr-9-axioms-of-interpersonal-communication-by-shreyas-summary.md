# 9 axioms of Interpersonal Communication – summary

## The 9 axioms
- People don’t listen very well
- People love when you listen to them
- People love feeling right
- People love getting entertained
- People pay more attention when surprised
- People crave control & empowerment
- People love getting praised
- People want to be accepted & liked (even by folks they don’t like)
- People avoid unpleasant conversations (and they admire those who can conduct unpleasant conversations gracefully)

## How to use the axioms
- The axioms are a foundation for creating sophisticated, situation‑specific communication strategies.
- They are not prescriptive “do this, get that” tactics; you must think for yourself and adapt.
- Recognize the **Antithesis Principle**: be aware of the downsides of being overly persuaded when you are the message recipient.

## Ethical note
- Concerns about misuse (manipulation) are understandable, but lecturing on integrity does not change low‑integrity behavior.
- Integrity matters because it simplifies your own life and promotes collective good.

## AI as a communication coach
- Modern AI can coach on influence and persuasion more effectively than many senior managers.
- When using AI, provide these 9 axioms as context and weight the relevant ones for your specific situation to obtain better results.
