# CERN to host Europe’s flagship open access publishing platform

## Overview
- CERN has been chosen to host the next phase of Open Research Europe (ORE), an open‑access publishing initiative backed by the European Commission and a consortium of national funding agencies.
- The project follows the 2022 Action Plan for Diamond Open Access and offers a community‑led alternative to traditional journals.
- When launched later this year, the platform will allow authors from institutions in consortium countries to publish free of charge, maintaining equity, diversity, and transparency.

## Consortium and Governance
- Funding consortium members: Austria, France, Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, and Switzerland.
- The European Commission participates as a permanent observer and provides dedicated financial support.
- CERN supplies the technical and operational infrastructure, built on the open‑source Open Journal Systems (OJS).
- Governance and editorial oversight remain the responsibility of the ORE consortium.

## Publishing Model
- ORE uses a publish–review–curate workflow:
  - Articles undergo an integrity and compliance check before open publication.
  - Peer‑review reports are published openly, and reviewed articles are curated into subject‑specific collections.
  - The model supports post‑publication review, combining rigorous quality assurance with openness.

## Platform History and Impact
- Launched by the European Commission in 2021 as a no‑fee OA platform for EU‑funded research.
- Over the first five years: more than 1,200 articles, over 6,300 authors, and participation from more than 3,000 institutions worldwide.
- Steady growth demonstrates broad community uptake.

## CERN’s Role and Expertise
- CERN leverages its long‑standing experience with open‑science services such as Zenodo, Invenio, and SCOAP³.
- Hosting ORE provides a neutral, reliable, and sustainable environment for the platform.

## Statements
- *Mar Capeáns, CERN Director for Site Operations*: “Hosting Open Research Europe is a natural extension of our commitment to an open, community‑led scientific infrastructure.”
- *Marc Lemaître, Director‑General for Research and Innovation, European Commission*: “ORE exemplifies a shared commitment to free knowledge flow across the European Research Area, enhancing public trust in science.”

## Future Outlook
- The partnership is expected to deepen collaboration among CERN, the European Commission, national representatives, and research organisations.
- Working with the OPERAS Research Infrastructure, outreach and engagement activities will expand to attract eligible authors.
- ORE aims to support an increasing number of research outputs each year, setting a benchmark for equitable publishing in Europe and beyond.
- More information: https://ore.eu
