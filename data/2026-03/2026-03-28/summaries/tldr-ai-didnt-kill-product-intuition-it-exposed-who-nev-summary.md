# AI didn't kill product intuition. It exposed who never had it.

## Key story
- A company launched an affiliate marketing platform based solely on the president’s past success, without validating domain knowledge.
- Engineers built integrations quickly, but no one understood the business rules; the president was unavailable for detailed input.
- The platform functioned technically but failed because the complex affiliate rules were never articulated or validated.
- The failure was not due to lack of answers, but to a lack of questioning and reason to surface hard assumptions.

## Product intuition vs. AI
- Traditional “product intuition” is often mythologized as a magical ability to spot winners instantly.
- Real intuition is the discipline of exposing fragile ideas early, asking skeptical questions before resources are committed.
- Large language models (LLMs) act as tireless, unbiased skeptics that can surface missing considerations (e.g., domain expertise, switching costs, evidence of problem severity).
- The AI does not generate new insights; it forces the product team to ask the right questions they already know they should ask.

## Practical workflow with LLMs
- Present a raw, half‑formed idea to the model rather than a polished slide deck.
- Prompt the LLM to act as a skeptical stakeholder and identify assumptions that could break the idea.
- Iterate through exchanges, refining market assumptions, user segments, and business logic before any engineering scoping.
- The resulting conversation becomes a “corpus” of evidence and objections, not a final deliverable.

## Steps to apply to your next idea
1. **Pause before building a deck** – treat excitement as a signal to increase scrutiny.
2. **Describe the idea plainly** – “I think we should do X because Y; the people who’d care are Z.” Prompt the LLM: “You are a skeptical Head of Product; find three assumptions that will break this.”
3. **Build a corpus, not a slide** – each interaction adds context, objections handled, and logic mapped, forming the ammunition for a later pitch.
4. **Separate surviving ideas from attachments** – keep what survives the interrogation; discard the rest.

## Final thoughts
- Feeding the affiliate platform idea to an LLM at the start would have highlighted the missing domain‑expertise question.
- Strong PMs use AI to dismantle bad ideas early; weak PMs use it to produce superficial proposals.
- The tool itself isn’t the differentiator; it’s how you leverage the “wreckage” to surface critical kill‑shot questions.
