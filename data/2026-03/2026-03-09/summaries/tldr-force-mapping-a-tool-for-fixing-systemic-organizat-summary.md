# Force Mapping: A Tool For Fixing Systemic Organizational Issues

## Introduction
- Companies often request help with product management but may not understand their true problems.
- The author prefers refusing superficial requests (option 2) to make a real difference, especially when not pressured by financial constraints.
- The core issue: organizations mistake symptoms for problems, leading to ineffective solutions.

## Symptoms vs. Real Problems
- Clients typically present a desired solution (e.g., “Agile maturity scan”) instead of describing the underlying challenge.
- Answers to “what problem are you trying to solve?” are usually surface‑level symptoms.
- Analogy: being overweight is a symptom of deeper causes (exercise, genetics); fixing it requires understanding the whole system.
- In complex systems, improving one area often worsens another; trade‑offs must be considered.
- Raising maturity scores can sometimes exacerbate issues because problems are not linear.

## Introducing Force Mapping
- Inspired by Michael Lloyd’s Dysfunction Mapping, Force Mapping is a framework‑agnostic method to visualize internal forces, problems, and positive influences.
- It creates a mental model that helps identify the most effective interventions.

## The 8‑Step Force Mapping Process (overview)

### Step 1 – Define a Big Hairy Audacious Problem (BHAP)
- Identify the single most critical problem to address.
- BHAP serves as a lens for all subsequent analysis.
- Examples: “Teams struggle to deliver,” “Too much firefighting,” “Shipping unused features.”
- Recognize both negative and positive forces shaping the status quo.

### Step 2 – List All Symptoms and Problems
- Gather every observable symptom and underlying issue related to the BHAP.

## Practical Variations of Force Mapping Workshops
- **Cross‑functional workshop:** leaders and team members collaborate.
- **Team‑level vs. management‑level:** compare maps to reveal blind spots and overlaps.
- **Pure team‑level:** use team insights to inform leadership discussions.
- Participants should represent diverse skill sets; full inclusion is not required.

## Key Takeaways
- Do not accept surface‑level requests; dig deeper to uncover systemic problems.
- Distinguish symptoms from root causes to avoid unintended negative side effects.
- Use Force Mapping to build a comprehensive view of forces affecting the organization.
- Start with a clear, singular BHAP to focus effort and generate actionable insights.
