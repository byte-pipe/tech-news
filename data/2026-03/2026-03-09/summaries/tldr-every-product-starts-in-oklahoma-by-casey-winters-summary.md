# Every Product Starts in Oklahoma – Summary

## Overview
- The article uses Oklahoma (Sooners) and Missouri (Missourians) as metaphors for two distinct customer types.
- It explains why early‑stage founders should focus on Sooners first and only later shift to Missourians.

## Sooners vs. Missourians
### Sooners (early adopters)
- Want to be early and “in the know.”
- Naturally curious and tolerant of rough product edges.
- Motivated by status, play, and social signaling.

### Missourians (later adopters)
- Skeptical and risk‑averse.
- Require concrete evidence, clear ROI, and financial grounding.
- Need proof before committing to a product.

## Targeting the Right Customer
- Early founders need Sooners to generate early engagement signals.
- Missourian feedback is valuable for polishing but should be deprioritized until the product has proven scale and value.
- Identify and separate the two personas quickly to avoid wasting resources on premature skepticism.

## Motivation & Chasm Crossing
- User behavior is driven by social, financial, or personal utility.
- Sooners seek social status and fun; Missourians seek money, risk reduction, and proven ROI.
- Crossing the chasm is less about timing and more about re‑architecting the product to satisfy the financial motivations of Missourians.

## Migrating from Oklahoma to Missouri
- Transition requires shifting from social capital to financial capital:
  - Play → business.
  - Attention → income.
- Direct network‑effect products often evolve into cross‑side network‑effect models (creator vs. consumer, buyer vs. seller).
- Examples:
  - Grubhub’s revenue came from order commissions, not ads; showing “ads” would have reduced orders.
  - A blog can monetize indirectly through leads and job opportunities rather than sponsorships.

## Key Takeaway
- Every product starts with Sooners (Oklahoma) and later must adapt for Missourians (Missouri).
- Building for both simultaneously is a mistake; recognize which “state” you’re in, listen to the appropriate feedback, and adjust the product accordingly.
