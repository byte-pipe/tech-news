# Nashville library launches Memory Lab for digitizing home movies

## Overview
- Starting Monday, the Nashville Public Library’s Donelson branch offers a free, self‑service Memory Lab where anyone can book a four‑hour session to digitize VHS tapes, photos, slides, and Polaroids.  
- The lab provides equipment, software, and staff assistance; users keep the digital files.

## Why it matters
- Many families have old media that become inaccessible as VCRs and projectors become obsolete.  
- The free service avoids typical private‑company costs of about $30 per VHS tape.

## Background
- Last year the library ran a “home movie project” that selected submissions for professional digitization, aiming to create a video time capsule of Nashville life.  
- The new Memory Lab expands this concept to a personalized, on‑site experience.

## Details
- Sessions are reservable online for four hours; bring an external hard drive for high‑resolution files.  
- The program is open to anyone, not just library cardholders.  
- Similar free digitization services exist in libraries in Washington, D.C., Los Angeles, Brooklyn, and nearby counties.

## Quote
- Library director Terri Luke said Memory Lab is “more than just technology — it’s a creative space where anyone can reconnect with their history and capture moments that otherwise might have been lost forever.”
