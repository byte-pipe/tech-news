# AI Research Is Getting Harder to Separate From Geopolitics | WIRED

# Summary of “AI Research Is Getting Harder to Separate From Geopolitics | WIRED”

## Background
- NeurIPS, the premier AI conference, announced new participation restrictions tied to U.S. sanctions.
- The rules would have barred services (peer review, editing, publishing) for entities on sanction lists, affecting Chinese firms such as Tencent and Huawei, as well as organizations from Russia and Iran.
- The announcement prompted a swift threat of boycott from Chinese AI researchers.

## Revision of the Rule
- NeurIPS clarified that the restrictions apply only to “Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons,” a narrower list mainly for terrorist and criminal groups.
- Organizers attributed the broader initial wording to a miscommunication between the NeurIPS Foundation and its legal team.
- The conference subsequently withdrew the more expansive rule.

## Immediate Backlash
- Chinese academic groups condemned the policy and urged scholars to avoid NeurIPS, favoring domestic conferences.
- The China Association of Science and Technology (CAST) announced it would stop funding travel to NeurIPS and would no longer count NeurIPS publications toward research‑funding evaluations.
- At least six researchers publicly declined invitations to serve as area chairs or reviewers because of the sanctions policy.

## Impact on International Collaboration
- The episode highlights growing tension between U.S. geopolitical concerns and the traditionally open nature of AI research.
- Thousands of Chinese scientists regularly attend NeurIPS; in 2025, about half of the papers had Chinese affiliations.
- Prior WIRED analysis showed continued U.S.–China collaboration at NeurIPS despite broader diplomatic strains, but the sanctions saga threatens to erode that cooperation.

## Notable Statistics
- Tsinghua University appeared on 390 NeurIPS papers in 2025, the highest institutional count.
- Alibaba received a best‑paper award for work on its open‑source AI model Qwen.
- Researchers from both sanctioned and non‑sanctioned entities have historically contributed to NeurIPS’s success.

## Outlook
- Experts view the incident as a potential “watershed moment” where AI research becomes increasingly entangled with political considerations.
- Maintaining basic AI research outside the political arena may become more difficult, influencing future participation of Chinese scientists in U.S. conferences and institutions.
