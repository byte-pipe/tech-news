# Under The Island review: a cozy Zelda‑style adventure

## Overview
- Indie action‑adventure that blends classic 16‑bit Zelda mechanics with the relaxed atmosphere of Stardew Valley.  
- Players control Nia, a newcomer to Seashell Island, who must prevent the island from sinking.  

## Gameplay
- 2D top‑down exploration reminiscent of *A Link to the Past*.  
- Combat uses a hockey stick that functions like a sword: bash enemies, hit switches, and cut grass.  
- Progression unlocks new weapons and abilities (e.g., bombs for hidden rooms, fire‑spitting flower for vegetation).  
- Puzzles are integrated into dungeon‑like buildings and natural environments.  

## Creative Boss Battles
- Boss encounters are framed as quirky set‑pieces rather than traditional monster fights.  
- Example: the first boss requires the player to help monsters stage a concert, managing lighting, crowd control, and an elevator.  
- These scenarios use standard combat and puzzle mechanics in unexpected contexts.  

## Tone, Humor, and World‑building
- Lighthearted, playful tone similar to Stardew Valley and EarthBound.  
- Enemies include walking onions wielding leeks and squirrels throwing explosive pinecones.  
- Health can be restored by drinking ramen purchased from a hot‑air‑balloon restaurant.  
- Dialogue is witty; a band of skeletons sings Billy Joel, and many NPCs have humorous side quests.  

## Comparison and Verdict
- Stands out among Zelda‑style indie titles by balancing nostalgic gameplay with inventive design and strong charm.  
- The game’s cozy aesthetic and humor make it enjoyable even when not focused on the main quest.  

## Availability
- Released on PC, Nintendo Switch, PlayStation, and Xbox.
