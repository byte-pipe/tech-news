# Summary of “Artist shares how he created Penguin Random House's Playful Penguins campaign”

## Overview
- Penguin Random House’s classic mascot, known internally as “Alan,” is being complemented by a new series of “Playful Penguins” illustrations.
- The new artwork, created by illustrator Matt Blease, shows penguins skateboarding, jumping, reading, and engaging in other lively activities.
- These illustrations will appear in seasonal campaigns, social media initiatives, point‑of‑sale displays, and help celebrate the publisher’s approaching 100th anniversary, giving the brand a refreshed, playful vibe.

## Creation Process
- Matt Blease shared development sketches on Instagram, revealing that he researched the Penguin Random House archives for inspiration.
- To achieve an analogue feel, Blease cut the penguin shapes out of lino blocks and printed each illustration in his studio.
- The series builds on the existing “Alan” mascot, which was last redesigned by Pentagram in 2003.

## Brand Impact
- The new penguin illustrations add a sense of fun while respecting the brand’s heritage.
- The article suggests that other iconic brands (e.g., McDonald’s, Coca‑Cola) could benefit from similarly joyful reinterpretations of their visual identities.
