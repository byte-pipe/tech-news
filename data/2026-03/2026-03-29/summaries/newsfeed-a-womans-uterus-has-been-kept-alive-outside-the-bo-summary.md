# A woman’s uterus has been kept alive outside the body for the first time | MIT Technology Review  

## Overview  
- Researchers at the Carlos Simon Foundation in Valencia, Spain, built a perfusion machine nicknamed “Mother” (officially PUPER – preservation of the uterus in perfusion).  
- The device kept a freshly donated human uterus alive for 24 hours by circulating modified human blood through it.  

## Device Design and Operation  
- Metal, wheeled chassis about one meter tall, covered with flexible tubing that mimics veins and arteries.  
- Blood from a bag is pumped, oxygenated, warmed, and filtered (glucose, oxygen, waste removal) before reaching the uterus via custom “arteries” and “veins.”  
- The uterus is positioned at a tilt, in a humid environment, and monitored by sensors and a remote camera.  

## Research Milestones  
- Early prototype tested on six sheep uteruses (≈4 years ago), each sustained for a day using the animal’s own blood.  
- After modifications, the team received a human uterus from a hospital hysterectomy (May 2023) and achieved a 24‑hour survival – the first such proof of concept for a human organ.  

## Scientific and Clinical Goals  
- Extend preservation to cover a full menstrual cycle (~28 days) to study uterine disorders (endometriosis, fibroids) and the implantation process of embryos.  
- Use stem‑cell‑derived embryo‑like structures (not actual embryos) to investigate early pregnancy events without crossing ethical boundaries.  
- Potentially improve uterus‑transplant logistics by allowing longer storage of deceased‑donor organs, which currently survive only a few hours outside the body.  

## Expert Perspectives  
- Bioethicist Keren Ladin calls the result “impressive” but notes it is early-stage work.  
- Transplant surgeon Gerald Brandacher highlights the advantage of extending organ viability beyond the current few‑hour window, though long‑term perfusion (30 days) remains unprecedented for any organ.  

## Future Outlook  
- The Spanish team is focusing first on research applications rather than immediate clinical gestation.  
- Founder Carlos Simon envisions a future where “Mother” could support full gestation of a human fetus, offering a new route to parenthood for individuals lacking a uterus.  
- Technical challenges such as clotting, pressure regulation, and sustained nutrient delivery must be solved before longer‑term or gestational use becomes feasible.
