# 10 Months Ago, We Were Barely Using Salesforce. Now It’s Our AI Agent Hub

## Background
- Ten months ago Salesforce was essentially shelfware for us; only three sales reps and two never logged in despite being paid.
- Data in Salesforce was stale, and we relied on Slack, tribal knowledge, and spreadsheets.
- Adding Momentum (auto‑logging of sales calls) caused a rep to quit because they didn’t want their activity captured automatically.

## Turning Salesforce into an AI Hub
- After deploying multiple AI agents, we realized we needed a central place for data and communication.
- Salesforce became that hub, handling data ingestion, agent coordination, and serving as the source of truth.
- Without a hub, agents would duplicate outreach, conflict, and generate chaotic data.

## Current Stack and How It Works
- **Momentum**: auto‑transcribes, summarizes, and pushes call data (next steps, objections, competitor mentions, decision‑maker signals) into Salesforce.
- **Attention**: adds additional call intelligence and context to each deal.
- **Four AI SDRs**: all sync back to Salesforce for outreach across different segments.
- **Artisan**: runs three campaigns (ticket sales, sponsorship outreach, VIP reactivation) – 15,000 messages in 100 days with 5‑7% response rates.
- **Monaco**: newest GTM agent handling outbound outreach, booking meetings from day one.
- **Qualified** (acquired by Salesforce): powers the inbound “digital Amelia” on saastr.com, using full visitor context from Salesforce to personalize qualification.
- **Agentforce** (Salesforce product): manages win‑backs and re‑engagement of leads that were previously ghosted, using full interaction history for contextual outreach.

## Results and Metrics
- Agentforce emails achieved a 72% open rate and over 10% response rate on leads considered dead.
- Approximately 3,000 emails sent to previously ignored leads resulted in closed deals.
- Artisan outbound campaigns maintain 5‑7% response rates; Agentforce’s warm‑lead outreach far exceeds cold‑email benchmarks (2‑4% open rates).
- Digital Amelia handled hundreds of thousands of sessions, qualified over 1,000 prospects, contributed >$1 M in closed sponsorship revenue and $2.5 M in pipeline.
- In one month, 71% of closed‑won sponsorship deals came from Agentforce‑qualified leads versus a historic 29‑34% inbound conversion rate.

## Pattern and Implications
- Salesforce is acquiring top AI agents (Momentum, Qualified) and integrating them under the Agentforce umbrella.
- The emerging model: CRM platforms become the operating system for AI agents, not just data repositories.
- For operators, the cost of individual agents now exceeds the cost of Salesforce itself; the value lies in the agents, while Salesforce provides the infrastructure.
- The next‑generation CRM survivability hinges on being the hub for AI agents.

## Lessons Learned
- Early mistake: assuming a lean team could operate without CRM discipline, relying on Slack and spreadsheets.
- Realization: even an AI‑first organization needs a disciplined, centralized data layer.
- Salesforce, when used as an AI hub, transforms from a passive database into the core operating system that synchronizes, contextualizes, and powers all AI‑driven business functions.
