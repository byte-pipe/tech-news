## Background and career  
- Former England flanker with 71 caps; British & Irish Lions tourist; multiple domestic and European titles with Leicester.  
- Known as “Mad Dog” for his relentless, collision‑heavy style and willingness to play through pain.  

## Diagnosis and MND context  
- Diagnosed with motor neurone disease (MND) in 2025, a degenerative condition that also affected players such as Rob Burrow, Doddie Weir, Joost van der Westhuizen and Ed Slater.  
- No proven causal link between rugby (or any sport) and MND, though elite athletes show higher incidence, possibly due to extreme exertion, genetics or environmental factors.  
- Moody stresses that public assumptions linking rugby to MND are “not the truth.”

- Acknowledges awareness of injury and concussion risks during his playing days, but felt the joy and reward outweighed them.  
- Describes a 2007 World Cup match against Tonga where he suffered multiple head impacts, leading to his first serious consideration of concussion.  
- Notes that rugby has evolved: stricter concussion protocols, lower tackle heights, high‑tech mouthguards, mandatory stand‑down periods, and independent medical review.  
- Believes the modern game is safer than ever, though he has witnessed former teammate Steve Thompson’s struggle with early‑onset dementia and ongoing legal action for better player protection.

- After going public with his diagnosis on BBC Breakfast, Moody adopts a “today‑only” mindset inspired by a Buddhist saying, focusing on living as normally as possible.
