# 4th U.S. Soldier Killed as Iran War Expands in the Middle East

## Overview
- The conflict sparked by Iran’s retaliation to U.S. and Israeli strikes on Saturday has spread across the Middle East and beyond.
- Strikes intensified on Monday, with Iran‑backed groups increasing attacks and a fourth U.S. service member killed in action.

## U.S. Military Casualties
- CENTCOM confirmed the fourth U.S. service member died from wounds sustained during Iran’s initial attacks; the soldier was part of ground forces stationed in Kuwait.
- General Dan Caine warned of expected additional losses while emphasizing efforts to minimize them.
- A separate friendly‑fire incident in Kuwait caused three U.S. F‑15E jets to crash; all six crew members survived and are in stable condition.

## Iranian and Israeli Actions
- Iran launched missiles, drones, and ballistic missiles at U.S. and Israeli targets after the Saturday strikes.
- Israel conducted airstrikes in Tehran aimed at security targets and responded to missile attacks on its cities, killing at least 10 people, including a caregiver from the Philippines.
- The Iranian Red Crescent reported over 555 Iranian deaths, including Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei and members of his family, and more than 168 schoolgirls killed in a school hit by a missile.

## Hezbollah and Lebanon
- Hezbollah fired rockets into Israel in retaliation for Khamenei’s death and ongoing Israeli strikes in southern Lebanon.
- Israel intercepted one missile and struck Hezbollah positions, reporting 31 Lebanese deaths and 149 injuries.
- Lebanese authorities announced plans to arrest those responsible for the rocket attack; civilians fled to shelters set up in schools.

## Regional Impact and Civilian Displacement
- Residents of Beirut’s southern suburbs and southern Lebanon sought refuge in school shelters, many having been displaced previously during the 2024 Israel‑Hezbollah war.
- A taxi driver from the Hezbollah‑stronghold of Dahiya described the hardship of repeated displacement and the desire for normal life.

## Wider Middle‑East Involvement
- Iran‑backed militias in Iraq fired on a U.S. base in Irbil and claimed a drone attack on Baghdad airport.
- A British air force base in Cyprus was struck by a drone; no casualties were reported.
- Iran’s Revolutionary Guard claimed to have shot down a U.S. F‑15 and launched cruise missiles at the Ali al‑Salem U.S. air base in Kuwait.

## Economic Consequences
- Global crude oil prices surged and stock markets fell as the war entered its third day.
- Iran’s Revolutionary Guard said it hit three U.S. and U.K. oil tankers and announced the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a key oil transit route.
- Saudi Arabia reported shooting down two drones targeting the Ras Tanura refinery; the resulting fire caused no civilian injuries.

## International Responses
- President Trump pledged to “avenge” the deaths of American troops and later claimed the U.S. had sunk nine Iranian warships and largely destroyed Iran’s naval headquarters, though CENTCOM could not verify the statement.
- The British Foreign Secretary indicated possible evacuation plans for hundreds of thousands of citizens in the region.
