# Apple keeps the iPad Air fresh with M4 chip upgrade and 12GB of RAM

## Overview
- Apple introduced a refreshed iPad Air that swaps the M3 chip for an M4.
- RAM is increased from 8 GB to 12 GB, improving iPadOS 26 multi‑window multitasking.

## Specifications and Pricing
- Sizes: 11‑inch model starts at $599, 13‑inch model starts at $799; both base models have 128 GB storage.
- Storage upgrades: 256 GB (+$100), 512 GB (+$300), 1 TB (+$500).
- Pre‑orders begin March 4; availability begins March 11.
- M4 chip details: 8 CPU cores (3 high‑performance, 5 efficiency) and 9 GPU cores; no 16 GB RAM option for the Air.
- Display: 60 Hz LCD (no OLED or ProMotion), Touch ID on power button, single‑lens 12 MP rear camera without flash, no nano‑texture coating.

## Design and Accessory Compatibility
- Retains the same chassis as the 2024 M2 Air and the prior M3 model.
- Supports the Apple Pencil Pro accessory; not compatible with the second‑generation Pencil.
- Color options: space gray, blue, purple, and pale gold “starlight”.

## Market Context
- Announcement is part of a series of reveals leading up to Apple’s “special experience” event.
- Alongside the iPad Air, Apple unveiled the iPhone 17e and is expected to introduce a low‑end iPad and a more affordable MacBook later.
