# Like the plane got cut in half: LaGuardia crash that killed two pilots now under investigation

## Overview
- Air Canada flight AC8646 collided with a fire truck on a LaGuardia Airport runway on Sunday at 23:40 local time.  
- The crash killed both pilots, injured 41 people, and shut the airport until Monday afternoon.  
- The National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) has opened an investigation.

## Timeline of events
- A fire truck was dispatched minutes earlier to another aircraft reporting an odor problem.  
- While the Air Canada plane was landing, air‑traffic control audio captured a tower operator shouting “Truck One, stop, stop, stop!”  
- The aircraft struck the truck, broke apart, and passengers evacuated by sliding down the wing.  
- Emergency responders arrived; the runway was closed for debris collection and evidence gathering.

## Casualties
- Pilots: Antoine Forest (30, Québec) and an unnamed co‑pilot were killed.  
- Passengers and crew: 41 injured, several taken to hospital; most have been released.  
- Fire‑truck crew: two members hospitalized.  
- One flight attendant survived after falling through a floor hole; injuries were serious but not life‑threatening.

## Witness accounts
- Leo Medina (23), on another plane nearby, described the aircraft as “cut in half” and spent over 12 hours sleeping on the tarmac.  
- Passenger Rebecca Liquori heard a loud “boom” after a rough landing and described passengers helping each other down the wing.  
- Jack Cabot recalled immediate chaos and screaming after impact.  
- Katie Rojas, preparing to depart, said she feared she could have been the one involved.

## Investigation
- NTSB Chairwoman Jennifer Homendy confirmed a preliminary probe, with investigators on site after 03:00 local time.  
- Cockpit voice recorder and flight data recorder were recovered intact.  
- Teams are documenting and processing extensive debris; runway closure will continue until evidence is processed.  
- Investigation faced delays due to TSA staffing shortages and security checkpoint backlogs.

## Official reactions
- U.S. Secretary of Transportation Sean Duffy emphasized seat‑belt safety.  
- FAA Administrator Bran Bedford called the loss of the two young pilots “an absolute tragedy.”  
- NYC Mayor Zohran Mamdani labeled the incident a “tragic collision” and praised first responders.  
- Canada’s Prime Minister (Mark Carney) expressed deep sadness; U.S. President Donald Trump called it “terrible” and “dangerous.”

## Aftermath and broader context
- Hundreds of flights were delayed or cancelled, creating major travel disruption at one of the nation’s busiest airports.  
- This is the first fatal incident at LaGuardia in more than 34 years.  
- Ongoing TSA staffing shortages and agency shutdowns have strained airport operations and delayed investigative personnel.
