# US and TotalEnergies reach ‘nearly $1 billion’ deal to end offshore wind projects  

## Agreement overview  
- On 23 March 2026, US Interior Secretary Doug Burgum and TotalEnergies CEO Patrick Pouyanné signed an agreement in Houston to cancel TotalEnergies’ US offshore wind leases.  
- The funds previously tied to the wind leases will be redirected to fossil‑fuel projects, chiefly natural‑gas development.  

## Financial details  
- The deal releases “nearly $1 billion” that was locked in lease deposits.  
- TotalEnergies’ $928 million investment in two wind leases (North Carolina and New York coasts) will be reallocated to US natural‑gas projects, especially the Rio Grande LNG plant.  

## Offshore wind projects affected  
- TotalEnergies had four GW of offshore wind under development: 3 GW for the New York Bight project and 1 GW for a North Carolina project.  
- In late 2024 the company placed the projects on hold, citing low prospects of obtaining federal licences under the Trump administration.  

## Political context  
- Under President Biden, US policy accelerated offshore wind construction as part of climate‑change mitigation.  
- The Trump administration reversed many of those policies, criticizing wind farms for aesthetics, cost, and “national‑security” concerns; five wind projects were put on hold in December, though courts later allowed work to resume.  

## Statements from the parties  
- Burgum: the partnership “unleashes nearly $1 billion” previously tied to subsidies for “expensive weather‑dependent offshore wind.”  
- Pouyanné: offshore wind “is not the most affordable way to produce electricity” in the US; redirecting the money to natural‑gas projects is a “win‑win” and a “smarter investment.”  

## Additional agreements  
- TotalEnergies signed a letter of intent with Glenfarne, the lead developer of the Alaska LNG project, for the long‑term off‑take of 2 million tons of LNG per year over 20 years.
