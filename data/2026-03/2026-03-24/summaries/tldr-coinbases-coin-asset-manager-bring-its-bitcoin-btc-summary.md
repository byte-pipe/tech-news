# Coinbase's bitcoin yield fund goes onchain with Apex's tokenization push

## Key points
- Coinbase Asset Management (CBAM) is launching a tokenized share class of its Bitcoin Yield Fund on Base, the Ethereum‑compatible blockchain owned by Coinbase.
- Apex Group, a $3.5 trillion fund‑services provider, acts as the transfer agent and will maintain NAV‑aligned records for the tokenized shares.
- The initial offering is limited to non‑U.S. investors; CBAM plans to create a U.S.‑focused tokenized share class later.
- The token uses the ERC‑3643 standard, embedding investor eligibility checks in the token itself, so only approved wallets can hold or transfer the asset.
- Apex aims to tokenize $100 billion of funds via its T‑REX Ledger by June 2027, after acquiring Tokeny, which has tokenized over $32 billion to date.

## Industry context
- Major asset managers (BlackRock, Fidelity, Franklin Templeton) are exploring tokenization to cut costs, speed settlement, and broaden distribution.
- Market forecasts for tokenized assets range from $2 trillion by 2030 (McKinsey) to $18.9 trillion by 2033 (BCG & Ripple).
- Tokenization is seen as a way to modernize capital markets, offering faster, cheaper issuance and trading while improving compliance automation.

## Fund mechanics
- The Bitcoin Yield Fund generates returns by strategies such as selling call options and participating in lending arrangements, providing yield while investors wait for price appreciation.
- Institutional capital already allocated to CBAM’s bitcoin and ether positions is expected to flow into the tokenized product for compounded returns.

## Apex’s tokenization roadmap
- After acquiring Tokeny, Apex plans to leverage the T‑REX Ledger to manage ownership and regulatory compliance across multiple blockchains.
- The goal is to tokenize $100 billion of funds by mid‑2027, positioning Apex as a leading provider of blockchain‑based fund services.

## Outlook
- The launch demonstrates a growing trend of bringing traditional fund structures onto blockchain rails.
- Success of the non‑U.S. rollout could accelerate the introduction of a U.S. tokenized share class, further integrating crypto‑based yield products into mainstream institutional portfolios.
