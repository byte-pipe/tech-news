# Two pilots dead after plane and ground vehicle collide at New York's LaGuardia Airport

## Key Facts
- An Air Canada regional jet (CRJ‑900, Flight 8646) collided with a Port Authority firefighting vehicle on the tarmac at LaGuardia Airport at about 23:40 local time (03:40 GMT).  
- The firetruck was responding to a United Airlines aircraft that reported an odor issue.  
- Two pilots on the Air Canada flight were killed; the remaining crew and all 72 passengers survived.  
- Forty‑one people were taken to hospitals; 32 were discharged, while others sustained serious injuries.  
- The two officers inside the firetruck were hospitalized in stable condition; one was released the next day, the other remained for observation.  

## Airport Operations and Passenger Impact
- LaGuardia was closed overnight and reopened at 14:00 local time (18:00 GMT) on Monday.  
- All arrivals and departures were cancelled or delayed until the afternoon; most Monday morning departures from Terminal B were cancelled.  
- Hundreds of flights from carriers such as American, Delta, Southwest, United, and Air Canada were affected, leading to long security queues and passengers sleeping on benches.  
- Travelers were advised to expect residual delays, check with airlines, and be prepared for further cancellations.
