# Crochet Artist Transforms Early Internet Icons Into Nostalgic Textile Art  

## Overview  
- Millennial crochet artist Nicole Nikolich, known as Lace in the Moon, creates oversized hand‑crocheted replicas of early‑2000s digital icons such as flip phones, Game Boys, chat rooms, and classic computer game screens.  
- Her work taps into collective nostalgia for a pre‑smartphone internet era that included MS Paint, Internet Explorer, Minesweeper, Neopets, MSN Messenger, and The Sims.  

## Recent Exhibitions  
- **“Can I Please Eat In The Computer Room Tonight?”** at Delaware Contemporary featured four large tapestries inspired by iconic game screens: The Sims 2, Windows Solitaire, Minesweeper, and the Neopets Marketplace. The installation evoked the feeling of a designated computer room as a personal oasis.  
- **Solo show “user_history”** at Paradigm Gallery in Philadelphia (open until March 29, 2026) presents the artist’s largest body of work to date, including crochet versions of retro cellphones, game consoles, and other early‑tech devices.  

## Notable Commissions  
- Microsoft commissioned Nikolich to crochet giant representations of the Windows 95 desktop and the Windows 11 home screen. After about two months of work, the pieces were combined into a double‑sided Windows‑inspired blanket.  

## Artistic Intent & Themes  
- Nikolich describes her installations as memorials to a “fever dream” era when internet access was limited to specific computer rooms, offering a space for self‑discovery away from adult expectations.  
- The tactile, pixel‑by‑pixel recreation of digital interfaces highlights the comfort, curiosity, and personal growth experienced by millennials during the rise of the internet.
