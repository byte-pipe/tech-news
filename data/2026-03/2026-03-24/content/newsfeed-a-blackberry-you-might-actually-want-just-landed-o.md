# A BlackBerry you might actually want just landed on Kickstarter

The Titan 2 Elite features a BlackBerry-inspired keyboard but runs Android 16.﻿

WhenThe Verge’sAllison Johnsonwenthands-on with the company’s Titan 2 Elite phoneat MWC 2026, Unihertz didn’t divulge pricing info. But the compact Android phone with a BlackBerry-inspired keyboard is now available for preorder through aKickstarter crowdfunding campaignand starts at $489, with a discount for early backers bringing it down to $389. It’s expected to ship as early as June 2026.

The Titan 2 Elite is a smaller and lighter version ofUnihertz’s Titan 2that debuted last June as a modernized version of theBlackBerry Passport. The Titan 2 Elite’s design is more reminiscent of theBlackBerry Boldlineup, but with a slightly boxier design to accommodate a 4.03-inch 120Hz AMOLED display. The Elite also features Android 16 with OS upgrades promised to Android 20, 5G connectivity with dual nano SIMs and eSIM, fingerprint and face unlock, a 4,050mAh battery, and a full QWERTY keyboard that can be used as a trackpad.

It’s not a thin phone by any stretch of the imagination, but it does include an extra button on the side you can customize to launch apps or shortcuts.
 
Photo: Allison Johnson / The Verge

On the back of the Titan 2 Elite you’ll also find a pair of 50MP cameras with telephoto capabilities, and a front-facing 32MP holepunch camera in the upper left corner of the display.

Unihertz is offering two versions of its modernized BlackBerry. The standard Titan 2 Elite features a Dimensity 7400 processor with 12GB of RAM and 256GB of storage. The slightly more expensive Titan 2 Elite Pro, which instead starts at $579 (but is temporarily discounted to $479), has a faster Dimensity 8400 processor, a bump to 512GB of storage, and a 50MP telephoto camera upgraded with optical image stabilization capabilities.
