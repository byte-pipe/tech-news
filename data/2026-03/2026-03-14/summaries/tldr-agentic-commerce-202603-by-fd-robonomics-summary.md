# Agentic Commerce | 2026.03 Summary

## Recap – Key recent developments
- **ChatGPT shift** – moving from an end‑to‑end checkout role to a discovery entry point that routes transactions to specialized commerce apps.  
- **Platform‑embedded agents** – major commerce platforms now embed AI agents directly in their products (e.g., Amazon “Buy for Me”, Booking.com, Expedia).  
- **B2B sourcing adoption** – early agentic use on the sourcing side of B2B commerce, where research‑intensive workflows benefit from AI assistance.

## Guiding principles likely to persist
- **AI democratizes information** – reduces layers of marketing, ads, and platform fees, shifting power toward buyers; especially impactful in opaque, fragmented B2B markets.  
- **Faster, cheaper, better** – price competition will intensify; scale advantages (e.g., Amazon logistics) remain critical; agents enable more personalized, context‑aware product matching.  
- **Top‑of‑funnel control matters** – Amazon’s $80 B+ advertising revenue shows the value of early shopping intent; if discovery moves upstream to AI interfaces, funnel economics could shift.

## Open questions in agentic commerce
- Can agents handle nuanced purchase details such as size, color, pattern, loyalty programs, travel rewards, or hotel room preferences?  
- Will the user experience differ materially between web and mobile environments?  
- How will payments and checkout flows operate when agents automatically complete forms?

## OpenAI / ChatGPT evolution
- Transitioning from an end‑to‑end shopping destination to a shopping entry point that hands transactions to connected commerce platforms or merchant sites (e.g., Target, Expedia, Booking.com).

## B2B commerce use case
- **Research‑heavy workflow** – buyers must define specifications, price, MOQ, logistics, certifications, risk, etc.  
- **Agent‑enabled workflow** – buyer describes need in plain English; AI creates a sourcing brief, shortlists suppliers, and compares key criteria.  
- **Alibaba Accio example**  
  - >1 M users by March 2025, >2 M by August 2025.  
  - New features: Business Research, Deep Search, Accio Agent, moving toward automated sourcing.

## Amazon initiatives
- **Rufus AI assistant** – ~250 M users in 2025, 60 % higher purchase completion, projected >$10 B incremental annualized sales, >$700 M indirect operating profit.  
- **Help Me Decide** – personalized recommendation and narrowing tool.  
- **Buy for Me** – completes purchases on external brand sites within the Amazon app when Amazon does not carry the item.  
- **Shop Direct** – directs users to merchant sites, covering >100 M products from 400 k+ merchants.  
- **Strategic aim** – position Amazon as the starting point of shopping intent, even when the final purchase occurs outside Amazon inventory.

## Shopify (SHOP) perspective
- **Evidence of progress**  
  - ChatGPT in‑app checkout works with merchants such as SKIMS, Glossier, and Spanx.  
  - AI‑driven orders up 15× since January 2025.  
- **Bull case** – could become the infrastructure layer for AI commerce, offering a universal product catalog via agent infrastructure to non‑Shopify merchants; may shift GMV from closed marketplaces to independent merchants.  
- **Bear case** – payment disintermediation, weakening of direct‑to‑consumer relationships; agents optimizing purely for price/availability could favor large aggregators over independent brands.  
- **Possible outcome** – checkout button value may diminish, but a low take rate (~3%) gives Shopify room to develop additional monetization layers.

## Online Travel Agencies (OTAs) – Booking.com, Expedia
- **Historical advantage** – supply aggregation of long‑tail hotels lacking integration capabilities.  
- **AI impact** – modern APIs enable direct distribution, reducing the OTA moat; Google Hotels shows >350 k hotels joining “Book Now” to bypass OTA commissions.  
- **Bull case** – travel remains structurally complex (inventory, availability rules, payments, cancellations, support), making full disintermediation harder.  
- **Bear case** – AI agents could erode OTA top‑of‑funnel advantage, pressuring take rates and brand loyalty.

## Outlook
- Agentic commerce remains exploratory; many assumptions will be tested as AI capabilities and integration ecosystems evolve.  
- The balance between buyer empowerment and platform control will shape the next few years of commerce architecture.
