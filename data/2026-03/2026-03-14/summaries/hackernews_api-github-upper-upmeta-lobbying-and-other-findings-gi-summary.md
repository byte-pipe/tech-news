# Meta Platforms: Lobbying, Dark Money, and the App Store Accountability Act – Summary

## Investigation Overview
- Open‑source intelligence (OSINT) study of Meta’s multi‑channel effort to push the App Store Accountability Act (ASAA), which would shift age‑verification costs to Apple and Google while leaving Meta’s platforms untouched.  
- Sources: IRS 990 filings, Senate LD‑2 disclosures, state lobbying registers, campaign finance data, corporate registries, WHOIS/DNS, Wayback Machine, investigative journalism.  
- Status: Active (research from 2026‑03‑11 onward); 47 proven findings, 9 plausible but unproven hypotheses, several pending FOIA replies.  

## Core Findings
- **Federal lobbying spend:** $26.3 M in 2025 (record level), with 86+ lobbyists operating in 45 states.  
- **Grassroots front:** Meta covertly funds the Digital Childhood Alliance (DCA), a 501(c)(4) astroturf group championing ASAA.  
- **Super‑PAC contributions:** >$70 M funneled to four state‑level super PACs (ATEP, META California, California Leads, Forge the Future).  
- **Dark‑money network:** Analysis of 4,433 grants (~$2 B) across the Arabella Advisors network found no dollars directed to child‑safety or tech‑policy groups, ruling out a direct grant pathway.  
- **State legislative success:** ASAA enacted in Utah, Texas (later blocked), and Louisiana; bills introduced in ~17 additional states.  

## Confirmed Influence Channels
1. **Direct Federal Lobbying** – 40+ firms, 87 lobbyists; LD‑2 filings list H.R. 3149/S. 1586 (ASAA) as a target. State‑level spending includes $338.5 k (Colorado), $1.0 M+ (California), $325 k+ (Louisiana).  
2. **Digital Childhood Alliance (DCA)** – 501(c)(4) with no independent EIN; donations processed through the For Good DAF, likely fiscally sponsored by NCOSEAction. Domain created Dec 18 2024; messaging exclusively attacks Apple/Google.  
3. **Super PACs** – $45 M to ATEP (Hilltop Public Solutions co‑lead), $20 M to META California, $5 M to California Leads, and an undisclosed amount to Texas‑based Forge the Future. All registered at state level, dispersing disclosure.  
4. **Arabella Network Connection** – Meta lobbyist Adam Eichberg chairs New Venture Fund (NVF). NVF routes $121.3 M annually to Sixteen Thirty Fund (501(c)(4)). Grant analysis shows zero funding to child‑safety entities, leaving only indirect routes (consulting fees, fiscal sponsorship) as possible pathways.  
5. **State Legislative Campaigns** – Direct support to bills, including Louisiana HB‑570 (passed 99‑0). Meta’s lobbying staff delivered legislative language to sponsors.
