# Adobe will pay $75 million to settle US cancellation fee lawsuit

## Key Details
- Adobe agreed to pay $75 million to the U.S. Justice Department to resolve a consumer‑protection lawsuit filed in June 2024.  
- The company will also provide $75 million worth of free Creative Cloud services to customers affected by the alleged “onerous and complicated” cancellation process and undisclosed early‑termination fees.  
- The DOJ alleged Adobe’s “annual paid monthly” plans concealed important terms and trapped subscribers in costly contracts, likening the fees to “heroin for Adobe.”  
- Adobe’s statement: it denies any wrongdoing, disagrees with the government’s claims, but says it has improved transparency around subscription terms, plan details, and cancellation procedures.  
- Settlement still requires final court approval; the current administration has shown a strong stance against hidden subscription fees.  

## Context
- The announcement follows news that Adobe CEO Shantanu Narayen will step down after 18 years, having overseen the shift from perpetual licenses to subscription‑only models.  
- The case reflects broader regulatory scrutiny of tech companies’ subscription practices.  

## Implications
- Customers may receive free services as part of the settlement.  
- Adobe’s reputation and subscription model could face increased scrutiny, influencing future policy and industry practices.
