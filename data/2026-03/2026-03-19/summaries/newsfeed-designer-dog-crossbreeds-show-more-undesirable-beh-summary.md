# Designer dog crossbreeds show more 'undesirable' behaviours, vets say

# Designer Dog Breeds vs Purebred Parents: More Problem Behaviors Identified

A recent study by the Royal Veterinary College (RVC) has shed light on the relationship between designer dog breeds, their purebred parents, and problem behaviors such as aggression, separation anxiety, and excitability. While popular designer breed types like "Doodles" (e.g., Cockapoos, Labradoodles, Cavapoos) are often touted for their ease of handling and trainability, research suggests that they can also exhibit undesirable behaviors not previously understood.

## Key Findings

* 9,402 UK dogs participated in an online survey between February 21st and April 21st, 2023.
* The study compared the behavioral data from popular designer breeds to purebred parents.
* In over half of all comparisons (82%), designer dogs showed more exaggerated problem behaviors compared to their purebred parents.

## Problems Reported by Owners

Some owners shared personal experiences with problems associated with their dog's breed, including:

* In 55% of cases where one parent would display some behavioral issues, the offspring were likely to exhibit a more severe manifestation.
* Difficulty training and leaving dogs alone were common complaints in over half of the study group.

## Sources and Risks

The RVC noted that the "Doodle" phenomenon is often attributed to characteristics such as high energy, mental resilience, and intelligence gained from their respective parent breeds. However, Dr. Rowena Packer emphasized that these traits can sometimes be detrimental if not properly nurtured.

"Precausal misconceptions about designer dogs being easy to train or safe with children can have serious consequences if they aren't supported by evidence," she warned in a statement.

## Conclusion

The RVC researchers' study reveals that designer dog breeds, like their purebred counterparts, are capable of displaying undesirable behaviors. As such, owners and prospective pet owners must be aware of these risks, allowing them to approach caring for a new animal with accurate understanding and preparation.

* The Royal Veterinary College has emphasized the importance of consulting a veterinarian before bringing a dog into one's life.
