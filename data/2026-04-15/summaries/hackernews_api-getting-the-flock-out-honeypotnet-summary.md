# Getting the Flock out

## User request
- Resident of California asked Flock Safety to delete all personal data about the user, their vehicle, and household members, citing the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).  
- Provided name and address in the request.

## Flock Safety response
- Stated the request cannot be completed because Flock acts as a data processor for its customers, not the data controller.  
- Advised the user to contact the organization that hired Flock for data handling.  
- Included a summary of their privacy practices:
  - **Customer contracts**: Data processing is governed by contracts with customers, who own the data and set usage limits.  
  - **No sale of data**: Flock may not sell, publish, or exchange the data; it processes only per customer instructions.  
  - **Information collected**: License Plate Readers capture only publicly visible vehicle characteristics, not names or addresses.  
  - **Purpose**: Data is used for security, public safety, crime solving, and providing objective evidence.  
  - **Retention**: Default retention is 30 days with automatic hard deletion; customers can extend retention to meet local laws or policies.  
- Directed the user to the company’s Privacy Policy and LPR Policy for more details.
