## Ironies of Automation
- Drawing on Lisanne Bainbridge’s 1983 “Ironies of Automation,” the author connects classic automation pitfalls to modern ML systems.
- **Deskilling:** Automation reduces operators’ practice of core skills, causing skill degradation. Software engineers report diminished coding ability after relying on code‑generation models; designers feel less creative; doctors using AI tools become poorer at detecting conditions.
- **Automation Bias:** Humans tend to trust automated outputs, allowing AI suggestions to sway judgments (e.g., radiologists misled by AI mammography).
- **Monitoring Fatigue:** Humans struggle to supervise fast, accurate automated processes, leading to reduced vigilance and missed errors. This explains phenomena such as journalists publishing fabricated LLM quotes and leaders overlooking failures in autonomous vehicle programs.
