# Thousands of rare concert recordings are landing on the Internet Archive — listen now

## Overview
- Chicago music enthusiast Aadam Jacobs has recorded concerts since the 1980s, accumulating over 10,000 cassette tapes.
- Concerned about tape degradation, he partnered with volunteers from the nonprofit Internet Archive to digitize the collection.

## Progress
- Approximately 2,500 tapes have been uploaded to the Internet Archive so far.
- The digital archive includes rare performances such as Nirvana (1989), early recordings from Sonic Youth, R.E.M., Phish, Liz Phair, Pavement, Neutral Milk Hotel, and numerous punk bands.

## Volunteer Process
- Volunteer Brian Emerick visits Jacobs’ home monthly to collect boxes of tapes.
- He uses vintage cassette decks to play the tapes, converting them into digital files.
- Additional volunteers clean up audio, organize files, label recordings, and research song titles for obscure tracks.

## Audio Quality
- Although Jacobs originally used modest recording equipment, the volunteer audio engineers have enhanced the sound quality of the digitized files.

## Notable Finds
- A 1988 Tracy Chapman recording and other previously unknown live tracks have been recovered and made publicly accessible.
