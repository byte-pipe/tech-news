# About 250 missing after boat carrying Rohin​ya refugees capsizes in Andaman Sea

## Key facts
- A trawler that left Teknaf, southern Bangladesh on 4 April capsized in the Andaman Sea, leaving about 250 people missing.
- The vessel was reportedly overloaded with roughly 280 men, women and children and sank due to heavy winds, rough seas and overcrowding.
- The Bangladesh Coast Guard rescued nine survivors on 9 April; one survivor described being burned by oil and floating for nearly 36 hours before rescue.

## Background
- The passengers were likely fleeing the overcrowded Rohingya camps in Cox’s Bazar, where more than a million refugees live after being displaced from Myanmar’s Rakhine State.
- Rohingya families regularly attempt dangerous sea journeys to Malaysia, Indonesia or Thailand to escape persecution and camp conditions.
- The 2017 Myanmar military offensive forced at least 730,000 Rohingya into Bangladesh and was later judged by a UN fact‑finding mission to involve genocidal acts.

## Rescue and aftermath
- The Bangladeshi flag carrier MT Meghna Pride, en route to Indonesia, spotted people drifting on drums and logs and rescued nine individuals, including one woman.
- Survivors reported that traffickers had lured them with promises of jobs in Malaysia and that some passengers died while confined in a holding area on the boat.

## International response
- UNHCR and the International Organization for Migration called on the global community to increase and sustain funding for life‑saving assistance to Rohingya refugees in Bangladesh and to support Bangladeshi host communities.
- The incident highlights the ongoing human cost of protracted displacement and the lack of durable solutions for the Rohingya population.
