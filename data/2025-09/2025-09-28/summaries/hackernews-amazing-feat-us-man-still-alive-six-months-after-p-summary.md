# ‘Amazing feat’: US man still alive six months after pig kidney transplant

**US Man Receives Kidney from Genetically Modified Pig, Still Alive Six Months Later**

A 67-year-old US man is still alive six months after receiving a genetically modified pig kidney. This remarkable case marks the longest surviving of a genetically modified pig organ in a human.

**Key Facts:**

* Tim Andrews received a kidney from eGenesis with three levels of genetic modification (antigen elimination, human gene addition, and viral deactivation) on compassionate grounds.
* The recipient, Andrews, had been receiving dialysis for more than two years due to end-stage kidney disease.
* He has been dialysis-free since receiving the kidney.
* Three patients are now alive six months after transplant.

**Implications:**

* This achievement demonstrates successful xenotransplantation (organ transplantation from one species to another).
* The use of genetically modified pig kidneys may accelerate development and improve outcomes in future transplants.
* Transplant surgeons, like Wayne Hawthorne at the University of Sydney, are optimistic about long-term outcomes for patients undergoing this procedure.
