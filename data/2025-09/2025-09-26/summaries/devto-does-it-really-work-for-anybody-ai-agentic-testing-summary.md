# Does it really work for anybody, AI agentic testing, or automation - DEV Community

Here is a concise and informative summary of the article:

**Does Aggenting Testing Replace Human Test Engineers?**

* The author has extensive experience in testing, automation, and system setup, having led numerous successful projects.
* They have worked with multiple companies to improve their test cases and approach.

**Challenges with Current Aggenting Providers:**

* Many Agnetic providers deliver flaky tests (500/1000) after just a few months,
* Most demos sites and ads focus on login or user registration setup without real testing capabilities.
* Common examples include Playwright, which may generate easy-to-run test scripts but lacks depth.

**Case Study:**
The author had the opportunity to assess several Aggenting providers, including those that claim to deliver excellent test coverage with minimal human interaction. However, they found that:

* The demos and websites focused on simple user registration and login scenarios.
* The code generated for these tests was often easy to understand but lacked depth.

**Conclusion:**
While some providers may deliver relatively successful results, the author's experience suggests that human test engineers are still essential for quality assurance. Aggenting testing tools are not a replacement for humans; their capabilities need to be evaluated in context with human expertise and judgment.
