# Apple under Ternus: what comes next for the tech giant’s hardware strategy

## Leadership transition
- John Ternus will assume the role of CEO later this year, succeeding Tim Cook.  
- Tim Cook grew Apple into a $4 trillion company with a strong services segment; Ternus brings a hardware‑focused background.

## Ternus’ background
- Joined Apple in 2001 and advanced through hardware engineering ranks.  
- Key contributions include AirPods, the Apple Watch, and Vision Pro.  
- Demonstrated early interest in robotics, having built a head‑controlled feeding arm for quadriplegics while in college.

## Shift toward hardware‑centric AI
- Strategy may prioritize AI‑enabled devices—handheld, wearables, and home products—over competing directly with large AI model developers.  
- Rumored upcoming products: smart glasses, a camera‑equipped wearable pendant, and AirPods with AI features, all expected to integrate with the iPhone and rely on Siri.

## Pending hardware initiatives
- Foldable iPhone, long‑rumored, is slated for a September launch under Ternus’s oversight.  
- Apple is exploring home robotics, including a tabletop unit with a robotic arm attached to a display, and mobile robots that could follow users or serve as moving FaceTime screens.  
- Experiments with humanoid robots are mentioned, though they remain years away.

## External challenges
- Ongoing memory‑chip shortages.  
- Fluctuating U.S. tariff policies under President Trump.  
- Heavy reliance on Chinese manufacturing (≈80 % of iPhones) with a recent shift toward India, now producing about 25 % of iPhones.
