# Work with the garage door up – Summary

## Main ideas
- **“Garage‑door‑up” communication**: sharing unfinished work, process, and thoughts publicly (e.g., livestreams, “Screenshot Saturday”, shower‑lecture style) rather than only announcing finished products.  
- **Benefits**:  
  - Builds deeper, more invested audiences over time.  
  - Encourages serendipitous inbound opportunities for niche, personally meaningful projects.  
  - Avoids the pitfalls of formal pitching; it’s simply daily work visibility.  
- **Maggie Appleton’s observation**: Public learning (digital gardening, podcasting, streaming) makes others assume higher competence, leading to invitations to exclusive, high‑achieving circles—even when the creator feels undeserving.  
- **Physical‑business analogy**: Open‑door workshops (glassblowing studio, woodworking shop) act as “signs” that announce presence and activity, enriching public space.  
- **Social‑media contrast**: Online platforms lack a comparable “open‑door” signal; silence leads to disappearance, while constant chatter dominates attention, creating a selection bias.  
- **Vision of a balanced view**: If one could see past the noisy bias, the internet would resemble a city where most people are quietly working, with only a small, noisy café representing the visible chatter.
