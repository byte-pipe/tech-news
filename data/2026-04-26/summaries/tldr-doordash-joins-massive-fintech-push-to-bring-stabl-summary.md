# DoorDash joins massive fintech push to bring stablecoin payouts to merchants

## Overview
- DoorDash is partnering with Stripe‑backed blockchain firm Tempo to introduce stablecoin‑based payouts across its global marketplace.  
- The initiative targets cross‑border merchant payments where speed and cost are critical.

## Key Participants
- **DoorDash** – operating in over 40 countries, generated nearly $75 billion in merchant sales last year.  
- **Stripe** – payments giant processing close to $2 trillion annually, co‑developer of Tempo.  
- **Coastal Bank** and **ARQ** (Latin American fintech) – also moving parts of their payment operations onto stablecoin rails with Tempo.  
- **Paradigm** – venture firm collaborating with Stripe on Tempo’s development.

## DoorDash Plan
- Roll out stablecoin‑powered payouts for merchants, beginning with international transactions.  
- Exact launch timing has not been disclosed by Paradigm.

## Stripe’s Strategy
- Uses Tempo as a core layer for its money‑movement products, enabling businesses to send, receive, and hold stablecoins alongside fiat.  
- Aims to make global payments “fast, cheap and borderless.”  
- Recent acquisitions: Bridge (stablecoin infrastructure) for $1.1 billion (2024) and crypto wallet provider Privy.  

## Stablecoin Market Context
- Stablecoins represent a $300 billion crypto asset class, pegged to fiat currencies.  
- They promise cheaper and faster alternatives to traditional banking rails, especially for cross‑border flows.

## Tempo Blockchain Features
- Designed specifically for payment workloads.  
- Offers sub‑second settlement, fixed fees, and private transaction channels for enterprise users.  
- Contrasts with general‑purpose blockchains that can suffer congestion and variable costs.  
- Infrastructure partners include Mastercard, UBS, Klarna, and Visa.

## Support Services
- Tempo announced a Stablecoin Advisory service to assist firms in migrating payment flows onto the blockchain.

## Significance
- The collaboration signals mainstream adoption of blockchain‑based money within established financial infrastructure, expanding the reach of stablecoins beyond niche crypto markets.
