# Build in Public: Week 1 - DEV Community

**Build in Public: Week 1 - DEV Community**

The first week of "Dev Community" was uneventful, primarily focused on transitioning from an older N8N (Node 8 Natively) setup to a normal backend. However, this process also brought the first team conflict and real issue with Bright Data.

A significant surprise occurred when the first post – "Build in Public: Day Zero" – received more attention than expected, even reaching Dev.to's Top 7 of the Week category. Several individuals expressed interest in starting their own Build-in-Public projects on LinkedIn and Twitter.

For team members, this outcome is considered positive as it encourages collaboration and creativity. If you're reading this, consider building something too.

**Backend Challenges**

The development journey has been complicated by a tech conflict between two team members: one with data engineering background (Python) and the other as a full-stack developer (Node.js). Despite efforts, they disagree on choosing Python, leading to a potential open-source dispute.

In the meantime, the team is focusing on Instagram as their primary backend. Bright Data's Instagram API scrapers were selected for performance reasons, but the choice between synchronous and asynchronous modes remains unclear.

**Instagram Profile Analysis**

Bright Data provides two alternative approaches: Synchronous (real-time) and Asynchronous. The developer has chosen the latter with OpenRouter (anthropic/claude-3.5-sonnet). For now, engagement rates, related accounts, and recent posts are accessible, although the actual analysis logic is still being refined using Pydantic AI.

**Conclusion**

The development process for "Dev Community" Week 1 has been marked by challenges, including a significant surprise post reaching high visibility. The team has shown dedication to collaboration, acknowledging that this outcome offers an opportunity to explore new ideas and build something together, potentially benefiting others in the community.
