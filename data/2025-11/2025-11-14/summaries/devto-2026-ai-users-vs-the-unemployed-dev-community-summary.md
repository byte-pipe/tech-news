# "2026", AI Users vs The Unemployed. - DEV Community

**The Emergence of AI-Generated Code: What This Means for Careers in Technology**

By 2026, it is predicted that ninety percent of all code will be generated by artificial intelligence (AI). This statistic marks a significant shift in the way developers approach their work, from writing entire applications to augmenting coding skills.

**Current State of Development**

According to recent industry data, over 95% of developers admit to using AI-generated code. Many are now recognizing that using these tools is not just a means to simplify tasks but an integral part of their workflow.

**The Effects on the Job Market and Supply**

Companies are struggling to find developers who can write code effectively due to a shortage of skilled professionals. Additionally, job market demand has decreased as companies opt for AI-optimized solutions to streamline operations.

**Understanding the Reality Behind the Numbers**

AI-generated code has various forms, such as:

* Code completion: 30-40% of what you type is autocompleted by AI.
* Boilerplate and scaffolding: Instantaneous assistance in setting up new projects or basic CRUD operations.
* Bug fixes and refactoring suggestions: AI-driven improvements are suggested after writing code.

**The Challenge to Self-Education**

Developers have been trained from a young age to approach development through these tools, leading them to rely more on AI for critical tasks. This highlights the need for developers to recognize their limitations when approaching coding issues and require support from AI tools.

In conclusion, the integration of AI-generated code into our daily coding processes presents both opportunities and challenges. While it may lead to increased efficiency, it shifts the responsibility towards AI, potentially reducing demand for human skills. As we adapt, it is essential to redefine the notion of development and maintain a balanced understanding between technology-assisted tools and self-sufficient coding expertise.
