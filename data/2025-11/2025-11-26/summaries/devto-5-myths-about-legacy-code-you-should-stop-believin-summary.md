## Debunking 5 Common Myths About Legacy Code:

### Myth 1: "Legacy means ancient or exotic."

* Legacy doesn't necessarily mean mysterious or hard-to-use technology.
* Sometimes, it's just outdated or unused code.

### Myth 2: "Legacy is the previous team's fault."

* Blaming people for legacy issues can be seen as easy and uncritical.
* Understanding context is often more productive than blame.

### Myth 3: "Migrating is just a dev obsession."

* Legacy migrations are not solely for developers' satisfaction; they require business consideration.
* Migrations involve risks, such as security and performance issues.

8,4. Myth 4: "A big-bang rewrite is always an antipattern."

* Big-bang rewrites may be cheaper or faster than multiple phases.
* However, they can lead to complex migration problems if not done right.

10. Myth 5: "The strangler pattern is the only correct approach."

* The strangler and big-bang patterns have trade-offs.
* Choosing a hybrid approach (strangler + regular refactoring) may be more effective for projects.

**Discussion Points:**

* Developing better understanding of legacy code can help developers overcome common challenges.
* It's essential to consider risk management when migrating old code.
* Context and business considerations should guide the migration process.
