# Don't get scammed on an interview. - DEV Community

## Don't Get Scammed on an Interview: Red Flags to Watch Out For

Whether it's your second "interview" or first impression, two interviews that turned out to be scams have taught us key red flags to watch out for.

*   **Initial Introduction**: The initial part of the interview should be friendly and informational. They will ask about your background and show you a project before asking for login credentials.
*   **Repository Requests**: Be cautious if they ask you to access or clone a repository. Look for any hidden files, suspicious pop-ups, or requests to install software.
*   **Project Collaboration**: If the conversation turns into collaboration on a project, it might be a phishing attempt.

## Key Red Flags to Watch Out For

Some common red flags include:

### Hidden Files and Pop-Ups
Look out for:
*   Pop-up windows or emails asking you to download files or enter sensitive information.
*   Invisible or modified system files.

### Suspicious Requests
Watch out for:
*   Requesting access to your development environment, login credentials, or sensitive resources.
