Contributors initially found it challenging to contribute due to the complexity and unfamiliarity with codebases. However, AI tools have significantly reduced this barrier, making it easier for maintainers to review contributions on their own.

**Reviewing Contributions with Copilot Code Review Tool**

The author sets up a Copilot code review tool in GitHub, which is configured to provide good catches of low-confidence issues (comments indicating "maybe" or "consider" feedback). Additionally, the tool's instructions guide contributors on what constitutes constructive criticism and how often it should be given.

**Review Philosophy and Priority Areas**

To encourage effective contribution, the author advises Copilot reviewers to focus on:

* Only providing confidence-in-high (>80%) that a problem exists
* Being concise (one sentence per comment)
* Providing actionable feedback instead of general observations
* Commenting on clarity issues only if they genuinely impede understanding or introduce errors

**Implementation and Effectiveness**

By implementing these guidelines, the tool's output is significantly reduced, making it easier for contributors to express their thoughts and improve project quality.
