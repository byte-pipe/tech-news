# How good engineers write bad code at big companies

**Why Bad Code Happens in Large Tech Companies**

Poor code quality is a common issue in large tech companies. Despite their high salaries and attractive work environments, engineers can still produce sloppy code due to several factors.

*   **Low Employee Turnover**: Many engineers only stay for a short period before switching jobs. This means they may not have enough time to understand and optimize the codebase or develop expertise in specific systems.
*   **Temporary Refreshments**: To retain employees, companies offer temporary yearly refreshes, but this process incentivizes some engineers to leave without feeling valued, particularly those from other companies who want the other half of their base salary.
*
  *   **Internal Mobility Concerns**
**Key Points:**

*   Most code changes are made by relative beginners
*   Experienced engineers are often overwhelmed and have limited time for deep reviews and involvement in decision-making processes
*   Long tenure or extensive experience can lead to a lack of investment in individual systems
