# Dear Junior Coders: Stop Chasing Shiny Objects - DEV Community

## Focus on Learning One Thing: Why Junior Coders Should Stop Chasing Shiny Objects

**Key Points**

- **Focus on fundamentals**: Instead of trying to learn many new things at once, focus on mastering a few key concepts.
- **Chase shallow learning over deep understanding**: Going down a rabbit hole of new shiny objects does not lead to deeper code writing skills.
- **Time tested approaches work**: Timeless principles such as SQL, HTTP, and C/C++ are still relevant today.
- **Soft skills matter more than coding speed**: Effective communication, negotiation, and problem-solving skills are crucial for success in the workforce.

**Original Perspective**

As a seasoned coder looking back on my own journey, I learned that the early days of learning code were similar to the IT/network guy's advice. While it's natural to be eager to learn new tools and techniques, a focus on mastering a few key concepts is often more effective.

However, the world of coding continues to evolve rapidly due to the emergence of new technologies like Python, PHP, and React. Even seasoned coders must stay up-to-date with changing trends and best practices.

**Summary**

- **Key concept**: Focus on learning one thing at a time.
- **Best practices**: Master timeless concepts such as SQL, HTTP, C/C++, data structures, design patterns, and clean code principles before moving on to new technologies.
- **Soft skills**: Effective communication, negotiation, and problem-solving are just as important as coding speed.
- **Lessons learned**: Time passed the test of time in many areas of coding; now it's up to junior coders to master fewer, more relevant concepts to remain competitive.
