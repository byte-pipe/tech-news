# Lawmakers Want to Ban VPNs—And They Have No Idea What They're Doing | Electronic Frontier Foundation

Here is a concise and informative summary of the article:

**Title:** Lawmakers Want to Ban VPNs – And They Don't Understand How They Work

**Key Points:**

* Wisconsin lawmakers have proposed an age verification bill that would force websites to block access by users using Virtual Private Networks (VPNs)
* The bill seeks to expand the definition of "harmful to minors" and also blocks VPN connections
* This is another example of lawmakers expanding a definition of "harmful to minors" to censor diverse content, including educational materials and activist resources

**Why It's A Terrible Idea:**

*VPNs mask users' real locations by routing internet traffic through servers in other places**
* Websites cannot determine if VPN connections are coming from specific locations (e.g. Milwaukee or Miami) because of this technical limitation
* This proposal would leave websites with no choice but to either block VPN users worldwide or cease operations in Wisconsin due to potential legal liability

**Unintended Consequences:**

* The provision could lead to widespread censorship and harm to free speech, as well as an uneven application of "harmful to minors" laws across different states
* This proposal would fail to address the real issues driving censorship efforts (e.g. protecting women's autonomy in reproduction and human anatomy) by targeting VPN users rather than addressing these broader concerns
