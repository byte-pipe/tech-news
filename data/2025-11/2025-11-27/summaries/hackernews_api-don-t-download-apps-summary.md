# Don't Download Apps

**Don't Download Apps in Taiwan: A Cautionary Tale**

In Taiwan, companies attempt to entice customers into downloading apps by offering discounts and enticing features. However, this practice has severe consequences.

**Never Hand Over Your Phone Over the Counter**

To avoid falling victim of such tactics, never give your phone over the counter when ordering from a shop staff. This is a second warning against downloading apps in Taiwan.

**Second Warning: Don't Download Apps**

Two primary concerns arise:

1. **Surveillance Capitalism**: Companies use data to charge higher prices based on your actions, creating an unfair economy where individuals are penalized for not buying more products.
2. **Binding Arbitration**: When you download and use an app, you're bound by the terms of service, which often include a clause for binding arbitration. This means that companies may avoid paying damages if they feel they can settle disputes privately.

**Common App Tactics**

Companies often employ tactics like:

*   Installing local e-commerce apps on your phone to collect data.
*   Using your new phone number and login details as login credentials.
*   Requesting your IMEI or biometric information.
*   Encouraging you to download and use their app for discounts, free shipping, or other incentives.
*   Demanding payment information, including credit card details.

**What to Do**

To avoid falling prey to these scams:

*   Be cautious of any offer that requires personal data or financial information.
*   Verify the legitimacy of any company before downloading an app.
*   Don't download apps without reading and understanding their terms and conditions.
*   Research the company's reputation online and look for reviews from other customers.

By being aware of these tactics, you can protect yourself from potential financial losses and maintain control over your personal data.
