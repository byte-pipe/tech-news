# The EU made Apple adopt new Wi-Fi standards, and now Android can support AirDrop - Ars Technica

# Improved Interoperability for Quick Share on Android Devices
Google has announced support for Apple's AirDrop feature in its latest update, allowing users to share files directly between their Apple devices and Android devices using a local peer-to-peer Wi-Fi connection.

## Key Features of the New Feature

*   Apple devices with AirDrop enabled will appear in the Quick Share device list on Android devices, similar to how they would be listed for Google Drive or other cloud-based services.
*   For users with the "Everyone" permission setting on their iPhone, Apple devices will show up in this list alongside other compatible devices.
*   The feature is only available on the Pixel 10 series and is expected to expand to more Android devices in the future.

## Security Enhancements

Google notes that files shared via Quick Share are transferred directly between devices without being sent to either company's servers, making security a significant advantage. This is due to the use of memory-safe Rust programming language by Android's compiler, which enforces strict ownership and borrowing rules at compile time.

Apple's Continuity features rely on wireless communication between devices using Bluetooth and fast peer-to-peer Wi-Fi connections, making this new feature a significant improvement in compatibility and integration with Apple devices.
