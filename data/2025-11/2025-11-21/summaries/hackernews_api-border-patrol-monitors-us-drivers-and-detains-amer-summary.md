# Border Patrol monitors US drivers and detains Americans for ‘suspicious’ travel | AP News

**Border Patrol's Secretive Program Identifies Suspicious Travelers in Millions of Drivers**

* **Program Details**: The US Border Patrol has launched a predictive intelligence program that monitors millions of American drivers nationwide. This program uses cameras and an algorithm to identify suspicious travel patterns.
* **How it Works**: Vehicle license plate information is captured, and the vehicle's route is tracked. If the routes and characteristics don't match expected patterns, the driver may be flagged for review by federal agents, leading to stops, searches, or arrests.
* **Increased Surveillance**: The program has grown significantly over the past five years as Border Patrol agencies have collaborated with other organizations, including private companies using license plate readers nationwide and local law enforcement programs funded through federal grants.
* **Expansion Beyond National Borders**: This surveillance system now extends into the country's interior, monitoring daily actions and connections of ordinary Americans for anomalies.
* **Concerns Raised**: Some individuals may be stopped or questioned unnecessarily, without being suspected of any wrongdoing. The lack of clear boundaries between policing national borders and monitoring daily life has raised concerns among civil liberties groups.

**Surveillance Expansion Under Trump Administration**

* The program is part of an effort by the US Customs and Border Protection to shift its focus from immigration enforcement to domestic intelligence gathering.
* As a result, there are plans to invest in more advanced border surveillance systems, including artificial intelligence and facial recognition technology.
* This expansion of surveillance has sparked criticism from civil liberties groups, who argue that it infringes on personal freedoms.
