Europe Scales Back Landmark Privacy and AI Laws Amid Big Tech Pressure

The European Union is revising its landmark General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and regulating artificial intelligence (AI) in a bid to alleviate red tape and stimulate economic growth, while also responding to pressure from big tech companies.

* Key Changes:
    * Simplifying EU cookie pop-ups for small businesses
    * Relaxing or delaying AI rules on high-risk systems
    * Revising the grace period for critical AI systems posing health, safety, or fundamental rights risks
    * Reducing requirements for authenticating personal data with AI models

* Policy Objectives:
    * Simplifying EU laws to reduce regulatory burden and innovation stifling effect
    * Providing space for start-ups, small businesses, and tech entrepreneurs in the EU market
    * Ensuring fundamental user rights are protected while embracing new technologies
