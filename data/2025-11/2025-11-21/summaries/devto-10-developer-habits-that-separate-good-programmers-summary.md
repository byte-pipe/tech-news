# 10 Developer Habits That Separate Good Programmers From Great Ones - DEV Community

## 10 Developer Habits That Separate Good Programmers From Great Ones

To become an exceptional programmer, one must cultivate habits that set them apart from their peers. While having knowledge or expertise in areas like algorithms or syntax can distinguish good programers from great ones, it is not the sole determining factor.

The key lies in daily practices and habits that are easy to implement but challenging to master. These habits will shape how engineers work and interact with each other on a project-by-project basis for years to come. Here are ten habits that will help you become the kind of developer others want on their team:

## Habit 1: They Read Code Far More Than They Write It

The amount of time spent reading other people's code compared to writing one's own depends greatly on its significance. To differentiate oneself from average programmers, reading through documentation and libraries extensively is essential but not sufficient.

## Habit 2: Prioritize Debugging Over Feature Addition

Understanding the intricacies of debugging tools, techniques, and methodologies is vital for any developer. Instead of immediately moving forward to add features, take your time to thoroughly debug anything that doesn't work as intended. Take it a step at a time and test every hypothesis carefully.

## Habit 3: Write Commented Code to Explain Your Thought Process

Including comments when writing code helps in explaining the rationale behind particular choices or decisions made during implementation. Effective commenting can facilitate better teamwork, self-reflection, and understanding among developers about how their work contributes to overall project goals.

## Habit 4: Join a Community of Developers Early on

Engaging with early days of your career is crucial for learning and development. Being part of such communities helps set priorities for skills acquisition from the beginning and often fosters camaraderie among fellow aspiring developers which keeps learning motivated despite tough times.

## Habit 5: Focus on Learning Over Perfectionism

Perfection in coding may delay progress, but it will hinder the flow of a team's productivity. Keep an open mind that you might make mistakes at first - however small they should be. Sometimes these are necessary in order to correct errors; learn from each mistake and get even better.

## Habit 6: Stay Organized

A organized workspace significantly improves the time spent debugging code as well, as well as allowing for quicker completion of tasks on a project, such as writing unit tests or analyzing error logs. Keeping everything easily accessible means minimizing confusion or frustration during development sessions.

## Habit 7: Write Tests First and Then Code

While it is tempting to dive right into implementing code before the actual logic is written in tests, keeping these separate shows true commitment to quality checks. Tests ensure that your software behaves as intended before the actual implementation makes any of its adjustments for runtime behavior - making development a more efficient task overall.

## Habit 8: Set Realistic Goals and Priorities

Every developer should start with simple goals such as fixing existing bugs or reducing time devoted to debugging; setting short-term objectives is much easier that aiming for complete satisfaction in your code when it first comes into being – it lets individuals have realistic targets where everyone can see progress, knowing how improvements impact the project overall.

## Habit 9: Learn and Refine from Mistakes

Everyone makes mistakes. Even professional programmers do not avoid them all time. However learning from them allows for improvement and growth. Identifying common pitfalls or errors often provides valuable lessons that are especially relevant in the context of specific programming jobs where different scenarios may arise.

## Habit 10: Be Willing to Ask Questions

The best way to achieve excellence is through continuous practice, patience, persistence and willingness to learn. No one can do the job alone, be willing to seek others expertise when you encounter any problem or a challenging piece of work.
