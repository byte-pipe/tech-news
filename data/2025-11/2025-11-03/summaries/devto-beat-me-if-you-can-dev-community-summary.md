# Beat Me If You Can - DEV Community

### Liquidcode: Where Functionality Meets Creativity

Liquidcode is a platform that challenges developers to prove their skills by building real projects within a short timeframe. The current stage involves the Markdown Editor challenge, where contestants can showcase their expertise in building user-friendly tools like markdown editors with headings, lists, bold, and other standard formatting features.

#### Marked with Creativity

The goal of this challenge is not just about coding; it's about innovation, creativity, and execution. Contestants have two weeks to build a functional markdown editor using React. The platform offers a range of possible approaches, from the clean interface of the Classic Approach to the distraction-free experience of the Medium Clone.

#### A Fight for the Founder Badge

The competition is open to anyone, with only one person having the chance to earn the Founder Badge: the permanent early adopter status and access to free features and lifetime perks. The winner's badge will be revealed after a community-driven voting process, where users can engage in discussions and share their thoughts on how to improve the platform.

#### Building Something Special

The key to success lies not just in achieving the desired milestone but also in showcasing one's skills through creative problem-solving and project execution. Whether it's solving real-world problems or pushing existing ideas forward, the spirit of innovation is what sets Liquidcode apart from other platforms.

### What Sets Liquidcode Apart

- **Unconventional Approach**: The use of React as a framework allows for dynamic and responsive solutions that can adapt to different user needs.
- **Community Engagement**: Contestants have the opportunity to showcase their work within a public environment, fostering engagement with users and setting the bar for future challenges.
- **Founders' Perspective**: Liquidcode's founder has expressed interest in seeing how developers can solve real-world problems using React.
