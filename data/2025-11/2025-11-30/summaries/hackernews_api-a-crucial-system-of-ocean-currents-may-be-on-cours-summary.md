# A crucial system of ocean currents may be on course to collapse. This country just declared it a national security threat | CNN

**The Importance of Global Ocean Currents for Iceland's Climate Security**

Iceland is located about 200 miles east of the capital Reykjavík, and its climate is significantly influenced by a network of ocean currents. Without these currents, the island would become much cooler and stormier, posing an exceptional threat to its environment and society.

**The Crucial Ocean Currents: The Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC)**

The Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation is a complex system of ocean currents that works like a giant conveyor belt, transporting warm water from the Southern Hemisphere and tropics to the Northern Hemisphere. As scientists have long recognized, this circulation plays a crucial role in regulating Iceland's climate.

**Risks Associated with Loss of Global Ocean Currents**

If the AMOC were to collapse, significant disruptions are likely:

*   *Extreme Weather*: The loss of the AMOC could lead to catastrophic changes in global weather patterns, including:
    *   Rising sea levels in parts of North America and Europe
    *   Disrupted monsoon systems in Asia and Africa
    *   A winter deep freeze in Europe with potential snowdrifts covering large areas
*   *Global Impacts*: The collapse could have far-reaching consequences globally, affecting various ecosystems and human societies:

    *   Coastal flooding and erosion
    *   Mass migration of species
    *   Disruption to global food chains
