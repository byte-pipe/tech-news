# Don't get scammed on an interview. - DEV Community

Here is a concise and informative summary of the article:

**Don't Fall for Scams on Interviews**

Two recent interviews, one through "scams", taught author how to spot fake ones. A third scammer was observed in the background.

**Key Takeaways:**

* Be cautious when receiving proposals from unknown individuals or companies.
* Don't provide access to your device without proper validation and verification steps.
* Verify repositories and software with reputable tools such as GitHub Codespaces, codesandbox with Copilot, or self-hosting full IDEs like VSCode.
* Use real company names and credentials (e.g., vscode.dev) instead of fake ones.

**Red Flags: Hidden in Plain Sight**

* Sudden demands for access to your device
* Fake demos or projects that seem too good to be true
* Requests for sensitive information (e.g., passwords, credentials)
* Unfamiliar apps or software

**How to Protect Yourself and Others**

1. Isolate new technologies and tools on separate devices before integrating them into production.
2. Use secure repositories with GitHub Codespaces or codesandbox with Copilot.
3. Verify company names and credentials instead of relying solely on intuition or experience.

Author has 10 years of experience interviewing at real companies, working on multiple projects as a remote contractor.
