# Rebble · Core Devices Keeps Stealing Our Work

**Core Devices Keeps Stealing Our Work: A Community Mourns the End of Pebble Support**

The Pebble community is reeling after learning that Core Devices, a company owned by Eric Migicovsky's former owner, David Kircus, wants to abandon their partnership with Rebble. This development comes as a shock, given the significant work that Rebble has done to maintain and improve the Pebble experience over the past decade.

**What Changed?**

Nine years ago, Pebble Technology Corporation went out of business, leaving behind hundreds of thousands of smartwatches still waiting for support from Rebelle (now known as Core Devices). In response, a dedicated community effort saved the data stored on the Pebble app store.

The result was a replaced app store API developed by Rebelle, as well as a new storage backend and dev portal created to manage the data. The data has been extensively cared for over the years, with hundreds of thousands of dollars spent hosting and maintaining it.

**The Current State of Things**

The core devices keeps stealing our work: a phrase that reflects the community's disappointment in seeing their work being taken by Corporate overlords. Core Devices' latest move - demanding access to Rebelle's entire development history from nine years ago – is seen as an attempt to undermine the partnership and take control of the Pebble experience.

**The Future of Pebble Support**

For now, the future of Pebble support looks grim. With Core Devices taking over data management responsibilities, it remains uncertain what will happen next. This comes at a time when Rebelle's dev Portal was just rebuilt in near decade-long to accommodate this change and rebuilt from the ground up.

**What Can the Community Do?**

While this seems like an impasse for the community, it also opens a window of opportunity for new beginnings. By pooling their collective knowledge and efforts again, they can create a system that can better support future Pebble devices and ensure that users continue to benefit from Rebelle's expertise.

The incident reminds us that even with dedicated volunteers there is no such thing as zero risk or zero impact on the outcome in our community, it's all about community leadership & collaboration.
