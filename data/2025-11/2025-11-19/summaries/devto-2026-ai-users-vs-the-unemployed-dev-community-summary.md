# "2026", AI Users vs The Unemployed. - DEV Community

**The Rise of AI-Generated Code: A Concern for Developers**

A recent statistic suggests that by 2026, ninety percent of all code will be predicted to be AI-generated. This trend is not speculation anymore; it is happening in real time. In 2024, developers wrote 256 billion lines of code, with a projected jump to 600 billion lines by 2025.

The implications of this shift are significant. For those who understand how development works, the numbers may seem overwhelming. However, the reality is that AI-generated code is becoming increasingly indistinguishable from human-written code. In fact, recent job postings indicate that many developers are now applying for jobs to get callbacks merely because they are using AI-generated code.

**The Numbers That Should Scare You**

According to industry data, over 95% of developers admit to using AI-generated code regularly. This may not be due to a lack of effort on the developer's part but rather an increase in adoption driven by the ease and effectiveness of AI tools. In response, job openings are down significantly. Companies are struggling to find human Developers who can handle tasks that AI will take over.

**What This Actually Means (Beyond the Hype)**

While 90% AI-generated code may seem like a blessing, it is crucial to recognize its implications for developers and the industry as a whole. The rapid rise of AI-generated code suggests that senior developers are becoming redundant. Instead, senior developers are working under the guidance of junior developers who do most of the "invisible" tasks.

This phenomenon is closely tied to how we come to be developers rather than simply being trained by them. Humans need to understand AI tools such as GitHub Copilot and ChatGPT to utilize their features effectively. The pressure from tech companies demanding more human involvement may exacerbate this issue, making it difficult for some individuals to adapt.

The future of development lies at the intersection of technology and human ingenuity. While AI-generated code has the potential to streamline many tasks, it requires a deeper understanding of how these tools work and their limitations. As developers, we must be aware of both the benefits and drawbacks of AI-generated code on our profession's role.
