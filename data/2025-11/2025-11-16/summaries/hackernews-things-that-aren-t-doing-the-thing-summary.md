# Things that aren't doing the thing

## Understanding What Is Not Doing the Thing

Preparing and making plans to do something are two different actions from achieving it. Similarly, taking concrete steps like creating a list, communicating with others, and staying motivated can feel as though they're not accomplishing anything.

Engaging in conversations about your intentions or even attempting to overcome obstacles often does little to achieve a goal. The more you dwell on what hasn't happened yet or what could go wrong, the less likely it is that it will be accomplished.

The act of reading about someone else's experiences with the thing, understanding their struggles, and gaining insights can sometimes hinder rather than help in achieving your own desired outcome.

The most straightforward action is taking the action itself.
