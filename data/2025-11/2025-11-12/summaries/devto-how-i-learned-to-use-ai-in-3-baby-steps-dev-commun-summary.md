# How I learned to “use AI” in 3 baby steps - DEV Community

Here's a concise and informative summary of the article:

**Coping with AI Fluency: A 3-Step Guide**

AI fluency refers to being able to effectively utilize artificial intelligence (AI) tools in personal and professional contexts. While many individuals worry about falling behind due to increased adoption, coders need not be intimidated. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you "use AI" and achieve fluency:

**Step 1: Establish a connection with your IDE**

Set up Cursor or Copilot in your Integrated Development Environment (IDE) without making any special adjustments. This helps you discover its value and fine-tune it for future use.

**Step 2: Learn to use inline editing**

Hover over red squiggly lines, highlight problematic code, and choose "Quick Edit" or "Fix with AI". When it suggests an edit that fixes your issue, accept the change. If not, revert to previous versions.

**Step 3: Leverage chat agents**

Utilize chat agents like Cursor when stuck on coding tasks. Ask for fixes or explanations, providing context to help the agent understand your code and its intended behavior. Review their edits with caution (around 20%).

By following these three steps, you'll be well on your way to becoming more comfortable with AI tools. Remember to stay curious, experiment, and practice using chat agents effectively in conjunction with your existing workflow.

**Additional tips:**

* Consider taking a course or training program to improve your chat agent skills.
* Familiarize yourself with other AI-powered tools and their strengths/weaknesses.
* Continuously evaluate and refine your coding practices to integrate AI fluency more seamlessly.
