# Meta's Chief AI Scientist Yann LeCun To Depart And Launch AI Start-Up Focused On 'World Models'

Meta has announced that its chief artificial intelligence scientist, Yann LeCun, plans to leave the company to launch his own AI start-up. This move comes amid an internal overhaul of Meta's AI strategy, with CEO Mark Zuckerberg shifting Meta's Fundamental AI Research Lab (FAIR) toward commercial AI products and large language models.

LeCun will focus on developing "world models," next-generation systems that can learn from visual and spatial data like humans do. These models aim to replicate human reasoning and understanding of the physical world. LeCun has confirmed he is initiating fundraising discussions for his new venture, which could be completed within a decade.

Meta's strategy shift has led to a significant change in AI leadership, with LeCun being replaced by Alexandr Wang, founder of Scale AI, who now leads Meta's Superintelligence division. This move allows LeCun to work on projects that focus on artificial intelligence and machine learning beyond his current role at Meta.

LeCun's departure signals both a philosophical shift within the company and a structural change in its AI program, with the emergence of a potential new rival in the race toward true artificial intelligence.
