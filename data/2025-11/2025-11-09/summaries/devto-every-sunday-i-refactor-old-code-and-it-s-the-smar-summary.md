# Every Sunday, I Refactor Old Code and It’s the Smartest Habit I’ve Ever Built - DEV Community

**Weekly Code Refactor Routine**
==============================

* Every Sunday, refactor old code without goal-oriented bug fixing or feature development.
* Start with a low commitment: spend no more than **2 hours**, even if in a productive state.

**Key Takeaways and Benefits**
------------------------------

#### 1. Clean-up Ritual
Refactors small improvements from past projects, improving readability and reducing clutter.

#### 2. Raw Progress and Honesty
Empties old code, removing unnecessary complexity, making progress feel authentic and rewarding.

#### 3. Small Wins and Long-term Benefits
Each tiny improvement represents a conversation with the former self; understanding growth through reflection.

#### 4. Readability Matters
Code readability is more important than novelty or cleverness when faced with obscure messiness.

**Lessons Learned Each Week**
------------------------------

* Readability trumps elegance in code complexity.
* Consistency beats complexity.
* Small refactors compound over time, making the process efficient and insightful.
* Old code from personal growth becomes a snapshot of individual progress.

**Challenges to Overcoming**

• **Fear of Perfection**: Stop after 2 hours to avoid burnout and reconnect with past self through code.
• **Lack of Structure**: Refuse tutorials or framework-led solutions; instead, aim for genuine learning from internal projects.
• **Difficulty in Applying This**: Reflect on why you want to continue this habit without making it into an external process.

**Inspirational Takeaways**
---------------------------

Don't chase new frameworks. Focus on fixing and improving what is working well.
Embrace the discomfort of not meeting perfection; look forward to connection with past selves through code.
