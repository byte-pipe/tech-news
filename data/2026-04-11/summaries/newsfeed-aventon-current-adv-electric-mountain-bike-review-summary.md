# Aventon Current ADV Electric Mountain Bike Review: Feels Just Like the Real Thing | WIRED

# Aventon Current ADV e‑MTB Review Summary

## Overview
- Price: $4,599 (Aventon)  
- Rating: 7/10 (WIRED)  
- Class 1 e‑mountain bike, switchable to Class 3 via app  
- Tested on dozens of trails over a week‑and‑a‑half

## Performance
- **Downhill:** Extremely stable; feels like riding on rails thanks to a long wheelbase and bike mass.  
- **Climbing:** Aventon Ultro X mid‑drive motor delivers up to 850 W and 120 Nm torque, handling the 300 lb bike and rider with ease.  
- **Battery:** 800 Wh, downtube‑mounted; claimed range up to 105 mi, but real‑world use (frequent mode changes) gave about 3 hours of riding with ~20 % battery drop.  
- **Power Modes:** Auto, Eco, Trail, Turbo, and a 30‑second Boost mode for extra torque on steep hills.

## Design & Components
- Frame: 6061 aluminum, full‑suspension (RockShox Psylo Gold front, 150 mm travel; RockShox Deluxe Select+ rear)  
- Wheels: 29‑inch double‑walled, tubeless‑ready, Maxxis Minion tires  
- Drivetrain: SRAM Eagle groupset  
- Dropper post: 170 mm X Fusion Manic  
- Saddle: Selle Royal SRX, highly comfortable  
- Electronics: Proprietary motor, but all other components are standard and serviceable; top‑tube touchscreen display shows speed, power mode, compass, airtime, etc.

## Pros
- Looks and rides like a conventional mountain bike despite being electric  
- High‑quality components for the price point  
- Very fun Boost mode for short bursts of extra power  
- Long wheelbase provides excellent stability on descents  
- Comfortable saddle and ergonomic design  

## Cons
- Identical beeps for each power‑mode make it hard to know the current setting without looking at the display  
- Top‑tube touchscreen requires the rider to look down, which can feel unsafe on technical terrain  
- Super‑long wheelbase (1,288 mm on XL) hampers tight turns, switchbacks, and technical sections compared to a typical analog bike (1,219 mm)  
- No handlebar‑mounted display option for safer glance‑away riding  

## Verdict
The Aventon Current ADV delivers a compelling mix of real‑mountain‑bike feel, strong motor performance, and premium components at a mid‑range price. While the display ergonomics and long wheelbase present minor drawbacks on technical trails, the overall package is highly enjoyable and stands out in its price category.
