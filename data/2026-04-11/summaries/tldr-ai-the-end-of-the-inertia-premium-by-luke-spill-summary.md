# AI: The End of the Inertia Premium - by Luke Spill

# AI: The End of the Inertia Premium – Summary

## Main Argument
- AI agents acting for consumers can eliminate the “inertia premium,” the hidden subsidy banks earn from customers who do not actively seek higher‑yield accounts or better credit‑card rewards.
- Removing this premium could compress net interest margins (NIM) and reduce overall banking profitability.

## The Inertia Premium Explained
- Traditional banks rely on net interest margin: interest earned on loans minus interest paid on deposits.
- Retail customers often keep money in low‑ or zero‑interest checking accounts, creating a margin boost for banks.
- AI agents can continuously compare rates and move funds, eroding this advantage.

## Size of the Problem
- US interest‑bearing checking accounts pay ~0.07%; savings accounts ~0.38%, while top online rates exceed 4% (≈10× difference).
- Non‑interest‑bearing demand‑deposit balances grew 28% CAGR (2021‑2026) vs. 3% for interest‑bearing equivalents.
- Globally, $23 trillion of $70 trillion consumer deposits sit in near‑zero‑rate checking accounts.
- If agents shift deposits to 80 % of top‑of‑market rates, McKinsey estimates a potential 10 % shrinkage of the $1.2 trillion global banking profit pool over 5‑10 years.

## Credit‑Card Inertia Parallel
- US credit‑card revenue $234 billion in 2024, heavily dependent on consumer inertia (unredeemed rewards, missed balance‑transfer offers).
- >20 % of cardholders did not redeem any rewards in 2024; 3‑5 % of rewards are forfeited annually.
- 75 % of revolving balances belong to prime consumers who could obtain cheaper alternatives but do not act.

## Precedent: Ant Financial’s Yu’e Bao
- Embedded a money‑market fund in Alipay (minimum ¥1) offering 3.96% vs. 0.35% bank deposits.
- Gained 150 million users and $93 billion AUM in 18 months; CAGR 125 % to become world’s largest money‑market fund by 2017.
- Demonstrated rapid capital migration when friction is removed.

## How AI Agents Can Optimize Customer Balances
- Sweep excess checking balances into high‑yield savings accounts and replenish as needed.
- Detect payment or investment opportunities via email/text/app alerts and act within pre‑authorized limits.
- On credit cards, route spending to the optimal rewards card, initiate better‑rate applications, and perform balance transfers before promotions expire.

## Misreading the Deposit Base in Current Models
- Existing NIM models assume a static, inertia‑driven deposit base.
- US banking NIM was 3.39 % in Q4 2025 (highest since 2019); future growth projected from NIM and non‑interest income.
- AI agents threaten both streams by automating rate comparisons and optimizing credit‑card balances.

## Strategic Responses for Banks
1. **Own the Agent Layer** – Develop cross‑institutional AI assistants (e.g., Bank of America’s Erica) to retain interaction control.
2. **Compete on Rates** – Offer deposit rates near market tops to stay visible to agent‑driven searches.
3. **Compete on Depth** – Leverage proprietary transaction data, licensing, and trust to provide superior credit decisions and advisory services where AI cannot easily replicate advantages.

## Durable Bank Advantages
- Regulatory licences
- Customer trust
- Proprietary data

These assets may sustain competitive edges in credit and advisory services, though their relevance to retail deposit gathering remains uncertain.
