# Battery recycler Ascend Elements files for bankruptcy – TechCrunch summary

## Bankruptcy filing
- Ascend Elements announced on Friday that it has entered Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings in the United States.  
- The filing follows nearly $900 million of investor capital that is now at risk.  
- CEO Linh Austin posted on LinkedIn that the company faced “insurmountable” financial challenges.

## Contributing factors
- A weakening U.S. electric‑vehicle (EV) market contributed to the company’s difficulties.  
- The Trump administration cancelled a $316 million federal grant for Ascend’s Kentucky plant; only $204 million had been disbursed, leaving a funding shortfall.  
- EV sales have stalled since the end of tax credits in September 2023, and automakers such as Volkswagen have reduced EV production plans.

## Company technology and facility
- Ascend developed a process to extract critical minerals from scrap and end‑of‑life batteries, aiming to reduce steps needed to turn shredded waste into precursor cathode materials.  
- The firm has been constructing a 1 million‑square‑foot recycling facility in Kentucky, which has faced lawsuits and construction delays.

## Industry context
- Battery‑related startups operate in a highly competitive market dominated by Chinese manufacturers with strong state support, driving down costs.  
- Other recyclers, like Redwood Materials, have diversified by repurposing battery packs for stationary storage, generating near‑term revenue while expanding recycling operations.
