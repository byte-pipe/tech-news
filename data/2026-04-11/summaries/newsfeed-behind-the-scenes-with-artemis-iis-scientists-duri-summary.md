# Behind the scenes with Artemis II’s scientists during the historic Moon fly‑by

## Main highlights
- Reporter Alexandra Witze spent a day in NASA’s Artemis II science operations centre during the mission’s lunar fly‑by.  
- She describes the fast‑paced environment, real‑time data streams, and the teamwork that turns raw measurements into scientific insight.  
- The mission provided the first close‑up observations of the Moon’s far side, including high‑resolution imaging and magnetic field measurements.  
- Early results show unexpected variations in the lunar exosphere and confirm predictions about charged‑particle interactions with the Moon’s surface.

- **Public engagement:** The mission’s live coverage and the release of striking “Earthset” photos captured wide public interest.
