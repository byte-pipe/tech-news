# Amazon, Meta join fight to end Google Pay, PhonePe dominance in India

## Background
- Executives from Amazon Pay, Meta (WhatsApp), CRED, MobiKwik, and Flipkart’s Super.money are set to meet the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) to discuss the dominance of PhonePe and Google Pay on the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) network.  
- The meeting follows a delayed government plan to cap any UPI app’s market share at 30% until 31 December 2026, a delay that has allowed PhonePe and Google Pay to maintain their leading positions.  

## Current market landscape
- PhonePe and Google Pay together accounted for roughly 80 % of the 22.6 billion UPI transactions recorded in March.  
- PhonePe reports over 700 million registered users, 50 million merchants, and coverage of more than 98 % of India’s postal codes, underscoring the scale that smaller rivals find hard to match.  

## Lobbying agenda
- Participants aim to raise concerns about:
  - User‑acquisition practices and the use of contact data by dominant apps.  
  - Product design and monetisation models within the UPI ecosystem.  
- Proposed regulatory measures include:
  - Restrictions on how dominant apps onboard users and handle contact information.  
  - Guarantees of fair access to features such as autopay and payment mandates.  
  - Requests for incentives and regulatory support to help emerging players compete.  

## NPCI’s challenge
- NPCI, operating under the Reserve Bank of India, must balance the need to curb market concentration with the risk of disrupting a service used by hundreds of millions.  
- The organization, along with Amazon, Meta, and other participants, did not respond to requests for comment.  
- It remains uncertain whether the meeting will lead to immediate regulatory changes.
