# ABC can beat Trump FCC's license threat if owner Disney is willing to fight – Ars Technica

## Legal background
- The 1996 Telecommunications Act made it extremely difficult for the FCC to cancel or deny renewal of broadcast licenses.  
- It eliminated comparative renewal hearings and requires the FCC to prove “willful or repeated” violations before revoking a license.  
- The Act limits the FCC’s ability to define “public interest” arbitrarily; the Supreme Court has ruled the FCC cannot ban controversial speech.

## Recent FCC action
- On the day after President Trump and the First Lady demanded Jimmy Kimmel’s firing, the FCC ordered Disney (owner of ABC) to file early renewal applications for its eight TV stations by May 28.  
- The order cites an investigation into possible violations of the Communications Act and anti‑discrimination rules, referencing Disney’s DEI practices.  
- Early renewal does not change the stations’ actual expiration dates (2028‑2031); it merely accelerates a routine administrative process.

## Political context
- The probe is widely viewed as retaliation for perceived network bias, following prior FCC threats in 2025 and a 2024 lawsuit by Trump that ended in a $15 million settlement.  
- Senators from both parties commented: Adam Schiff (D‑CA) warned against “renting” presidential favor, while Ted Cruz (R‑TX) criticized the FCC’s role as “speech police.”

## Likelihood of license loss
- Legal experts agree the FCC has little room to deny renewals under current law.  
- Proving “willful or repeated” violations is a high burden and would take years, during which ABC can continue operating.  
- The renewal standards require: (A) service to the public interest, (B) no serious violations, and (C) no pattern of abuse—criteria most broadcasters easily meet.

## Practical implications
- If Disney chooses to contest the FCC’s actions, the process will be lengthy but the stations’ licenses remain intact throughout.  
- The burden of proof rests on the FCC at every stage, and the commission’s Democratic member, Commissioner Anna Gomez, has publicly opposed content‑based attacks.  

**Bottom line:** While the FCC’s early‑renewal order appears politically motivated, the 1996 statutory framework makes outright denial of ABC’s licenses highly unlikely, provided Disney is prepared to engage in a prolonged legal defense.
