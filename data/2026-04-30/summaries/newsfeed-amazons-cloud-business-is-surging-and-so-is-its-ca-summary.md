# Amazon's cloud business is surging — and so is its capital spending | TechCrunch

# Summary of “Amazon's cloud business is surging — and so is its capital spending”

## Key Financial Highlights
- AWS net sales rose 28% YoY to $37.6 billion, the fastest growth in 15 quarters.  
- Amazon’s total revenue increased 17% YoY to $181.5 billion (12% in North America, 19% internationally).  
- Free cash flow fell to $1.2 billion for the trailing twelve months, a 95% drop from the prior year’s $25.9 billion, driven by $59.3 billion in property and equipment purchases.

## AWS Growth Drivers
- The AI boom is the primary catalyst; AWS provides compute resources for AI workloads.  
- CEO Andy Jassy noted that AI technology is growing faster than any prior tech, with AWS’s AI revenue run rate now over $15 billion—about 260 times the $58 million run rate three years after AWS launched.  
- Companies continue to choose AWS as the preferred platform for AI development.

## Capital Expenditure Strategy
- AWS’s rapid growth requires increased short‑term capex for land, power, data‑center buildings, chips, servers, and networking gear.  
- Jassy framed these expenditures as a long‑term investment: data‑center assets have >30‑year lifespans; servers and networking gear last 5–6 years.  
- He acknowledged that capex growth outpacing revenue growth temporarily pressures free cash flow.

## Impact on Free Cash Flow
- The surge in infrastructure spending directly reduced free cash flow in the reporting period.  
- Jassy reassured investors that the pattern mirrors the earlier AWS growth wave, expecting future free cash flow to improve as the new AI‑driven revenue stream matures.

## Overall Company Outlook
- Amazon beat Wall Street’s first‑quarter earnings expectations, reinforcing the view that AI “picks and shovels” providers are being rewarded.  
- Management remains confident that the current AWS growth wave will generate larger downstream revenue and eventually restore stronger free cash flow.
