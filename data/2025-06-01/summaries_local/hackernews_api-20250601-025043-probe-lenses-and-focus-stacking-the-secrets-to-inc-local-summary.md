• Here's a summary of the article, formatted as bullet points and tailored for a technical audience:
• **Innovative Imaging Technique:** Charles Brooks utilizes a highly specialized imaging pipeline combining probe lenses (initially Laowa, adapted with medical scopes), extensive adapter/magnifier stacking, and focus stacking/panoramic stitching. This allows high-resolution photography *inside* complex instruments without disassembly, overcoming limitations of small apertures and shallow depth of field.
• **Technical Challenges & Solutions:** The process is fraught with technical hurdles: extreme light falloff (effectively f/250+), heat management to protect delicate instruments (limiting flash duration), diffraction limits of small scopes, and vignetting. Brooks addresses these through dark frame subtraction, temperature monitoring, and meticulous image acquisition for blending/correction.
• **Workflow & System Specs:** The setup centers around a Micro Four Thirds Panasonic Lumix G9 II, chosen for its ability to maximize magnification within system limitations. Final images routinely involve stacking 100+ individual shots, requiring significant post-processing to achieve sharpness and correct for optical aberrations.
