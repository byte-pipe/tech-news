# Artemis II, NASA's boldest mission in generations, launches crew to the Moon - Ars Technica

# Artemis II Launch and Mission Overview

## Key Event
- Launch from Kennedy Space Center (LC‑39B) at 18:35 EDT on 5 April 2026 using NASA’s 322‑ft Space Launch System (SLS) – the most powerful human‑rated rocket to date.
- Four astronauts aboard Orion: Commander Reid Wiseman (U.S. Navy), Pilot Victor Glover (U.S. Navy), Mission Specialist Christina Koch (U.S.), and Canadian astronaut Jeremy Hansen.
- SLS generated 8.8 million lb thrust (four RS‑25 hydrogen engines + two solid boosters), surpassing the Saturn V.

## Mission Objectives
- Demonstrate the complete Earth‑to‑Moon transportation system (launch, orbit insertion, free‑return trajectory, re‑entry).
- Conduct manual piloting, trajectory correction, life‑support checks, and rendezvous/proximity operations with the upper stage.
- Fly a “free return” path around the Moon, reaching a maximum distance of 252,799 mi (406,840 km) on 6 April.
- Provide crew observations of the lunar far side for comparison with robotic imagery.
- Conclude with splashdown in the Pacific Ocean off California on 10 April.

## Timeline Highlights
- **T+0 min:** Liftoff; sound wave heard miles away.
- **T+1 min:** SLS exceeds Mach 1.
- **T+2 min:** Solid boosters separate after burnout; fall into Atlantic (not recovered).
- **T+8 min:** Core stage engines shut down; separation from Orion and upper stage.
- **T+~40 min:** Orion deploys solar panels; coast phase.
- **T+~50 min:** Upper‑stage RL10 burn places Orion into low‑Earth orbit.
- **T+~2 h:** Second RL10 burn sends Orion on high‑elliptical orbit (>40,000 mi apogee).
- **T+~3.5 h:** Orion separates from upper stage; begins manual control demonstration.
- **Subsequent hours:** Rendezvous and proximity operations (≈90 min) with Orion approaching within ~10 m of the upper stage, thruster firings, voice‑activated communications.
- **T+~4 days (6 April):** Closest approach to Moon; free‑return slingshot.
- **T+~5 days (10 April):** Re‑entry and splashdown.

## Crew Roles & Activities
- **Reid Wiseman:** Commander, provides overall mission updates; assists with manual piloting demo.
- **Victor Glover:** Pilot, executes manual control of Orion, performs six‑degree‑of‑freedom maneuvers, provides real‑time verbal feedback.
- **Christina Koch:** Mission specialist, monitors procedures and systems during piloting tests.
- **Jeremy Hansen:** Canadian astronaut, contributes to mission operations and future lunar exploration planning.

## Technical and Program Context
- Artemis II is the first crewed flight of the Artemis program; last humans left lunar vicinity in 1972.
- No landing on this flight; lunar landing targeted for Artemis IV (≈2028) using human‑rated landers from SpaceX and Blue Origin; Axiom Space developing new lunar suits.
- Orion’s cockpit features physical hand controllers and a cursor device, contrasting with SpaceX’s touchscreen‑based Crew Dragon.
- Mission validates Orion’s autopilot and manual control capabilities needed for future docking with lunar landers.

## Significance
- Marks a major milestone after nearly $100 billion invested in Artemis over two decades.
- Positions NASA in competition with China for lunar return.
- Sets records for distance traveled from Earth and provides unprecedented human views of the Moon’s far side.
