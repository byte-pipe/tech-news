## Article overview
- Published 02/04/2026 by the Financial Times (subscription required).
- Describes growing resistance among NHS staff to the Federated Data Platform (FDP) provided by US firm Palantir.

## Key points
- **Contract details**
  - Palantir awarded a £330 million contract in 2023 to aggregate NHS operational data, including patient records and waiting‑list information.
- **Ethical concerns**
  - Palantir’s involvement in U.S. defence projects and the political affiliations of its leadership have sparked controversy.
- **Staff resistance**
  - Some employees are officially refusing to use the FDP (“workplace adjustment”).
  - Others deliberately work slowly when forced to use it or avoid it altogether, citing better alternatives.
- **Adoption status**
  - 123 of 205 English hospital trusts are currently using the FDP.
  - The project has been rated highly for on‑time and on‑budget delivery despite dissent.
- **Political and union pressure**
  - MPs and medical unions are urging the government to remove Palantir from NHS systems.
  - Ministers have consulted on invoking a contract break clause.
- **Palantir’s response**
  - Louis Mosley, executive vice‑chair of Palantir UK, claims the opposition is ideologically driven and warns it could harm patient care.
