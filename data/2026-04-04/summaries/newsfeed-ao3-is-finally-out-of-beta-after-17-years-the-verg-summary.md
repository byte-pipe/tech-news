# AO3 is finally out of beta after 17 years

## Main announcement
- The Archive of Our Own (AO3) officially exits beta 17 years after its 2009 launch.  
- Announcement made by the Organization for Transformative Works (OTW) on April 2 2026.

## Reasons and context
- AO3 has expanded through volunteers, coding contributors, and contractors funded by user donations.  
- Notable features added over the years: comprehensive tagging system, fanwork download options, privacy settings that let creators limit access, and more.  
- Leaving beta is primarily a cosmetic change; the platform remains stable but not “finalized”.

## Ongoing development
- Volunteer coders and community contributors will keep improving AO3 daily.  
- Future updates are tracked on AO3’s Jira project board.  
- The tiny “beta” label has been removed from the logo (it was temporarily changed to “omega” for an April Fools’ joke).
