# Amazon hits sellers with 'fuel surcharge' as Iran war roils global energy markets | TechCrunch

# Amazon introduces 3.5% fuel surcharge for FBA sellers

## Context and Reason for the Surcharge
- Global oil markets are destabilized by the war in Iran, causing U.S. gasoline prices to rise sharply.  
- Higher fuel and logistics costs are pressuring e‑commerce and shipping carriers.

## Details of the New Policy
- Amazon will add a 3.5% fuel surcharge to orders fulfilled through its Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) service.  
- Effective date: April 17, 2026.  
- The surcharge is described as “temporarily” applied and will be reviewed as market conditions change.  
- Amazon claims the surcharge is lower than those imposed by other major carriers.

## Company Statement
- A spokesperson said Amazon has absorbed rising costs so far but now needs to recover a portion of the expense.  
- The surcharge is positioned as a temporary measure, not a permanent fee increase.

## Historical Comparison
- Amazon first used a similar surcharge in 2022 when crude oil prices exceeded $100 per barrel after Russia’s invasion of Ukraine.  
- The current situation mirrors 2022: geopolitical conflict (Iran) disrupting oil supply routes, especially the Strait of Hormuz, which handles about 20% of world oil shipments.

## Impact on Sellers
- The surcharge will affect the large, unspecified number of merchants that rely on FBA, which underpins the majority of third‑party sales on Amazon’s platform.  
- Potential cost increase for countless sellers using Amazon’s logistics network.
