description: 'last year, i put together something i hadn''t seen anyone really doing—lifecycle abm—and it worked, so i''m gonna drop the overall strategy here 👀 now, i did put a dollar value on it—with a goal of driving $300k in what i call LIR: lifecycle influenced revenue. because lifecycle can drive revenue beyond plg, i really wanted to test lifecycle abm and see if a) it''s a thing and b) it could work for upsell & cross-sell. it ended up being a key influence in driving over $470k in expansion revenue 🫣 if you want to try something similar, here are my strategic hot tips: ⚡ step 1: pick your target accounts • ask Sales for 1-2 accounts per AE that would genuinely benefit from upsell • narrow with contract status, usage data, and lifecycle signals • i landed on 12 accounts, personally, but you don''t need a TON of target accounts pro tip: figure out who''s most likely to drive or champion the upsell. a CMO isn''t using your tool the way a content marketing manager is—target the real,
  everyday user. ⚡ step 2: do your research (without burning out lol) • pull what you can from your CRM and lifecycle tools—renewal dates, key stakeholders, power users, existing use cases • ask CS and Sales to fill the gaps • some data > no data at this stage, tbh ⚡ step 3: map gaps to activities • what''s missing in your current customer experience? • for me, it was in-app support and customer nurture • i built a deeper in-app program that covered stuff like event-based triggers, automated Q&A mid-build, guided onboarding, user recs, and real-time surveying • test use case-specific activities (think: group demos and office hours for target accounts) pro tip: you can start by choosing just 2-3 things, and optimizing. ⚡ step 4: measure what actually matters • customer success first (are they getting value?) • retention second (are they sticking around?) • revenue third (this is the result, not the goal) you can''t influence or drive revenue if you don''t know whether customers are actually
  getting value, or plan on sticking around 🤗 the whole approach here is crawl, walk, jog, run. - you don''t need a massive team or fancy tools - you need intentionality, a few strong cross-functional relationships, and the discipline to start small IMO the compounding effect of journey management and optimization is SO underrated in b2b lifecycle 🥲 if you choose to do just one of these activities per quarter, and focus on your customer, you can''t really lose. butttt the cool part of all of this is that you don''t need to be in lifecycle marketing to drive upsell revenue 🥰 #lifecyclemarketing #lifecyclemarketingtips #lifecyclemarketingstrategy #b2blifecycle #b2bmarketing #lifecycle #b2bmarketingstrategy'
