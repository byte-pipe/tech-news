# KIT - Secuso Research - Results - Privacy Friendly Apps - Good-bye Google

The article discusses the SECUSO research group's decision to discontinue offering their "Privacy Friendly Apps" on the Google Play Store and instead focus on distributing them through the F-Droid store. This decision appears to be driven by a few key factors:

Problem/Opportunity:
The SECUSO group originally created these privacy-focused apps to provide alternatives to mainstream apps that demanded more permissions than necessary. This addresses a clear user pain point around data privacy and control.

Market Indicators:
The article mentions the project has grown to over 30 apps with more than 350,000 installs, indicating significant user adoption and demand for these types of privacy-friendly apps. However, the maintenance burden of keeping the apps updated on the Google Play Store has become unsustainable for the research group.

Technical Feasibility:
As a research group, the SECUSO team likely has the technical skills required to develop and maintain these apps. However, the article suggests the Google Play Store imposes more onerous requirements that make it difficult for a small team to keep up with.

Business Viability:
The apps are available for free, so there is no direct revenue model. However, the large user base and focus on an underserved privacy need suggests there could be opportunities for a solo developer to monetize these types of apps through premium features, subscriptions, or partnerships. The existing competition seems limited, and distribution through F-Droid provides an alternative channel.

In summary, this article highlights an opportunity for a solo developer to build on the SECUSO team's work and further develop privacy-focused mobile apps. The large user base, clear user pain point, and potential to monetize these types of apps make it a potentially viable business opportunity for the right developer.
