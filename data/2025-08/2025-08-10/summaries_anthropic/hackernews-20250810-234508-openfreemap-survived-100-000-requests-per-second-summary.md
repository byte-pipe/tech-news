# OpenFreeMap survived 100,000 requests per second

This article discusses an interesting problem and opportunity for a solo developer running the OpenFreeMap project. Here's a 3-4 paragraph analysis from a solo developer business perspective:

The problem being discussed is the sudden and massive spike in traffic to the OpenFreeMap service, reaching over 100,000 requests per second. This was caused by the unexpected launch of a new collaborative drawing website, Wplace.live, which was built using OpenFreeMap and quickly gained 2 million users. This influx of traffic, estimated to cost over $6 million per month on commercial map services, threatened to overwhelm the solo developer's infrastructure.

The market indicators here are quite positive. The massive user adoption of Wplace.live, generating billions of requests, shows a strong demand and willingness to pay for the mapping capabilities provided by OpenFreeMap. The developer was able to serve 96% of the requests successfully, indicating the technical feasibility of handling this scale as a solo developer. Additionally, the developer was able to quickly reach out to Cloudflare for sponsorship and architectural guidance, suggesting good distribution channels and partnerships.
