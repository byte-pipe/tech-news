# The Future is NOT Self-Hosted

The article "The Future is NOT Self-Hosted" discusses the problem of losing ownership and control over our digital assets and data when relying on centralized cloud services provided by large tech companies. The key points from a solo developer business perspective are:

1. Problem/Opportunity:
   - The article highlights the issue of users not truly "owning" their digital content (e.g., books, files, photos) when it is hosted on platforms like Amazon, Google, and Apple. These companies can unilaterally change the terms of access and ownership.
   - This presents an opportunity for solo developers to build solutions that give users more control and ownership over their digital assets.

2. Market Indicators:
   - The article mentions growing user frustration with the lack of control over their digital lives, indicating a potential market demand for alternative solutions.
   - It cites specific examples of Amazon changing Kindle book access policies and the general trend of users "renting" their digital content rather than owning it.

3. Technical Feasibility for a Solo Developer:
   - The article acknowledges that self-hosting, while providing more control, is technically complex and time-consuming, requiring skills in system administration, server management, and software installation/configuration.
   - This suggests that the technical barrier for a solo developer may be high, requiring significant time and effort to set up a fully-fledged self-hosting solution.

4. Business Viability Signals:
   - The article suggests a willingness to pay for solutions that offer more control and ownership over digital assets, as users are frustrated with the current state of cloud-based services.
   - However, the article also highlights the challenges of creating a self-hosted solution that still allows for easy sharing and collaboration, which may limit the viability of a purely self-hosted approach.

In summary, the article identifies a genuine problem and market opportunity for solo developers to create solutions that give users more control and ownership over their digital assets. However, it also cautions that a purely self-hosted approach may not be the best long-term solution, as it can be technically complex and limit the ability to collaborate and share resources. The article suggests that the future may lie in finding a balance between self-hosting and leveraging the benefits of cloud-based services in a more user-centric way.
