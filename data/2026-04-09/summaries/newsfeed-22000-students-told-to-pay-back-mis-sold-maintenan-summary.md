# 22,000 students told to pay back 'mis-sold' maintenance loans

The Student Loans Company (SLC) has informed over 20,000 students that their maintenance loans and grants were given in error. This decision has affected more than 15 universities and colleges nationwide, including London Met, Bath Spa, Leeds Trinity, Southampton Solent, and Oxford Brookes.

**Key Points**

* Over 20,000 students received incorrect letters stating their courses had never been eligible for maintenance loans or childcare grants.
* Students need to repay any "over-payment" they may have made.
* The issue reportedly stems from a government-related decision, and institutions involved are considering legal action, but the government has accused students of incompetence or misuse of the system.

**Student Loan System**

* Maintenance loans cover living costs such as accommodation and food, covered in instalments after graduation.
* Tuition loans for degree courses are paid directly to universities.

**Students' Reactions**

* Many students feel "devastated" and "worried", citing financial difficulties due to repayment of the incorrect loans.

**Key Points from Universities Involved**

* Institutions will consider a legal challenge amidst concerns that government decisions may have been "abrupt" and led to student mis-sold.
* Some institutions operate franchise deals where the university awarding the degree has a contract with another organisation to deliver the course.
