description: These 9 habits made me a way better social media manager. (aka the habits that keep me sane, strategic, and on top of my game) 1. Track Top Posts Monthly → Know what’s working (and why) so you can double down next month 2. Build a Response Library → Store common questions and approved responses for quick, on-brand replies 3. Celebrate Small Wins → Whether it’s a post doing numbers, a great comment, or a fun new idea, enjoy the moment because this job can be so up and down at times 4. Keep an Ideas List for Every Account → Every account needs its own dedicated space to document ideas and inspo without it getting lost or mixed up (I recommend a Notion page) 5. Coffee Chats → Connect with others in the industry, it’s good for the soul and for your career (and they honestly are so much fun and not weird I promise you) 6. Engage, A Lot → Don’t neglect the social part of social media. You have to be an active participant of your community to understand and build with them 7. Set Boundaries
  → Social media is 24/7, you don’t have to be. Use tools like screen time limits or even a Brick to find a balance that works for you 8. Take Screen Breaks → Not only for your eyes but for your mind to reset and refresh ( I promise you’ll come back stronger) 9. Plan with Flexibility → Plan what you can in your content calendar, but leave space for trends, last minute updates, and whatever else the internet may throw your way 😅 We all know that social media changes daily but your habits can play a big role in keeping you sane, strategic, and steady through it all. It’s all about building systems that work with you, not against you. ✨ Your turnnnn, what small habits have leveled up your game as a marketer or social media manager? | 28 comments on LinkedIn
