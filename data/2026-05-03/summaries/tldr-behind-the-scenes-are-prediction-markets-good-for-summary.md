# Behind the scenes: Are prediction markets good for anything?

## Key takeaways from the interview

- **Public‑goods shortfall** – Dan confirms his prior belief that prediction markets have not delivered the expected public value; they fall short of the “millions of viewers, billions of dollars” vision imagined two decades ago.  
- **Early‑warning limitation** – Markets tend to reflect risks already identified elsewhere; they rarely surface unknown events (e.g., an Iran oil embargo) before mainstream news picks them up.  
- **Taxonomy revision** – Dan revised his classification of possible public values, recognizing that “early warning” is not a realistic function for most current markets.  

## Comparison with Metaculus

- **Accuracy** – Dan believes Metaculus is more accurate than prediction markets, though he lacks definitive data.  
- **Question difficulty** – Prediction‑market questions are generally easier and more “gossipy,” while Metaculus focuses on harder scientific problems; this makes direct head‑to‑head accuracy comparisons problematic.  
- **Calibration** – When difficulty is mentally adjusted for, Metaculus’s calibration appears “scarily accurate.”  

## Liquidity, infrastructure, and the “money” factor

- **Liquidity still nascent** – Polymarket has about 18 months of notable liquidity; Kalshi about six months. In financial‑market terms, this is a very early stage.  
- **Institutional participation** – Smart institutional money stays away because prediction markets lack risk‑hedging mechanisms common in mature markets.  
- **Market concentration** – Activity is heavily dominated by Polymalue and Kalshi, with Polymarket handling roughly eight times the volume of “useful” questions compared to Kalshi.  

## Outlook and optimism

- **Infrastructure dependence on a single player** – Future improvements hinge largely on Polymarket’s actions; the company has invested heavily in crypto‑based infrastructure but lacks many features of traditional finance.  
- **Forecast** – Dan predicts Polymarket will continue on its current trajectory, implying limited near‑term breakthroughs in market performance.  

## Overall assessment

- Prediction markets have not yet realized their promised public‑good contributions.  
- Metaculus, despite lacking monetary stakes, currently offers superior calibration on harder questions.  
- Significant growth in liquidity, institutional tools, and diversified platforms would be required before prediction markets can fulfill their early optimism.
