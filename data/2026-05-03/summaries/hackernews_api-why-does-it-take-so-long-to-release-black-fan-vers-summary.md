# Why does it take so long to release black fan versions?

## Main reasons
- Black versions are developed after the beige/brown versions because colour pigments change the injection‑moulding process.
- The additional tooling, mould‑tuning, and validation steps introduce at least 6 months of delay; more if re‑iterations are needed.

## Injection‑moulding challenges
- High‑performance fans use tight tip clearances (0.5 mm for 120 mm, 0.7 mm for 140 mm) that are at the limit of moulding consistency.
- Any variation in material or process can push the fan outside these tolerances.

## Effect of black pigments
- Carbon‑black particles are much smaller and have a larger surface area than the metal‑oxide pigments used for standard colours.
- They increase melt viscosity, alter heat absorption and crystallisation, and therefore affect flow, cooling and dimensional stability.
- This requires separate optimisation of geometry, cooling, gating and mould parameters for the black version.

## Tooling and validation timeline
- First mould for a new high‑performance fan undergoes multiple tuning iterations; severe issues may force a complete redesign.
- Tooling for black versions is started only after mass production of the standard colours is stable, to avoid extra cost and risk.
- Each new black version must pass a long‑term high‑temperature test lasting several months; preparation and evaluation add further time.
- Minimum delay between standard and black releases is about 6 months; with mould‑tuning or re‑testing it can extend to 12 months or more.

## Current status
- The chromax.black version of the NF‑A12x25 G2 is slated for release roughly 10 months after the beige/brown launch, which is close to the fastest achievable schedule given the constraints.

## Other influencing factors
- Component availability, production capacity, and logistics can add extra delays.
- Example: a three‑year gap between the first‑generation NF‑A12x25 and its black version was caused by the pandemic, strong demand, disrupted supply chains and logistical issues.
