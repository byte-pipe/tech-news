# Apple raises the Mac Mini’s starting price

## Key points
- Apple removed the $599 Mac Mini with 256 GB storage from its online store; the new base model now starts at $799 and includes 512 GB storage and the M4 processor.  
- The change follows Tim Cook’s earnings‑call comment that “supply constraints” will affect several Mac models, including the Mac Mini and Mac Studio, for several months.  
- Cook cited “higher‑than‑expected demand,” especially from users pairing the devices with AI agents such as OpenClaw.  
- A global memory (RAM) shortage is driving “significantly higher memory costs,” which Apple expects to impact its business and contribute to price increases.  
- Apple has also halted sales of the Mac Studio with 512 GB of RAM and raised starting prices for the new MacBook Air and MacBook Pro models.  
- The broader industry is seeing similar price hikes or model discontinuations as suppliers grapple with the AI‑driven RAM shortage.
