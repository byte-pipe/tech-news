# Apple may take "several months" to catch up to Mac mini and Studio demand

## Overview
- Mac mini and Mac Studio are increasingly difficult to purchase; many configurations appear as “currently unavailable” and others have weeks‑to‑months shipping delays.  
- The top‑end Mac Studio with 512 GB of RAM was removed from Apple’s store entirely.  
- CEO Tim Cook addressed the shortages on the Q2 earnings call, attributing part of the issue to unexpected demand from users running AI agents and related tools locally.

## Reasons for Shortage
- **AI‑driven demand:** Both desktops are seen as strong platforms for AI and agentic tools, leading to higher‑than‑expected sales.  
- **Manufacturing constraints:** Limited capacity at advanced TSMC nodes reduces Apple’s ability to request additional chips; this constraint primarily affects iPhone production but also impacts Macs to a lesser extent.  
- **Additional factors:** General chip shortages, upcoming replacements with M5‑series chips later in the year (which often cause shipping delays before new models launch), and an industry‑wide RAM shortage that is raising memory costs.

## Apple’s Outlook
- Cook estimates that it may take “several months” for supply and demand to balance for the Mac mini and Mac Studio.  
- The timeline likely includes the rollout of new M5‑based models and the period needed to satisfy pent‑up demand after those releases.

## Comparison with MacBook Neo
- The MacBook Neo also saw strong demand, with a March record for new Mac customers attributed to the Neo.  
- Neo availability remains better: orders from Apple typically arrive in two to three weeks, and the device is still offered for same‑day shipping or pickup at retailers such as Amazon, Walmart, and Best Buy—unlike most Mac mini or Studio models.

## Financial Context
- Q2 2026 revenue: **$111.2 billion**, a **17 %** increase year‑over‑year, driven by strong iPhone 17 sales and Services growth.  
- The Mac segment grew **6 %** year‑over‑year despite the shortages affecting the mini, Studio, and Neo.  
- Apple expects “significantly higher memory costs” in Q3, which will increasingly impact the business.
