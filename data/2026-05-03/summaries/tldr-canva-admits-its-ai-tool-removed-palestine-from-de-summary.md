# Canva Admits Its AI Tool Removed ‘Palestine’ From Designs, Apologizes for Any Distress It Caused

## Overview
- Canva’s “Magic Layers” AI feature changed the word “Palestine” to “Ukraine” in user designs.
- Issue first reported by X user @ros_ie9; other users were able to replicate it.
- The word “Gaza” could still be used without alteration.

## Canva’s Response
- A spokesperson confirmed the problem, stated it has been fixed, and added that additional checks are being implemented.
- The company launched an audit to determine how the issue arose and is reviewing internal testing processes.
- Canva apologized for any distress caused by the incident.

## Technical Context
- Magic Layers, introduced last month, is designed to convert flat images and static AI outputs into fully editable, multi‑layered designs.
- The unexpected text substitution suggests possible bias in the tool’s training data or instruction set.

## Prior AI Bias Incidents
- Meta’s generative AI in WhatsApp produced an image of a boy with a gun when prompted to create a picture of a Palestinian.
- In 2023, ChatGPT refused to answer affirmatively to “Should Palestinians be free?” while answering similarly for other populations.

## Implications
- The incident underscores the importance of rigorous testing for generative AI, especially regarding politically sensitive language.
- Canva’s review of testing and auditing processes aims to prevent similar unintended outputs in the future.
