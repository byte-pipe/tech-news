# California to begin ticketing driverless cars that violate traffic laws

## New DMV regulations
- Effective 1 July, the California Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) will allow police to issue a “notice of AV non‑compliance” directly to the manufacturer of an autonomous vehicle (AV) that commits a moving violation.  
- The rules are part of a broader 2024 law that expands state oversight of AV technology.  
- The DMV calls the regulations “the most comprehensive AV regulations in the nation.”

## Enforcement provisions
- Police may cite AV companies when their vehicles break traffic laws.  
- Companies must answer calls from police or emergency officials within 30 seconds.  
- Penalties will be imposed if an AV enters an active emergency zone.

## Recent incidents prompting action
- **September (San Bruno):** A Waymo robotaxi made an illegal U‑turn at a traffic light; officers could not issue a ticket because no driver was present and had to contact the company instead.  
- **December (San Francisco blackout):** Multiple Waymo vehicles stalled in busy intersections during a citywide power outage, worsening congestion.  
- San Francisco Fire Department officials have repeatedly reported robotaxis obstructing emergency responses.

## Companies operating AVs in California
- Waymo runs fully self‑driving robotaxis in the San Francisco Bay Area and Los Angeles County.  
- Tesla, among other firms, holds permits to test autonomous vehicles in various California cities.  

## Official statements
- DMV Director Steve Gordon emphasized that the updated regulations reinforce California’s commitment to public safety while maintaining its leadership in AV development.
