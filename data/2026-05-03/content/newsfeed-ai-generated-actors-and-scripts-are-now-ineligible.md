# AI-generated actors and scripts are now ineligible for Oscars

The organization behind the Academy Awardsreleased new Oscar ruleson Friday, including several that address the use of generative artificial intelligence.

The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences said that only performances “credited in the film’s legal billing and demonstrably performed by humans with their consent” will be eligible for Academy Awards. Similarly, the academy said that screenplays must be “human-authored” to be eligible.

The academy also said it has the right to request more information about a film’s AI usage and “human authorship.”

These rule changes come as an independent film is in the works withan AI-generated version of Val Kilmer, as AI “actress” Tilly Norwoodkeeps making headlines, and asnew video modelsare causing at least a few filmmakers to make sweeping declarations of despair. AI was also one of the main sticking points inthe actors’ and writers’ strikesback in 2023.

Outside Hollywood, at least one novel has beenpulled by its publisherdue to the apparent use of AI, and other writers’ groups are declaring that AI usage makes workineligible for awards.
