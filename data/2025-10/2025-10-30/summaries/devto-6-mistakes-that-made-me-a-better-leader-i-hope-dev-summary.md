# Leadership Lessons Learned

## Leadership is Not About Having All the Answers, Making Decisions, or Fixing Everything

When I became a team lead, I thought that leading meant having all the answers. However, my experiences taught me the opposite.

### Key Takeaways:

*   Trying to do everything yourself as a leader was detrimental to its success.
*   Silencing difficult conversations is not only toxic but also leads to frustration; it's better to have honest feedback and clear perspectives.
*   Measuring output rather than impact is where true leadership shines. Leaders should focus on the work they're doing, making users and the product safe. Others can lead as leaders have influence of responsibility which enables them to contribute even before being assigned as such titles.
*   Focusing solely on task execution when not understanding its place in the overall vision can lack motivation; teams must get more than just what's asked from them.
*   Celebrating small wins is vital to developing a positive team culture.

## Acknowledging Mistakes and Opportunities for Growth

I made mistakes along my leadership journey but those did teach me valuable lessons. As I reflect on these successes and failures, it becomes clear that true growth came from:

Acknowledging the need to learn faster than any mistake; that progress is not just about perfection, but about being humble and reflective.

Leadership isn't perfect; it's a continuous process of learning, reflecting, and growing with humility.
