## **Keeping Android Open: A Threat to Digital Sovereignty**

Google has announced its intention to end user-centered app development on the Android platform without a government-approved registration process starting next year.

The new requirement includes:

*   Paying an application fee
*   Agreeing to Google's terms and conditions
*   Providing identification documents
*   Uploading a private signing key

This change means that users will no longer be able to develop apps directly, as they would need to request permission from Google before doing so.

The implications of this policy alteration include:

*   Wiping away the "open" Android ecosystem promise, making it vulnerable to government control and regulations.
*   Disregarding consumer autonomy in app development, which has been guaranteed since the platform's inception.
*   Imposing significant penalties on creators who fail to comply with Google's demands.

Resistors of both individual developers and governments may find ways to counter this move, such as signing up to early access programs and contacting regulators directly.
