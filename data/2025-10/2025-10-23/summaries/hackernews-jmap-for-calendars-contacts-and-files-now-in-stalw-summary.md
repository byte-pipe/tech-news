# JMAP for Calendars, Contacts and Files now in Stalwart | Stalwart Labs

**Stalwart Labs Announces Major Milestone: Full Implementation of JMAP for Calendars, Contacts, and Files**

After four years of development, Stalwart is thrilled to announce the full implementation of JMAP (Java Access to Method Abstract Protocol) for calendars, contacts, and files. This significant milestone marks a new era in groupware, as Stalwart becomes the first JMAP server to support the entire family of collaboration protocols.

**Building on JMAP Extensions**

Over the past few years, the IETF has introduced new protocol extensions to improve synchronization, sharing, and file storage capabilities in emails, calendars, and contacts. These include:

* **JMAP for Calendars**: a modern replacement for CalDAV and scheduling
* **JMAP for Contacts**: a powerful alternative to CardDAV
* **JMAP for File Storage**: a replacement for WebDAV-based file storage
* **JMAP Sharing**: a new successor to WebDAV ACL

**Limitations of Yesterday's Technology**

While yesterday's solutions, such as CalDAV and CardDAV, have served the internet well, they are now facing limitations:

* Their XML-based design is verbose, inconsistent, and prone to compatibility issues
* iCalendar and vCard formats have accumulated technical debt over decades

**JMAP: A Modern Solution for Modern Needs**

As a more efficient replacement than IMAP and SMTP submissions, JMAP excels in simplicity, clarity, and network efficiency. It continues to grow into a robust solution for the entire collaboration stack:

* **Full Implementation**: Stalwart is now fully supporting the full range of JMAP protocols
* **Clean API for Modern Data Models**: JSCalendar and JSContact replace accumulated JSON-based clutter with easy-to-implement, human-readable formats

By integrating these new protocol extensions and modern data models, JMAP provides an exceptional experience for users, ensuring coherence, elegance, and efficient collaboration across personal and group contexts.
