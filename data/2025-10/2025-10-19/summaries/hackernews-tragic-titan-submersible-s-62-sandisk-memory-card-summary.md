# Tragic Titan submersible’s $62 SanDisk memory card found undamaged at wreckage site — 12 stills and nine videos have been recovered, but none from the...

**Titan Submersible Recovery: Mysterious SD Card**

* Discovery: A sealed Titan submersible has been uncovered at its wreckage site in the Gulf of Mexico with a hard-drive capable $62 SanDisk memory card still intact.
* Camera details:
 + SD card recovered and appears to be from an Olympus system on module (SoM) - likely Inforce 6601.
 + SD card is almost certainly a SanDisk Extreme Pro 512GB, dated October 2025 at the time of recovery ($62.99).
 + Casing remains intact.
* Data recovery process:
 + Investigators "made an exact binary image of the SD card" for preservation.
 + Preliminary analysis aims to determine details captured and stored by the submersible's camera system.

**Key Findings**

* A hard-drive capable $62 SanDisk memory card was recovered at the Titanic wreckage site in the Gulf of Mexico, suggesting the camera was intact despite some damage to its housing and components.
* The recovered SD card is described as nearly identical to a 2018 SanDisk Extreme Pro 512GB model ($62.99 at the time), which is confirmed through forensic analysis.
* The recovery team may not have damaged the underlying data stored on the memory card, paving the way for further investigation into what was captured by the camera system.

**Additional Details**

* Investigators were able to preserve a "exact binary image" of the SD card, allowing them to analyze and potentially replay captured events.
* Preliminary analysis suggests the submersible's camera system may have captured critical details about its operations during this ill-fated mission.
