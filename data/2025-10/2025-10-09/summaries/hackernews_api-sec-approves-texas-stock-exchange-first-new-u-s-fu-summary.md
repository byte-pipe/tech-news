# SEC approves Texas Stock Exchange, first new U.S. fully integrated exchange in decades - CBS Texas

**Texas Stock Exchange Receives SEC Approval to Launch as First New U.S. National Exchange in Decades**

The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has approved the Texas Stock Exchange (TXSE), marking a significant milestone in the development of a new national exchange in the United States. This achievement paves the way for TXSE, the first new fully integrated U.S. stock exchange in over 40 years.

**Key Points:**

- The Texas Stock Exchange received SEC approval as a national securities exchange.
- It is the first new, fully integrated US stock exchange since 1982.
- Approved to launch trading services, ETPs (Exchange-Traded Products), and corporate listings by 2026.
- Backed by renowned financial firms including BlackRock and Citadel Securities.

**What are Exchange-Traded Products (ETPs)?**

- Exchange-traded products are financial instruments that track underlying assets such as stocks or indexes.
- Investors can purchase and sell ETPs throughout their trading day at market prices, with real-time price fluctuations.
- Examples of ETPs include individual stock ETPs, options ETPs, and bond ETPs.

**The Texas Stock Exchange**

- Headquarters in Dallas.
- Launched this spring, opening to support growth and expansion for a strong economy like Texas' in the US national exchangers market.

**Comparison with Existing Regional Exchanges**

- The TXSE follows in the footsteps of regional exchanges such as the Philadelphia Stock Exchange, Boston Stock Exchange, and Chicago Stock Exchange, all which merged with either the Nasdaq or the New York Stock Exchange after legislation by the SEC promoting automated trading platforms.
