# Social Cooling - Big Data's unintended side effect

## Social Cooling: The Unintended Consequences of Big Data

The impact of big data on society is multifaceted and far-reaching. This concept, known as "Social Cooling," refers to the long-term negative effects of living in a reputation economy where personal behavior is scrutinized.

### Key Points:

* **Personal Profiling:** Data collectors analyze individual behaviors, preferences, and characteristics to assign scores.
* **Bias and Biases:** Algorithms are often designed with biases built-in, leading to unfair treatment of certain groups.
* **Conscience Pressure:** Pressure to conform to social norms can lead users to make choices that contradict their personal values.
* **Censorship and Restrictions:** Social media platforms restrict access to content or use data to gauge users' moral character.

### Key Findings:

* The "algorithmic economy" often uses "targeted profiling" even when targeting specific groups, exacerbating existing issues of discrimination and prejudice.
* When doctors attempted to help advanced cancer patients by attempting treatments other than those that had produced better outcomes in early stages, this led them to have higher mortality rates due to the perceived lower quality treatment options.
* People are becoming more cautious about their online presence, choosing less risky behavior despite potential benefits.

### Long-term Consequences:

* The growing awareness of the negative consequences may lead to greater self-censorship and a culture of risk-aversion.

### Implications and Concerns:

* This is just the beginning of a widespread societal change.
* Potential risks include limiting personal freedoms, hindering open discussions about sensitive topics.
* Governments and institutions must carefully consider these issues when developing regulations on data collection.
