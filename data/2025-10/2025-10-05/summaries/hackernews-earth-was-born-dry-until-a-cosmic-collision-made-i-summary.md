# Earth was born dry until a cosmic collision made it a blue planet | ScienceDaily

**Earth's Composition and the Emergence of Life**

# Introduction

Scientists have found that Earth's basic chemistry solidified within just three million years of the Solar System's formation. The initial state of the planet was barren and inhospitable, with missing water and carbon compounds.

## Earth's Formation and Early Chemistry

At this time, only a later planetary collision with Theia likely delivered the essential ingredients for life. This event changed everything, bringing water and volatiles that made life possible.

## Key Findings

* Three million years after formation, the chemical composition of Earth was considered complete.
* In contrast to other planets in the Solar System, liquid water and a stable atmosphere were found on Earth.
* However, these conditions were not conducive to life initially.

**The Role of Volatile Elements**

Volatiles such as hydrogen, carbon, and sulphur are essential for life. These elements could not exist in their gaseous form in the inner Solar System due to high temperatures.

## Conclusion

The study shows that Earth became a habitable planet only after a later collision with Theia, resulting in the delivery of water and volatiles necessary for life. However, it is still unclear when this event occurred and how it influenced the emergence of life on Earth.
