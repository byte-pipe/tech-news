# Amazon's Ring to partner with Flock, a network of AI cameras used by ICE, feds, and police | TechCrunch

**Amazon Partners with Flock for AI-Powered Surveillance Cameras**

In a move that may raise concerns about racial biases in law enforcement's use of surveillance technology, Amazon's Ring has announced a partnership with Flock, a network of AI-powered surveillance cameras used by ICE (U.S. Customs and Border Protection), the FBI, and other agencies.

**Key Points:**

* Flock cameras can request that users share footage to assist with "evidence collection and investigative work."
* The cameras also allow users to search through their video footage using natural language.
* However, the development of AI-powered technology used by law enforcement has been linked to racial biases.

**Partner Details:**

* Amazon has purchased Flock's network of surveillance cameras, which could potentially impact millions more cameras currently under license from Ring.
* This partnership comes after concerns were raised about Ring's poor track record regarding customer data security and potential misuse of surveillance footage for law enforcement purposes.

**Regulatory Response:**

* In response to the news, 404 Media reported that U.S. Customs and Border Protection (ICE), the FBI, and the Navy have access to Flock's network.
* The incident also comes as Amazon faces criticism over its own handling of customer data security concerns in recent years.
