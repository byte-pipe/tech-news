## Keep Your Android App Private: What You Need to Know About Google's Developer Registration Requirement

### Introduction
Google has announced that starting next year, app developers will be required to register centrally with the company for all apps that want to distribute them outside of the Google Play Store. This new requirement may impact independent app stores like F-Droid.

### Key Points:

*   Developers must pay a fee to Google
*   Agree to Google's Terms and Conditions
*   Provide government identification
*   Upload evidence of an app's private signing key
*   List all current and future application identifiers

### What Matters Most for Independent App Stores:

*   Government ID:
    *   Many governments offer free or low-cost government ID documents
    *   Examples: USA, EU, UK, Brazil
*   Private Signing Key:
    *   Developers need to create a private signing key for each app
    *   This is required for the app to be signed and verified by Google
*   Centralized Registration:
*   Government-Backed Developer ID
*   Transparency Requirements

### How Independent App Stores Can Benefit:

*   Open-source Android apps will have an easier time finding customers outside of Google Play Store
*   New sources of revenue for independent developers
*   Increased transparency and openness among app stores

*   Sign the Open Letter or draft your own Open Letter to Google on Mandatory Developer Registration in Third-Party App Distribution
*   Share feedback with US Department of Justice Antitrust Report, Android developer verification requirements survey,
*   Write blog posts encouraging people not to support this policy
