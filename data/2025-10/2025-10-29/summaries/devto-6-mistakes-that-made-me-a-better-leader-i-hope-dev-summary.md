# 6 Mistakes That Made Me a Better Leader (I hope 😅) - DEV Community

# Leadership Lessons Learned from Mistakes

## Shaped Me into a Better Leader

My leadership journey was initially defined by a flawed understanding. I thought being a good leader meant handling everything, making all decisions, and solving every problem myself. However, as a team lead, these misconceptions led to burnout, slow team progress, and poor communication.

## Mistakes Made

1. **Trying to do everything myself**: Ignoring delegation led me to exhaust the team and neglect my own growth.
2. **Avoiding difficult conversations**: Silent on issues meant avoiding tough feedback that could have improved performance or clarity.
3. **Measuring output, not impact**: Overemphasizing numbers overlooked quality over quantity.
4. **Thinking leadership is about titles**: Focusing too much on being promoted led to inauthentic leadership.
5. **Overcommunicating tasks, undercommunicating vision**: Talking less often than people did about their work made it harder for them to understand why their efforts mattered.
6. **Forgetting to celebrate small wins**: Chasing milestones without recognizing progress distracted team members and eroded motivation.

## Lessons Learned

These mistakes forced me to slow down, reflect, and change how I work with people. By embracing these lessons:

- **Delegation is a strength, not a weakness**; trusting my team allows them to grow.
- **Honest feedback builds trust**, even if it's uncomfortable.
- **Impact is more important than just output**; measuring success by what it achieves matters.
- **Leadership isn't about titles**, but about influence and taking responsibility.
- **Why should we communicate tasks and vision?** Teams motivated when given purpose.
- **Celebrating small wins reminds us progress matters**, even in complex projects.

## Leadership Lesson: Progress, Reflection, Humility

Every step back, every mistake, and every awkward feedback session are part of the growth story. Leaders must learn faster than they repeat their mistakes to develop into effective leaders.
