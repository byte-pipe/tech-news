# Journalists exit Pentagon rather than agree to new reporting rules | AP News

### Journalists Pushback Against New Reporting Rules at Pentagon

Dozens of reporters turned in their access badges and exited the Pentagon on Wednesday, rejecting new government-imposed restrictions on their work and pushing them further away from the seat of the US military.

*   **Main Points:**
    1.  Reporters rejected new rules imposed by Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth that would leave journalists vulnerable to expulsion if they sought to report on classified or otherwise sensitive information.
    2.  Many reporters spent time together at a deadline set by the Defense Department, awaiting departure from the building until their access badges were turned in.
    3.  Access badges were issued to members of the Pentagon press corp and returned when new rules imposed restrictions on journalists' work.
    4.  Reporters chose instead to leave together after meeting at a 4 p.m. deadline set by the Defense Department.

### Key Takeaways

*   The rejection of the new reporting rules can be seen as a sign that many in the media feel their freedom and flexibility to operate are being curtailed.
*   The move is also a pushback against increased security measures imposed on journalists and an effort to limit their ability to gather information.
