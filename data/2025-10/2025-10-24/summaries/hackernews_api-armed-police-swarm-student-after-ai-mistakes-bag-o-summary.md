# Armed police swarm student after AI mistakes bag of Doritos for a weapon

A 16-year-old student at Kenwood High School in Baltimore was targeted by armed officers after an artificial intelligence (AI) system mistakenly flagged his lunch bag as a gun.

The incident occurred on October 20, when multiple police cars arrived at the school shortly after Taki Allen had finished football practice. The AI surveillance technology used by Omnilert, which is part of Baltimore County Public Schools, was said to have detected the Doritos bag and alerted officers in real-time, who handcuffed and searched Allen.

"I thought I was going to die," Allen told WBAL-TV 11 News. "And then they showed me a picture that looked like a gun, and I'm like, 'no, it's chips.'"

Omnilert later admitted the incident was a "false positive" but claimed its purpose is to prioritize safety through rapid human verification. Baltimore County Public Schools has offered counseling services to students impacted by the incident.

Allen says no one from the school has reached out to him personally and that he feels unappreciated for the attention brought on his arrest and detention.

The case has sparked a discussion about the reliability of AI surveillance tools in schools, highlighting concerns over the potential misuse of technology to target specific individuals.
