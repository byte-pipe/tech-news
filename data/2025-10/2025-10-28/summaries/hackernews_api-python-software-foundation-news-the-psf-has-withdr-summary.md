# Python Software Foundation News: The PSF has withdrawn a $1.5 million proposal to US government grant program

## The PSF Withdraws Proposal for $1.5 Million US Government Grant Program

### Background

In January 2025, the Python Software Foundation (PSF) submitted a proposal to the US government National Science Foundation (NSF) under the Safety, Security, and Privacy of Open Source Ecosystems program. The proposal aimed to address structural vulnerabilities in Python and PyPI.

## Proposal Background

The PSF is its first time applying for government funding due to navigating an intensive process. Principal Investigator Seth Larson and Deputy Executive Director Loren Crary led a multi-round proposal writing process followed by months-long vetting.

### Review Process and Restrictions

The proposed project received positive feedback when recommended for funding, with only 36% of new NSF grant applicants meeting this criterion. However, upon being presented with the terms requiring unanimous agreement on DEI-related programs or projects.

## Grant Terms and Restrictions

Grant recipients are obligated to agree to certain conditions:

1.  All security work directly funded by the grant is exempt from DEI obligations.
2.  Any activity supporting PSF's values must adhere to this requirement, including all community projects.
3.  In case of non-compliance, NSF can "claw back" previously approved funds.

## Challenges and Disagreement

Disagreements arose when PSF members tried to interpret the restrictive terms, causing uncertainty about how to proceed.

### Seeking Clarification and Collaboration

Attempts were made to consult with NSF contacts and peer organizations in similar situations. However, disagreements still persisted regarding adherence to these new conditions.

### Final Decision and Impact on Community

Despite the challenges faced, PSF decided not to accept the grant as per their values. They expressed disappointment over the outcome but recognized that protecting millions of PyPI users from potential harm was a critical responsibility for the organization.

The proposed project aims to advance Python security and provide better protections for open-source communities. Alternative paths or adjusted approaches might be considered in the future but remain under evaluation based on current priorities.
