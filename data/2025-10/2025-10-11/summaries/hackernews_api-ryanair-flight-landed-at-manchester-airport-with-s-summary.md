# Ryanair Flight Lands with Just 220 kg of Fuel in Manchester After Being Diverted Due to Storm Amy

A Ryanair flight (FR3418) took off from Pisa in Italy and was diverted due to high wind speeds caused by a storm named Storm Amy on Friday evening, landing at Manchester airport instead. The pilots, who had been taking passengers to Prestwick in Scotland, were unable to land as the winds made it impossible to reach the runway.

*   Due to extreme wind conditions, the flight landed with just 220 kg of fuel left in its tanks.
*   After three failed attempts at touchdown, the pilots requested assistance and ultimately diverted to Manchester airport.
*   The Boeing 737-800 had enough reserve fuel to complete another attempt but was unable to make it land safely.
*   The incident has been confirmed by an ongoing investigation conducted by the Air Accidents Investigation Branch (AAIB) after receiving information from Ryanair.
