# France pulls last gold held in US for $15B gain

France has withdrawn the remaining gold stored in New York and replaced it with equivalent amounts of gold bars in its vaults in Paris. The Bank of France (BdF) reports that this represents 129 tonnes, or about 5% of its total holdings.

## Background on Gold Holdings

The Bank of France had stored gold with the Federal Reserve Bank of New York since the late 1920s as part of an arrangement to store some of its bullion. Despite this agreement, France still held a small portion of its gold in the US due to technical reasons and the lack of modern international standards for storing gold.

## Gold Reserves Upgrade

Under recommendation from an internal audit, the Bank of France decided to replace its "older" or "non-standard" gold holdings with bars that meet modern international standards. This involved sending refined and transporting the gold to Europe but instead opting to sell the bars and purchase new bullion in Paris.

## Financial Impact

The move generated a capital gain of €13 billion ($15 billion), which brought France's net profit for 2025 to €8.1 billion after a loss of €7.7 billion in 2024. The overall size of its gold reserves remained unchanged at roughly 2,437 tonnes.

## Conclusion

France will now hold the higher-standard gold bars in its vaults in Paris indefinitely, ensuring compliance with international standards and generating capital gains as part of its financial performance.
