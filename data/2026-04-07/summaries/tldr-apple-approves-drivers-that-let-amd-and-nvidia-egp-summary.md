# Apple approves drivers that let AMD and Nvidia eGPUs run on Mac — software designed for AI, though, and not built for gaming | Tom's Hardware

**Apple APPROVES Drivers for Nvidia eGPUs on Mac**

*Summary*

 Apple has recently approved internal drivers that enable NVIDIA eGPUs to run on Macs, marking a significant milestone in AI processing.

**Main Points:**

* Apple has officially approved the drivers for Nvidia eGPUs on both AMD and NVIDIA-based Macs.
* The approval allows users to pair their eGPUs with Macs for Advanced Language Models (LLMs) processing.
* This makes it possible for people who may not want to own two separate PCs for work and gaming to access the eGPU capabilities.

**Notable Developments:**

* Tiny Corp, behind the tinybox AI accelerator, has become more prominent after a long-standing feud with AMD.
* The company has sold its previous high-end eGPUs, including the green v2 Blackwell with four RTX Pro 6000 Blackwell GPUs, citing design challenges and driver issues with Nvidia.

**Additional Facts:**

* High-end Apple computers, such as the Mac Studio, have seen a renewed interest in AI processing due to adoption by AI agents like OpenClaw.
* Limited eGPU options are expected for certain high-end models, including limited unified memory capacities.
