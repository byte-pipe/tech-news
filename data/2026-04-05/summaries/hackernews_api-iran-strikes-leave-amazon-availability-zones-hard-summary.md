# Iran Strikes Leave Amazon Availability Zones “Hard Down” in Bahrain and Dubai, Per Internal AWS Communication

## Summary
- Iranian missile strikes have rendered one availability zone in each of Amazon Web Services’ Bahrain and Dubai regions “hard down” and another zone “impaired but functioning.”
- AWS internal communications advise employees to **deprioritize** these regions and scale services to the minimal footprint needed for customer migration.
- The company expects the affected zones to remain unavailable for an extended, undefined period and has no timeline for normal operations.
- Amazon is urging customers with workloads in the impacted zones to migrate to other AWS regions; many have already done so.
- The strikes are part of a broader campaign by Iran’s IRGC targeting U.S. tech infrastructure, including threats toward Microsoft, Google, and Apple.
- The conflict is now in its sixth week, with multiple hits on Amazon facilities in Bahrain and the UAE, including a fire‑causing strike in Bahrain.

  - One zone in each region is classified as “hard down,” meaning completely non‑functional.

- **AWS Internal Guidance**
  - Employees are instructed to free and reserve as much capacity as possible in the affected regions.

- **Customer Communication**
