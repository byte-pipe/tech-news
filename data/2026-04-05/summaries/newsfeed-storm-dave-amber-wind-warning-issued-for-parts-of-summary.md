# Storm Dave: Amber wind warning issued for parts of UK on Saturday

## Overview
- An amber wind warning has been issued for Saturday evening as Storm Dave approaches the UK.
- The warning covers northern England, north‑west Wales and southern Scotland.
- It runs from 19:00 BST on Saturday until 03:00 on Sunday.

## Warning details
- **Amber wind warning** (19:00 – 03:00): northern England, north‑west Wales, southern Scotland.
- **Yellow wind warnings**: Northern Ireland (14:00 – 03:00), northern England/southern Scotland/northern Wales (17:00 – 07:00), almost all of Scotland (18:00 – midday Sunday), Orkney and Shetland (midnight – midday Sunday).
- **Yellow snow warning**: west Highlands, Argyll and the Western Isles (14:00 – 03:00 Sunday).

## Expected impacts
- Gusts of 60–70 mph possible across much of the UK; coastal and exposed sites could see gusts up to 80 mph.
- Risk of injuries or danger to life from flying debris.
- Good chance of power cuts.
- Up to 30 cm of snow may fall in Scotland, with strong wind‑blizzards and drifting snow likely.

## Travel and safety advice
- Stay indoors where possible and secure any loose items outside.
- RAC advises extra caution on exposed, higher routes and when overtaking high‑sided vehicles.
- ScotRail urges passengers to check journeys before travelling.
- National Highways recommends planning ahead and adjusting travel plans.
- Winds are expected to ease on Sunday, with a calmer Easter Monday forecast.

## Context
- Storm Dave follows Storm Chandra, which brought high winds and heavy snow to parts of the UK in late January.
- This is the second named storm of the year affecting the region.
