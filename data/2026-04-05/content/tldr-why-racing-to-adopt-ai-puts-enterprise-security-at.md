# Why Racing to Adopt AI Puts Enterprise Security at Risk

Delinea's Art Gilliland on Securing AI Agents With Least-Privilege Controls

Mathew J. Schwartz
 (
euroinfosec
) •
 
March 31, 2026
    
 

* Credit Eligible
* Get Permission

Art Gilliland, CEO, Delinea 

Enterprises are racing to adopt artificial intelligence, but most lack the controls to manage what they're deploying, said Art Gilliland, CEO at Delinea.

AMarch 2026 Delinea report, based on surveys of roughly 2,000 IT professionals, found that board pressure to move fast is pushing organizations to relax governance policies, even as AI agents proliferate across enterprise environments with broad, unmonitored access. The report revealed a confidence paradox at the heart of enterprise AI adoption: while 80% of organizations claimed AI readiness, only one in three had the tooling to locate all AI activity across their environment.

"You've got these automated machine things that are connected to your whole environment that can create a lot of damage really fast, and if you're not monitoring it, you don't have a way to even stop it," Gilliland said.

In this video interview with Information Security Media Group atRSAC Conference 2026, Gilliland also discussed:

* Why adversaries are using AI to attack at machine speed, requiring AI-enabled defenses;
* How Delinea's platform delivers visibility, posture scoring and real-time control over AI agents;
* Why zero standing privileges - granting access only at the moment it's needed - is the right model for securing AI.

Gilliland has extensive experience in enterprise software, cybersecurity and SaaS operations. He previously led the Symantec Enterprise Division at Broadcom and held executive roles at Skyport Systems, HP, Symantec and IMlogic, focusing on product development and go-to-market strategy.
