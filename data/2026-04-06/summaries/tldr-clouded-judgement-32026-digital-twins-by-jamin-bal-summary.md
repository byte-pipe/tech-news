# Clouded Judgement 3.20.26 – Digital Twins

## Core premise
- Digital twins are emerging as a pivotal concept for scaling AI and making knowledge actionable for agents.  
- The author argues that the main barrier to widespread AI adoption is not model intelligence but the representation of knowledge.

## Definition of a digital twin
- Originates from manufacturing: a digital replica of a physical asset for simulation and monitoring.  
- In the AI era, a digital twin is a digital representation of any form of knowledge (person’s expertise, siloed data, company history, customer behavior) that an agent can act upon.

## Primary flavors of digital twins
- **Knowledge capture (workflows)** – digitizing tacit process knowledge (e.g., quote‑to‑cash, onboarding) so agents can execute without getting stuck.  
- **Institutional memory** – preserving the judgment and pattern‑recognition of departing employees, not just static documentation.  
- **Expert twin** – replicating top performers (sales reps, SOC analysts, SREs) to provide training, real‑time coaching, and raise the overall skill floor.  
- **Customer twin** – a persistent, queryable model of the customer base for rapid market‑research scenarios.  
- **Knowledge multiplication** – converting one‑to‑one knowledge assets (single survey, single expert) into one‑to‑many resources.  
- **Personal monetization** – allowing individuals (designers, executive coaches) to scale their unique style or methodology through a digital twin, removing time constraints.

## Strategic implications
- Companies that quickly create digital twins of their critical knowledge can outpace competitors and mitigate job‑displacement concerns.  
- The “agentic era” will be driven by how effectively organizations digitize and expose hidden knowledge rather than by improvements in model performance.
