# Anthropic says Claude Code subscribers will need to pay extra for OpenClaw usage | TechCrunch

## Key Points
- Starting at noon Pacific on April 4, Claude Code subscribers can no longer use their subscription limits for third‑party tools such as OpenClaw.
- Access to OpenClaw and other third‑party harnesses will shift to a pay‑as‑you‑go model billed separately from the subscription.
- Anthropic’s head of Claude Code, Boris Cherny, said the change is due to “usage patterns” of these tools and the need to manage growth sustainably.
- OpenClaw creator Peter Steinberger, who recently joined OpenAI, noted that negotiations only delayed the price increase by a week and criticized the timing.
- Anthropic emphasizes support for open source, offering refunds to affected subscribers and citing engineering constraints as the reason for the policy shift.

## Details
- **Policy rollout:** The restriction begins with OpenClaw on April 4 and will be extended to all third‑party harnesses shortly.
- **Company rationale:** Cherny explained that existing subscription plans were not designed for the heavy usage of third‑party integrations, prompting a move to explicit, separate billing.
- **OpenClaw context:** After Steinberger’s move to OpenAI, OpenClaw remains an open‑source project with OpenAI support. Steinberger and board member Dave Morin attempted to negotiate with Anthropic but only achieved a brief postponement.
- **Anthropic’s stance:** Cherny highlighted the team’s “big fans of open source” attitude, mentioning personal contributions to improve prompt cache efficiency for OpenClaw. He framed the change as an engineering necessity and offered full refunds to subscribers unaware of the limitation.
- **Industry backdrop:** OpenAI recently shut down its Sora video generation app to reallocate computing resources, reflecting a broader focus on serving software engineers and enterprises that rely on tools like Claude Code.
