# Deontay Wilder calls out Anthony Joshua after Chisora victory

## Fight outcome
- Deontay Wilder (40) defeated Derek Chisora by split decision at London’s O2 Arena.  
- Scores: 115–111 and 115–113 for Wilder; 115–112 for Chisora.  
- It marked Wilder’s 45th win in 50 professional fights, though his performance has been viewed as a decline since his trilogy with Tyson Fury.

## Post‑fight call‑out
- After the bout, Wilder walked past Anthony Joshua, fist‑bumped him and said, “let’s do it,” adding “He’s scared” as he left.  
- Wilder later clarified he told Joshua, “Now let’s get it on,” and expressed readiness to fight any heavyweight, describing himself as “Mr Clean” who wants to “clean up the whole division.”

## Anthony Joshua’s recent activity
- Joshua (36) was ringside supporting friend Chisora, who is expected to retire after this fight.  
- He last fought on 19 December, stopping Jake Paul in the sixth round, improving his record to 29‑4.  
- Joshua has indicated interest in a future bout with long‑term rival Tyson Fury, who is set to fight Arslanbek Makhmudov on 11 April.

## Promotion and future possibilities
- Eddie Hearn, Joshua’s promoter, said the Wilder‑Joshua fight could become a “big fight” if Wilder wins in style.  
- A Wilder‑Joshua matchup had been planned for 2024 but was cancelled after Wilder’s upset loss to Joseph Parker.  
- Hearn later confirmed Joshua would fight Wilder “no problem” despite the icy stare.

## Additional context
- Joshua has been out of the spotlight following a car crash in Nigeria on 29 December that killed two friends; he sustained minor injuries.  
- Wilder mentioned a possible small fracture in his hand after the Chisora fight.
