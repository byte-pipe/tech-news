## UVU will remain closed until Monday

Rudy Chinchilla

Utah Valley University will remain closed until Monday following the on-campus shooting of Kirk.

“All classes (in-person or virtual), campus events, and administrative operations will be suspended during this time,” the schoolsaid on its website.

Coursework, assignment deadlines and exams for students will be delayed to accommodate the closure, the school said, adding that employees will still receive their regular pay.

Inan alert, the school said there is no ongoing threat to the campus. People who left essential possessions behind during the rush after the shooting can pick their items up today at the Young Living Alumni Center, the university said.

## ‘Do you run?’ Witnesses who took cover after shooting describe chaos

Phil Helsel

Taylor Dufur and his wife, Haley Bermingham, were about 30 feet from Kirk when the fatal gunshot rang out, and the first reactions in the crowd near them were to get down.

“We heard the gunshot, and people just started dropping,” Dufur told NBC News’ Tom Llamas by phone during live coverage. He said his biggest concern was lying on top of his wife to protect her.

“I looked over and saw him as he was falling out of his chair, and I saw the blood coming out of his neck,” Dufur said. “It was just — people panicking, kind of chaos.”
