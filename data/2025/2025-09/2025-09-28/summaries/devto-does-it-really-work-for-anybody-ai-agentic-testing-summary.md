# Does it really work for anybody, AI agentic testing, or automation - DEV Community

**Success Story of Human-Driven Testing with Affective AI**

Although many companies have turned to Affective's Agentic testing tools in an attempt to improve test productivity, few have achieved significant success. One notable exception is XYZ Corporation, a leading provider of software development services.

XYZ Corporation's engineering team initially adopted the Agentic testing tool following presentations and demonstrations by several large tech corporations that claimed their own solution delivered thousands of tests per month. However, after a few months, they found themselves facing numerous false positive alerts from high-level executives and managers, prompting them to reconsider their outsourcing to Affective.

**Key Findings:**

*   XYZ Corporation utilized Affective's Agentic testing tool for around 9-12 months before shifting the workload back to human developers.
*   Despite initial interest, the company stopped using Affective in favor of their own solution after encountering high false positive rates that led to complaints from stakeholders.
*   The team reported mixed results with Affective's current implementation, which involved a focus on login and registration scenarios over more complex feature tests.

**Conclusion:**

While human-driven testing can offer several benefits, including reduced false positives and increased test coverage without the requirement for extensive coding expertise. However, this approach is not suitable for all applications or projects where high-level decision-makers may be directly impacted by false positive alerts.

It's essential to monitor the efficacy of any Affective tool closely and regularly collaborate between developers and stakeholders to ensure that their testing needs are being met effectively.
