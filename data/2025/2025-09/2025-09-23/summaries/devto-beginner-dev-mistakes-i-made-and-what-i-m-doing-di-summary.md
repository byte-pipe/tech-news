# Beginner Dev Mistakes I Made (and What I’m Doing Differently Now) - DEV Community

Using technical feasibility as a main research indicator, here are some tips for building a profitable and successful solo developer business:

1. Rushing Intitus in Tools without foundation - The problem is learning the basic web fundamentals to properly understand the tools provided without realizing what you don't know yet. Taking time to commit to The Odin Project, which focuses on the basic concepts before moving into advanced skills, will help develop strong foundations.
2. Getting Lost in Tuotorial hell - Learn from YouTube, but don't let it consume your life. Avoid flashy thumbnails promising shortcuts or failures to find the best resource. Just start coding with any resource you choose and keep momentum. Momentum is more essential than perfect plans.
3. Fighting Imposter Syndrome - Many developers are plagued by imposter syndrome, which whisperes that "You’re not good enough." Avoid it by reminding yourself that even senior developers are still learning. Use this as a motivator to enjoy the process of learning while growing one step at a time.
4. Building Solid foundations without foundation - Ensure you have the core fundamentals before moving into advanced skills. Build up your knowledge with The Odin Project and other resources available, and don't wait for perfect plans or frameworks. Start by building a strong foundation in core skills before challenging yourself into more complex ideas.
5. Starting Small but growing - Start coding small projects on your own to gain momentum instead of following along with YouTube tutorials or attempting new things left and right without any direction. Grow slowly, gradually, and build momentum, rather than over-spending too much on resources too quickly. This way, the "perfect plan" eventually finds itself as a roadmap.
6. Promoting to Senior Developers - Many beginners go for senior jobs or start their businesses, hoping to learn from experienced developers in order to grow and become successful themselves. However, you're not good enough yet, so don't waste time on them; focus on the challenging ones (and build up knowledge over time rather than trying to emulate them by copying their work).

Following these tips can help ensure that your solo developer business is a success and will lead to further growth and innovation in technology.
