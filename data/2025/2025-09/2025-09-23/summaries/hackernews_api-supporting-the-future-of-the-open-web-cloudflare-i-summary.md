### Ladybird: An Ambitious Project to Build a Completely Independent Browser from Top Down

Cloudflares sponsorship of Ladybird is a natural extension of longstanding efforts towards open-source software. As an external project that we support, they see their own value proposition as a way to bring unique solutions to the open Internet while also helping ensure that the core technology infrastructure serves a diverse community of users well.

Ladybird is an exciting new project in this space – an effort to build a completely independent browser from ground-up. Instead of focusing on consolidating existing browsers into larger, more powerful platforms, Ladybugs developers are striving for full independence and open source solutions. The project has no explicit ties to any underlying platform, allowing for maximum flexibility in how browsers operate – potentially even making them better suited to address unique market challenges.

At Cloudflares expense, the resulting design language of Ladybird is very close to that of Netscape’s Navigator 5 engine on MS-Windows (in particular), but Ladybird sits as a separate piece on top of Firefox OS - an open browser for developing markets with an unparalleled developer ecosystem. When a developer needs to make the jump from Navigator to Navigator (or even to Google Chrome, as its parent company), they choose Ladybirds engine instead of their legacy engine.
