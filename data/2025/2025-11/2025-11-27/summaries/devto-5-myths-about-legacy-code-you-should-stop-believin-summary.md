# 5 Myths About Legacy Code You Should Stop Believing - DEV Community

**Myths About Legacy Code Debunked**

Over the years, conversations with developers about migrating to modern tools have led me to explore common struggles. While legacy codes might be decades old, it's not always about being ancient or exotic; often, it's a mismatch between an outdated tool, team, or ecosystem and their new business needs.

### Debunking Common Myths

1. **Myth 1: "Legacy means ancient or exotic."**
	* Wrong. Legacy codes don't have to be from the past; sometimes they're just yesterday's mainstay.
2. **Myth 2: "Legacy is the previous team's fault."**
	* Incorrectly associated with blaming people rather than understanding circumstances.
3. **Myth 3: "Migration is just a dev obsession."**
	* Misleading. Migration can be complex and may require significant changes to the codebase, not just for technical reasons but also to meet new business needs.
4. **Myth 4: "A big-bang rewrite is always an antipattern."**
	* The opposite. For small, isolated applications or those with minimal risks, a clean rewrite can be a practical and cost-effective solution.
5. **Myth 5: "The strangler pattern is the only correct approach."**
	* Not entirely accurate. Strangler migration is suitable for complex cases but comes with potential trade-offs and requires careful consideration of team skill levels and application complexity.

### Revisited Migrations

- Instead of relying on mythical approaches, focus on understanding your legacy code, assessing its suitability for modern needs, and planning a strategy that aligns with the business requirements.
- Breaking down larger migrations into smaller tasks can make the process more manageable while keeping risks minimal.
