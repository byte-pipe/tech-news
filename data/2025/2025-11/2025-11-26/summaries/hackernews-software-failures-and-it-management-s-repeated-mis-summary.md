# Software Failures and IT Management's Repeated Mistakes - IEEE Spectrum

**Software Management Failures: Why AI Won't Solve Them**

As IT managers, we often hear that software development, modernization, and operational improvements require artificial intelligence (AI) tools and coding partners. However, this sentiment is misguided. Despite significant advancements in technology, the reality is that software failures are universally unbiased and consistently occur worldwide.

**The Problem with AI: Hard Limits on AI's Role**

To control large-scale IT projects successfully, there are hard limits on what AI can bring to the table:

*   **Limited understanding of complex systems**: AI may struggle to grasp intricate interactions between various systems, including organizational politics.
*   **Inability to handle complexity and risk**: AI may not be able to comprehensively evaluate project risks or accurately manage system integrations.
*   **Human factor limitations**: AI may only have knowledge up to a certain point; they cannot replace human judgment, empathy, and decision-making.

**What Software Project Management Failures Really Are**

Software failures are frequently driven by:

1.  **Unrealistic project goals**
2.  **Inability to handle complexity**
3.  **Uncoupling of functions and risks"
4.  **Human error or biases"

**Lessons Learned from Historical Failure Patterns**

Historical examples, such as those noted by Stephen Andriole:
*   Cases where IT projects failed due to miscommunicated goals
*   Discoveries after-action reports highlighting avoidable failure factors

A lack of attention to the root causes of software failures and repeated mistakes highlights why we cannot rely solely on AI solutions.
