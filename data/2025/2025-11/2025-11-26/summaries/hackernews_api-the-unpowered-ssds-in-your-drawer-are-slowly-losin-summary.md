# The unpowered SSDs in your drawer are slowly losing your data

## Key Points:
- Single-level cell (SLC) SSDs can safely store data for up to 10 years without power, while multi-Level Cell (MLC) and Three-Dimensional Epitaxial (3D) NOR Flash-based SSDs retain data for 5, 2, and 3 years, respectively.
- Non-volatile memory cells retain data temporarily, allowing them to be safely stored without power, but the duration is limited by write cycles and the type of NAND flash used.
- Most consumer SSDs store data long-term under these limitations, making them less reliable for extended periods.
- However, enterprise-level high-capacity SSDs can operate reliably for extended timeframes with proper maintenance.

## Summary:

* Single-level cell (SLC) SSDs are sufficient for primary storage but may lose data due to voltage loss after extended use without power.
* Multilevel Cell (MLC) and Three-Dimensional Epitaxial (3D) Non-Volatile Memory (NVM) SSDs can retain data for 5, 2, and 3 years under proper conditions.
* Consumer SSDs are less reliable due to the limitations on voltage loss retention times.
* Enterprise-level high-capacity SSDs offer greater reliability with proper maintenance.

## Additional Information:

* The reliability of a single SSDDP is more about its specific model rather than the type of NAND flash or manufacturing process, although these factors also influence overall write cycle count and power consumption.
* Most people storing multiple data sets in their SSDs do not encounter permanent loss of data due to intermittent power use; however, those living far from home may lose their files over time even in a secured location where they are not accessed frequently.
