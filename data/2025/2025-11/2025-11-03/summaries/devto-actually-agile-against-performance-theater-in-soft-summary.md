# Actually Agile: Against Performance Theater in Software Development - DEV Community

**The State of Agile: Not an Agile Process**

* The term "agile" has become synonymous with performance theater
* Daily standups, sprint reviews, retrospectives, and backlogs have replaced true collaboration
* Jira tickets instead of working software, certifications instead of experience, and PowerPoint presentations instead of real innovation

**Reflection and Reevaluation**

Agility didn't emerge by chance; it was deliberately captured through industry practices. Roles like Scrum Master, Product Owner, Agile Coach, Release Train Engineer, have become omnipresent. This has led to more intermediaries than ever before, mediating interactions and limiting direct communication.

**The Certification Cartel: A Barrier to True Agility**

Continuous Professional Development (CPD) courses are now ubiquitous, priced high as they promise equal levels of expertise. Certification programs ensure conformity rather than genuine understanding. With such high costs and stringent requirements, only professionals can afford to pursue specialized knowledge. Those asking "What does this mean?" get labeled as unsophisticated.

**Understanding Agile: Moving Beyond Language**

The term "agile" originally came from the Latin verb "agere," meaning "to act withrightness; not speed." In nature, true agility involves acting deliberately rather than rushing to action. It relies on discernment and effective decision-making, not simply forceful execution or rapid iteration.

**Reevaluating Agile: The Root Imperative**

Rather than adopting another methodology, ask questions from the outset before each sprint, meeting, or decision:

* Are we heading in a clear direction?
* What must remain unchanged?
* How can we best achieve our objectives?

By reflecting on these fundamentals and asking ourselves whether agile values are still being genuinely applied or merely superficially practiced, we may begin to move beyond the limitations of performance theater and toward true agility.
