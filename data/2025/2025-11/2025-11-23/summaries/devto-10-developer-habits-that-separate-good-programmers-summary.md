# 10 Developer Habits That Separate Good Programmers From Great Ones - DEV Community

## 10 Developer Habits That Separate Good Programmers From Great Ones

The difference between good programmers and great ones lies not in their knowledge or expertise but in the daily habits they cultivate. These invisible practices, such as reading code far more than writing it, forming a habit to deliberately practice productive habits in the absence of immediate results.

### Habit 1: They Read Code Far More Than They Write It

*   Great developers read and analyze other people's code without condoning or replicating, whereas junior developers rarely engage with existing code beyond simple fixes.
*   This habit shapes their understanding of software engineering principles and improves problem-solving skills.

This habit is the foundation upon which great programming skill is built. To separate good programmers from great ones, you must commit to reading code extensively before writing a new line of code.

### Habit 2: They Take Code Reviews Seriously

*   The act of receiving constructive feedback on their codes can be intimidating and uncomfortable.
*   Taking code reviews seriously helps developers refine their skills through peer-to-peer learning and accountability, preventing mistakes from being repeated in other projects.

By practicing this habit, developers become more self-assured when sharing their work with others and adapt the insights gained during reviews into future contributions to their teams.

### Habit 3: They Practice Debugging without Getting Angry

*   Effective debugging involves focusing on understanding the issue rather than becoming defensive or frustrated.
*   This approach helps developers improve their problem-solving skills, identify common pitfalls, and make data-driven decisions.

When frustration creeps in during development cycles, reminding oneself that everyone has bad days helps. It's essential to shift focus from individual problems to systemic root issues and seeks solutions through collaboration with peers and experienced colleagues.

### Habit 4: They Work on One Project at a Time

*   The discipline required to dedicate quality time and energy to any single project is key in identifying areas of improvement.
*   By focusing solely on one task, developers avoid multitasking pitfalls and allow themselves sufficient downtime for creativity, organization, and maintenance.

This habit enables the development team to concentrate efforts on problems rather than spreading itself thin across many unrelated projects.
