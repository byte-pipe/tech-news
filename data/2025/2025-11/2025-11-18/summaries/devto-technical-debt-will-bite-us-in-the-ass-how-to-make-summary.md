# "Technical Debt Will Bite Us in the Ass": How to Make Non-Technical Stakeholders Actually Care - DEV Community

**The Technical Debt Conundrum**

We struggle to make non-technical stakeholders care about refactoring code because they don't understand its impact on our ability to deliver features quickly and meet business goals. However, this disconnect lies in the translation gap between technical jargon and what drives stakeholders' decisions.

### The Problem: Ignoring Technical Debt

Technical debt accumulates over time due to changes in architecture, and ignoring it can lead to:

* Slowing progress on current projects
* Increasing financial costs as we maintain legacy codebase

Product managers blame us for not addressing this issue, which creates a rift in relationships and understanding of the problems.

### The Moment Everything Changed

Changing communication style helped bridge the gap. When product managers talk about technical debt as a solution without context or understanding, it makes them seem like we're only caring about features.

We should demonstrate that refactoring code addresses core business needs: delivering promised features to customers, meeting revenue targets, and maintaining customer satisfaction scores.

### Finding Shared Language

To overcome the communication challenge:

* We need to use technical terms in a way that resonates with stakeholders who value efficiency.
* Our vocabulary should be aligned with what drives stakeholder decisions (features shipped, revenue generated).

This requires a shift from focused on features ("we need to refactor") to understanding core business outcomes. It's not about dumbing things down but finding language that connects us across different departments and priorities.

### Key Takeaways

- **Communication matters**: When discussing technical debt, we must understand its impact on the team's ability to deliver projects.
- **Linguistic barrier exists**: Technical terms may be confusing for non-technical stakeholders; reframing discussions in their language can help overcome this gap.
- **Find shared goals and values**: Aligning our understanding of business objectives with technical terminology will strengthen our relationships and improve communication.
