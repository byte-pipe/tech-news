# Agile Theater: Exposing the Performance

## Introduction
In most organizations, "agile" has become associated with performance theater - an elaborate show of words and rituals rather than actual software development. This article challenges that notion by exploring what agility truly means in practice.

## The Problem
Daily standups that drag on for 45 minutes, sprint reviews where PowerPoint slides are more important than working software, and retrospectives filled with non-readable notes are all examples of the "agile" practices being used today. Jira tickets replacing actual thinking, backlogs replaced by architectures - this is not agility.

## A Brief History
Agility didn't truly die; it was hijacked by consulting firms. The concept of scaling agile practices to large teams has been amplified and commercialized. This transformation has led to a culture where "certification" and "conformity" have become essential components of an "agile" organization.

## The Real Issues
- **Overemphasis on Process**: Organizations prioritize following established protocols over actual problem-solving.
- **Loss of Team Autonomy**: Certified professionals act as gatekeepers, stifling open communication.
- **Growth through Jargon**: Tools, acronyms, and certifications obscure the essence of software development.

## The Reality
In nature, agility is achieved by acting directly with accuracy, rather than resorting to elaborate shows. The cat pounces without rushing into battle, and a tree bends in storms while resisting its own gravity.

## Reversing the Script
Instead of looking for answers in methodologies or tools, let's explore questions that will fundamentally alter our approaches:

- **What Direction Does the Project Really Need?**
    - What core values do we want to uphold?
-   Are there specific outcomes we aim to achieve?

## Conclusion
In conclusion, "agile" has been misinterpreted as a series of rituals and techniques, when in reality it is about actually being agile. By examining our behavior and questioning where progress truly lies, we can break free from the shackles of performance theater and rediscover what true agility means.
