# Developers vs AI: How Smart Tools Make Us Dumber Coders - DEV Community

## The Dark Side of Intelligence: Overreliance on AI and the Consequences
As technology advances, we often rely more heavily on artificial intelligence (AI) tools, such as writing assistants like Copilot or ChatGPT. However, this dependence can have negative consequences for developers who are not aware of these limitations.

### Problem Solving → Pattern Matching
When using AI-powered tools, it becomes easy to focus solely on recognizing patterns and automating tasks, rather than exploring edge cases and debugging code thoroughly. This can lead to stagnant growth as developers become less skilled at solving problems intuitively.

A key difference between relying on an AI tool and developing a skill through trial and error is the distinction between learning and prompting. We ask for help when we're unsure or don't have time to solve something ourselves.

### From Learning to Prompting
In today's AI-centric development environment, it's easy to bypass the process of learning by simply asking an AI for an answer. This approach lacks both understanding and practical experience, resulting in knowledge transmission rather than generation.

To stay sharp in developing intelligence using AI, try these steps:

*   **Mentorship**: Work alongside experienced developers who can teach you how to think critically beyond what the tool provides.
*   **Practice**: Set aside time to tackle challenges on your own before asking for help. This helps build resilience and encourages critical thinking.
*   **Self-Reflection**: Regularly track when AI genuinely assists versus just saving time by automating tasks.
