# Actually Agile: Against Performance Theater in Software Development - DEV Community

## Agile Performance Theater vs. True Agility

Although agile has become popular, most organizations have strayed from its true principles.

### The Evolution of Agile

The concept first began with the idea of self-organizing teams and was meant to reflect real-world complexity. However, over time:

* Daily standups became an opportunity for people to say the right words
* Sprint reviews turned into PowerPoint presentations, and retrospective discussions went unproductive
* Backlogs were replaced by architectures, and Jira tickets filled up with tasks instead of actual work
* Certifications substituted for genuine experience

### The Root Cause: Performance Theater

This transformation started when more roles were introduced – Scrum Master, Product Owner, Agile Coach, Release Train Engineer. Organizations took advantage of this system but neglected the fundamental question:

* Are we actually building better software?

## Agility vs. Conformity

Agility as a technique has become synonymous with process and tools rather than genuine collaboration and continuous improvement.

### The Certification Cartel: A Barrier to Informed Decision-Making

Certifications like Scrum Master, Product Owner, or Agile Coach came with hefty prices (up to $2,000), indicating that true agility is not a learned skill but an expensive luxury. Moreover, the training became unnecessary as one could simply recite the manual and conform.

### Jargon as Gatekeeping: Suppressing Communication

The adoption of Jira terminology like "Sprint" or "Velocity" hindered communication because it replaced authentic interaction patterns with meaningless buzzwords. Those who dared to question agile practices were labeled as ignorant, adding fuel to the fire of performance theater.

### Replacing True Understanding with Process-Driven Thinking

We equate agile concepts solely through numerical values ("story points," "economies of scale"), ignoring other essential aspects like the relationship between problem and solution (as observed in nature, it's not enough to know how many story points a task requires).

## What Agile Means Today

Agile is no longer associated with collaborative problem-solving but rather with rigid process guidelines, tools enforced by intermediaries.

### A Reflection on True Agility

True agility involves moving at the pace of the market ("acts with speed"), rather than trying to optimize for speed and efficiency without understanding the real cause.

### Re-evaluating Questions

Instead of following methodologies like Scrum or Agile, organizations should ask questions before committing resources:

* Does this project's future depend on meeting our current version control?
* Are we prioritizing functionality over feasibility?

By reframing the conversation around genuine collaboration, continuous improvement, and understanding, organizations can break free from performance theater and focus on building better software.
