# Jean-Baptiste Kempf receives the European SFS Award 2025 at SFSCON - FSFE

**Jean-Baptiste Kempf Receives European SFS Award 2025 at SFSCON**

*   The Free Software Foundation Europe (FSFE) and LUGBZ present Jean-Baptiste Kempf with the European SFS Award 2025, recognizing his long-term dedication to the VLC project.
*   Born in 1996 as a student initiative that evolved into an essential media player across billions of users worldwide.
*   Kempf joined the project as a student and transformed it into the video player we rely on today.

**Key Highlights:**

*   This award honors individuals whose work has strengthened software freedom, community building, and ethical foundations of technology in Europe.
*   Previous recipients include Francesca Rossi (2023) for her leadership with Nextcloud and posthumously awarded Bram Moolenaar (2024).
*   TheEuropean SFS Award is presented jointly by the FSFE and LUGBZ to recognize outstanding contributions.

### Recipient's Quotes:

*   "For many people running non-free operating systems, it was the very first Free Software they ever installed. For many people running Free Software, it saved them from installing and booting into a proprietary operating system."
*   Jean-Baptiste Kempf: "It’s small, fast, friendly, and seems to 'understand everything you throw at it.' I have always thought of it as 'the program that eats everything', said Raphael Barbieri."
