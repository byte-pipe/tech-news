# Collaboration sucks - by Charles Cook

## Collaboration Sucks: The Dangers of Over-Dependence on Others

### Be the Driver

As Charles Cook notes, the phrase "if you want to go far, go together" is often a tongue-in-cheek warning against over-reliance on others. In business, this can manifest in two ways: too much collaboration that doesn't add value or a lack of effective execution.

## The Risks of Collaboration

* Over-collaboration leads to inefficient use of time and resources.
* It erodes individual motivation, confidence, and effectiveness.
* Feedback is invaluable, but forced into unnecessary discussions can slow progress.

### Be the Driver Myth: Reality Check

Charles Cook shares his own experience with "collaboration sucks" in the company PostHog. Despite its name being a mantra for prioritizing autonomy over others, many companies end up struggling as a result. When workers are free to work independently and make decisions without external input.

## Overlooked Risks of Collaboration

In contrast, when individuals are given autonomy and minimal input from others, they:

* Show greater self-motivation and effectiveness
* Are more likely to build on their own ideas rather than relying solely on feedback
* Can move faster towards completion and achieve better results in the long run.

### Benefits of Being a Good Driver

Charles Cook suggests that understanding when to slow down and explain things is key. While it may seem counter-intuitive, this approach allows individuals to focus on what they know best – making decisions and driving progress forward.

## Conclusion

In conclusion, while collaboration has its benefits in some contexts, over-reliance on others can lead to inefficient outcomes. It's essential to find a balance between autonomy and necessary input from others to maximize individual strengths and achieve better work as a team.
