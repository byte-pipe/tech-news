# 10 Developer Habits That Separate Good Programmers From Great Ones

As a developer with over a decade of experience, I've observed the differences between good programmers and great ones. While knowing more algorithms or memorizing syntax can be useful, it's not about natural talent. The real separation happens in the subtle habits and daily practices that compound over time.

## Habit 1: They Read Code Far More Than They Write It

* When mentoring junior developers, I ask them if they read other people's code extensively compared to writing their own.
* The answer is often the same: "not much" or "rarely".

This habit separates great from good programmers because:

* Great code readers analyze and learn from others' work
* They understand how to critique and improve existing code

## Habit 2: They Write Tests Early in Their Career

Most experienced developers write tests early in their careers. However, this doesn't happen immediately.

At the beginning of my career, it was a struggle for me to put myself in someone else's shoes. I'd stare at a bug or a confusing problem and feel lost. Only after years of experience did I start thinking like code readers and writer.

If you want to become a great developer:

* Write tests early on
* Understand the impact of test-driven development

## Habit 3: They Refactor Code Intelligently, After Reviewing Documentation

A well-coded framework isn't just about the implementation; it's also about how developers handle complexity.

When I was learning Python in college, I used to spend hours reading and re-reading documentation. It took a lot of practice or help from someone more experienced before I could confidently write code without error.

If you want to become a great developer:

* Refactor code intelligently after doing extensive review
* Understand the role of dependencies and libraries in your project

## Habit 4: They Work with Their Mentorship as Much As for an Outside Project

The relationship between mentors and mentees can help develop valuable skills, but this doesn't ensure success.

I still consider my mentor when I solve a difficult problem. It's not just about learning from someone else; it's also about gaining the experience and confidence to take on new challenges.

If you want to become a great developer:

* Work with your mentor as much as for an outside project
* Learn how to communicate technical issues effectively

## Habit 5: They Write About Software Development

Many aspiring developers view writing blogs or articles as an easy way to demonstrate their expertise. However, this isn't just about sharing knowledge; it's also about documenting personal experience.

Writing a blog can be a good opportunity for someone to share how they overcame problems and developed coding skills.

If you want to become a great developer:

* Write about software development
* Document your personal experiences and code

## Habit 6: They Are Not Afraid of Breaking Stuff or Failing

A true believer in this habit has no qualms about experimenting with new ideas, even if they fail. Instead, they learn from their mistakes and apply that knowledge to future projects.

If you want to become a great developer:

* Be not afraid to break stuff or fail
* Use failure as an opportunity for growth and improvement
