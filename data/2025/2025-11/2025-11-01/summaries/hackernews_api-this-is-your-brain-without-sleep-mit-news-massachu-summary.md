# This is your brain without sleep | MIT News | Massachusetts Institute of Technology

# Brain Function and Sleep Deprivation Research

## Introduction
A new study from MIT has investigated the effects of sleep deprivation on brain function, revealing a key aspect of how our brains process attention during periods of lack of sleep.

#### Key Findings:

* During periods of sleep deprivation, a significant wave of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) flows out of the brain, potentially washing away waste products that have built up during the day.
* This CSF flow is believed to be necessary for maintaining a healthy brain function and occurs due to attempts by the body to catch up on this cleansing process initiated after sleep deprivation.

## Process
The study found that when individuals are sleep-deprived:

* Their attention fails despite having an intruding wave of CSF flow in certain moments.
* This loss of attention is linked to a shift away from normal brain activity patterns during sleep.

#### Consequences and Implications

* The research suggests that while sleep deprivation has numerous negative effects, including impairments in cognitive functions and mood disturbances, there may be potential mechanisms for mitigating some of these impacts by maintaining or restoring proper CSF flow during sleep.
* Future studies could explore the specific roles of different brain components, such as the olfactory system or the brainstem, involved in this process.
