# "Technical Debt Will Bite Us in the Ass": How to Make Non-Technical Stakeholders Actually Care - DEV Community

# Refactoring Technical Debt: A Critical Communication Breakdown
===========================================================

## Key Points

*   Technical debt can lead to "architecture getting messy" and slowing down development.
*   Product managers may not understand the impact of technical debt on customer satisfaction scores, revenue, and features shipping deadlines.
*   Effective communication is crucial to resolve this issue through reframing the conversation around non-technical stakeholders and visualization techniques.

## The Problem: Non-Technical Communication
----------------------------------------

Non-technical stakeholders often misunderstand or misinterpret technical information. This leads to delays in feature development and affects project outcomes. Key areas of concern include:

*   Using technical jargon that resonates with other business units.
*   Describing technical debt as "optional polish" rather than a pressing issue with performance implications.

## The Solution: Reframing the Conversation
-----------------------------------------

To address this issue, it's essential to break the communication loop and develop shared language. This entails:

*   Changing the terminology used by product managers to better understand customer value and features.
*   Using storytelling techniques and analogies to explain technical concepts in a non-technical manner.

## Leveraging Visualization Tools
-------------------------------

Implementing visualization tools can help stakeholders grasp technical debt's impact on the codebase. By employing such methods, it becomes easier for users to visualize their "wounds" (i.e., issues with half-cut-off fingers) and understand why changes are necessary for a complete solution.

The following is a brief summary of the main points:

*   Technical debt accumulates due to incomplete or poorly planned solutions.
*   Product managers may not comprehend how technical debt affects customer satisfaction metrics, revenue, and deadlines.
*   Reframing conversations around non-technical stakeholders often requires bridging language gaps.
*   Effective communication through storytelling with analogies can explain complex technical problems in simple terms.
