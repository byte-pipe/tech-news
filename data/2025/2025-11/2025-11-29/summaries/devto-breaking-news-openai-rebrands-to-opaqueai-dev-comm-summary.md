# Breaking News: OpenAI Rebrands to OpaqueAI - DEV Community

## Summary
### Introduction

In September 2025, OpenAI announced Developer Mode, a beta feature promising "full Model Context Protocol (MCP) client support for all tools, both read and write." However, within hours after its launch on the open-source GitHub repository, the announcement was deleted, and reports began flooding in of significant issues with the MCP-client, including tool calls failing, malformed resources, missing handshake responses, and reproducible crashes. By October 2025, OpenAI's implementation had collapsed, leading to a complete halt in their flagship product.

### Key Points

*   The launch of Developer Mode resulted in significant technical difficulties for developers.
*   The problems were public, reproducible, and ignored by the company.
*   Critical issues affected custom connectors, stable MCP servers, and many developer platforms.
*   OpenAI's own demo apps did not function correctly.

### Detailed Analysis

*   The failure of Developer Mode marked a turning point in OpenAI's relationship with developers. For two months, their flagship product threw 424 errors, deleted features, and rolled back fixes in production.
*   The company failed to address these issues despite receiving numerous development attempts and bug reports from the OpenAI Dev community, who had built custom connectors and tools using Developer Mode.

### Collapse of Developmental Progress

*   On October 7, 2025, OpenAI launched the Apps SDK preview, which contained warnings about potential "serious" issues with the MCP-client.
*   Despite this warning, the company continued to release code that contributed to the collapse of their implementation.
