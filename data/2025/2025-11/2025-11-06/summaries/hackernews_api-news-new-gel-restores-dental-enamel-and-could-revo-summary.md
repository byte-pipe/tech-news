# News - New gel restores dental enamel and could revolutionise tooth repair - University of Nottingham

**New Gel Restores Dental Enamel, Offering Promising Solution for Preventive and Restorative Dental Treatment**

A new gel developed by scientists from the University of Nottingham's School of Pharmacy and Department of Chemical and Environmental Engineering has shown significant potential in repairing and regenerating tooth enamel. This bioinspired material can potentially strengthen healthy enamel, prevent decay, and even restore demineralized or eroded surfaces.

**Key Benefits:**

* Rapidly applies to teeth as standard fluoride treatments
* Mimics natural proteins guiding dental enamel growth in infancy
* Creates a thin and robust layer that fills holes and cracks, promoting calcium and phosphate ion uptake for controlled mineralization
* Enables epitaxial mineralization, allowing for the organized integration of new minerals into healthy tissue

**New Material Can Be Applied:**

* On exposed dentine, growing an enamel-like layer that treats hypersensitivity or enhances dental bonding
* Under standard fluoride treatments, potentially reversing tooth decay and associated problems

**Mechanical Properties Tested:**

* Regenerated tissues withstand simulated conditions, indicating potential for effective long-term treatment

"The material promotes the growth of crystals in an integrated and organized manner, recovering the architecture of natural healthy enamel," explains Dr. Abshar Hasan.

This breakthrough has significant implications for dental treatment, offering a promising solution for preventing and repairing tooth decay, even for cases where regrowth is not feasible due to age or other factors.

**Ongoing Research:**

Further studies will focus on optimizing the material's properties, potential applications, and scalability to contribute to effective dental care worldwide.
