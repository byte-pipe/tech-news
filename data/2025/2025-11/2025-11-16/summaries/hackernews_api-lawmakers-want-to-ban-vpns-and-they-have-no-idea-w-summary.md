# Lawmakers Seek to Ban VPNs Amid Security Concerns

Lawmakers in Wisconsin, Michigan, and other states are pushing forward with legislation to ban Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), citing concerns about government access to users' online activities. This move aims to clamp down on websites that use VPNs to protect user privacy, particularly in cases where content is deemed "harmful to minors."

# Background: Age Verification Laws

The proposal in question is linked to a Wisconsin bill, A.B. 105/S.B. 130, which requires websites that distribute potentially exploitative content to implement an age verification system and block users connected via VPNs. This bill seeks to broaden the definition of "harmful to minors," extending its scope beyond previously limited categories like speech.

# Legislative Approach

In response, lawmakers aim to ban all use of VPNs, not solely targeting those with age-verification requirements. Wisconsin's proposed law will broadly require websites to block access for users connected via VPN if they are deemed to be visiting areas where the government has assumed jurisdiction over online activities:
