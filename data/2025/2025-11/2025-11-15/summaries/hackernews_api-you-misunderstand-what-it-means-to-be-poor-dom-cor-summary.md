# You misunderstand what it means to be poor - Dom Corriveau

## You misunderstand what it means to be poor - Dom Corriveau

### The Problem with Advice from Those Who Are Poor
The more I speak about being poor, the more I realize how many people misunderstand what it truly means to be impoverished compared to being broke. Their advice often stems from a good place, but it needs some refining. Here's an attempt to address each of the issues these individuals face when providing guidance.

### When You're Broke
When someone is broke, they have money; however, not enough goes towards discretionary activities like traveling or enjoying leisure pursuits. In extreme cases, this scarcity limits individual freedom to make choices that truly enhance one's life. For instance:

•   They can afford basic necessities but cannot afford entertainment options such as going to the movies.
•   They may even go home from work and stay in their house if they're not feeling motivated.

This is not a time to save for the future; it's about being able to make choices today that truly impact one's quality of life.

### The Endless Runner Game
In the context of poverty, this means no relief on money earned. Instead of enjoying every dollar and then spending less to enjoy new things in the future:

•   It's more likely that someone who is broke will continue to live below their means if they start saving for a better life.
•   This mindset can be likened to playing an endless game, where progress appears to accelerate as one gets closer to an unattainable goal.

However, this "game" is never won and often leads to financial instability over time.

### Poor People Are Not Lazy
The misconception that people who are poor are lazy is misleading. It's essential to recognize the skills required for any activity and whether or not someone has the means to afford it:

•   Investing in one's education is a clear example of investing effort into one's future.
•   Even something as simple as learning how to use technology properly requires time and dedication.

While there can be skill gaps, this doesn't make an individual lazy; rather, they are facing unique challenges.

### The Truth About Getting Help
When considering employment or financial aid, it's not about lacking skills. It's about having financial means:

•   Many people would find alternative ways to meet their needs due to financial constraints.
•   They might even take on extra work or volunteer for projects that help pay back loans or grant the family enough income.

This doesn't mean you're a failure; it just means there are other options at play in situations where people feel they need immediate assistance.
