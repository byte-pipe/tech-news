# The Patent Office Is About To Make Bad Patents Untouchable | Electronic Frontier Foundation

### The Proposed Rule Changes Threaten Civil Liberties

The US Patent and Trademark Office's proposed rule changes, which would make it nearly impossible for the public to challenge improperly granted patents, are a significant step towards undermining the fair patent system.

#### Key Points of Concern

* The proposed rules could limit access to publicly funded courts, allowing patent trolls to pursue costly lawsuits without interruption.
* These changes would allow USPTO officials to bypass normal review processes and impose their own decisions on challenging bad patents.
* The public's right to challenge patents is essential for innovation and everyday technology users.

#### Why the Proposed Rule Changes Are Unacceptable

The proposed rule changes are a thinly veiled attempt by patent trolls to stay ahead of abusive litigants. Patents issued in error, as well as poorly granted ones that cannot be challenging through the conventional process, will now become effectively untouchable at their source—the Patent Office itself.

#### Take Action to Opppose These Rules

The public must speak up against these changes through public comments. Individuals can use the federal government's public comment system to file their opposition.

### Sample Comment:

"We oppose USPTO’s proposed rule changes for inter partes review (IPR), which would effectively end our ability to challenge improperly granted patents at their source—the US Patent Office itself."
