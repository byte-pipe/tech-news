# 10 Developer Habits That Separate Good Programmers From Great Ones

Developer habits have a significant impact on their ability to code effectively. Here are the top 6 developer habits that separate good programmers from great ones:

*   **They Read Code Far More Than They Write It**: Many developers do not read or understand the code of other people's projects, focusing instead on writing their own.
*   **They Write Tightly, Not Just Quickly**: Good programming is about delivering high-quality code in a timely manner, rather than just producing rapid fire code without much thought. Writing tightly-structured code that follows best practices is essential for making software efficient and reliable.
*   **They Break Down Problems into Manageable Pieces**: Rather than attacking complex problems head-on, many developers tend to break them down into smaller, more manageable pieces. This approach makes the problem feel less overwhelming and allows for more focused coding.
*   **They Learn from Their Mistakes**: Good programmers do not view mistakes as failures or setbacks, but rather as opportunities to learn and improve. They actively seek out feedback, test their code regularly, and analyze errors in an effort to minimize their impact.
*   **They Code with Accountability**: Having others watching one's back can make the task of writing code feel less daunting and more sustainable in terms of time, energy, and motivation.

These habits have been observed across various developers, from beginners to experienced professionals. They compound over time as the individual develops a strong practice that will help them tackle even the most challenging coding tasks effectively and efficiently.
