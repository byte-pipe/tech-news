# DHS wants more biometric data - even from citizens • The Register

# Expanded Biometric Collection for Immigration Benefit Applications
The US Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has proposed a sweeping expansion of its biometric collection for immigration benefit applications. This proposal would require the submission of additional biometric data by many individuals associated with immigrants who are applying for benefits or related forms.

## Key Points

* The proposed rule would permit DHS to collect biometrics from various groups, including immigrants and US citizens linked to them.
* The expansion includes enhanced collection requirements for immigrant applicants, both at least once and annually unless exempted.
* The policy change aims to prevent fraud in immigration benefit applications while ensuring identity verification and accurate record-keeping.
* Biometric data will be collected for family-based visa petitions and those associated with submission of a formal application.

## Changes to Current Policy

* DHS wants to begin collecting new types of biometric data, such as ocular imagery, voice prints, and DNA samples using the broad term "biometrics".
* The proposed rule also defines what is considered valid biometric data by government agencies.
* New technologies like ocular imagery will be utilized for biometric collection.

## Potential Implications

* This expansion may have significant implications for individuals in immigration families or groups not previously subject to biometric monitoring.

# Notes on the US Department of Homeland Security (DHS) Biometric Collection Expansion Proposal

* The proposed rule aims to broaden DHS's authority over immigration benefit applications while minimizing potential data privacy concerns.
* Implementation and finalization await approval from the Office of Management and Budget (OMB).
