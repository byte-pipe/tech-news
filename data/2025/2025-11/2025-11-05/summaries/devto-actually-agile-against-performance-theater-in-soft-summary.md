# Actually Agile: Against Performance Theater in Software Development - DEV Community

## Understanding Agile: Separating Performance Theater from True Agility

Agile has become synonymous with "performing well" and efficiency, rather than truly building better software. This phenomenon is referred to as "agility theater."

### A Critical Reevaluation of Agile Practices

Agile practices such as daily standups lasting 45 minutes, sprint reviews using PowerPoint slides, retrospectives focusing on post-it notes, and backlogs replacing architecture, methodologies have failed to deliver a positive return on investment.

### The Consulting Industrial Complex and Certification

The consulting industry has grown exponentially, with new roles emerging specifically to manage these practices. This has led to a proliferation of certifications in agile management, which many believe demonstrate competence but ultimately provide little value.

### Jargon and Gatekeeping: Misusing Language

Agile terms have become jargon, distracting from meaningful communication and decision-making processes. The use of buzzwords like "velocity" and "burndown charts" has created a sense of compliance rather than understanding.

### Rethinking Agile Tools and Techniques

We often rely on complex tools and methodology to execute agile practices, but these approaches misrepresent the true nature of collaboration and problem-solving.

### A More Nuanced Definition of Agile

True agility involves moving well in response to changing circumstances. It does not require excessive velocity or metrics-driven decision-making.

#### Asking the Right Questions

Before implementing new agile processes or decisions:

* Are we setting clear goals? Can they be achieved through a single sentence?
* Are there any changes that must remain constant?

By asking these questions and critically reevaluating Agile practices, organizations can embark on a journey back from "agility theater" to true agility.
