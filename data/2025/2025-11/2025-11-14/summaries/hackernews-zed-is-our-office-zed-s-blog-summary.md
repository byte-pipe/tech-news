# Zed Is Our Office — Zed's Blog

## Overview of Collaboration in Zed's Office

*   The weekly meeting in Zed is designed for collaboration with all offices to set goals and understand the project requirements.
*   Nathan, who left an earlier team he worked, created a collaborative editor approach called gpui, which was later developed into Zed.

### Key Findings and Goals

1.  **Collaboration Platform**: The platform prioritizes minimal distractions and interaction with code.
2.  **Responsive User Interface**: The latency between keystrokes and re-rendering should be imperceptible for responsive design.
3.  **Focus on Productivity**: The interface should stay out of the way to facilitate productivity and minimize distractions.

### How Collaboration is Built into Zed

The software was developed with a collaborative vision, inspired by Nathan's experience working in two-person setups at Pivotal Labs prior to founding Zed Industries.

*   **Past Competitors:** Other editors have explored similar models but have fallen short of the desired performance standards.
*   **Technological Innovations:** The current product is built using different technologies not yet seen in other collaborative editors.

### Current Status and Challenges

1.  **Setup and Technical Implementation:** The setup process requires a level of technical expertise, making it tedious for many users.
2.  **Sharing Resources:** Multiple collaborators often require concurrent edits, which can negatively impact resource usage and performance.
3.  **Conflict Management:** The current conflict resolution mechanism, using Change Reaction Detection (CRDTs), ensures that the system remains in-sync.

### Future Plans

While Zed has made significant strides toward creating a robust collaborative editor experience, ongoing improvements and enhancements are planned to address currently identified challenges.

1.  **Optimized CRDT Implementation:** A more efficient conflict resolution technique will be developed.
2.  **Resource Efficient Collaboration:** Streamlined setup procedures and improved sharing tools will enhance the overall collaboration process.
3.  **Enhanced User Interface Features:** Additional features, such as better notification systems or integration with user data storage, may be developed.
