# Protecting our Merchants: Standing up to Extortion

**Protecting our Merchants: Standing up to Extortion**
==============================================

**Summary**
------------

Checkout.com was targeted by a criminal extortion attempt in which an unauthorized access resulted in gaining access to a legacy third-party cloud file storage system. Despite not having any merchant funds or card numbers accessed, the attackers made a ransom demand for approximately $2 million.

*   Checkout.com attributed the incident due to compromised permissions and improper decommissioning of the system.
*   The company has stated that it takes responsibility for its mistake and is turning the attack into a donation to support research in cybersecurity against cybercrime.
*   Instead of paying the ransom, Checkout.com will invest in security measures to protect merchants.

**Main Points**
----------------

*   Checkout.com was targeted by an extortion attempt
*   Compromised legacy cloud file storage system gained access to data connected to the company
*   Company attributed issue due to improper decommissioning and compromised permissions
*   Donation to support research for cybersecurity against cybercrime instead of paying ransom
