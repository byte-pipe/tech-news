# Discord says 70,000 users may have had their government IDs leaked in breach | The Verge

# Discord Breach: 70,000 Users Potentially Affected by Government ID Photo Leak

* Discord claims approximately 70,000 users may have had their government-issued photo IDs exposed in a security incident involving a third-party customer service provider.
* The breach was initially announced as a customer service data leak but is now being scrutinized for potential misuse or extortion.
* Discord spokesperson Nu Wexler has confirmed that the company and its vendor were not involved in the leak, and users have been contacted to verify their accounts.
* The affected users may have been exposed through government ID photos, including names, usernames, emails, last four digits of credit cards, and IP addresses.

Discord's response to the incident:

* The company is taking responsibility for protecting user personal data.
* Users have been notified via Twitter and have been contacted with additional information from Discord.
