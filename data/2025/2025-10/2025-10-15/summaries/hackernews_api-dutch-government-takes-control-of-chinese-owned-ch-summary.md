# The Dutch Government Takes Control of Chinese-Owned Semiconductor Maker Nexperia
-------------------------------------------------------

• The Dutch government has invoked control over Nexperia, a Chinese-owned semiconductor manufacturer based in the Netherlands, under its Goods Availability Act.
• This move is aimed at ensuring a sufficient supply of chips remains available in Europe amid rising global trade tensions.

## Key Points

* 745-SZ shares plunged 10% after the news, hitting their daily limit on Shanghai's stock exchange
* Nexperia specializes in producing chips for various industries, making its product critical for European technological supply chains
* The "Goods Availability Act" allows the Dutch government to intervene in private companies to prevent situation where chip production becomes unavailable
* The move comes despite concerns about governance shortcomings and actions within Nexperia

## Governance Changes

• Wingtech's chairman Zhang Xuezheng has been suspended from his roles as executive director of Nexperia Holdings and non-executive director due to the government order
• The move is seen as excessive intervention driven by geopolitical bias rather than a fact-based risk assessment, according to Wingtech
