# Brilliant, Broken, and Frustrating: My Deep Dive into Amazon’s Kiro AI IDE, the Flawed Junior Developer - DEV Community

## Kiro Ide: Taming AI-Powered Development with Structured Workflow

### Key Points:

*   Kiro IDE promises to reduce "vibe coding" chaos by injecting engineering discipline into AI-assisted development.
*   Its core mission is focused on a structured, spec-driven workflow that aims to align actual requirements with detailed plans and architecture diagrams.
*   While providing rigidity, early user feedback highlights potential drawbacks in terms of slow implementation and specs not auto-updating.

### Structured Insights:

## Reality 1: Rigorous Workflow vs. Quick Code

*   Kiro IDE's structured workflow (three phases: high-level prompt, formal engineering artifacts) replaces the unstructured "prompt-and-pray" approach of traditional AI-powered development.
*   This new architecture increases discipline, but some users may find it too slow and rigid compared to the instant gratification they're accustomed to.

## Reality 2: Amazon Web Services (AWS) Product Strategy

*   Kiro is developed by AWS, which has been cautious in their developer tool space for years.
*   Using Kiro on its own website with common login providers like Google or GitHub supports its independent offering and avoids the need for an AWS account.

### Realities Frustrations:

## Reality 1: Technical Requirements Drift

*   While rigorously planning and clarifying requirements, Kiro's process can make it challenging to have ongoing feedback that specs not auto-update.
*   This may lead some developers who rely on iterative implementation flow.

## Reality 2: Maintenance Nightmare Over Time

*   Without clear design documentation, future changes become guesswork as prototypes transform into actual products.
