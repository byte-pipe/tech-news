## A Definition of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

Artificial General Intelligence, also known as Artificial General Intelligence  or AGI for short, refers to a hypothetical AI system that possesses human-like intelligence and cognitive abilities comparable to those found in humans. The definition of AGI aims to bridge the gap between today's specialized AI systems and human-level cognition.

## Key Points

- **Defining AGI**: AGI is typically defined as matching the cognitive versatility and proficiency of a well-educated adult.
- **Cognitive Domain Dissection**: The framework for defining AGI divides general intelligence into ten core cognitive domains, including reasoning, memory, and perception, with established human psychometric batteries to evaluate AI systems.
- **Application of Framework**: Application reveals both rapid progress and the substantial gap remaining before achieving AGI.
