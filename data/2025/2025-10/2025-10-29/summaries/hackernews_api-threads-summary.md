# Threads

## Threads Summary

**Key Points**

* Threads are vertical sequences of text posts on social media platforms like Twitter, Instagram, and TikTok.
* Created in 2017 by Jack Dorsey, Chris Best, Noam Miller, and Ehren Hara.
* Initially titled "Twind," the app was later rebranded to its current name, Threads.

**Structure**

* Users can create and upload posts that are up to 5MB in size.
* Posts consist of text-only content with optional images and videos that can be threaded into a conversation.
* Notifications sent when another user replies or comments on a post.

## Features

* Relevance: Thread's algorithm prioritizes content based on relevance, so users tend to see posts from friends and accounts they interact with regularly.
* Timing: Threads introduces an option for users to view their timeline in reverse chronological order, allowing them to see all of its activity in one place.
* Discoverability: Users can browse the "For You" feed, which is personalized based on their interests and preferences.

## Impact

* Social relevance: Threads allows users to engage with friends and accounts in real-time, strengthening social connections.
* Content sharing: The app facilitates the sharing of various types of content, including text posts, images, videos, and even Reels (a short-form video feature).
* Community building: Users can connect with others who share similar interests or concerns, fostering meaningful conversations.

## Evolution

* Over time, Threads has introduced new features such as polls, GIF support, and even a shopping app for users to purchase threads made of physical fabrics.
* Despite these additions, the core idea remains the same – a platform to engage in threaded conversations on platforms like Twitter.
