# Beyond Hacktoberfest: Building a True Open Source Journey - DEV Community

# Cybersecurity Journey: Reflections from an Experienced Security Professional

**Key Points**

*   Contributing to open-source projects can be overwhelming for new contributors
*   It's essential to create opportunities or seek them out rather than simply searching for issues to work on
*   Not respecting contribution ethics and fairness when contributing oneself can lead to wasted time and respect from maintainers
*   Giving back to the community through contributing oneself is a valuable way to make an impact

## Introduction to Open-Source Contributions

As a seasoned security professional, I've come across many newbies who struggle to contribute to open-source projects. This article aims to provide insights on building meaningful open-source journeys.

### Understanding Hacktoberfest and Contributing

Hacktoberfest is a monthly event that celebrates all things Linux and the open-source community. It's an opportunity for newcomers to participate and give back.

**The Key to a Meaningful Contributions**

Discovering and creating unique paths in contributing has become essential for successful collaborations. In my journey, I've learned that it's not about searching for opportunities but actively seeking them out.

## Creating Opportunities

To make open-source contributions meaningful, it's crucial to find and create opportunities rather than simply submitting issues with assigned maintainers. This approach can be time-consuming but ultimately leads to greater progress on projects.

A classic example of this is the experience where I assigned someone an issue after the Hacktoberfest event ended. They replied promptly to acknowledge their completion, acknowledging that we could have made more progress together. The impact and satisfaction from contributing oneself has been invaluable.

## Maintaining Respectful Contributions

Contributing oneself without waiting until everyone else finishes can raise concerns about respect for maintainers' time and efforts.

The article highlights an incident where the maintainer responded by assigning the issue to someone else, implying that those who contributed themselves were considered inactive. I believe this reaction misrepresents the open-source community's values of giving back and collaboration.

## Benefits of Participating to Gain a Reward

Participate in projects to gain rewards, but avoid taking issues from others without first working on them and seeking permission. This approach ensures respect for maintainers' efforts and maintains the spirit of collaboration that open-source values.

## Conclusion

Building an open-source career requires patience, persistence, and a willingness to take on unique challenges. By creating opportunities or actively seeking them out, newcomers can develop meaningful contributions while respecting the community's values.

The journey ahead is not without its obstacles, but with time and experience, one will come across such experiences where one needs to make adjustments in contributing themselves.
