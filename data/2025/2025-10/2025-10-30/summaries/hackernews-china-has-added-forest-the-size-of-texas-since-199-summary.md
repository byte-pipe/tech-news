## China Adds Forest Size of Texas Since 1990

China has significantly added forest area since 1990, with over 170 million acres (the size of Texas) added to its forests. This represents around 20 million acres lost annually for the past several years.

### Key Points:

*   China started adding forest size in Yanchi County since 1990, which is roughly the size of another country's land area.
*   The world has lost 170 million acres of forest per year on average.
*   The main drivers of deforestation are farming and ranching clearances, as well as wildfires fueled by climate change.
*   In some countries, forests are regrowing. For example, the US, Canada, Russia, and much of Europe have seen forest regeneration.
