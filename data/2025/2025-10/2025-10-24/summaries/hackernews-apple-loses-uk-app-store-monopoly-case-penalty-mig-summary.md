# Apple loses UK App Store monopoly case, penalty might near $2 billion - 9to5Mac

* **Apple Loses UK App Store Monopoly Case: Potential Penalty of Up to $2 Billion**
*   A London tribunal has ruled against Apple in a case concerning the company's practices on its App Store, potentially costing up to $2 billion.
*   The ruling states that Apple abused its dominant position through unfair commissions and excessive pricing for app developers.

## Ruling Details

*   Apple was found to have shut out competition in the app distribution market from October 2015 until the end of 2020.
*   Commissioned by iPhone and iPad users, the tribunal concluded that Apple abused its dominant position.

## Damages at Stake

*   Estimated $2 billion in damages is at stake.
*   A hearing will be held to decide how damages are calculated.

## Expert Reaction

*   Apple has expressed dissent with the ruling, stating it takes a flawed view of the app economy.
*   App developers passed on 50% of the overcharge to consumers.
