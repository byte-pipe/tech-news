# Beyond Hacktoberfest: Building a True Open Source Journey - DEV Community

## Key Points

* Hacktoberfest is an event for appreciating, giving back, and supporting open source projects
* It's not a competition to grab and work on as many issues as possible
* The correct approach to contributing to open source involves creating opportunities rather than searching for them
* This mindset shift can help true beginners find meaning in their contribution journey

## How Hacktoberfest Works

Hacktoberfest is an event where people celebrate the open source community by making contributions to various projects. However, some participants take it too far and prioritize personal interests over fair and respectful collaboration.

As a project maintainer, I've observed that many contributors focus on finding issues that they think are "good first" tasks rather than creating opportunities from scratch. This is particularly prevalent around Hacktoberfest, where new faces often appear to be rushing to complete assigned projects first.

This approach can lead to wasted time and resources as maintainers reject submissions that are not properly peer-reviewed or addressed by maintainers.

## Creating Opportunities vs. Searching for Them

The key to a meaningful open source journey is no longer finding opportunities but creating them. I've learned this truth by experience, including the challenges of working on projects during Hacktoberfest and other events where contributors prioritize personal tasks over fair collaboration.

By recognizing that contributing to open source involves more than just solving problems or completing assigned tasks, we can foster a culture that values collaboration, generosity, and fair contributions.

## The Importance of Respectful Contribution

Respectful contribution is crucial in open source projects. When maintainers assign issues and reject submissions without explanation, it erodes trust and shows that the project has given up on working with contributors who are trying to make meaningful progress.

By abandoning these practices and focusing on creating opportunities from scratch, we can build a stronger sense of community and foster more cooperative and productive collaboration within our open source projects.
