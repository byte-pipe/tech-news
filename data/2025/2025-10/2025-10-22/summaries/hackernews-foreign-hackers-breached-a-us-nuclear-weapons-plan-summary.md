# Foreign hackers breached a US nuclear weapons plant via SharePoint flaws | CSO Online

## Foreign Hackers Breach US Nuclear Weapons Plant via SharePoint Flaws

### Key Findings:

* A foreign threat actor infiltrated the National Nuclear Security Administration's Kansas City National Security Campus through vulnerabilities in Microsoft's SharePoint browser-based app.
* The breach targeted the facility, which produces critical non-nuclear components for US nuclear weapons.

### Incident Details:

* The attack occurred at the Kansas City campus of the National Nuclear Security Administration (NNSA), with Honeywell Federal Manufacturing & Technologies (FM&T) managing the site under contract to the NNSA.
* Microsoft discovered two recently disclosed SharePoint vulnerabilities - CVE-2025-53770 and CVE-2025-49704 - on July 19, which have remote code execution (RCE) and spoofing flaws.
* The attackers exploited these flaws on July 22, with the Department of Energy saying it was one of several organizations hit by attacks enabled by the SharePoint vulnerabilities.

### Impact:

* The breach raises questions about the need to solidify further federal IT/OT security protections.
* Experts stress that operational technology systems (e.g., those controlling nuclear production) are more vulnerable than typically thought and should be given prioritized attention in terms of system hardening and patch management.
* A small number of systems at the Kansas City facility were impacted, while most involved were restored promptly.
