# Canadian bill would strip internet access from 'specified persons' | National Post

**Bill C-8: Restricting Internet Access from "Specified Persons"**

**Key Points:**

* Bill C-8, a proposed bill in Canada, would allow Ottawa to unilaterally pull internet services from specified individuals without prior notice or judicial review.
* The measure would be made possible through a provision under the Telecommunications Act, requiring telecom providers to isolate such customers.
* A Public Safety Minister, currently Gary Anandasangaree and Mélanie Joly, will have the power to issue these orders.

**Summary:**

The Canadian government's proposed Bill C-8, which aims to combat "unprecedented cyber-threats," would grant Ottawa the authority to restrict internet access from individuals whose services are deemed unsuitable. The bill would allow ministers, under consultation with industry and telecommunications regulator, to issue prohibitions on specific persons without requiring prior notice or judicial review.

**Main Terms:**

* Bill C-8
* Unilateral internet shutdowns
* Telecommunications Act
* Internet service providers (ISPs)
* Ministerial powers

**Additional Context:**

The Liberal government has floated this measure in response to international statements framing internet access as a human right. The proposed legislation is part of ongoing efforts to ensure safe online interactions, despite concerns about increased surveillance and restrictions on individual freedoms.

**Current Status:**

Bill C-8 is undergoing second reading in the House of Commons. While it has not yet received Royal Assent, similar measures have been adopted by other countries to restrict internet access for security, health, or safety reasons.
