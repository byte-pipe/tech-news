**Data Engineering 101 - A Real Beginner's Approach**

As a web developer with a interest in data engineering, I wanted to dive into this complex field, so I started researching by searching for topics on Google. To my surprise, clicking on different articles led me to an article about Data Engineering called "Feeling Lucky". The author shared their personal experience of starting from scratch with new topic and wanting to learn more.

The article introduced the concept of Data Engineering and offered insights into various aspects such as:

* Governance: understanding who owns the data and ensuring quality.
* Orchestration: coordinating dependencies and workflows using tools like Apache Airflow or Dagster.
* Transformation (ETL/ELT): cleaning and standardizing data.
* Ingestion and Streaming: connecting sources and moving data in real-time.

The author highlighted the importance of understanding an entire ecosystem to produce effective data products.

**Building Our First Pipeline**

The article then shifted focus to building a data pipeline, breaking it down into four key stages:

1.  **Ingesting from your source to a data lake**: collecting and storing data.
2.  **Formatting and validating the data (Staging Layer)**: preparing the data for further processing.
3.  **Designing the analytics (Mart) layer**: creating a solution for extracting insights.
4.  **Full preview**: testing and iterating on the pipeline.

Throughout the article, the author emphasized the importance of:

*   Understanding complex concepts like governance and orchestration.
*   Familiarity with tools like Apache Airflow or Dagster.
*   Iteration and improvement to produce accurate data products.
