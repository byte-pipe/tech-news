# United MAX Hit by Falling Object at 36,000 Feet

## Investigation Focus: Weather Balloon Data Package?

The NTSB is now investigating the possibility that a piece of weather balloon's data package struck a United 737 MAX over Colorado on Thursday.

## Findings and Details:

* The plane was hit by an unidentified flying object (UFO or meteorite), not long after taking off from Denver.
* A photo shows a pilot's arm peppered with small cuts and scratches, indicating that the incident occurred at high altitude.
* The data package is believed to have caused damage to the windshield and frame on the plane.

## Background:

* Weather balloons can carry sensitive electronic components, including cameras and communication equipment, which can be damaged by extreme temperatures or physical trauma.
* At 36,000 feet, a piece of debris hitting the plane could potentially cause significant damage.

## No Incidents or Fatalities Reported:

* The incident was reported to have occurred at approximately 10:45 AM local time over Colorado.
* No passengers were killed and no one was injured.

## Investigation Update:

* The investigation is focused on determining if a piece of weather balloon's data package caused the incident.
* Initially, authorities thought it could be a "space debris" but more recent analysis suggests otherwise.
