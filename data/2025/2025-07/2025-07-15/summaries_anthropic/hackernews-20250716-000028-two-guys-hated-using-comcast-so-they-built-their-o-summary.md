# Two guys hated using Comcast, so they built their own fiber ISP - Ars Technica

Here's a 3-4 paragraph analysis of the article from a solo developer business perspective:

The article discusses the problem of poor internet service, particularly from large providers like Comcast, and the opportunity for a small startup to build a better fiber internet service. The founders, Samuel Herman and Alexander Baciu, were frustrated with Comcast's slow upload speeds and unreliable service in their hometown of Saline, Michigan. This pain point is a common one for many households and businesses that rely on fast, reliable internet.

The market indicators are promising - the founders were able to sign up over 100 customers in just 6 months, and they estimate they need to capture 30% of the 4,000 homes in their initial build area to be profitable. The founders are offering competitive pricing, with gigabit fiber service for $80/month, which undercuts Comcast's offerings. This suggests a strong willingness to pay for better internet service.

From a solo developer perspective, the technical feasibility seems achievable, as the founders already had experience in construction and fiber installation through their work. They also sought guidance from an experienced fiber ISP founder, Jared Mauch, which helped them navigate the technical and operational aspects. The main challenges would likely be around managing the construction, operations, customer support, and sales - areas that may require a team rather than a solo developer. However, the founders' background in construction and their local hires for installation and support suggest these challenges can be overcome. Overall, the business appears viable, with a clear customer pain point, competitive pricing, and a founder team with relevant experience.
