# NIH Is Far Cheaper Than The Wrong Dependency

**Analysis of NIH vs Wrong Dependency: Market Indicators**

The author mentions that one of the biggest fallacies in coding is treating dependencies as free functionality without any drawbacks. However, market indicators suggest a different story. The example of TigerBeetle, a financial database written with Vanilla Zig, highlights the importance of evaluating dependencies more thoroughly.

* According to the author, the cost of large, complex dependencies like those found in containerization or bundling setups can lead to expensive re-write cycles.
* The introduction of new components requires significant time and effort to understand and integrate, which may not be feasible for solo developers with limited resources.

**Technical Feasibility**

The complexity of dependencies, stability issues, and tight coupling between components make it challenging for a single developer to manage and maintain. The author suggests that using dependency-free libraries or frameworks (e.g. Zig) can simplify the codebase and improve productivity.

* Dependencies like those in containerization or bundling setups often include multiple pieces of software, making it difficult to understand dependencies and their interactions.
* The complexity of implementing these "dependency management" tasks may be overwhelming for solo developers with a limited budget and expertise in specialized topics (e.g. containerizing software).

The existence of willing-to-pay customers suggests that there is a market demand for solutions that simplify dependency management.
