The article discusses a significant problem facing life insurance companies - the impact of GLP-1 medications on their ability to accurately assess and price risk. GLP-1s (glucagon-like peptide-1 agonists) are a class of weight loss drugs that can dramatically improve key health metrics like BMI, blood pressure, and cholesterol within 6 months of use. However, the article notes that around 65% of GLP-1 users quit the medication within the first year, causing those health improvements to quickly reverse. This "mortality slippage" is costing life insurers millions, as they end up underpricing policies for high-risk individuals.

The clear customer pain point is the inability to accurately assess risk and price policies accordingly.
