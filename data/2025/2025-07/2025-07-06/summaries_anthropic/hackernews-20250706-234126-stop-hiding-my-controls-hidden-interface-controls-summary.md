# Stop Hiding My Controls: Hidden Interface Controls Are Affecting Usability | ACM Interactions

Here's a 3-4 paragraph analysis of the article 'Stop Hiding My Controls: Hidden Interface Controls Are Affecting Usability' from a solo developer business perspective:

The article discusses the problem of hidden interface controls, which make it difficult for users to discover and use important functionality. This is a significant usability issue that can negatively impact user adoption and satisfaction. As the article notes, the trend towards hiding controls in modern interfaces, especially on mobile devices, is a step backwards from the more intuitive "knowledge in the world" approach pioneered with drop-down menus.

From a business perspective, this problem represents a clear opportunity for a solo developer to build solutions that address the pain points around hidden controls. Users are clearly frustrated by having to memorize arcane gestures and button combinations just to access basic functionality. A developer who can create more intuitive, discoverable interfaces could tap into a large market of users and businesses looking to improve usability and productivity. The article provides several concrete examples of hidden controls, like unlocking a car or using Apple Maps in CarPlay, that highlight the scale of this problem.

In terms of technical feasibility, a solo developer with strong UI/UX design skills and some mobile app development experience could likely build solutions to address hidden controls. The core challenge would be identifying common pain points, designing elegant interfaces that surface critical functionality, and integrating with existing platforms and devices. While this would require a significant investment of time and effort, the potential payoff could be substantial if the developer can create a product that solves a real user need. Additionally, the article notes that users are often willing to pay for solutions that improve usability and save them time, suggesting a viable business model for a solo developer.
