# I couldn't submit a PR, so I got hired and fixed it myself

This article discusses an interesting problem and opportunity from the perspective of a solo developer building a profitable business.

The problem being addressed is a search quality issue on the Mintlify documentation platform, which the author had previously identified as the founder of Trieve, the company powering Mintlify's search. The race condition in the debounced search queries was causing inconsistent and subpar search results, which was frustrating for users and reflected poorly on Trieve's product.

The market indicators suggest this is a real user pain point that businesses are willing to pay to solve. Mintlify has over 30,000 documentation sites, indicating significant user adoption. While the issue was not a high priority previously, the author was able to fix it himself after joining the Mintlify team, suggesting a willingness to pay to address these types of "boring" but impactful problems.

From a technical feasibility standpoint, the fix seems relatively straightforward for an experienced developer - adding an AbortController to the debounced search function. This is the kind of targeted, incremental improvement that a solo developer could reasonably take on.

The business viability signals are also promising. The author notes the satisfaction of being able to directly fix a problem that had been bugging him, which speaks to the intrinsic motivation and sense of ownership a solo developer can have. And by improving a core part of the Mintlify product, the author has demonstrated value that could translate into revenue opportunities, either through consulting, contracting, or potentially even acquiring the search functionality altogether.
