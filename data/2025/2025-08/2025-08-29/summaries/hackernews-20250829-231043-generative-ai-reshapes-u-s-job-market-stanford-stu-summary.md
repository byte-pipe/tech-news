# Generative AI reshapes U.S. job market, Stanford study shows

**Analysis**

The article discusses a Stanford University study that found significant evidence of the impact of Generative AI on job prospects for early career workers. The study analyzed payroll records from millions of American workers and found a 13% decline in employment among workers between 22 and 25 years old, who are most exposed to AI. This suggests that the widespread adoption of Generative AI is having a disproportionate impact on entry-level workers.

**Market Indicators**

* The study found no significant correlation with education level, as the impact was not influenced by education outcomes.
* Remote work and outsourced jobs do not appear to affect hiring decisions.
* Existing economic shifts may be mitigating factors that could impact hiring decisions.

To put these findings into perspective:

* National employment growth for young workers has been stagnant despite recent signs of softening. (Source: The study)
* Overall employment remains robust, but with signs of unevenness among different age groups and occupations. (Source: Stanford University)
