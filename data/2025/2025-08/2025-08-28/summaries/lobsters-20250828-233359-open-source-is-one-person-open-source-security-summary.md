**Analysis**

The article highlights a concerning trend in open-source software development, suggesting that many popular projects are maintained by a single individual. This is not only a problem of resource utilization but also raises questions about the value of diversity among open-source contributors.

From a solo developer business perspective, this trend warrants attention:

*   Market indicators: The article mentions that over 15% of open-source projects have more than 1 person maintaining them. Assuming 100% market adoption and assuming 90% of all projects in the open-source software development ecosystem are maintained by one person or a small team.
*   Technical feasibility: Writing an entire project can be time-consuming, requiring advanced programming skills, documentation, testing, and community engagement. It's feasible for solo developers to maintain large projects with complex technical requirements.
*   Business viability signals: Existing competition in the market suggests that open-source development is viable without relying on a single developer. However, new entrants may face challenges in establishing a similar ecosystem.
