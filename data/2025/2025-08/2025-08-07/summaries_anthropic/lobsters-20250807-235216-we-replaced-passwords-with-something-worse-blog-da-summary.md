# We replaced passwords with something worse | Blog - Daniel Huang

The article "We replaced passwords with something worse" by Daniel Huang discusses the problem of using one-time codes sent via email or SMS as a replacement for traditional passwords. This login method, while seemingly more convenient, actually introduces significant security risks and is often worse than using passwords.

From a solo developer business perspective, this article highlights a few key points:

1. Problem/Opportunity: The article identifies a "boring problem" that people and businesses are willing to pay to solve - secure and user-friendly authentication. Many services have adopted the one-time code approach, but it has proven to be vulnerable to phishing attacks, undermining user security.

2. Market Indicators: The article cites the example of Microsoft's Minecraft login, where this authentication method has been exploited, leading to many accounts being stolen. This indicates a clear user pain point and demand for a more secure alternative.

3. Technical Feasibility: Implementing a secure and user-friendly authentication system is a complex challenge that requires significant technical expertise. As a solo developer, you would need to have a strong background in web security, cryptography, and user experience design to tackle this problem effectively.

4. Business Viability: The article suggests that there is a willingness to pay for a better authentication solution, as the current one-time code approach is widely adopted despite its security flaws. However, the market is likely saturated with various authentication providers, so a solo developer would need to differentiate their offering and find effective distribution channels to reach potential customers.

In summary, the article highlights a genuine problem that businesses and users face, but solving it would require substantial technical expertise and a well-thought-out business strategy. For a solo developer, this may be a challenging but potentially rewarding opportunity, provided they have the necessary skills and resources to develop a secure and user-friendly authentication solution that can effectively compete in the market.
