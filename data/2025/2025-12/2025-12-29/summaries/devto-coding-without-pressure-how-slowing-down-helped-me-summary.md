# Coding Without Pressure: How Slowing Down Helped Me Learn Faster - DEV Community

**Coding Without Pressure: How Slowing Down Helped Me Learn Faster**

The struggle is real. For a long time, I was under the assumption that learning to code required intense daily goals, long hours, and constant progress. However, this approach often led me to feel burnt out and unfulfilled.

I realized that I was failing not because I wasn't making enough progress (although that's an important distinction), but because I would let internal pressure hold me back.

**The Invisible Timer in Your Head**

As a learner, you have an invisible timer hiding inside your mind. It's constantly reminding you to understand the material, and when things don't click, it criticizes you. This pressure is easy to get caught up in, but it's essential to recognize that learning shouldn't feel like a race.

**Fast Learning Without Shallow Learning**

When I attempted to learn new topics too quickly, I found myself falling into shallow learning patterns. I would "cover" multiple topics at once or focus on extremely basic concepts without fully understanding their substance. This led me down a path of consuming information rather than truly learning it.

However, something shifted when I began intentionally putting things off and deliberately doing less. The payoff was surprising: I started to understand and apply concepts more deeply.

**Slowing Down Wasn't Easy (But It Didn't Have To Be)**

One of the most challenging aspects of slowing down is that you'll encounter situations where everything seems simple, but in reality, it's still messy code waiting for your attention. At times, I will go back to "smaller things" like refactoring old code or tweaking small features to improve overall cohesion.

**What "Slow Learning" Looks Like**

For me, slowing down looks like:

* Reading my own code before asking why
* Letting myself struggle without Googleing (yes, it's uncomfortable at times!)
* Writing simple solutions instead of fancy ones (a mindset shift)
* Taking breaks when my brain feels tired (no guilt taken)

This slower pace hasn't led to stagnant progress; rather, I've noticed concepts become more memorable and connected patterns emerge.

**Confidence and Confidence Growth**

By removing pressure, I gained confidence that doesn't rely on finishing tasks quickly. Here are some key takeaways:

* Comparisons cease; your progress becomes tangible.
* The desire for perfection disappears.
* Fears become manageable: "Am I good enough?" becomes a question about what you can understand better.

**Learning Faster by Moving Slower**

The best part is that slowing down has actually helped me learn faster. It wasn't about covering more topics, but rather about retaining and connecting concepts in the long run.

Ultimately, learning isn't urgent; it's an ongoing process.
