# Steam Machine today, Steam Phones tomorrow | The Verge

* **Steam Machine Development**: Valve is reviving its Steam Machine initiative, aiming to bring Windows games to other platforms such as ARM chips used in Android devices.

* **Open-Source Technologies**: The company has been funding open-source technologies like Proton and Fex, which allows Linux-based phones with the Arm processor architecture to play PC games on them, including a compatibility layer called Proton.
* **Bridge from x86 to Arm**: With Fex as an emulator, Valve is bridging the gap between x86 and ARM architectures to enable Windows games to run smoothly.
* **Arms' Potential**: As a result, Valiant plans to create hardware capable of supporting Windows game development using ARM chips could lead to other innovations such as gaming laptops, smartphones, and desktops.
