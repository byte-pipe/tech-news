# The Illusion of Intense Learning

Learning to code should not feel like a race or a daily goal-set. Instead, it can become an ongoing process when one balances intensity with patience and self-motivation.

## The Invisible Timer in Our Heads

When we dive into coding tutorials, social media, or online communities, an unspoken timer runs through our minds. It asks questions such as "Should I understand this now?" or "Am I behind others?"

### Realizations:

* We learn more when we focus on understanding than by rushing through topics quickly.
* Speed is relative; what matters most is how deeply one understands the concepts.

## Breaking Free from Pressure

Pressure to progress has been a driving force in many coders' lives. It prompted them to push harder, try to achieve faster results, or compare themselves with peers.

### The Reality:

Rushing forward can lead to shallow learning where focus shifts from understanding to consumption of information quickly.

## Slowing Down Wasn't Easy (At First)

Intentionally slowing down was initially met with resistance. In its early stages, it felt like waste of time and a lack of progress.

### New Found Understanding

However, as coding became more familiar, focusing on "simple solutions" instead of complex ones led to the discovery that things were sticking at a deeper level.

## Lessons Learned:

* Slowing down requires intentional effort but leads to lasting improvements
* The pace of learning should be balanced with self-motivation and understanding rather than pressure

## Confidence Shift:

### Removing Pressure Creates Trust

Removing external pressure empowers developers to learn without comparing themselves. It frees them from the guilt that comes with "not meeting expectations" and begins to foster a growth mindset.

## What Has Changed:

* No longer trying to finish courses, forcing productivity on bad days or measuring progress by speed
* Focusing instead on understanding concepts naturally at their own pace

### Learning Becomes Ongoing

In conclusion, slowing down is key to retaining the process of learning rather than just achieving a specific deadline. By embracing this new approach and developing self-motivation, developers can make genuine improvements.

## Final Thoughts:

This mindset shift has come from one developer's experience: they stopped comparing their progress to others or trying to finish chapters within weeks but instead learned gradually at a pace that feels comfortable for them.
