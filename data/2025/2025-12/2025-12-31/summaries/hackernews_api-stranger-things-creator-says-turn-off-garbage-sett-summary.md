# Stranger Things Creator Says Turn Off "Garbage" Settings

**Stranger Things Creator's Audioadvice to Turn Off "Garbage"TV Settings**

As part of a recent Instagram post, Ross Duffer, one of the creators behind the hit Netflix series "Stranger Things", advised viewers to turn off several popular TV settings before watching the season 5 premiere. The recommendation is attributed to Duffer's frustration with the features that can alter the original look of the content.

*   The most crucial setting to disable before watching the season 5 premiere are those that affect the "dreaded soap opera effect", which includes truemotion and smoothmotion if not disabled correctly.
*   The best solution is to use advanced viewing presets, such as Dolby Vision Movie Dark for color accuracy.

Duffer's advice highlights a common phenomenon in video production where features designed for casual viewers can create a significant visual change that alters the original look of the content. By following his tip, fans can preserve the director's vision and watch "Stranger Things" exactly how it was meant to be seen.
