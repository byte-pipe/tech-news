# Nvidia Acquires Assets from AI Chip Startup Groq for About $20 Billion

## Summary

Nvidia has agreed to acquire assets from nine-year-old chip startup Groq for about $20 billion, the company's largest purchase ever. The deal is a non-exclusive licensing agreement where NVIDIA will acquire all of Groq's assets except its cloud business, CloudGenie, which will continue to operate without interruption.

## Key Details

* **Acquisition Value:** About $20 billion in cash
* **Deal Structure:** Non-exclusivity license agreement with terms not disclosed
* **Target Company:** Google's tensor processing unit (TPU) founder creators of the Tensor Processing Unit (TPU)
* **Key Players:**

  * Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang: agrees to integrate Groq's low-latency processors into AI factory architecture, extending capabilities
  * Groq Co-Founder Jonathan Ross and President Sunny Madra to join NVIDIA to scale licensed technology
  * Colette Kress, Nvidia CFO: Declines to comment
