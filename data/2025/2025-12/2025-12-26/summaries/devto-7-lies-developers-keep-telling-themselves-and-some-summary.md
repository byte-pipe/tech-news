# 7 Lies Developers Keep Telling Themselves (And Somehow Still Believe) - DEV Community

**Developer Lies Telling Themselves**
=====================================

### 1. "This will only take a moment"

   - Comment from the post: Many developers make promises to themselves about how quickly they can complete tasks.

### 2. "I'll refactor this later"

   - Comment from the post: Developers often put off refactoring code due to fear of delays or perceived complexity.

### 3. "We'll definitely finish this by the end of the sprint"

   - Comment from the post: Teams may underestimate the amount of work required to complete a task, leading them to delay it until the end of a sprint.

### 4. "I'll write the tests later"

   - Comment from the post: Testing is often done in phases or postponed due to technical difficulties.

### 5. "It's just an edge case, no one will ever do that"

   - Comment from the post: Developers may minimize the probability of certain issues occurring, downplaying their impact on production.

### 6. "This is definitely not on my side"

   - Comment from the post: This statement highlights the tendency to overestimate individual control over software systems.

### 7. "If it works on my machine, it'll work everywhere"

   - Comment from the post: Developers often forget that software compatibility can vary across different environments.

**Common Patterns**
-------------------

* Overconfidence in one's abilities
* Prioritization of minor issues to avoid complexity
* Underestimating long-term consequences of tasks
* Minimization of individual responsibility

By recognizing and reflecting on these developer lies, professionals can develop a more realistic view of their work and improve efficiency in their daily routines.
