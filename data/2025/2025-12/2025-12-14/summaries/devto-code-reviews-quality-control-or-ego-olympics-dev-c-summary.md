# Code Reviews: Quality Control or Ego Olympics? - DEV Community

**Code Reviews: Quality Control or Ego Olympics?**

The debate around code reviews has reached a boiling point. While some argue it's essential for quality control and promoting learning, others perceive it as an ego-stroking exercise. To clarify the situation, let's examine the key issues.

### **Motivations Behind Code Reviewing**

*   Some senior developers may find it enjoyable to review junior code, even if they don't intend to provide constructive feedback.
*   Others might be trying to demonstrate their expertise and control the conversation around a particular bug or feature.

**The Reality: Too Much Ego Showmanship**

Code reviews have become more about self-promotion than providing genuine guidance. This approach can lead to three problems:

*   **Perfectionism**: Trying to fix every minor issue, even if they're not critical, creates unnecessary stress and slows down the team's progress.
*   **Nitpicking**: Relying on obscure coding practices and reference points instead of assessing the code's overall quality is a waste of time and feedback resources.

### **The Impact on Junior Developers**

**Code Review Etiquette: A Recipe for Disaster**

1.  **Not Reviewing**: Failing to provide feedback can lead to junior developers feeling frustrated, uncertain, and unfulfilled.
2.  **Misuse of Feedback**: Ignoring constructive suggestions or leaving vague comments can hinder the team's progress, slow down development, and demotivate developers.

### **A More Effective Approach**

Instead of focusing on perfectionism and ego, teams should prioritize quality control by:

*   **Embracing Open-Source Code Review**
*   **Setting Clear Expectations**: Ensure that feedback is actionable, consistent, and respectful.
*   **Establishing a Feedback Culture**: Foster an environment where developers feel comfortable sharing their concerns or suggestions.

By adopting a more structured approach and emphasizing constructive feedback, teams can create a collaborative atmosphere where everyone feels valued and able to improve.
