# I Tried Gleam for Advent of Code, and I Get the Hype :: The Tymscar Blog

## Exploring Gleam as My Advent of Code Language


Oscar Molnar
|
| 8:45 pm, December 12, 2025

I tried Gleam for Advent of Code this year and got all the stars. I don't say it's brag-worthy since I said that earlier.

### A Few More Years to Go

Advent of Code is usually 25 days, but this year it was 12 due to Eric's rules on how long the journey will be.


#### Making the Most Out of a Shorter Year


The puzzle list for last year had about 50 parts. This year, I got 24 parts instead. Although shorter doesn't mean easier, in terms of time management and pacing, it does turn out much better that way.

The earlier days are easier than later ones. But they're both fun to work through. What's even more enjoyable is tackling the challenging problems later on.


### Why Gleam Lacked Some Attraction


Gleam is not as efficient in terms of time management and task difficulty. By day 10, you had a solid set of tools that allowed you to quickly solve puzzles smoothly. But that was about building up to that point when hard days kicked in.

I still needed the right tools during the harder parts.


### What I Enjoy Most About ADVENT OF CODE Languages


Gleam has some helpful features: clean syntax, helpful compiler, good error messages, and pipes everywhere which is very useful for my tasks. The editor experience with IntelliJ was fantastic. It used Gleam's extension pretty naturally, making the experience even better.

#### FP and Pipelining

Additionally, I've come to appreciate functional programming (FP) greatly during this Advent of Code. At first, it might seem easier but actually getting results is different from following instructions.


### First Glimpse of wasecho

The basic idea of wasecho for printing values in Gleam's echo feature was super neat and made writing code a breeze. It allowed me to inspect values anywhere in the pipeline without worrying about formatting anything beforehand.

I had missed string interpolation initially, especially when it came to building text for the file glpsolis' output.


### The Next Steps

If you're considering trying Gleam as your language of choice for this year's Advent of Code challenge, consider yourself lucky if you can take all 24 parts on. Keep in mind that speed and difficulty will matter more than being an easy-to-use language.
