## Discussion

Large Language Models (LLMs) are a significant breakthrough in the last five years, potentially changing the way we work. As with any extraordinary powerful tool, LLM use has both promise and peril. The rapid evolution of LLMs is creating questions about how and when they should be used.

## Values in LLM Usage

Our values guide the use of LLMs at Oxide, prioritized as follows:

### Responsibility

Oxide employees bear responsibility for the artifacts created by LLMs. Human judgment remains central to decision-making processes, even with automation helping generate content.

### Rigor

LLMs can promote vigor through careful use but risk being counterproductive if not applied thoughtfully. They must be used cautiously to foster sharp thinking and critical evaluation.

### Empathy

The human users of LLM-generated content demand empathy from authors and readers alike. This includes understanding the feelings and intentions behind their writing or input.

### Teamwork

Collaboration with Oxide team members is vital when using LLMs. Caution must be exercised to preserve trust in our working relationship by avoiding dependence on generated work product.

### Urgency

Rising levels of productivity have led some organizations to neglect guidance and direction, instead pursuing rapid progress without prioritizing essential tasks or values like responsibility and rigor.

## How LLMs be Used at Oxide
Oxide maintains three key aspects when it comes to the use of LLMs:

- **Human judgement**: While LLMs can amplify human capabilities, their output remains under human control.
- **Controlled Use**: Responsible for the artifacts created by generated content are ultimately attributed to human users.
- **Responsibility and Teamwork**: Emphasis on rigor, empathy, urgency should be key considerations when deciding how much of Oxide's efforts with LLMs.

Ultimately, effective use demands recognizing the power dynamics involved in adopting LLMs into our daily operations while still considering the broader context.
