# YouTube Overview
## Introduction to YouTube

YouTube is a video-sharing platform where users can upload, share, and view videos. It was launched in 2005 by three former PayPal employees.

### Key Features:

- **Upload and Monetization**: Users can create an account and upload their own videos, which can be monetized with ads.
- **Discovery Channels**: YouTube offers various discovery channels featuring popular content creators.
- **Community Features**: The platform allows users to engage with each other through comments, likes, and shares.
- **Personalized Experience**: Video recommendations are based on a user's watch history and preferences.

### Getting Started:

1. Create an account or log in to your existing one.
2. Upload videos by clicking the "+" button under "Upload".
3. Customize video settings such as title, description, tags, and thumbnail.
4. Promote your channel through YouTube's ad options.

## What Makes YouTube Unique?

YouTube stands out from other video-sharing platforms due to its vast content library, engagement features, and user-friendliness.

### How YouTube Works:

- **Video Uploads**: Users can upload files up to 12 GB in size.
- **Video Playback**: YouTube provides various playback settings such as the ability to skip ads or play videos in slow motion.
- **End Screens**: Videos with end screens feature cards at the beginning and end of a video that provide links to other playlists, channels, or websites.

## What's Next for YouTube?
