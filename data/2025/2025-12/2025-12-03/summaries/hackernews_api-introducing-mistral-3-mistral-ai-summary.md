# Introducing Mistral 3 | Mistral AI

## Introducing Mistral 3

Mistral 3 is the next generation of open multimodal and multilingual AI models. Developed by a team of experts, it includes three new variants: compact models (14B, 8B, and 3B) and the most capable model to date, Mistral Large 3.

### Key Features:

- **Three New Model Variants:** Compact models with reduced computational requirements, as well as the larger Mistral Large 3 model for frontier instruction-tuning.
- **Advanced Pretraining Models:** Mistral Large 3 has been pre-trained on NVIDIA's high-performance H200 GPUs. It also represents a substantial step forward in permissive open-weight models.
- **Distributed Intelligence:** All models are released under the Apache 2.0 license, empowering the developer community and making AI more accessible.

### Strengths:

* Efficient inference support for TensorRT-LLM and SGLang through NVIDIA's co-design approach
* High-bandwidth HBM3e memory utilization from NVIDIA's extreme design collaboration

## Mistral Large 3: A State-of-the-art Open Model

Mistral Large 3 is a state-of-the-art model trained on scratch for the first time, employing NVIDIA's proprietary mixture of experts architecture with sparse MoE techniques. After fine-tuning on 3000 H200 GPUs, it boasts:

* **Excellent Permissive Pretraining:** Demonstrates parity in results to some of the world's best open-weight models during post-training
* **Image Understanding and Language Capabilities:** Acquires outstanding performance in multilingual conversations

## Deployment Details

Mistral Large 3 releases two versions: base ( Apache 2.0 licensed) and instruction fine-tuned, along with reasoning capabilities to be soon implemented.

### Partnering AI Researchers for Accessible Open-Source Intelligence

Combining the strengths of NVIDIA, vLLM, and Red Hat creates a single, unified platform providing open-source community access to advanced models like Mistral Large 3. An optimized checkpoint in NVFP4 format is available for efficient runtime on select hardware, enabling fast execution on Blackwell GPUs and distributed nodes.

### Enhancing Open-Source AI Development

Delivering high-performance infrastructure relies on broad optimization enabled by NVIDIA's design collaboration and expert work with vLLM and Red Hat, leading to enhanced expertise sharing across open-source environments.
