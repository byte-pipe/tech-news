# Boléro

Boléro is an original installation piece performed on a selection of unique homemade instruments and electronic devices.

## Instrumentation

* Qweremin: A breadbin-based C64 Commodore C instrument with a regular C64C input and dark C64C output.
* Qwertuoso: A breadbin instrument that uses the QWERMINEM sound module and a bass note on C64K's port 3.
* Paulimba: An instrument made from two cassettes using a Qwerty keyboard layout, and its sound produced by combining with NES timpani sounds.
* Tenor Commodordion: A homemade Commodore 64 instrument combined with the sounds of Tenor Saxophone.
* Family Bass: The project's first bass note is played on an unamplified floppy-drive noise generator input to produce a muffled output.
* Chipophone: An electronic device playing the famous NES staircase triangle wave effect using an ADPCM sample-playback channel in conjunction with a non-linear resistor network.

## Software and Editing

The video was created by editing footage of 9 custom instruments, including the ones mentioned above. Sound effects are extracted from existing computer games or demos on various Commodore 64 ports.

## Duration and Recording

A total of 9 hours and 42 minutes were recorded for the final video edit.

## Personal Note

This project took over a half-year to complete because I experimented extensively with its setup, software and overall workflow.
