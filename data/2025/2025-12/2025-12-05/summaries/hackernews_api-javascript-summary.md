Oracle has long abandoned the JavaScript trademark, causing widespread confusion and disruption in the programming community.

### Current Situation: Is JavaScript a Trademark?

*   The problem with JavaScript is that it has become a generic term used by many individuals and companies, regardless of any connection to Oracle products.
*   Unlike other trademarks, which are specifically defined, JavaScript falls under the radar due to its common use across various applications.

### Laws and Regulations

### Is There Any Evidence?

*   According to Trademark Law 1127 of the United States Code, abandonment is determined by a combination of non-use and conduct.

### Example: Netscape, Sun, Oracle

*   The JavaScript trademark was initially held by Netscape, who had partnered with Sun Microsystems in 1995.
*   In 2009, Oracle acquired Sun and took over its ownership of the trademark.

### Conclusion

Oracle's hold on the JavaScript trademark is clearly a non-use case that fits the legal definition. With no evidence to suggest otherwise, the company has abandoned the mark by choosing not to renew it in recent years.
