# Transparent Leadership Beats Servant Leadership

**Transparent Leadership: A Better Approach Than Servant Leadership**

As a manager who entered my role without knowing how to do it, I initially sought guidance on effective management practices. However, reading about servant leadership led me to reevaluate this approach. From my own experience, I've identified several problems with servant leadership:

*   **Lack of personal responsibility**: Servant leaders can become overworked and overwhelmed, leaving nobody else to handle obstacles.
*   **Isolation from the organization**: Without effective problem-solving skills, servant leaders can leave their team isolated and uncertain about their roles.
*   **No career growth opportunities**: Effective communication with direct reports is critical for career development. Lack of clear expectations and training can hinder professional growth.

To create a better approach, I'd like to propose "transparent leadership." Here are some key principles:

*   **Coaching people**: Establish strong relationships with team members to guide their growth and make informed decisions.
*   **Connecting people**: Foster open communication and collaboration at all levels to ensure everyone is on the same page.
*   **Teaching problem-solving**: Train employees in methodical problem-solving techniques to tackle technical challenges efficiently.
*   **Value-based leadership**: Explain organizational values and principles clearly, empowering team members to make informed decisions.
*   **Career growth opportunities**: Gradually take over leadership responsibilities to ensure continued progression within the organization.
*   **Continuous training**: Train replacement managers to maintain skills and knowledge in high-demand areas.

While servant leadership has its merits, it can lead to burnout and inefficiencies. Transparent leadership offers a more effective approach by empowering team members to drive results and manage their own responsibilities.
