# Ford Kills the All-Electric F-150 as It Rethinks Its EV Ambitions | WIRED

# Ford's Shift in Electric Vehicle Strategy

**Key Points:**

* Ford is reevaluating its electric vehicle (EV) plans, shifting focus from meeting 40% global sales by 2030 to a broader range of hybrids and plug-in hybrid models.
* The company will make hybrid versions of every vehicle in its lineup and repurpose an electric vehicle plant in Tennessee for gas-powered cars.
* Ford no longer plans an all-electric truck but will produce extended-range electrics, also known as "extended-range e-trucks" or EREVs.

**Main Ideas:**

1. Ford is revising its EV strategy due to challenges with powertrain technology and decreased government support.
2. The company will focus on hybrid models instead of meeting 40% global sales by 2030.
3. Hybrids will be made for almost every vehicle in the lineup, while EREVs will offer extended towing capacity and a longer range.

Ford is making significant changes to its electric vehicle plan, shifting the focus from meeting a specific sales target to a broader range of hybrid and plug-in hybrid models.
