# 7 Lies Developers Keep Telling Themselves (And Somehow Still Believe) - DEV Community

**Development Industry Lies: A Collection of Humorous Truths**

### Key Points
- Many developers fall victim to similar struggles and frustrations.
- Industry-wide problems, challenges, and mistakes are common among professionals in the development field.

### Most Common "Lies"

*   **"This will only take a moment"**: The feeling of overwhelm and the struggle to separate work from personal tasks can lead individuals into taking on tasks that seem trivial but ultimately cause longer periods of effort. This statement reflects this feeling, as many developers find themselves in similar situations.

*   **"I'll refactor this later"**: Refactoring tasks are often put off until the last minute or buried at the bottom of a backlog. This habit can significantly increase the chances of not completing it before another deadline looms.
*   **"We’ll definitely finish this by the end of the sprint"**: Meeting project goals within an allotted timeframe without delaying work can be challenging. It's essential to balance short-term pressure with realistic expectations and buffers. Buffering work, in addition to planning, allows for flexibility.

*   **"I'll write the tests later"**: While it's good practice to develop comprehensive testing strategies, this mentality is often not followed. Writing tests right away can help alleviate the stress associated with unexpected issues.

*   **"It’s just an edge case, no one will ever do that"**: Everyone faces unique challenges in various projects and contexts. Embracing this mindset can lead to overlooking potential dangers and difficulties altogether.

*   **"This is definitely not on my side"**: Assuming developers place their tasks only at the very front of a sprint is incorrect and may undermine trust among team members when they inevitably hit roadblocks during development.
*   **"If it works on my machine, it’ll work everywhere"**: Testing software thoroughly ensures that cross-platform compatibility is maintained. Failing to conduct proper testing can lead to unexpected problems in the distribution stage.

### Advice from a Humorous Perspective

*   Work effectively by breaking tasks into manageable parts to avoid feeling overwhelmed.
*   Be realistic regarding completion times for your efforts as well as potential mistakes or complexities during development processes.
*   Always plan ahead and use buffers for both quick fixes and complex refactoring tasks.
