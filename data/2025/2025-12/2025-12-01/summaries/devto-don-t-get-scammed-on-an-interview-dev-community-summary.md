# Don't get scammed on an interview. - DEV Community

### Don't Get Scammed on an Interview (Summary)

Two recent experiences have led to a realization that not all "interviews" are legitimate. Both were scams targeting individuals looking to secure positions with companies.

## **The Playbook of Scammers**

Scammers employ the following tactics:

1.  Giving them access to your computer or device.
2.  Demanding you share your screen, including your personal files and home work.
3.  Asking for remote access to your computer or project.

### The Red Flags

Red flags in these scams can be subtle but significant:

*   Hidden scripts or fake icons on the victim's operating system
*   Fake company logos, such as MetaMask or Figma
*   Conversations that feel unprofessional or unnatural

## **Protecting Yourself**

To avoid falling prey to these scammers, follow these steps:

1.  Research the company beforehand and verify its legitimacy.
2.  Use secure methods to access shared files, such as GitHub Codespaces or code sandboxes with Copilot.
3.  Be cautious of demos and trials that demand remote access to your computer.
4.  Install security software to prevent malware from spreading on a victim's device.

By being aware of these tactics and taking necessary precautions, you can significantly reduce the risk of falling prey to these scams.
