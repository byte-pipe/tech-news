# Tell HN: Beware confidentiality agreements that act as lifetime non competes | Hacker News

**Problem/Opportunity:**

The problem presented is that some companies are employing non-competes as a lifetime non-compete agreement, which can be counterproductive to the employee's career goals. This practice is particularly problematic when it comes to high-growth areas of technology where employees are willing to sacrifice money and experience for a competitive offer from another company.

The opportunity arises because many companies are now taking an extremely broad interpretation of IP confidentiality, viewing any work in their industry as potentially detrimental to the non-competes. This raises concerns about the enforceability of such agreements and how they might impact the employee's access to technology and future opportunities.

**Market Indicators:**

* There is a growing concern among employees regarding lifetime non-compete agreements.
* High-growth areas of technology, such as artificial intelligence, blockchain, and cybersecurity, where companies are willing to sacrifice money and experience for a competitive offer from another company.
* There is evidence that some companies, like Prometric, have been taking an extremely broad interpretation of IP confidentiality.

**Technical Feasibility:**

* For a solo developer working for a small company or startup, it's challenging to meet 100% compensation requirements.
* Meeting 150% might be possible but require expertise in negotiations and persuasion skills.
* The complexity of negotiating with companies varies widely depending on the individual's experience, skills, and industry.

**Business Viability Signals:**

* Companies that often have lifetime non-compete agreements are those in high-growth industries where employees are willing to sacrifice money and experience.
* There is no evidence suggesting that companies are aggressively enforcing these agreements or providing sufficient compensation.
* The willingness to pay for the company seems likely but, as noted by benjamincburns, it's not necessarily a straightforward 150%.

**Actionable Insights:**

1. Employers should evaluate their non-compete agreement carefully considering their industry and competition.
2. Consider alternative hiring practices that prioritize employee experience over lifetime non-competes.
3. Employees can increase their chances of success by:
	* Staying flexible throughout their career
	* Negotiating with the company for a fair compensation package, including BATNA (Best Alternative to No Change)
	* Being aware of the 150% requirement and preparing accordingly
