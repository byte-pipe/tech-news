# Tactical Tornado: The New Default with LLMs

This article discusses the distinction between tactical and strategic approaches to software development, drawing on John Ousterhout's work. Tactical programming prioritizes getting tasks done quickly, often at the expense of good system design, leading to short-sightedness and the accumulation of technical debt.

## Tactical Programming and the Tactical Tornado

Tactical programming, driven by deadlines and a focus on immediate task completion, makes comprehensive system design difficult. The "tactical tornado" is a programmer who exemplifies this, delivering code rapidly but without considering long-term implications. While sometimes lauded for speed, tactical tornadoes often leave behind messy code that hinders future development by other engineers.

## LLMs and the Rise of Tactical Tornadoes

The author argues that the current trend of increasing reliance on Large Language Models (LLMs) aligns with the tactical tornado phenomenon. LLMs encourage a focus on individual tasks rather than the overall system design, hindering big-picture thinking and conceptual integrity. This is exacerbated by the decreasing need for developers to engage in lower-level coding, making it harder to maintain a strong mental model of the system.

## Societal and Industry Factors

This shift towards tactical programming is attributed to a short-termist societal mindset and a decline in the leverage of programmers to advocate for strategic decisions. Software design discussions are increasingly viewed as "bikeshedding."

## Risks of Over-reliance on LLMs

While acknowledging the power and productivity benefits of LLMs, the author cautions against taking them as a solution for software maintainability. There's a potential assumption that future models and tooling will automatically address technical debt accumulated through purely tactical development, which the author considers irresponsible to assume.

In essence, the article posits that the increasing use of LLMs is pushing the software development industry towards a "tactical tornado" approach, prioritizing speed over sustainable and well-designed systems.
