# Stephen Colbert Claims CBS Censored Interview Due to FCC Concerns

- Stephen Colbert stated on his show that CBS executives informed them they were prohibited from airing his interview with Texas state Representative James Talarico.
- Colbert was told they couldn't have Talarico on the broadcast and even couldn't mention this restriction.
- The unaired interview with Talarico, who is running for U.S. Senate, discussed the FCC's increased scrutiny of media outlets critical of President Trump, including a probe into ABC's "The View."
- Talarico suggested President Trump is concerned about Texas potentially shifting its political alignment.
- Colbert highlighted that the FCC, under Chairman Brendan Carr (appointed by Trump), has been increasingly assertive with networks perceived as critical of the administration.
- Carr has specifically emphasized the FCC's equal time rule regarding political candidates, though news and interview exceptions exist.
- Colbert accused Carr of being motivated by partisan purposes, suggesting the Trump administration wants to silence criticism on television.
- FCC Commissioner Anna M. Gomez condemned CBS's decision as "corporate capitulation" to political pressure and asserted the FCC has no legal authority to pressure broadcasters for political reasons.
- This incident follows a similar situation where ABC temporarily pulled "Jimmy Kimmel Live!" from the air after Carr criticized comments Kimmel made about Charlie Kirk.
