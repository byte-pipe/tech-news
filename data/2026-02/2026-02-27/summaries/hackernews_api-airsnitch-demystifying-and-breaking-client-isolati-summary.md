**Introduction**

* The article discusses the concept of client isolation in Wi-Fi networks
* Client isolation refers to the separation of individual devices from each other, allowing each device to connect to its own network

**Key Points**

* Traditional Wi-Fi networks use a hub-and-spoke model, where all devices are connected to the same switch and share the same IP addressing.
* This leads to broadcast interference and packet loss among client devices
* To mitigate this issue, clients must be isolated from each other using Quality of Service (QoS) and Traffic Management techniques

**Demystifying Client Isolation**

* QoS is used to prioritize traffic from specific clients based on their importance or priority
* Traffic management techniques are employed to ensure that essential applications like VoIP and video streaming have sufficient bandwidth
* Client isolation can be achieved using technologies like IGMP snooping, VLANs (Virtual Local Area Networks), and Quality of Service controllers

**Breaking Down Client Isolation**

* Using a combination of IP address assignment, DNS server configuration, and QoS-based traffic prioritization ensures that clients are not interfering with each other
* Implementing techniques like IPv6 address partitioning and QoS control helps to eliminate inter-client interference

	+ Increased user experience by minimizing packet loss and dropped connections
