# A New Start After 60: How Vickie Hardin Woods Baked Her Way Through Retirement

Vickie Hardin Woods retired from her career as a city planner in Salem, Oregon, after 30 years of dedication and hard work. Worried about losing her identity as a professional, she decided to take matters into her own hands.

In an unusual plan, Woods began baking pies every day for the next year, using fresh ingredients local to her home and giving each pie away to strangers around Salem. For one day, she would bake in someone's kitchen or give them a pie directly from the oven.

Throughout the year, Woods continued this practice, changing her recipient list and location almost daily. She gave pies to:

*   Her brother
*   A 88-year-old aunt
*   Several high school friends (most had twins)
*   A homeless man who received an unexpected pie from him

The recipients responded with kindness, affection, and gratitude. Woods felt comforted by the interactions, which reminded her of the sense of community she experienced as a teenager when living with her aunt and uncle.
