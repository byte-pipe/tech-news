# Adobe Firefly's video editor can now automatically create a first draft from footage | TechCrunch

**Adobe Firefly's Quick Cut Feature: A New Era in Video Creation**

The Adobe Firefly software has introduced a feature called Quick Cut that utilizes AI technology to automatically create a first draft of the final video based on user instructions. This innovative tool streamlines the video editing process, allowing users to describe their desired outcome and the AI-powered system will edit out irrelevant parts of the footage.

**Key Features of the Quick Cut Feature**

*   Users can describe their desired video in natural language, allowing for fast turnaround times
*   The feature automatically edits out unnecessary footage and selects frames from the B-roll library
*   Selective use of B-roll footage enables users to create seamless transitions between cuts
*   Prompt box within the Firefly video editor allows users to set settings such as aspect ratio and pacing
*   Optional additional B-roll footage can be added to further enhance the video

**Noteworthy Insights**

Adobe emphasizes that Quick Cut is designed with creators in mind, aiming to deliver a quick draft of their project while still allowing for customization. The feature is intended to help users achieve fast turnaround times and creative freedom.

Regarding technical details, Adobe has pushed regular updates to its video-related tools, leading to the development of new features like the advanced timeline-based video editor with layers and prompt-based editing. This evolution demonstrates Adobe's ongoing commitment to improving its software and staying ahead in the rapidly evolving landscape of digital content creation.
