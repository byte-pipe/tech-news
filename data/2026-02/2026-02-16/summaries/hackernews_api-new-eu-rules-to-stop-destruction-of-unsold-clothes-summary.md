# New EU Rules to Stop Destruction of Unsold Clothes and Shoes

## Summary
- The European Commission has adopted new measures under the Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR) to prevent the destruction of unsold apparel, clothing accessories, and footwear.
- This initiative aims to reduce waste, minimize environmental damage (estimated 4-9% of unsold textiles destroyed annually, generating ~5.6 million tons of CO2 emissions), and foster a more circular economy for businesses.
- Key measures include requiring companies to disclose information on discarded unsold goods (from February 2027) and introducing a ban on the destruction of these products for large companies starting July 19, 2026, with medium-sized companies following in 2030.
- Specific circumstances for destruction (e.g., safety reasons, product damage) will be outlined in a Delegated Act, with national authorities overseeing compliance.
- Companies are encouraged to improve stock management, handle returns effectively, and explore alternatives like resale, remanufacturing, donations, or reuse instead of discarding unsold stock.
- The new rules apply to large companies from 2026 and will extend to medium-sized companies by 2030. Disclosure requirements under the ESPR already apply to large companies and will also apply to medium-sized companies in 2030.
- Commissioner for Environment, Water Resilience and a Competitive Circular Economy, Jessika Roswall, highlighted the textile sector's leadership in sustainability and the need for these measures to boost competitiveness and reduce dependencies.
- The destruction of unsold goods is a significant issue, with substantial financial losses in countries like France and Germany. The ESPR aims to make products more durable, reusable, and recyclable.

## Key Points
- **Ban on Destruction:** Large companies will be prohibited from destroying unsold apparel, clothing accessories, and footwear from July 19, 2026. Medium-sized companies will follow in 2030.
- **Disclosure Requirements:** Companies must report the volumes of unsold consumer goods they discard, starting February 2027.
- **Exceptions:** Specific, justified circumstances (safety, product damage) will allow for destruction, overseen by national authorities.
- **Circular Economy Focus:** The measures encourage businesses to adopt more sustainable practices like improved stock management, returns handling, and exploring reuse options.
- **ESPR Integration:** These rules are part of the broader ESPR, which aims to improve product sustainability across the EU market.
- **Environmental Impact:** The destruction of unsold textiles contributes significantly to CO2 emissions and environmental damage.
