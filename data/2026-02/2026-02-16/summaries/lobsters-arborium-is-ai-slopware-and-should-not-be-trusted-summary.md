# Arborium is AI slopware and should not be trusted

This article details the author's experience integrating Arborium, a tree-sitter based syntax highlighting tool, into their website and their subsequent concerns that the project is largely generated by artificial intelligence.

## Initial Interest and Integration Attempts
The author, who prefers the Lzeer parsing and highlighting system, was intrigued by Arborium's potential for web use. They quickly integrated it into their site, but encountered issues with the `window` global object, which is not available in environments like Deno. They attempted workarounds, including manually loading language grammars and using Deno-specific dynamic imports, but continued to face problems.

## Recurring Bugs and Developer Response
Despite efforts to resolve the bugs, the author encountered the same issues repeatedly. They filed two issues on Arborium's GitHub repository. The maintainer, Amos Wenger, quickly responded with a pull request that seemingly fixed the problems.

## Concerns about AI Generation
While the bugs were resolved, the author developed a strong suspicion that Arborium is primarily written by AI. This suspicion is based on several factors:
* **Website inconsistencies:** The Arborium website contains AI-like phrasing, incorrect/outdated links, unhighlighted code blocks, and a lack of comprehensive documentation.
* **Lack of documentation:** The documentation is limited to basic use cases, leaving users without guidance for more complex implementations.
* **Amos' history with AI:** The author was aware of Amos Wenger's experimentation with AI and felt the depth of AI-like qualities in the project was concerning.

## Recent Drama and Community Impact
The author's experience aligns with recent controversy surrounding Arborium. Amos Wenger was accused by Amosaccusedzkat of "witch hunting" after zkat added Amos to a list of "open slopware" (software and developers using AI). This led to backlash against the "open slopware" list and harassment of zkat, who subsequently announced their departure from the open-source community.

## Conclusion
Ultimately, despite the bug fixes, the author decided against using Arborium due to their concerns about its potential AI-generated nature and the associated issues. They plan to stick with their existing Lzeer integration.
