# Europe's $24 Trillion Breakup With Visa and Mastercard

## Summary
- The European Central Bank (ECB) President Christine Lagarde has urged Europe to develop its own digital payment infrastructure to reduce reliance on American payment networks like Visa and Mastercard, which currently process approximately $24 trillion in annual transactions and handle 56% of EU cashless payments.
- A coalition of 16 European banks has launched the European Payments Initiative (EPI) and the EuroPA Alliance to create a pan-European interoperable payment network called Wero. This system aims to allow Europeans to make payments across borders without involving American networks.
- The EPI and EuroPA Alliance have signed an agreement connecting approximately 130 million users across 13 European countries, covering roughly 72% of the EU and Norway population. Retail payments are being rolled out in several countries in 2026-2027.
- Previous attempts at creating a unified European payment system have failed due to fragmentation and competing national solutions.
- The development of Wero is being complemented by the ECB's digital euro project, a central bank-backed digital currency for the eurozone.
- While challenges remain, including significant investment costs and entrenched consumer habits, the political momentum for European payment sovereignty is strong, driven by geopolitical factors and a desire for greater economic independence.

## Key Points
- Europe's payment infrastructure is heavily reliant on American companies like Visa and Mastercard, raising concerns about data sovereignty and potential disruptions.
- The EPI's Wero system is a private-sector initiative designed to provide a European alternative, leveraging existing instant credit transfer systems.
- The EuroPA Alliance partnership significantly expands Wero's reach across multiple European countries.
- The project aims to address the vulnerabilities exposed during the sanctions against Russia, which disrupted its domestic payment systems.
- The digital euro project is a parallel initiative by the ECB to create a central bank-backed digital currency.
- Despite skepticism, the initiative benefits from strong political support and a growing need for European strategic autonomy.
