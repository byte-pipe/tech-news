# Discord Launches Teen-by-Default Settings Globally

* Discord is rolling out enhanced teen safety features globally, making a teen-appropriate experience the default for all users aged 13 and above.
* This update, launching in early March, includes updated communication settings, restricted access to age-gated spaces, and content filtering.
* Age assurance is central to this new experience, allowing users to verify their age through facial age estimation or identification, with options for future methods.
* Privacy protections are a key aspect, with on-device processing for facial scans, quick deletion of submitted documents, straightforward verification processes, and private age verification status.
* Default safety settings include content filters requiring adult verification, restricted access to age-restricted channels and commands, a separate inbox for unknown message requests, friend request alerts, and stage restrictions for adults only.
* Discord is also launching a Teen Council of 10-12 teens to provide input on product features, policies, and educational resources to ensure teen safety and privacy.
* This initiative builds upon Discord's existing safety ecosystem, which includes tools like Ignore, Family Center, and a warning system.
* Discord emphasizes its commitment to creating a privacy-forward and age-appropriate platform that empowers teens for meaningful connections and digital well-being.
* Interested teens aged 13-17 can apply to join the Teen Council until May 1, 2026.
* Discord is a communication platform with over 200 million monthly active users, focused on building meaningful connections around gaming through voice, video, and text.
