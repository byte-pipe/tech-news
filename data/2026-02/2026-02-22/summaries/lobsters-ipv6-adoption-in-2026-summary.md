# IPv6 Adoption in 2026

This article discusses the slow progress of IPv6 adoption in 2026, 30 years after its definition in 1995. Despite the availability of IPv6 support from major providers like Akamai, Cloudflare, and Google, adoption rates remain around 40-45% of HTTP traffic.

Key findings include:

* **DNS:** Approximately 98.75% of top-level domains (TLDs) use IPv6 for their name servers. However, only 69.30% of the top 1 million domains have at least one dual-stack name server (supporting both IPv4 and IPv6).
* **HTTP:** Slightly more than a third of the top 1 million domains are IPv6 enabled, despite the availability of IPv6 support from CDNs. This is attributed to historical reluctance and the disabling of IPv6 by some organizations.
* **SMTP:** Fewer than half of the top 1 million domains' mail servers support IPv6. This is partly due to challenges with SMTP abuse prevention mechanisms and a lack of consistent implementation.
* **Overall:** The lack of widespread IPv6 adoption is frustrating and is exacerbated by a lack of exposure to IPv6 in educational institutions, leading to a preference for older technologies like NAT and RFC1918 addresses.

The author concludes that without IPv6 being considered a fundamental requirement, its adoption will likely remain slow.
