# Keep Android Open

# Keep Android Open: Summary

This article reports on Google's announcement that starting September 2026, app development for Android will require central registration with Google. This registration involves fees, agreeing to Google's terms, providing identification, uploading developer keys, and listing all app identifiers.

## Implications for Users
* **Loss of Open Platform:** Consumers will lose the ability to install any software they choose on their Android devices, as Google will control which apps are permitted.
* **Restricted App Development:** Developers will need Google's approval to share apps directly with users, hindering the open nature of the Android ecosystem.
* **Erosion of Digital Sovereignty:** Governments will cede control over their citizens' access to software to Google, potentially impacting essential services.

## Google's Stance
Despite vague mentions of a potential "advanced flow," Google maintains that all apps will require developer verification for installation on certified devices starting September 2026. Unregistered apps will be blocked.

## How to Help
**Developers:** Refuse to sign up for the verification program and discourage others from doing so.
**Everyone:** Install F-Droid, provide feedback to Google, raise awareness on social media, combat misinformation, and contribute to the Keep Android Open project.
**Website Owners:** Add a countdown banner to their sites.
**Consumers:** Contact national regulators with polite and specific complaints about the potential harm to consumers and competition. The article provides contact information for regulators in various countries.

The article emphasizes that developer acquiescence is crucial for Google's plan to succeed and urges collective action from developers, users, website owners, and consumers to resist this change.
