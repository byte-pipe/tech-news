# We mourn our craft

This post by Nolan Lawson reflects on the impact of AI tools on software development. The author expresses concern that AI's increasing ability to generate code will devalue the skills of human programmers, potentially leading to reduced job roles and diminished professional satisfaction.

Key points:

* AI tools are becoming increasingly proficient at writing code, posing a significant challenge to human programmers.
* The author anticipates a future where junior developers may surpass senior developers due to AI assistance, potentially impacting compensation and career progression.
* There is a sense of loss for the traditional aspects of programming, such as the problem-solving process, the satisfaction of creating original work, and the camaraderie within the developer community.
* The author acknowledges the inevitability of this shift, comparing it to the obsolescence of older tools and suggesting that the current era may be remembered with a mix of nostalgia and understanding.
* The post invites a collective mourning for the way software was traditionally created, recognizing that the future of the craft is changing.

The author concludes by stating that while the change is unavoidable, it is a time for reflection and mourning the loss of a particular way of working.
