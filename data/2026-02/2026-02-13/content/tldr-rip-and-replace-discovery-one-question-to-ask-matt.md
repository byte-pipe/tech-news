description: 'If I had to choose only ONE question to ask during discovery conversations when I''m trying to rip and replace an incumbent, it would without a doubt be this one: "What is it about your current solution that makes you not love it a hundred percent?" Here''s why it works (IMO): "What don''t you like about your current vendor?" triggers defensiveness. They may have been the ones who bought the thing. Admitting they hate it feels like admitting they made a mistake. "What challenges are you facing?" gets bullshit answers. They''ve said it in six other vendor calls this month. But "what makes you not love it a hundred percent?" gives them permission to be honest without requiring them to trash anyone. Nobody loves anything completely. There''s always friction. Always something slightly off. And when you create space for that admission, they can open up. So that''s my one question. That said, the important stuff is what happens next. After they answer, resist the urge to pitch whatever
  you''re selling. Go deeper instead. - "Tell me more about that." - "How long has that been going on?" - "What have you tried so far to fix it?" The second and third layers are where the REAL pain lives. Surface answers get surface deals. Deep answers get signed contracts. Here''s a quick discovery framework that builds on this whole idea: Call it the 3x3 model. 3 types of questions. 3 layers deep on each. 1. Situational questions to establish context. - What does your current workflow look like? - Who''s involved? - What tools are you using today? Now go three layers deeper: - What''s the process? - Who owns it? - How long has it been this way? 2. Gap questions to uncover friction. - What''s not working the way you''d want? - Where do things break down? - What takes longer than it should? Again, go three layers deeper: - What''s the gap? - What''s causing it? - What has that cost you? 3. Future state questions (to help reveal the buying vision). - If this worked perfectly, what would change?
  - What would you be able to do that you can''t do today? - What would success look like in six months? Aaaaaand again, go three layers deeper: - What''s the goal? - Why does it matter? - What happens if you don''t get there? Each layer doubles the value of the insight. Situational questions tell you what exists. Gap questions tell you what hurts. Future state questions tell you what they''ll pay for. That''s the whole game. | 71 comments on LinkedIn'
