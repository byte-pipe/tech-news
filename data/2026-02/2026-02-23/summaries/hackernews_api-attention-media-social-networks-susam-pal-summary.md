# Attention Media ≠ Social Networks
By Susam Pal on 20 Jan 2026

* Early web-based social networks (around the early 2000s) were genuinely social, allowing users to connect with known individuals and receive their updates through meaningful notifications. This coincided with optimism around Web 2.0 and the internet's potential.
* Between 2012 and 2016, these platforms shifted significantly. The introduction of infinite scroll disrupted the user experience, and notifications became arbitrary and self-serving, losing their relevance to the user.
* Over time, timelines became dominated by content from strangers, transforming these platforms into "attention media" designed to capture and monetize user attention through endless scrolling of often irrelevant content.
* Mastodon emerged as an alternative, reminiscent of early Twitter. It prioritizes following a small number of genuinely interesting people, resulting in a timeline of updates solely from those connections.
* Mastodon offers a more controlled and predictable social networking experience, where content is a direct result of user choices rather than a system designed to maximize attention capture. It feels closer to the original concept of social networking.
