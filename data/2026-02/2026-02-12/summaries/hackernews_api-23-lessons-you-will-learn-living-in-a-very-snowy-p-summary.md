# 23 Lessons Learned Living in a Very Snowy Place

This article provides practical advice for surviving and coping with heavy snowstorms, drawing on the experiences of someone living in a snowy region.

## Preparing for a Snowstorm

*   **Power Outages:** Be prepared for potential power outages by having supplies readily available.
*   **Power Restoration:** Understand that sudden brief power returns might not signify a sustained restoration.
*   **Instruction Manuals:** Utilize the instruction manuals for household equipment like cars, snowblowers, and wood stoves for useful information.
*   **Essential Supplies:** Maintain a stock of batteries, flashlights, shelf-stable food, warm clothing, and drinking water.

## Shoveling Snow

*   **Fresh Snow is Easiest:** Shoveling is easiest when snow has just fallen, as it hasn't had time to become dense.
*   **Snow is Heavy:** Recognize the significant weight of snow.
*   **Grit Over Strength:** While physical strength helps, determination ("grit") is often more important for shoveling.
*   **Neighborly Help:** Enlist the help of neighbors with snowplows.
*   **Roof Clearing:** Clear snow from your roof to prevent structural damage. Use appropriate tools and wear a hooded coat.

## Staying Warm

*   **Propane Dependence:** Even with propane heating, electricity might be needed to run it, so have backup plans like a wood stove.
*   **Kindling Alternatives:** Cardboard can be used to start a fire if kindling is scarce.
*   **Waterproof Outerwear:** Own rain pants or snow pants to stay dry.
*   **Layered Clothing:** Keep a change of dry clothes readily accessible for brief outdoor trips.
*   **Avoid Getting Wet:** Prioritize staying dry and warm.
*   **Gaiters:** Use gaiters to prevent snow from getting into your boots.

## Additional Preparedness

*   **Burning Man Finds:** If possible, live in a house that receives unexpected useful items from a Burning Man camp.
*   **Emergency Resources:** Be aware of potential emergency resources like stored firewood.

## Generators

*   **Safe Generator Placement:** Never run generators indoors or in enclosed spaces. Place them in a well-ventilated, elevated structure away from occupied buildings.
*   **Generator Maintenance:** Regularly check and winterize generators according to the manufacturer's instructions.

## Water

*   **Well Water Backup:** If you have a well, ensure you have a supply of stored water.
*   **Toilet Flushing:** To flush a toilet without a tank, use about a gallon of water in the bowl.
*   **Snow Melting for Water:** Snow can be melted for drinking water using a wood stove.

## Morale

*   **Limited Leisure:** While you might have time for hobbies, you might find little joy in them and may long for engaging entertainment.
*   **Reading for Perspective:** Consider reading books like "Shadows on the Koyukuk" for historical context and resilience.
*   **Modern Advantages:** Remember that modern conveniences offer advantages over the hardships faced by people in the past.
