# FOSDEM 2026 + Hackathon

This article summarizes the recent FOSDEM 2026 and associated Hackathon events, highlighting key activities, discussions, and outcomes.

## FOSDEM 2026

* **Stand:** The postmarketOS stand featured version v0.3 of the ophone-harnessPCB, which generated significant interest. They also offered phone stands, shirts (donated), namecards, and a QR code for donations. Interactions were plentiful, including assistance with phone updates.
* **Talks:** The FOSS on Mobile devroom was well-attended. Several postmarketOS-related talks were presented, including:
    * postmarketOS: Reliability in 2026 by Oliver
    * Mainline kernel for Fairphone 2026 update by Luca
    * Phosh: What's new and where are we going? by Evangelos
    * Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 Mainline: From Day-1 Patches to Product Reality by Neil
    * Running mainline Linux on the Unisoc-based Jolla C2 by Affe
    * The Linux Phone App Ecosystem (2026) by Peter
    * Photos and Video Recording on Mobile Phone by Pavel M.
    * UnifiedPush - Push notifications. Decentralized and Open Source by Daniel G. and S1m
    * Collabora Office Can Finally Run on Mobile Linux by Skyler
* **Podcast:** A podcast recording took place in the parking lot with special guests from other projects.
* **Hardware CI Setup:** Federico, who co-created the PCB with Nicola, was present to discuss the hardware CI setup with interested attendees.

## Hackathon

* **Venue:** The Hackathon utilized two floors, with the top floor primarily for sleeping and the lower floor featuring a living room for discussions and hacking.
* **Schedule:** A one-hour session schedule was created using post-it notes, with a total of eight slots per day, including parallel sessions and lunch breaks. The schedule was largely adhered to.
* **Evening Activities:** Fun activities were held each evening, and additional time was used for discussions and project improvement.

### Monday

* **Reliability:** A three-hour discussion focused on new requirements for the main category (PMC R0009), emphasizing hardware CI and the project's endorsement of devices in this category. Plans to rework the PMC were discussed.
* **AI Policy:** The current AI policy will be updated to explicitly prohibit the use of generative AI in the project. The policy will be split into a core policy and separate reasoning.
* **HW-CI PCB Workshop:** Federico demonstrated the HW-CI PCB, and attendees spent time setting up CI-tron and connecting devices. Federico's presentation was well-received, and a standalone talk at a conference was recommended.
* **Financial Review & Budget Approval:** A new budget was approved, with a separate blog post planned.
* **Security:** A presentation by Alpine Linux on vulnerability monitoring sparked discussion about potential collaboration for postmarketOS.
* **PipeWire for Audio:** Discussions continued to advance the transition to PipeWire for audio.
* **Movie Night:** The movie night featured "COSMIC Journey" (1936).

### Tuesday

* **Push Notification Blockers:** Efforts were made to unblock push notification blockers.
* **Legal Entity:** Pablo presented research on establishing a legal entity (e.V.) as a backup plan, and it was agreed that they should proceed.
* **Power Delegation and Teams:** Discussions focused on PMC R0008 for better organization, aligning with the upcoming legal entity. The "Board" will be renamed "Financial Team," and a new "assembly" of Trusted Contributors will make important decisions.
