# How I use Obsidian

This article details Steph Ango's personal workflow using Obsidian, a note-taking and knowledge management application. Their approach is described as "bottom-up," emphasizing flexibility and emergent structure over rigid organization.

## Vault Structure and Tools
- The vault is a simple folder of files, aligning with a file-over-app philosophy for long-term data control.
- Key tools integrated with Obsidian include:
    - Mini theme with Flexo color scheme for aesthetics.
    - Obsidian Web Clipper for saving web content.
    - Obsidian Sync for cross-device note synchronization.
    - Obsidian Bases for category-based note views.
    - Obsidian Maps for visual organization in specific templates.

## Personal Rules and Organization Philosophy
- Steph emphasizes consistent style as a way to reduce decision fatigue.
- Core rules include avoiding multiple vaults and folders, sticking to standard Markdown, always pluralizing categories and tags, using internal links, using YYYY-MM-DD dates, employing a 7-point rating scale, and maintaining a single weekly to-do list.
- Organization is primarily driven by the categories property, with most notes residing in the root for personal content.
- Reference notes are kept in dedicated folders (References and Clippings), while administrative elements (Attachments, Daily notes, Templates) are in separate folders for clarity in the downloadable vault.

## Linking and Knowledge Connections
- Extensive use of internal links is central to Steph's workflow, often linking the first mention of concepts.
- Unresolved links serve as breadcrumbs for future connections.
- Journal entries often weave together various topics through linking, connecting to reference notes for details and clippings for external content.
- This linking style facilitates tracing idea evolution and identifying branching paths.

## Fractal Journaling and Review
- Daily thoughts are captured using a dedicated hotkey, creating timestamped notes.
- Regular reviews (daily, monthly, yearly) of these fragments create a fractal web of ideas, allowing for zooming in and out of detail and identifying emerging themes.
- Periodic "random revisits" using a hotkey enable serendipitous discovery of old ideas and maintenance of the vault.

## Properties and Templates
- Templates are used extensively to streamline note creation and pre-populate properties.
- Properties capture key information like dates, people, themes, locations, and ratings, designed for reusability across categories.
- Templates are designed to be composable, allowing for the addition of specific information to a base note.
- Short property names and default to list types for potential future expansion are preferred.

## Rating System
- A 7-point rating scale is used to express the quality or value of things, with higher numbers indicating stronger positive experiences.

## Publishing to the Web
