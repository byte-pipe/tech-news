description: 'GTM is the MOST overused phrase in tech today. But GTM ≠ Product Launch ≠ Product Marketing ❌ Too often, the lines between them get blurred. But understanding how they fit together is key to driving real impact. Here is how I define them: ✔️ A GTM strategy is the broad, company-wide plan for reaching customers and driving revenue. It’s what’s a company operates on. ✔️ A Product Launch is a subset of GTM, focused on introducing something new to the market through a MARKETING strategy ✔️ Product Marketing (PMM) is the FUNCTION that bridges insights, positioning, messaging, and enablement—playing a role in both GTM and launches. Think of it this way: 🏈 GTM Strategy = The full game plan. (Expanding into a new market, pricing shifts, sales motions, etc.) 🎯 Product Launch = A play within the game. (New feature release, app launch.) 💪 PMM = The strategist on the field. (Positioning, storytelling, enablement.) A lot of PMMs think we are quarterbacks in driving the GTM, but other functions
  are also crucial because you cannot win in the long run with just a strong quarterback and a disconnected team. This is why it’s SO important for PMMs to influence and build cross-functional relationships with other teams, and why the role is ultimately a connector role.
