# Canva acquires startups working on animation and marketing | TechCrunch

# Canva’s Dual Acquisition of Cavalry and MangoAI

## Overview of Acquisitions
- Canva announced the purchase of two startups: **Cavalry**, a UK‑based 2D motion animation platform, and **MangoAI**, a stealth startup focused on reinforcement‑learning‑driven video ad performance.
- Both acquisitions aim to expand Canva’s Creative OS beyond static design into motion editing and advanced marketing analytics.

## Cavalry – Motion Animation
- Specializes in 2D motion animation for advertising, marketing, gaming, and generative art.
- Will be integrated with Canva’s professional suite **Affinity**, which already offers photo, vector, and layout editing.
- Canva’s blog states the combination “closes the motion editing gap,” delivering a full‑stack solution for professionals.
- Expected outcome: a unified toolset covering photo, vector, layout, and motion editing within Canva’s platform.

## MangoAI – Ad Performance AI
- Built by former Netflix and Roblox data experts Nirmal Govind (now Canva’s first Chief Algorithms Officer) and Vinith Misra (joining Canva’s marketing product team).
- Develops reinforcement‑learning systems that help clients create, launch, and iteratively improve video ads.
- Aligns with Canva’s recent launch of **Canva Grow**, a growth‑focused tool for asset creation and performance measurement.

## Strategic Implications
- Enhances Canva’s positioning as an end‑to‑end marketing solution, adding video creation and granular performance analytics.
- Complements earlier moves: acquisition of **MagicBrief** (marketing intelligence) in Jan 2025 and the release of Canva Grow.
- Signals Canva’s intent to compete with larger creative and ad‑tech platforms by offering a comprehensive, professional‑grade Creative OS.

## Recent Performance Highlights
- Closed 2025 with **$4 billion** in annualized revenue.
- User base: **265 million** total users, **31 million** paid subscribers.
- Canva Grow is reported to be “doing incredibly well,” especially for static content on Meta platforms, with plans to expand into multi‑platform video creation.
