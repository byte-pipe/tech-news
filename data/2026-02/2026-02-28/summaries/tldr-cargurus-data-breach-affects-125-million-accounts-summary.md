# CarGurus data breach affects 12.5 million accounts

## Overview
- Automotive marketplace CarGurus suffered a data breach exposing names, email addresses, phone numbers, and physical addresses of millions of customers.
- Have I Been Pwned reported that **12.5 million** CarGurus accounts were compromised.

## Breach details
- Stolen data included:
  - User account ID mappings
  - Finance pre‑qualification application data
  - Dealer account and subscription information
- The breach has been contained according to CarGurus.

## Attribution
- Have I Been Pwned attributed the breach to the **ShinyHunters** hacking group.
- ShinyHunters are known for social‑engineering tactics, such as impersonating employees to reset passwords.
- Prior activities include data theft from several universities, over a billion records from Salesforce customers (including Google and Workday), and alleged hacks of Pornhub and fintech lender Figure.

## Company response
- Spokesperson Maggie Meluzio confirmed the incident and stated:
  - No evidence of compromise to dealer data feeds, APIs, core systems, or products used by consumers or dealer partners.
  - Operations remain fully functional without interruption.
  - Affected individuals will be notified in compliance with applicable laws.
- CarGurus did not dispute the 12.5 million figure reported by Have I Been Pwned.

## Additional context
- This is the second automotive‑related breach reported by Have I Been Pwned in 2024.
- The previous incident involved CarMax data (≈ 431 k unique email addresses, names, phone numbers, and physical addresses) after a failed extortion attempt.
