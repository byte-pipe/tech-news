# AI music generator Suno hits 2M paid subscribers and $300M in annual recurring revenue

## Overview
- Suno, an AI‑driven music generation platform, announced 2 million paid subscribers and $300 million in annual recurring revenue (ARR).
- The announcement was shared by founder and CEO Mikey Shulman on LinkedIn.

## Recent Growth
- Three months earlier Suno reported $200 million ARR after a $250 million funding round that valued the company at $2.45 billion.
- The latest figures indicate rapid revenue growth within a short period.

## Product Highlights
- Users create music by entering natural‑language prompts, enabling people with little musical training to produce audio quickly.
- Generated tracks have been competitive enough to appear on Spotify and Billboard charts.

## Legal and Industry Developments
- Musicians and record labels sued Suno for alleged copyright infringement, claiming the AI model was trained on existing recordings.
- Warner Music Group settled its lawsuit and struck a deal allowing Suno to launch models that incorporate licensed music from Warner’s catalog.

## Notable Success Story
- Telisha Jones (31, Mississippi) used Suno to turn her poetry into the viral R&B song “How Was I Supposed to Know.”
- The track led to a record‑deal with Hallwood Media reportedly worth $3 million.

## Industry Reaction
- Prominent artists such as Billie Eilish, Chappell Roan, and Katy Perry have publicly criticized the use of AI in music creation.
