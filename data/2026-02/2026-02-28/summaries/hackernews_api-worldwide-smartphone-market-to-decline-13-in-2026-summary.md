# Worldwide Smartphone Market to Decline 13% in 2026, Marking the Largest Drop Ever Due to the Memory Shortage Crisis, according to IDC

## Forecast Overview
- IDC projects worldwide smartphone shipments to fall 12.9% YoY in 2026, reaching 1.1 billion units.
- The 2026 volume will be the lowest in more than a decade.
- Average selling price (ASP) is expected to rise 14% to $523, a record high.

## Causes & Impact
- A severe memory‑chip shortage is described as a “tsunami‑like shock” affecting the entire consumer‑electronics supply chain.
- Rising component costs will squeeze margins, especially for low‑end Android manufacturers.
- Apple and Samsung are positioned to weather the crisis better and may gain market share.
- The shortage is seen as a structural reset, reshaping total addressable market, vendor landscape, and product mix.
- Sub‑$100 segment (≈171 million devices) is projected to become permanently uneconomical.

## Regional Outlook
- Markets dominated by low‑end smartphones will see the steepest declines:
  - Middle East & Africa: –20.6% YoY
  - China: –10.5% YoY
  - Asia‑Pacific (ex‑Japan & China): –13.1% YoY
- IDC forecasts modest recovery of 2% in 2027 and a stronger rebound of 5.2% in 2028 as memory prices stabilize.

## Market Implications
- Expect consolidation as smaller vendors exit the market.
- Low‑end vendors face sharp shipment drops due to supply constraints and reduced demand at higher price points.
- No return to “business as usual” for both vendors and consumers in the near term.
