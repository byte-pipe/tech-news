# Ministry of Justice Orders Deletion of UK's Largest Court Reporting Database

* The Ministry of Justice has ordered the deletion of Courtsdesk, the UK's largest digital archive for tracking criminal court cases.
* The deletion is reportedly set to occur within days after HM Courts & Tribunals Service (HMCTS) ordered the wiping of all records.
* Courtsdesk was used by over 1,500 journalists from 39 media outlets to search magistrates' court lists and registers.
* The platform claimed that media was frequently not notified of hearings, with two-thirds of courts regularly holding cases without informing journalists.
* Courtsdesk was launched in 2020 following an agreement with HMCTS and approval from the Lord Chancellor and former Justice Minister Chris Philp.
* HMCTS issued a cessation notice in November, citing "unauthorised sharing" of court information.
* Courtsdesk founder Enda Leahy stated the company made 16 attempts to save the service, including appeals to the Information Commissioner's Office and direct contact with current Justice Minister Sarah Sackman, but these were unsuccessful.
* Leahy highlighted that HMCTS's own records were inaccurate 4.2% of the time, and 1.6 million criminal hearings proceeded without prior notification to the press.
* HMCTS stated that the press will continue to have full access to court information to support accurate reporting.
* The decision to delete Courtsdesk was made to protect sensitive data after the platform shared information with a third-party AI company.
* Journalists' access to court information is not affected, as listings and records remain available.
