# Anthropic Attempts to Conceal Claude's AI Actions, Drawing Developer Criticism

## Summary
Anthropic updated Claude Code, its AI coding tool, by changing the progress output to hide the names of files being accessed. This change has been met with strong opposition from developers who argue that visibility into file access is crucial for security, context understanding, and auditing. The new default output provides limited information, and while a verbose mode was introduced, it was deemed insufficient by many users. Anthropic's creator defended the change as a simplification for focusing on code diffs and bash/mcp outputs, claiming their internal developers appreciated the reduced noise. However, developers emphasize the importance of transparency for debugging, error prevention, and managing token usage. The debate highlights concerns about the opacity of AI tools and the potential for mistakes to go unnoticed without clear visibility into their operations.
