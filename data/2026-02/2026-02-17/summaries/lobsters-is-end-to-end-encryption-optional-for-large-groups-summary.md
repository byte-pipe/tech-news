# Is End-to-End Encryption (E2EE) Optional For Large Groups? - Dhole Moments

This article discusses the debate around prioritizing end-to-end encryption (E2EE) when developing alternatives to Discord. The author argues against the notion that E2EE is optional, emphasizing its crucial role in user privacy and the viability of a new platform.

## Opposition to Prioritizing E2EE

The author references 0xabad1dea's arguments against prioritizing E2EE, stating that user experience and core functionality are more important for adoption than encryption. 0xabad1dea suggests that features like federation and multipart E2EE are not essential for replacing Discord.

## Why E2EE Remains Essential

Despite the arguments against it, the author contends that E2EE is still vital for a Discord alternative due to several reasons:

* **Age Verification Laws:** Centralized platforms are likely to face age verification laws, and without E2EE, these platforms would be compelled to provide user data to authorities.
* **Targeting of Federated Systems:** While federation offers advantages like avoiding central points of failure and jurisdictional restrictions, it also makes instances attractive targets for law enforcement and malicious actors. E2EE protects user data even if instance operators are pressured.
* **Protection for Infrastructure Operators:** E2EE safeguards the people operating the platform by preventing the disclosure of user data to authorities.

## E2EE Should Be Ubiquitous

The author advocates for E2EE to be a fundamental requirement for all communication software, given the increasing data collection by advertisers and large language models. They also stress the importance of user understanding and caution regarding the limitations of encryption.

## Improving E2EE

The article notes that current E2EE implementations in some protocols like Matrix have usability issues. To make E2EE truly ubiquitous, developers need to improve the user experience and scalability of these technologies.

## Scalability and Message History

The author confirms that E2EE can scale to thousands of users, citing the TreeKEM protocol as an example. They also address the possibility of maintaining message history with E2EE through encrypted backup features.

## Conclusion

The author concludes that E2EE is not optional for a successful Discord alternative. It is essential for protecting user privacy, ensuring the resilience of federated systems, and safeguarding the infrastructure operators. The article emphasizes that E2EE should be a core feature, and improvements in its usability are necessary.
