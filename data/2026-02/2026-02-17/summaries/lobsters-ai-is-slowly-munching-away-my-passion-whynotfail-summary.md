# AI is Slowly Munching Away My Passion | WhyNot.Fail

This article explores the author's feeling of diminishing passion for programming due to the rapid advancement and integration of Large Language Models (LLMs) into the field. While acknowledging the impressive capabilities of current AI models, the author expresses concern about the shift in the programming community's focus towards AI and the resulting impact on their personal connection to the craft.

## Key Points:

* **Shifting Community Focus:** The programming community is increasingly oriented towards AI, a term the author finds loaded and disassociating. This includes using AI, building it, and building products around it.
* **Loss of Core Identity:** The author identifies a core aspect of their identity as "the guy that automates stuff" and someone who enjoys the literal, predictable nature of programming. AI now automates these tasks more efficiently, diminishing this sense of self.
* **Emotional Response to AI:** The author surprisingly admits to feeling anger towards AI's outputs, a feeling akin to frustration with human UX choices or color palettes. This highlights a deeper emotional connection to the process of creation and problem-solving.
* **Erosion of Credibility:** The widespread use of AI in code generation raises concerns about the author's personal credibility, as their work might be perceived as AI-generated rather than a result of their own effort.
* **Mourning the Craft:** Ultimately, the author expresses a sense of mourning for the craft of programming as it transitions from a personal skill and source of identity to a more automated and readily available action.
* **Proposed Solution:** The author intends to use AI for work-related tasks but avoid it for personal projects where the learning and the process are valued.

## Underlying Themes:

* **The changing nature of work and skill:** The rise of AI is fundamentally altering what it means to be a programmer and the value placed on different skills.
* **The human element in technology:** The author emphasizes the importance of the human connection to technology, beyond mere functionality.
* **The potential for alienation:** The rapid advancement of AI can lead to feelings of displacement and a loss of purpose for those who derive satisfaction from the process of creation.
