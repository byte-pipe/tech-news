# Western Digital Reports Hard Drive Supply Sold Out for 2026

* Western Digital, a major hard drive manufacturer, has already sold out of its storage capacity for the entire year of 2026, with over ten months remaining.
* This information was disclosed by Western Digital CEO Irving Tan during the company's recent quarterly earnings call.
* The majority of the 2026 storage capacity has been allocated to the company's top seven customers, three of whom have agreements for 2027 and 2028.
* The consumer market now represents only 5 percent of Western Digital's revenue due to increased demand from enterprise customers.
* The surge in demand from artificial intelligence (AI) companies is significantly impacting computer hardware supply chains, leading to price increases for products like processors and video game consoles.
* The tech industry has been experiencing a memory shortage driven by AI demand, causing PC makers to raise RAM prices and prompting video game console manufacturers to consider delaying releases.
* The ongoing high demand from AI companies for memory and storage suggests that shortages and price hikes for consumers are likely to continue in the near future.
