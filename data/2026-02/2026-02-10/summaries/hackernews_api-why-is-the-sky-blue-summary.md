# Why is the sky blue?

This article explains why the sky appears blue, addressing common questions about color perception and atmospheric scattering.

## Key Points:

*   **Color Perception:** The color of an object is determined by the wavelengths of light it emits and how those wavelengths interact with the observer's eye.
*   **Blue Light Scattering:** Sunlight passes through the atmosphere, and blue photons are scattered more effectively than other colors due to their shorter wavelengths. This phenomenon is called Rayleigh scattering.
*   **Why Blue, Not Violet?:** While violet light scatters even more than blue, our eyes are less sensitive to violet, and the sun emits less violet light.
*   **Sunset Redness:** During sunrise and sunset, sunlight travels through more of the atmosphere. This causes blue and green light to scatter away, leaving the longer wavelengths like red and orange to reach our eyes.
*   **Cloud Whiteness:** Clouds are composed of water droplets or ice crystals that scatter all wavelengths of visible light equally, resulting in a white appearance.
*   **Mars' Red Sky:** Mars has a thinner atmosphere than Earth, and its dust scatters red light more effectively, giving the sky a reddish hue. Martian sunsets are blue due to the scattering of blue light by the dust particles.
*   **Resonant Frequencies:** Molecules in the atmosphere have resonant frequencies. When a photon's frequency matches a molecule's resonant frequency, it is absorbed rather than scattered.
*   **Scattering Power:** Scattering increases with the fourth power of the frequency of light. This means that higher frequency light (like violet) scatters much more than lower frequency light (like red).
