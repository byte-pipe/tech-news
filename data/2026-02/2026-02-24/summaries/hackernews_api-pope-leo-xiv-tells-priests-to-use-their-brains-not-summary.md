*   Pope Leo XIV has instructed priests to prioritize their own intellect and reflection over using artificial intelligence (AI) to write homilies.
*   The Pope expressed concerns that relying on AI could lead to homilies lacking genuine pastoral care and the personal touch expected from a priest.
*   He emphasized the importance of priests engaging their own "brains" – their intellect, experience, and understanding of their flock – in crafting homilies.
*   The directive is framed as a call to maintain the human element in preaching and to ensure homilies are rooted in authentic faith and pastoral sensitivity.
*   The Vatican's guidance suggests a caution against the potential for AI to produce generic or impersonal homilies that may not resonate with the specific needs of a particular community.
*   The Pope's statement underscores the enduring value of human creativity and discernment in spiritual communication within the Church.
