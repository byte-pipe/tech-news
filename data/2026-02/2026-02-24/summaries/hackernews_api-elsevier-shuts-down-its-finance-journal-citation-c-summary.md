# Elsevier Shut Down Its Finance Journal Citation Cartel

### 12 papers retracted, 7 editor positions removed, and the "open secret" of Elsevier’s elite paper mill exposed.

* Elsevier retracted 12 economics papers across four of its finance journals (International Review of Financial Analysis, Finance Research Letters, and International Review of Economics & Finance).
* All 12 retracted papers shared Brian M Lucey, Professor of International Finance and Commodities at Trinity College Dublin, as a co-author.
* The stated reason for retraction was that Lucey, as a co-author, oversaw the editorial process and final decision-making for these papers, violating journal policies and bypassing peer review.
* Lucey was removed as an editor from five journals: International Review of Financial Analysis, International Review of Economics & Finance, Finance Research Letters, Financial Management, and Energy Finance.
* This situation has been an "open secret" within the academic community for years, with previous criticisms of Lucey's practices documented online.
* Elsevier's finance journals ecosystem allows for the transfer of rejected manuscripts to other journals within the system without resubmission, which was allegedly exploited for citation stacking.
* A preprint paper detailed a citation ring involving Elsevier editors, with Samuel Vigne, a former PhD student and frequent co-author of Lucey, as a central figure.
* The citation cartel involved co-authorship trading, where multiple authors repeatedly collaborated on papers within the Elsevier system, and citation stacking, where papers systematically cite each other to inflate citation counts.
* The Elsevier ecosystem is coordinated by Brian Lucey, who is appointed by Elsevier and oversees the journals within the system.
* Wiley, the publisher of the Journal of Economic Surveys where Lucey is editor-in-chief, stated they are aware of the concerns and are monitoring the situation.
* Lucey's response to a paper detailing the citation ring was considered weak, focusing on semantics rather than addressing the data and structural model presented.
