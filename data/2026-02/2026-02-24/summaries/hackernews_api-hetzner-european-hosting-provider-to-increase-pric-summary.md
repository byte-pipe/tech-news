# Hetzner to Increase Prices by Up to 38% Due to High Demand and Hardware Costs

Hetzner, a European hosting provider, is increasing prices by up to 38% for its Cloud VPS offerings. This is attributed to a surge in demand from businesses and developers migrating to European alternatives, leading to capacity challenges and server sell-outs.  Additionally, worldwide hardware and RAM prices have increased over the past year, contributing to the price hike.

## Key Points:

*   **Price Increase:** Hetzner is raising prices by up to 36% (originally reported as 38% but corrected in the comments).
*   **Reasons:** The price increase is driven by high demand and rising costs of hardware components like RAM.
*   **Demand Surge:**  A growing number of businesses and developers are choosing European hosting providers like Hetzner.
*   **Capacity Issues:** Hetzner has struggled to keep pace with the increased demand, resulting in frequent server sell-outs.
*   **Hardware Costs:**  Global increases in the price of hardware, particularly RAM, are contributing to the price hike.
*   **Cloud Act Concerns:** Some users are concerned about the US Cloud Act and are migrating to Hetzner for data protection reasons. Hetzner clarifies that their services are primarily governed by EU jurisdictions.
*   **Historical Cost-Saving Measures:** Hetzner has historically saved money by building servers from desktop computer components instead of using more expensive server-grade components.
*   **Industry-Wide Trend:** The price increase is not isolated to Hetzner and is expected to be a trend across the hosting industry.



## Additional Information:

*   The PX line of servers is considered their most reliable offering.
