# My AI Adoption Journey

**Initial Implosion (Phase 1)**
I underwent an initial phase of inefficiency, where I didn't see the value in adopting AI tools. I was comfortable with my existing workflow and chose not to force myself through new technologies.

 **Awakening to Potential**
This phase ended when I started using chatbots for simple tasks, but they failed to deliver real results. This marked a turning point, acknowledging that AI tooling could bring significant productivity gains without being forced through complex adoption processes.

## Step 1: Drop the Chatbot

Immediately abandoned using chatbots for most tasks due to their limited capabilities and high maintenance costs. The realization of wasted time reimagining code in chat interfaces led me to reconsider the utility of these tools.

**Emergence of Interest (Phase 2)**
I gained a new appreciation for the power of AI tooling, particularly with the development of agents that can repeat tasks, execute programs, and make HTTP requests. I recognized the potential to streamline tasks, but still had concerns about implementing them effectively.

## Step 2: Reproduce Your Own Work

The next pivotal phase involved finding the right agent to automate my work. Initially, I struggled to produce meaningful results from sessions with Claude Code, which didn't live up to expectations. This experience taught me to be more discerning when selecting an AI tool and to have realistic expectations.

## Step 3: End-of-Day Agents
I transitioned to using agents designed specifically for tasks at the end of each day. These agents were able to automate routine tasks efficiently, freeing up time for more complex work. This phase marked a significant shift towards adopting AI tools that can run continuously without requiring human intervention.

**Exploring Further Options**

This journey will continue as I explore further options and methodologies in my quest to optimize my workflow using AI tooling.
