# Gaming Beat Me, Development Won't - DEV Community

### Gaming Breakthrough: Quits Sea of Thieves for FreeCodeCamp

I quitSea of Thieves this week, making it clear that a season was time to let go. As someone with ADHD and a history of working in Big Tech (including Microsoft's gaming studios), I've decided to step away from the game for now.

Season 18 made it clear that it was time to move on, but what's missing is something more fulfilling: real-world rewards embedded in every update like Return to The Devil's Roar. What initially became a fun world update turned into a countdown due to seasonal limited-time events turning to permanent gaps – you had either shown up for it just when it happened or lost access.

Instead of wanting to log back in daily, I checked my calendar before planning sessions and kept detailed mental notes of missed updates. The sandbox remained untouched, but deadlines fenced in the fun by manufacturing manufactured urgency. Breaking away felt oddly familiar; breaking a login streak was reminiscent of walking away from Sea of Thieves.

I've made this habit of logging off for months before deciding to return: signing up for freeCodeCamp at the start of the year and showing up daily – even if it's just one lesson or exercise, reviewing tricky concepts. The latest version of the freeCodeCamp curriculum is excellent – structured yet flexible; clear yet forgiving.

The DEV Community has made a significant difference, replacing the sense of belonging I got from gaming with motivation derived from growth curiosity, and quiet support from others on the same path. My progress in learning remains intact, and real-world rewards aren't fleeting. Rather, I find motivation in the quiet growth and quiet community support – something more aligned with my personal development goals.

The tech world keeps touting AI magic stories: overnight mastery, unicorn success, instant career transformation. But staying focused on becoming better within freeCodeCamp's structure means escaping these distraction narratives.
