# Above the API: What Developers Contribute When AI Can Code - DEV Community

**Understanding the Divide: Above and Below the API**

The debate surrounding Active Research in Artificial Intelligence (AI) has shifted from exploring when to use AI systems versus ensuring that developers truly understand how they operate within their context. The field now highlights a distinct separation between using AI below and above the Application Programming Interface (API).

### **The Traditional Divide: Above the API**

We see this division on several aspects commonly used in software development:

*   **Guiding Systems:** These are systems operated directly by human judgment, with minimal reliance on AI output. However, many engineers recognize an issue when they choose to use AI systems for guidance, rather than relying on their own understanding.
*   **Understanding and Verification:** Above the API, humans engage in tasks such as judging whether AI's suggestions or insights hold merit. It is less likely that junior engineers will execute these judgment activities.

### **The New Divide: Below and Above the API**

A different separation emerges when we delve beneath the surface of AI:

*   **Executing Tasks:** This encompasses executing what an AI system suggests, not determining or understanding its reasoning.
*   **Guiding Systems:** When developers use AI to implement a solution, they often focus on simplification, maintaining patterns across codebases, and refining architectural thinking. However, there remains an issue when they might not understand why certain decisions were made.

### **The Question: Which Side of the Divide Are You On?**

Developers cannot easily transition from one side to the other without careful consideration of their priorities. They should reflect on whether their focus is more about executing tasks efficiently (Below), judging when AI's suggestions are effective and reliable, or ensuring proper maintenance and validation at all levels of development.

### **Why Some AI Users Score Higher Than Others**

In recent studies, junior engineers have shown higher success rates in utilizing AI without relying heavily on it to perform tasks. This shift reveals a critical aspect: The value lies not only within the capabilities we provide through AI but also in the understanding and judgment when implementing our solutions.

### **What Does the Divide Mean for Your Current Development Environment?**

It means that you should be actively developing your skills in areas beyond what is readily automated by machines. You will find new challenges and opportunities to develop those insights, ensuring your work stays true to its standards, rather than relying solely on their tools.

For juniors, this shift highlights the importance of understanding AI's capabilities within specific domain limitations. Developers must consider whether to use or integrate machine learning concepts into smaller projects while recognizing that building larger-scale solutions requires a deeper grasp of those technologies. Meanwhile, seniors find the value in simplifying and refining complex systems for clients, leaving room for human judgment and critical consideration throughout the development process.
