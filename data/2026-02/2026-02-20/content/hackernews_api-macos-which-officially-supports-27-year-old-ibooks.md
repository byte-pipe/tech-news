[–]just_itsi2 points3 points4 points10 hours ago(1 child)

Moore’s Law is real.

Moore’s Law has been dead for ~10 years now.

Moore’s Law (originally) refers to transistor density doubling every two years - this has historically been done by shrinking the size of transistors by using more precise manufacturing (smaller process nodes)

In 2020 the leading process node was 5nm, in 2025 it was 3nm. If Moore’s Law was alive we should have seen something like:

2020: 5nm
2022: ~2.5/3 nm
2024: ~1.5/2 nm
2025: ~1.5 nm - and we have 3nm, 2x larger than predicted by Moore’s Law

While the differences between processes nodes definitely appears to be almost negligible, if you think about it in terms of % you can see we’re running behind.

Today, in order to make a CPU faster, manufacturers optimise it for specific use cases such as video encoding/decoding, crypto and compression acceleration and so on. CPUs cannot rely on Moore’s Law for their speed any more - a lesson Intel learned the hard way.
