## Summary of the Breathing Sleep Indicator Light

* The "Sleep Indicator Light," officially the "Sleep Indicator Light," was first introduced in 1999 with the iconic iMac G3.
* Initially located in the hinge, it was later integrated into various Apple computers including laptops (Power Mac, Cube, iMac) and desktops.
* The light evolved from a green LED to a white one, with the key feature being its pulsating animation designed to mimic human breathing (12 breaths per minute) for a comforting effect.
* Later iMac G5 models incorporated a light sensor to prevent the light from being too bright in dark environments, and older models would dim the light at night based on the internal clock.
* In subsequent MacBooks, the light was cleverly integrated by perforating the aluminum casing, creating the illusion of light shining through the metal.
* Apple also utilized display connectors to transmit data and power, enabling the sleep light to function.
* A notable, unadvertised feature was the synchronization of the sleep light on the Mac with a connected Apple Cinema Display.
* The breathing light was a functional detail, providing reassurance that the computer was truly in sleep mode, which was particularly important during an era when laptops frequently experienced sleep issues. It also indicated hard drive parking, allowing for safe movement.
* The pulsating animation was a humanistic touch that contributed to the light's comforting nature.
* The breathing light was discontinued in the early 2010s, a casualty of efforts to remove seemingly unnecessary features like the battery indicator and illuminated lid logo.
* The author expresses a fondness for this detail, highlighting its functional importance and the unique "quirkiness" of the breathing animation.
* Attempts have been made by enthusiasts to recreate the breathing effect in custom implementations.
