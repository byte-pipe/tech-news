# Permacomputing

* Permacomputing is a concept and community focused on building resilience and regenerativity into computer and network technology, drawing inspiration from permaculture.
* It aims to address significant environmental and societal issues within contemporary computing, similar to how permaculture challenged industrial agriculture.
* Permacomputing is explicitly framed as an anti-capitalist political project, influenced by various ideologies including anarchism, decoloniality, intersectional feminism, post-Marxism, degrowth, and ecologism.
* It is presented as a utopian ideal requiring substantial rethinking, rebuilding, and technical design. The wiki contains largely technical material reflecting this.
* Permacomputing is not a product to purchase but an invitation to collectively and radically rethink computational culture. It is not a technology solution seeking a problem.
* The use of the term "permacomputing" is open, but understanding its purpose and ethos is encouraged.
* The wiki provides resources including introductory information, principles for design, discussions of issues in current computing, a starter's manual, a library of texts and media, a selection of related projects, and technology assessments.
* A community aspect exists for networking, collaboration, and discussion.
