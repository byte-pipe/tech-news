# Disappointing Phones

This blog post details the author's predicament regarding their aging OnePlus 5T running LineageOS, which will soon become unusable due to the discontinuation of 3G networks in early 2026. The author dislikes buying new phones due to environmental concerns (e-waste) and a preference for their current device. However, the phone's degraded battery, storage issues, and the impending 3G shutdown necessitate finding a replacement.

## Requirements for a New Phone

The author has identified five key requirements for a new phone, prioritizing at least three:

*   **Cameras:** Excellent both software and hardware capabilities, particularly for zoom beyond 2x, as the current phone struggles with this. Optical zoom is preferred over digital zoom.
*   **Software Look and Feel:** A strong preference for clean, bloatware-free software, similar to LineageOS, and a dislike for manufacturer-imposed software choices. The ability to switch to LineageOS is considered an important feature.
*   **Bloatware:** A strong aversion to pre-installed, unwanted software that cannot be removed.
*   **Repairability:** The ability to get the phone repaired quickly and easily by local repair shops, with readily available spare parts. The author recounts a frustrating experience with their OnePlus 5T where a simple volume button issue resulted in a two-week repair delay due to parts availability.

The author has been researching potential phones over the past few months to find a suitable replacement that meets these requirements. They are considering both Android and Apple devices, acknowledging the trade-offs between software customization and ease of use.
