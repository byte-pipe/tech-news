# Discord cuts ties with Peter Thiel-backed verification software after code found tied to U.S. surveillance

Discord has terminated its partnership with Persona Identities, an age verification software partially funded by Peter Thiel's Founders Fund, following the discovery of accessible code on the open internet and government servers. Researchers found nearly 2,500 accessible files containing Persona's facial recognition checks against watchlists and screening against politically exposed persons. The software performed 269 distinct verification checks, including screening for "adverse media."

Discord confirmed the partnership lasted less than a month and involved a small number of users, with submitted information stored for up to seven days before deletion. This incident is the latest in a series of privacy concerns for Discord, including a data breach in 2025 where government IDs of over 70,000 users were accessed.

Persona CEO Rick Song stated the accessible files were uncompressed versions of publicly available source map information and not a vulnerability. He maintains the partnership was successful and that data was redacted upon processing and deleted promptly after verification. Song denied any ties to Palantir or ICE, though Persona is pursuing FedRAMP authorization for workforce security applications.

Discord's recent announcement to default all accounts to teen-safety settings and require age verification via Persona sparked backlash. The company later clarified that age verification would remain optional and that government IDs were intended for age confirmation only, with identity not being associated with accounts. However, a deleted FAQ version suggested temporary storage of submitted information for up to 7 days.

The partnership with Persona has ended, and Discord emphasizes its commitment to user privacy and security through multiple privacy-forward options and the deletion of uploaded identifying documents shortly after age confirmation.
