# AI drags down smartphone sales - by Jérôme Marin - Cafétech

# AI-Driven Smartphone Sales Collapse

## Key Points:

* IDC forecasts a 12.9% decline in smartphone sales this year, which would be the lowest level since 2013.
* Surging memory chip prices are a primary cause of the downturn, particularly at the entry-level end of the market.
* The decline is most severe in China's top three brands: Xiaomi, Oppo, and Vivo, where shipments plummeted by 19%, 10%, and 7% respectively.

## Structured Analysis:

### Impact on Market

* The collapse would bring the smartphone market down to its lowest level since 2013.
* The decline is expected to be historic, with prices rising by significant amounts due to increased demand for computing power in AI-related applications.

### Regional Disparities

* China's three biggest brands are particularly hard hit, with Xiaomi shipments dropping by 19%, Oppo by 10%, and Vivo by 7%.
* Prices in emerging markets have jumped by 40% - 50%, while some devices may be withdrawn from the market altogether.

### Industry Trends

* The rapid expansion of infrastructure for generative AI is driving demand for smartphones, but also placing pressure on chip manufacturers.
* Manufacturers are shifting production toward more profitable AI-oriented components, at the expense of less profitable DRAM and NAND memory used in other applications.
