# AI Use in the Real World: AI’s Design Problem Is Organizational, Not Technological

# AI's Design Problem: Organizational vs Technological Challenges

The article highlights the multifaceted challenges of incorporating Artificial Intelligence (AI) into businesses. The author identifies three main design problems:

- Reshaping enterprise behavior due to uneven capabilities and product adoption.
- Addressing usability flaws, job losses among juniors, and severe algorithmic trust issues in people's experiences with AI.

## Macroeconomic Data vs Micro-Level Metrics

The article points out that while macroeconomic data shows immense productivity gains and US dominance over Europe, micro-level metrics reveal real-world issues:

- Productivity improvements do not necessarily translate to increased employment.
- Usability and job performance vary greatly across individuals and companies.
- Algorithmic trust issues create unease about using AI technologies.

## Two Reports Provide Data-Driven Insights

Two recent reports offer insights into how people interact with AI and its performance in real-world settings:

1. **NBER Report: Mind the Gap**: This paper focuses on workplace AI adoption, examining who uses AI on the job, why there's a gap between countries (US vs Europe), and how much time users report saving.
2. **Stanford HAI Index 2026**: A wide-angle survey covering various aspects of AI performance, including technical capabilities, investment, public sentiment, environmental cost, and governance.

## Designing for Real People

The author emphasizes that when designing products, it's essential to consider the unique characteristics of different users and stages of familiarization, authorization, and dependence:

- You are not designing for 'AI users' in isolation but rather for people at various stages of AI adoption.
- The denominator matters. For product strategy, you should focus on what works best in real-world contexts rather than general assumptions about AI.

The takeaway recommendations from these reports aim to help business leaders and organizations navigate the complexities:

- Recognize that real-world challenges are different from macroeconomic data-driven insights.
- Understand the varying levels of familiarity and trust users have with AI technologies.
- Tailor product strategies based on what works for people at each stage of AI adoption.
