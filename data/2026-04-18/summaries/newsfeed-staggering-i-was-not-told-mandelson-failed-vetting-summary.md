# 'Staggering' I was not told Mandelson failed vetting, says PM

## Key Points:

• Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer claimed that he was informed by no one about Lord Peter Mandelson's failure to pass initial security vetting checks when appointed as US ambassador.
• The recommendation against Mandelson's appointment was made by foreign vetting officers but overruled by the Foreign Office, leading to his dismissal.
• Former Prime Minister Jeremy 'Lord Mandelson' Kennedy had hinted that vetting officers had recommended against him after failing initial checks in December 2024.
• Tory MP Kemi Badenoch is calling for Sir Keir's resignation amid criticism of his explanation as "completely preposterous" and accusing him of being "so blinded by his own righteousness that he cannot see what everybody else can see".
• The opposition is urging MPs to remove Sir Keir from office due to the lack of transparency surrounding Mandelson's vetting process.
## Government Response:

* Prime Minister Starmer said, "That I wasn't told [about Mandelson failed vetting] is staggering... Number 10 was not told that he had failed security vetting - that is completely unacceptable."
* Senior minister Darren Jones earlier said Sir Keir did not lead MPs to believe a lie when he said due process was followed.
