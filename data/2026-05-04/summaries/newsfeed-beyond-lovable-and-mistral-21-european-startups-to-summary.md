# Beyond Lovable and Mistral: 21 European startups to watch

## Overview
- The list was compiled by asking investors from Europe’s leading venture funds to name two startups each: one from their own portfolio and one external that they admire.
- Editorial picks were added to reach a total of 21 companies.
- The selected startups span a range of development stages and sectors, illustrating how deep‑tech talent can help Europe compete in the global AI race.

## Startups

- Alta Ares – develops AI‑powered counter‑drone systems; offers cheaper defense solutions in response to heightened security concerns after the war in Ukraine.  
- Apron – provides invoice management tools for small‑business owners; targets the large and willing fintech market of SMBs.  
- Botify – helps brands improve visibility in AI‑driven searches (generative engine optimization); already works with major clients such as Macy’s and The New York Times.  
- BottleCap AI – builds efficiency‑focused foundational large language models and applications, including the Pulse AI news app; founded by a former Meta seller and two AI researchers.  
- Cailabs – creates photonics for aerospace, defense, and industrial use; plans to deploy 50 optical ground stations for laser satellite communications.  
- Cala – offers a knowledge‑graph layer for AI agents; founded by former Apple‑acquired entrepreneur Elisenda Bou‑Balust.  
- Flower – manages renewable energy with AI and battery storage to smooth wind and solar variability; recently raised over $60 million in bonds.  
- Fundamental – provides a foundation AI model called Nexus for big‑data analysis; emerged from stealth in February and is valued at $1.4 billion after a $255 million Series A.  
- Gradium – supplies multilingual AI voice models for real‑time text‑to‑speech; a spin‑out of French AI lab Kyutai that raised a $70 million seed round.  
- HappyRobot – builds AI agents for complex use cases with a focus on ROI; backed by a16z and Y Combinator, co‑founders are Spanish.  
- Inbolt – delivers physical AI robots for factory automation across automotive, electronics, and home‑goods production; active in more than 70 factories.  
- Legora – provides an AI platform for lawyers, combining legal tech with marketing; uses Jude Law as brand ambassador and has moved headquarters to New York while retaining Swedish roots.  
- Macrodata Labs – develops infrastructure for creating high‑quality AI training datasets; upcoming platform will offer tooling for solid training data.  
- Multiverse Computing – offers compressed versions of open‑weight models (e.g., from OpenAI, Meta, Mistral) to run cheaper on‑premise; raised $250 million.  
- Optics11 – designs fiber‑optic sensing systems for harsh environments such as underwater infrastructure; secured venture debt to expand.
