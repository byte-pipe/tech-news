# Freepik rebrands as Magnific: a bootstrapped, profitable $230M ARR AI creative platform

# Freepik rebrands as Magnific: key points

## Overview
- Freepik, the Málaga‑based AI creative platform, announced a rebrand to **Magnific**, unifying all its products under a single name.  
- The change consolidates the former Freepik stock library, the Magnific AI upscaler (acquired in May 2024), and other AI tools into one cohesive platform.

## Key Metrics
- Annualised recurring revenue (ARR): $230 million.  
- Paying subscribers: >1 million.  
- Enterprise customers: >250, including BBC, Puma, Amazon Prime Video, Carl’s Jr, DeliveryHero, Huel, R/GA, Damm, Job&Talent.  
- Images generated daily: >4 million.  
- Business plan for small teams (launched Jan 2026): 2,000 subscriptions in six weeks, growing at ~150 new teams per week.  
- 72 % of new creators identify as beginners.

## Product Stack
- AI image and video generation (up to 4K with audio).  
- Original AI upscaling and enhancement technology.  
- Real‑time collaborative workspace.  
- Exclusive 3D and virtual scene tools.  
- AI assistant and training Academy.  
- Library of 250 million+ creative assets.  
- Model‑agnostic orchestration: users can select third‑party video AI models (e.g., Google’s Veo 3.1, ByteDance’s Seeddance 2.0) alongside Magnific’s own tools.

## History & Funding
- Founded in 2010 in Málaga by Joaquín Cuenca and Alejandro Cuenca.  
- Joaquín previously co‑founded Panoramio, acquired by Google in 2007.  
- Magnific founded in Murcia by Javi López and Emilio Nicolás; viral launch with 30 k users in 24 h, 725 k registered users without paid ads. Founders remain post‑acquisition.  
- The company has never taken external venture capital; it is profitable and bootstrapped.

## Market Position & Competition
- Competes with Midjourney, Runway, Leonardo, Adobe Firefly, and other US AI creative platforms.  
- Differentiator: an integrated end‑to‑end workflow that reduces friction of using multiple AI tools, rather than a superior underlying model.  
- Recognized by Andreessen Horowitz as Europe’s top generative AI web company by user count.

## Future Vision
- Cuenca describes a “no‑collar economy,” positioning AI‑enabled creativity as a new class of work beyond traditional blue‑ and white‑collar roles.  
- Predicts that movies will be created like books: a single visionary with AI tools to execute.  

## Notable Quote
- “If we raise capital, it will be to grow the DNA of the company, not because of financial necessity.” – CEO Joaquín Cuenca.
