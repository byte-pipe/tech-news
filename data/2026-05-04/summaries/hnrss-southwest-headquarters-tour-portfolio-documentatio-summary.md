## Morning  

### Flight Attendant Training
- Dallas LEAD Center is one of 13 U.S. facilities where flight attendants complete training.
- Training includes cabin service, land/sea evacuation, emergency tool use, fire fighting, and self‑defense.
- Annual refresher courses keep procedures up‑to‑date with real‑world trends.
- Demonstrations:
  - 100‑lb raft inflates partially for sea‑evacuation drills; life vests provide safety for non‑swimmers.
  - Fire‑fighting area shows oxygen hood use and techniques to locate fire base.
- Vintage uniforms and memorabilia displayed throughout the campus.
- Pilot uniform shop noted that only 6 % of Southwest pilots are women; airline supports Women in Aviation.
- Participants tried a safety demonstration, including emergency door and slide evacuation.

### Pilot Training
- Pilots must place an oxygen mask on a passenger with one hand in eight seconds while controlling the aircraft; facial hair restrictions apply for mask seal.
- Fixed‑motion simulators cost about $1 million each; 23 are in use.
- Full‑motion CAE 737‑700/800/MAX‑8 simulators can replicate any route, extended over‑water flights, and emergencies; 26 simulators total, each costing $14.2 million.
- Simulators can log turbine hours but may cause motion sickness.
- Facility built to withstand an F3 tornado; reinforced concrete walls protect the Network Command Center.

### TechOps Hangar
- Southwest operates the world’s largest Boeing 737 fleet (800+ aircraft); four are based at Dallas Love Field.
- Tour of the “party hangar” where night‑time maintenance occurs.
- Veteran technicians (30+ years) and sheet‑metal specialists highlighted; mastery takes ~10 years.
