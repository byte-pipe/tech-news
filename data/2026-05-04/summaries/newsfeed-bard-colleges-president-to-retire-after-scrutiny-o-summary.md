# Bard College's president to retire after scrutiny of relationship with Jeffrey Epstein

## Announcement and retirement details
- Leon Botstein, president of Bard College for about 50 years, announced he will retire at the end of June 2026.  
- He will remain on Bard’s faculty as a teacher and musician.  
- In his retirement note he said he waited to announce publicly until an independent review of his relationship with Jeffrey Epstein was completed.

## Context of Epstein relationship
- U.S. Justice Department documents released in 2024 showed Botstein met Epstein several times, including arrivals by helicopter.  
- Botstein invited Epstein to the 2013 graduation ceremonies and suggested meeting for an opera performance.  
- After a 2018 Miami Herald report on Epstein, Botstein emailed Epstein expressing hope he was “holding up as well as can be expected.”  
- He referred to his “friendship” with Epstein in at least two separate emails.

## Financial aspects
- Epstein contributed $150,000 to Bard in 2016; Botstein said the money was donated to the college.  
- Botstein has denied a personal connection, characterizing his contacts with Epstein as fundraising‑related.

## Independent review findings
- Bard’s trustees hired the law firm WilmerHale to review communications between Botstein and Epstein.  
- The review concluded Botstein did not break any laws but made decisions that reflect on his leadership.  
- It noted Botstein “minimized and was not fully accurate” in describing his relationship with Epstein.  
- Botstein argued that a convicted sex offender could be presumed rehabilitated, calling such offenders “an ordinary sex offender.”  
- He emphasized the college’s need for funds, stating, “I would take money from Satan if it permitted me to do God’s work.”

## Board and institutional response
- The Executive Committee of the Board of Trustees thanked Botstein for decades of service while acknowledging that recent concerns were “serious and deeply felt.”  
- Money linked to Epstein will be redirected to organizations that support survivors of sexual harm.  
- Bard’s media relations office described Botstein as “a transformative leader with the vision and unwavering commitment that has shaped Bard into the world‑class educational institution it is today.”

## Additional notes
- Botstein has not been accused of involvement in Epstein’s sexual exploitation of girls and women.
