# Summary of “Bluesky built a new AI tool that wants to free you from social algorithms”

## Overview
- Bluesky announced **Attie**, an AI‑driven app that lets users create their own algorithmic feeds by simply chatting with the assistant.  
- The announcement was made at the Atmosphere conference, where attendees received first‑access to a private beta.  
- Attie runs on the AT Protocol, Bluesky’s open social framework, allowing it to draw from activity across the entire ecosystem rather than a single walled garden.

## How Attie Works
- Users sign in with their Atmosphere login (the same credentials used for Bluesky and other AT Protocol apps).  
- Attie already knows a user’s interests from shared data across apps.  
- By asking Attie for posts on a topic, vibe, or specific accounts, the app instantly generates a personalized timeline.  
- No coding or complex settings are required; the feed is built through natural language conversation.

## Motivation and Philosophy
- Bluesky positions Attie as a counter‑point to major platforms that embed AI primarily to increase engagement, harvest data, and control algorithms.  
- Former CEO Jay Graber, now chief innovation officer, emphasizes that AI should serve people, not platforms.  
- Interim CEO Toni Schneider describes Attie as the first “agentic social app” on the AT Protocol and a step toward letting anyone build on Atmosphere without writing code.

## Current Status and Future Plans
- Attie is currently in an invite‑only closed beta limited to Atmosphere conference participants.  
- A broader release date and pricing model have not been announced.  
- Long‑term goals include enabling users to “vibe‑code” full social applications, turning the AT Protocol into a foundation for a wide range of decentralized social experiences.
