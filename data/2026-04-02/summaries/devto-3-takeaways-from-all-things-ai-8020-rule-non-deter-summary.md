# 3 Takeaways from All Things AI: 80/20 Rule, Non‑Deterministic Humans, and Why We’re Still Early

## MLH AI Roadshow
- Helped run the MLH AI Roadshow on Day 1, a series of ~6 hands‑on activities where participants use a ready‑to‑go laptop to learn a new AI skill.
- Events include snacks, drinks, and networking; they occur roughly once a month, sometimes as standalone events or at partner conferences (e.g., All Things Open in the fall).
- Future stops can be tracked via the Luma page.

## The 80/20 Rule in Practice
- Inspired by Justin Jeffress’s talk: AI often gets you 80 % of the way to a solution.
- To handle the remaining 20 %, simply feed the output back into the AI tool and repeat the loop.
- This iterative “send‑back” approach works well with coding agents and highlights the power of simple repetition, though it may be less effective for outcomes that can’t be easily tested.

## Humans Are Also Non‑Deterministic
- From Calvin Hendryx‑Parker’s talk: developers worry about AI’s non‑determinism because code needs predictable results.
- The reminder that humans themselves are non‑deterministic reframes the concern and underscores the need for process‑driven thinking and guardrails when working with AI.

## We’re Still So Early
- Despite heavy AI coverage on social media, most people have not yet adopted AI tools, especially at their full potential.
- Even at an AI‑focused conference, many attendees had never used a coding agent.
- The early stage of the AI wave presents an opportunity: learning by doing and experimenting with tools now is the best way to stay ahead.
