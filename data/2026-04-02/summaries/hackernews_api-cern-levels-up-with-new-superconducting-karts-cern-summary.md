# CERN levels up with new superconducting karts

## Key points

- CERN engineers have created a superconducting kart to replace the bicycles currently used in the 27‑km underground Large Hadron Collider (LHC) tunnel during the upcoming Long Shutdown 3 (LS3) and the conversion to the High‑Luminosity LHC.  
- Each kart is equipped with 64 superconducting engines; when cooled below their critical temperature the Meissner effect levitates the vehicle, allowing it to travel at high speed through the tunnel.  
- Early tests have shown a noticeable speed boost compared with the previous bicycle system. The next phase will involve testing several kart designs in an underground “race.”  
- Safety coordinator Luigi Fratello will provide drivers with Safety and Health Equipment for Long and Limited Stays (SHELLS); informal requests such as bananas in the tunnel have been declined.  
- The Knowledge Transfer Group is discussing aerospace and anti‑gravity applications with the European startup Quantum Mushroom, highlighting potential societal benefits beyond CERN’s research program.  
- The project originated from a collaboration with children from an onsite nursery school; their design ideas inspired the engineered karts, which have been nicknamed “Luma.”  
- The karts will be delivered to underground work sites via large green pipes as part of the LS3 infrastructure upgrades.
