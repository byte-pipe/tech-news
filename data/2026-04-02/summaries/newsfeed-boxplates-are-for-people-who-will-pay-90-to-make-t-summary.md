## Overview
- The article reviews **BoxPlates**, a $89.99 snap‑on cover for the PlayStation 5 that replaces the console’s curvy design with a flat, matte‑black and semi‑transparent aesthetic reminiscent of the Xbox One S/X.
- Created by PC enthusiast **Devyn Johnston**, the plates are sold through his site DevynPC and target the disc‑based and disc‑drive‑less editions of the PS5 Slim and PS5 Pro; they are not compatible with the original 2020 PS5 model.

## Design and Build
- Top and bottom plates cover about 60 % of the surface in matte black (or gray) and the remaining area with semi‑transparent slats, leaving the fan section visible.
- Ports, power button, and the power‑indicator LED remain unobstructed; a small gap allows optional magnets to be attached.
- Build quality is comparable to other console covers, though without etched symbols.

## Installation

- The author suggests the purchase is worthwhile only for those whose aversion to the original design matches that of the creator.
