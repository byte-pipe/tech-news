# Eurail data breach impacted 308,777 people

## Incident overview
- Hackers accessed Eurail’s network on 26 December 2025 and transferred files containing customer data.  
- The breach was discovered in early 2026; Eurail notified 308,777 affected individuals on 9 April 2026.  
- A sample of the stolen data was later posted on Telegram and offered for sale on the dark web.

## Data compromised
- Personal identifiers: name, surname, date of birth or age.  
- Travel documents: passport or ID numbers, expiry dates (no passport copies stored).  
- Contact information: email address, postal address, country of residence, phone number.  
- Financial reference: bank account IBAN (no payment card data).  
- Additional details: order and reservation information, travel companion data, health‑related information (where provided).

## Company response
- Immediate activation of incident‑response procedures and termination of the unauthorized activity.  
- Engagement of third‑party cybersecurity experts and law‑enforcement agencies.  
- Notification of authorities in line with GDPR requirements.  
- Ongoing investigation to determine the full scope and impact.  
- Direct outreach to affected customers when contact details are available.

## Threat actor activity
- Stolen data were listed for sale on the dark web in February 2026.  
- Samples of the data set were distributed via a Telegram channel.  
- No evidence that payment card details or passport scans were stored by Eurail.

## Recommendations to customers
- Remain vigilant for unsolicited calls, emails, or messages requesting personal information.  
- Update passwords for the Rail Planner app and any related online accounts.  
- Review and change passwords for email, social media, and banking services if needed.  
- Monitor financial and travel accounts for unusual activity.  
- Report any suspicious activity to the relevant bank or authority.
