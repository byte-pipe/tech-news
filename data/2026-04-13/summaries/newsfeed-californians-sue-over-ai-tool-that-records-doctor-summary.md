# Californians sue over AI tool that records doctor visits - Ars Technica

# Summary of “Californians sue over AI tool that records doctor visits – Ars Technica”

## Key facts of the lawsuit
- Several Californians filed a proposed class‑action suit in federal court (San Francisco) against Sutter Health and MemorialCare.
- The complaint alleges that, over the past six months, medical staff used the Abridge AI transcription tool during patient visits.
- Plaintiffs claim they were not given clear notice that their conversations would be recorded, transmitted outside the clinic, or processed by a third‑party AI system.
- Recorded data included personally identifiable health information such as medical histories, symptoms, diagnoses, medications, and treatment discussions.

## About Abridge AI
- Abridge’s software captures, transcribes, and summarizes patient‑doctor conversations to generate clinical notes.
- The tool has been adopted by major health systems including Kaiser Permanente, Mayo Clinic, Duke Health, and others.
- The company was valued at $5.3 billion as of June 2025 and asserts its product improves outcomes for clinicians, nurses, and revenue‑cycle teams.
- Abridge did not immediately respond to comment requests.

## Responses from the defendants
- Sutter Health, partnered with Abridge for two years, said it takes patient privacy seriously and evaluates technology for legal compliance (spokesperson Liz Madison).
- MemorialCare declined to comment on the pending litigation (spokesperson Wendy Dow).
