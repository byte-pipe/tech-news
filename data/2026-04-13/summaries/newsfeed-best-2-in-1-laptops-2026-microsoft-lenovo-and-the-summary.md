# Best 2‑in‑1 Laptops (2026): Microsoft, Lenovo, and the iPad | WIRED

## Introduction
- Owning separate laptop and tablet still has use cases, but 2‑in‑1s aim to combine both.
- No current 2‑in‑1 excels equally as a laptop and a tablet; each leans toward one mode.
- The convenience of a single device often makes the price more acceptable.
- The guide highlights models that should satisfy most users; other buying guides are referenced for cheap laptops, tablets, and iPads.

## Detachable Tablets
- **Definition**: Tablets that attach to a keyboard, emphasizing easy switching between tablet and laptop modes while retaining full tablet functionality.
- **Microsoft Surface Pro 13‑inch (11th Edition, 2024)**
  - Runs full Windows; includes a built‑in kickstand.
  - Powered by Qualcomm Snapdragon X Elite (and Plus) for improved performance and battery life.
  - Available with an OLED screen (120 Hz) to compete with iPad Pro.
  - Keyboard (Type Cover) can be used detached from the screen, adding flexibility.
- **Microsoft Surface Pro 12**
  - Smaller (12‑inch) and more affordable than the 13‑inch model.
  - Only 256 GB storage option.
  - Still feels premium and portable; lacks OLED option.
- **Apple iPad Air (2026, M4)**
  - Strong tablet ecosystem with many touch‑first apps.
  - When paired with the Magic Keyboard, becomes a solid 2‑in‑1 at a lower total cost than Surface Pro with keyboard.
  - Supports iPadOS windowing and improved cursor handling.
  - Drawback: small palm rests on the Magic Keyboard.
- **Asus ROG Flow Z13** (mentioned briefly)
  - Gaming‑focused detachable 2‑in‑1; keeps heat away from hands, though tablet use is limited.

## Convertible Laptops
- **Definition**: Traditional laptops with a 360° hinge and touchscreen, allowing multiple usage modes (laptop, tablet‑like, tent, stand).
- **Lenovo Yoga 9i (14‑inch, 2025)**
  - Premium design with OLED screen, thin chassis, and built‑in speakers in the hinge.
  - Intel processor delivers strong battery life and performance.
  - No major compromises for the 2‑in‑1 form factor.
- **Framework Laptop 12**
  - Emphasizes repairability and upgradability (processor, motherboard, etc.).
  - Thicker chassis but affordable; DIY edition available for educational projects.
- **Acer Chromebook Plus Spin 514**
  - Best 2‑in‑1 Chromebook and best under‑$600 2‑in‑1 overall.
  - Uses efficient MediaTek chip, offers good battery life, higher‑resolution screen, and 12 GB RAM.
  - Represents a strong budget‑friendly convertible option.

## Overall Takeaways
- Detachable tablets excel when you need a true tablet experience with optional laptop functionality; Surface Pro line offers full Windows, while iPad Air provides a richer touch‑first app ecosystem.
- Convertible laptops provide a more traditional laptop feel with added touchscreen versatility; Yoga 9i stands out for premium quality, Framework for sustainability, and Acer Chromebook for budget constraints.
- Choosing a 2‑in‑1 depends on your priority: performance and Windows compatibility (Surface), app ecosystem and price (iPad), premium design (Yoga), or repairability and cost (Framework/Chromebook).
