# Summary of “Apple reportedly testing four designs for upcoming smart glasses”

## Key Points
- Apple aims to launch its first smart glasses in 2027, with a possible reveal at the end of 2026.  
- Bloomberg’s Mark Gurman reports that Apple is evaluating four distinct frame designs and may release one or multiple versions.  
- The glasses will focus on core functions (camera, calls, music, Siri) rather than mixed‑reality displays.

## Design Options
- **Large rectangular frame**  
- **Slim rectangular frame** (similar to the glasses worn by CEO Tim Cook)  
- **Large oval or circular frame**  
- **Small oval or circular frame**  
- Color variations under consideration: black, ocean blue, light brown.  

## Planned Features
- No built‑in display; functionality aligns more with Meta’s Ray‑Ban style glasses.  
- Oval camera lenses for capturing photos and videos.  
- Ability to answer phone calls, play music, and interact with an upgraded Siri.  

## Timeline & Market Context
- Target commercial release: 2027.  
- Potential unveiling: late 2026.  
- Represents a shift from Apple’s earlier, more ambitious mixed‑reality roadmap that faced delays and mixed reception (e.g., Vision Pro).  

## Comparison to Competitors
- The design and feature set are closer to Meta’s Ray‑Ban smart glasses than to full AR/VR headsets, emphasizing lightweight, everyday wearability.
