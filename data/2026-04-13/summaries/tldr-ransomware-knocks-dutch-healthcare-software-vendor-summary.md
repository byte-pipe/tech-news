# Ransomware knocks Dutch healthcare software vendor offline

## Key points
- ChipSoft, a Dutch vendor supplying patient‑record software to about 80 % of the country’s hospitals, had its website taken down by a ransomware attack on 7 April 2026; the site remains inaccessible, though email services still work.
- The attack was confirmed by Z‑CERT, the Netherlands’ healthcare‑sector computer emergency response team, which is coordinating with ChipSoft, hospitals and partners to assess impact.
- The perpetrators have not been identified. Most hospitals continue to use ChipSoft’s patient portals; only 11 hospitals (nine of them heavy users) have temporarily disconnected the software.
- Z‑CERT advises healthcare organisations to audit ChipSoft systems for abnormal traffic and report any anomalies.
- Ransomware and extortion remain the top cyber‑threats for Dutch healthcare, with recent high‑profile incidents such as the 2025 Nova ransomware breach of Eurofins Clinical Diagnostics and the January 2026 ransomware attack on Belgium’s AZ Monica hospital network, which caused prolonged outages and patient transfers.
- Z‑CERT’s director emphasizes that digital outages directly affect patient care and urges hospitals to maintain robust disaster‑recovery plans.
