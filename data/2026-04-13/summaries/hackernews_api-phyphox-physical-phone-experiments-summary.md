# phyphox – Physical Phone Experiments

# phyphox – Physical Phone Experiments

## Features
- Sensors: use phone sensors for experiments (e.g., pendulum frequency via accelerometer, Doppler effect via microphone).
- Data Export: export data in common formats and share through any app on the phone.
- Remote Control: control experiments from any web browser and download results directly to a desktop.
- Custom Experiments: create new experiments with the Wiki and web‑editor.

## Awards
- Ars legendi‑faculty award (2020) – awarded to Prof. Heidrun Heinke, Dr. Sebastian Staacks and Prof. Christoph Stampfer for phyphox’s impact on academic teaching and school physics.
- Teaching award (2019) – granted by the AG Physikalische Praktika of the German Physical Society (DPG).
- Archimedes award (2019) – granted by the “Verband zur Förderung des MINT‑Unterrichts” (MNU), sponsored by the Westermann group.
- Teaching award for “best supporting teaching” (2018) – awarded by the Fachschaft Mathematik/Physik/Informatik of RWTH Aachen University.

## Support
- RWTH Aachen “Exploratory Teaching Space” (ETS) program funded app development (2016‑2017), a Bluetooth interface (2017‑2018) and distributed experiments (2018‑2019).
- Hans Hermann Voss Foundation (since end of 2019) supports teacher training, communication and user support.
- Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF) backs the “Lehrerbildung Aachen” (LeBiAC2) project, developing Bluetooth‑based data acquisition for teacher education.
- Stifterband NRW fellowship (2017) funded modular worksheets that include phyphox‑based experiments.
- Stifterverband’s “Wirkung hoch 100” initiative provides financial support, coaching and advancement opportunities.
