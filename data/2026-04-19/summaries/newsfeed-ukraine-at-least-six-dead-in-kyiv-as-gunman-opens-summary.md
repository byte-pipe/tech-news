# Ukraine: At least 6 dead as gunman opens fire and takes hostages in Kyiv

On Saturday, a gunman opened fire and took multiple hostages in the southern Kyiv district of Holosiivskyi. Ukrainian officials say at least six people were killed and others injured.

According to reports, the incident began with gunfire on the streets of southern Kyiv, where the gunman allegedly fired at passing people, leading to casualties. He then entered a nearby supermarket and demanded that police surrender hostages while taking several other individuals hostage as well.

The exact number of victims is still unknown, but Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky reports that 14 people were injured, including a 12-year-old boy who died in the hospital. Two officials - one woman, thought to be around 30 years old and another identified as a hostage killer or attacker by police - both lost their lives at the scene.

During negotiations with the shooter, Ukrainian police attempted to persuade him to cease his actions but he refused, leading them to instruct authorities to kill him due to the high risk of harm to innocent bystanders.

Ukraine's Interior Minister Ihor Klymenko explained that a 58-year-old man who lived in the eastern Donetsk region and had not previously been involved in violent activities was identified by police as the shooter.

The shooting occurred in one of Kyiv's southern districts, where tensions with Russia have historically fueled attacks and violence. The attacker was believed to be armed with an automatic firearm, which has already been registered during the investigation into the issue.

Zelensky later stated that for a long time, the gunman had been living in southeastern parts of Ukraine. Zelenski does not know what his reasons were, but his motives are still being investigated by Ukrainian police.
