# Before he wrote AI 2027, he predicted the world in 2026. How did he do?

# Daniel Kokotajlo's Predictions: A Closer Look

## Background and Methodology

Daniel Kokotajlo, the founder of the AI Futures Project and author of the influential report "AI 2027," published a previous essay, "What 2026 Looks Like," in August 2021. The essay was a long-form prediction that examined various potential scenarios for each year from 2022 to 2026.

## Key Predictions

The predicted scenario showed hype developing throughout 2023 with increasing revenue to cover training costs. This occurred in real-life when OpenAI achieved $2 billion-plus ARR at the end of 2023. Similarly, predictions also included:

* The U.S.-China chip restriction battle happening in 2024 instead of 2022
* A shift towards longer model runtimes rather than just increased model sizes

## Evaluation and Critique

The evaluation of Daniel's predictions reveals that while many came true or closely resembled actual events, others were less accurate. One notable prediction was the decision to delay releasing models with trillions of parameters.

Criticism highlights a potential trend in AI development where researchers are prioritizing runtime over parameter count, suggesting an increase in model complexity without necessarily leading to improved performance gains. However, this does not necessarily indicate an impending crash, as the pace and direction of advancements will rely on technological and societal factors beyond immediate predictions.
