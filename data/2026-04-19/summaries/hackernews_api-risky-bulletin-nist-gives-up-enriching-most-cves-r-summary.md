# Risky Bulletin: NIST gives up enriching most CVEs - Risky Business Media

### **Risky Bulletin: NIST Gives Up Enhancing Most CVEs**

#### **April 17, 2026**

#### **Written by:** Catalin Cimpanu
#### **News Editor:** Corelight

#### **News Source:** Risky Business Media

---

**Background**: The US National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) announced a new policy regarding the Vulnerability Management Database (VMD). Starting later this year, NIST will only enhance vulnerabilities that are critical to the safe operation of US government networks and its private sector.

#### **Reasons Behind the Change**

*   NIST has struggled to keep up with the numerous security flaws discovered, leading to tens of thousands of bugs remaining without detailed information.
*   Budget constraints due to the Trump administration's cuts have hindered progress in enhancing CVEs.

**Impact on Vulnerability Management Companies**: This change may disrupt current workflows and data integration strategies employed by vulnerability management companies. They will need to adapt their tools and processes to incorporate this new approach, either by extracting necessary information from NIST-enhanced databases or finding alternative sources of data to enrich vulnerabilities.

#### **Critical Software Enrichment Criteria**

NIST has identified three critical software categories for enrichment:
*   CISA KEV (CIA Key Vulnerability Entries)
*   CVEs in software used by US federal agencies
*   and CVEs classified as "critical software," covering major operating systems, web browsers, security software, firewalls, backup software, and VPNs.

These specific categories indicate the breadth of covered software applications that will now undergo enriching.
