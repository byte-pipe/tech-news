## What Makes a Cargo Ebike Different?  
- Larger frame geometry to accommodate cargo boxes or longtails.  
- Higher payload ratings (often 300–500 lb).  
- Low center of gravity for stability when loaded.  
- Often equipped with stronger motors, larger batteries, and robust brakes.  

## How to Choose a Cargo Ebike  
1. **Define the primary use** – kids, groceries, tools, or mixed loads.  
2. **Determine required payload** and compare against manufacturer specs.  
3. **Consider motor class and power** (Class 2 throttle vs. Class 3 pedal‑assist).  
4. **Check battery range** with typical load; dual‑battery systems add flexibility.  
5. **Assess fit and geometry** for rider height and comfort.  
6. **Evaluate serviceability** – brand dealer network vs. proprietary components.  

## Motor Selection Guidance  
- **Hub motors** (common in many models) are simple, low‑maintenance, and provide adequate torque for flat terrain.  
- **Mid‑drive motors** (e.g., Bosch) deliver better hill‑climbing performance and more balanced weight distribution, preferred for heavier loads.
