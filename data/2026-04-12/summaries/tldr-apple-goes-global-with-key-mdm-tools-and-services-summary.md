# Apple goes global with key MDM tools and services for business

## Overview
- Apple expands its Business Essentials suite beyond the United States, re‑branding it under the new **Apple Business** portal.
- The rollout begins on April 14 2026 and reaches more than 200 countries and regions.
- The move positions Apple as a viable alternative to existing enterprise suites such as Google Workspace and Microsoft 365, especially for small and mid‑sized businesses (SMBs).

## Core components of Apple Business
- **Unified portal** that combines Apple Business Connect, Apple Business Essentials, and Apple Business Manager.
- **Free mobile device management (MDM)** features for SMBs, including:
  - Managed Apple accounts that separate personal and corporate data via identity provider integration.
  - Configuration of employees or groups.
  - Device‑setting and security controls.
  - App deployment and configuration through Blueprints and the App Store.
  - Zero‑touch hardware deployment.
- **Storage**: 5 GB per employee account at no cost; additional storage starts at $0.99 per person.
- **AppleCare+ for Business**:
  - Per‑device plan at $6.99 per month.
  - Per‑user plan (up to three devices) at $13.99 per month.
  - Includes 24/7 phone support, IT/admin training, and up to two device repairs annually.
- **Productivity and collaboration tools**:
  - Custom‑domain email, calendar, and directory services.
  - Free Apple apps such as Pages, Keynote, Messages, FaceTime, and FreeForm.
  - Branding options (logos in outbound email, location‑based advertising on Apple Maps).

## Competitive positioning
- Apple Business now offers an integrated suite that rivals Google Workspace and Microsoft 365 in:
  - Domain‑based email and calendar.
  - Company directory and contact cards.
  - Collaboration tools native to the Apple ecosystem.
- The free MDM and reduced entry‑costs make the platform attractive for businesses already using Apple hardware.

## Author’s perspective
- Jonny Evans notes the surprise that Apple has not consolidated these services under a single “Apple Works” brand.
- He views the expansion as a strategic push to attract more business users to Apple’s ecosystem.
