# French govt agency confirms breach as hacker offers to sell data

## Overview
- The Agence nationale des titres sécurisés (ANTS), part of the French Ministry of the Interior, manages driver’s licences, national ID cards, passports and immigration documents.  
- ANTS announced a security incident detected on 15 April 2026; the investigation is ongoing and an undisclosed number of individuals may be affected.

## Exposed data
- Login ID  
- Full name  
- Email address  
- Date of birth  
- Unique account identifier  
- Postal address (for some)  
- Place of birth (for some)  
- Phone number (for some)

## Agency response
- ANTS is notifying the users identified as impacted.  
- No immediate action is required from users, but they are advised to stay vigilant for suspicious SMS, phone calls or emails that appear to come from ANTS.  
- The agency has informed the French data‑protection authority (CNIL), the Paris public prosecutor and the national cybersecurity agency (ANSSI).  
- It emphasized that the leaked information does not grant access to ANTS portals and that selling or disseminating the data is illegal.

## Threat‑actor claim
- On 16 April 2026 a hacker using the alias **breach3d** claimed responsibility for the breach on hacker forums, stating that up to 19 million records were stolen.  
- Claimed contents: full names, contact details, birth data, home addresses, account metadata, gender and civil status.  
- The data was offered for sale for an undisclosed amount; it has not been widely leaked yet.  
- BleepingComputer contacted ANTS for comment but had not received a response at the time of publishing.
