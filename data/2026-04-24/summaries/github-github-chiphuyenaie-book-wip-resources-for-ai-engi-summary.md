# AI Engineering Book and Repository Overview

## About the Book
- Focuses on adapting foundation models (LLMs and LMMs) to real‑world problems.
- Presents a framework, not a tutorial; minimal code snippets.
- Available on Amazon, O'Reilly, Kindle, and other technical book retailers.
- Emphasizes fundamentals over specific tools, aiming for lasting relevance.

## Core Topics Covered
- Decision criteria for building AI applications.
- Evaluation methods, including using AI to assess AI outputs.
- Understanding, detecting, and mitigating hallucinations.
- Prompt engineering best practices.
- Retrieval‑augmented generation (RAG) concepts and strategies.
- Designing, building, and evaluating agents.
- Guidelines for when to fine‑tune models.
- Data requirements and quality validation.
- Optimizing models for speed, cost, and security.
- Creating continuous feedback loops for improvement.
- Navigation of model types, benchmarks, and diverse use cases.

## Relationship to “Designing Machine Learning Systems” (DMLS)
- AIE complements DMLS: DMLS deals with traditional ML (tabular data, feature engineering, model training); AIE deals with foundation models (prompt engineering, context construction, parameter‑efficient fine‑tuning).
- Both books are modular and can be read independently.
- Overlapping concepts are referenced with pointers to DMLS where appropriate.

## Intended Audience
- AI engineers, ML engineers, data scientists, engineering managers, technical product managers.
- Teams building or scaling AI applications, addressing issues like hallucinations, security, latency, or cost.
- Tool developers seeking gaps in the AI engineering ecosystem.
- Researchers interested in practical AI use cases.
- Job candidates aiming to understand AI engineering skill requirements.
- Anyone wanting a deeper grasp of AI capabilities, limitations, and impact on roles.
