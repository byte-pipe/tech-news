# A Humanoid Robot Set a Half-Marathon Record in China | WIRED

## Half-Marathon Record Set by Honored Robot in China
### Chinese Company Honor Sets New World Record at Beijing E-Town Half-Marathon


A human record was shattered across 13.1 miles on weekend in Beijing, China, when a robot from the company Honor set a new benchmark for speed. The record-breaking time achieved by human runner Ugandan Olympic medalist Jacob Kiplimo stands at 57 minutes, 20 seconds compared to 50 minutes, 26 seconds recorded by the robotic competitor.

### Key Facts and Features of the Robot

*   **Design Inspiration**: The winning robot incorporated features inspired by elite runners, including long legs (almost a meter), advanced balance systems, and a liquid cooling mechanism, similar to smartphones.
*   **Autonomous Operation**: Many participating robots operated autonomously, adjusting their pace, maintaining balance, and adapting to terrain in real-time using artificial intelligence algorithms.
*   **No Control by Human:** The Honor robot achieved a remarkable speed of 50 minutes, 26 seconds while operating autonomously.
