# Absurd study suggests eating fruits and vegetables leads to cancer - Ars Technica

**Main Points:**
- The claim that eating fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can lead to increased risk of lung cancer is likely flawed.
- A recent study's abstract has been widely reported without peer review or proper methodology.
- The study analyzed dietary survey data from 166 non-smokers who developed lung cancer under age 50.
- Researchers found higher scores for consuming fruits, vegetables, and whole grains compared to the general population.
- Lack of control group, no appropriate comparisons with a similar group of age-matched adults, and speculative explanations drive concerns.

**Summary in Bullet Points:**
* A recent study published at the American Association for Cancer Research conference has raised eyebrows due to flawed methodology and conclusions.
* The researchers analyzed dietary survey data from 166 non-smokers who developed lung cancer under age 50.
* They scored participants' diets on a scale based on presence of "produces with high pesticide levels" which may increase lung cancer risk.
* The study's authors believe the result may be due to differences in lifestyle and diet without controlling for other factors such as smoking.
* Experts have raised concerns about bias, lack of control group, and speculative explanations.
* Multiple studies have found a link between fruit and vegetable consumption and reduced lung cancer risk.
