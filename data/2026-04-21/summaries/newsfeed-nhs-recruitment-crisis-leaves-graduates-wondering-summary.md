# NHS recruitment crisis leaves graduates wondering if they will get work

## NHS Funding Students Despite Job Vacancy Concerns

The National Health Service (NHS) has announced changes to its recruitment efforts, as Welsh university-run paramedic courses have been paused indefinitely due to a shortage of jobs available in the region.

### Key Points:

* Paramedic Science courses at Swansea University and Wrexham University in Wales will be discontinued from September.
* The decision was made by university staff with support from the Welsh government, health boards, and graduates.
* These schools are expecting significant recruitment difficulties in the near future.
* Students who completed nursing or midwifery degree programs may face job uncertainty at the end of their studies.

### Student Concerns:

* Many students have expressed concerns about finding employment after graduation due to insufficient availability of jobs available in Wales.
* They often left secure full-time jobs due to shifts and weekend schedules to pursue their chosen profession.
* Some students, like Emma Coomer, opted out from working full-time and taking up part-time or freelance positions.

### Implications:

* The BBC article's focus on paramedic courses as a prime example of recruitment challenges for the NHS suggests that similar issues may spread to healthcare disciplines such as nursing and physiotherapy.
* This trend could affect graduates planning to apply to these professions, further exacerbating concerns about job availability.
