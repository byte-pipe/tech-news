# Why the $25,000 car is going extinct

**Analysis**

The article "Why the $25,000 car is going extinct" highlights the trend of affordable vehicles disappearing from the market, which may initially appear puzzling. However, from a solo developer's perspective, this phenomenon can be analyzed as a commentary on the challenges faced by manufacturers in producing and marketing cars at affordable prices.

**Problem or Opportunity**

The main issue discussed in the article is the disparity between the affordability of new cars and the high demand for them. The sudden surge in popularity of the $25,000 Maverick model from Ford serves as an example of this phenomenon. This could be perceived as a problem by consumers who crave more affordable vehicles but are not willing to wait or compromise on quality.

**Market Indicators**

The article presents various market data points that support the idea of cheap new vehicles disappearing:

* A 4.8% share of overall new auto sales under $25,000 in February 2025, down from 23% during the same month in 2019 (Edmunds).
* Automotive pricing sources reveal significant price increases across various brands associated with budget-friendly vehicles, such as Hyundai, Toyota, and Kia (Kelley Blue Book data). Conversely, luxury brands saw fewer price hikes to remain competitive.
* Vehicle classes like compact cars, subcompact cars, minivans, and mid-size cars have increased prices due to the same inflationary pressures.
* Automakers face higher profit margins when producing affordable vehicles, making it a challenging scenario for manufacturers.

**Technical Feasibility**

For a solo developer, technical feasibility becomes an important consideration:

* Higher margins on similar costs imply that producing cheaper vehicles would be more profitable and attractive to consumers.
* Engines, transmissions, and other components can contribute to the high profit margins of affordable vehicles. A well-designed and highly efficient vehicle platform can help mitigate these costs.

**Business Viability Signals**

Looking at business viability signals:

* The article mentions Consumer Reports' classification of the Maverick as one of the worst deals for new cars, which could indicate a low willingness-to-pay or loyalty among consumers.
* The increased prices of affordable vehicles are not surprising given that automakers need to maintain profitability in today's market environment.

**Actionable Insights**

From the analysis, actionable insights can be derived:

1. **Differentiate with quality and unique value proposition**: Offer high-quality components, innovative features, or a compelling brand story to justify higher prices for your affordable vehicle.
2. **Invest in efficient technologies**: Develop vehicles that are not only affordable but also highly efficient to maximize profit margins without compromising on performance.
3. **Explore niche markets**: Cater to specific niches where consumers might be willing to pay more for an affordable option, such as environmentally focused or family-oriented vehicles.
4. **Offer bundled services and experiences**: Provide bundles with extras like maintenance plans, insurance options, or subscription-based features that can offset the higher price of your vehicle.

By understanding the context and identifying opportunities and challenges in the market, a solo developer can create a successful product strategy that justifies premium positioning for affordable vehicles.
