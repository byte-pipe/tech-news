# NIH Is Far Cheaper Than The Wrong Dependency

**Analysis of "NIH Is Far Cheaper Than The Wrong Dependency"**

From a solo developer business perspective, this article presents a crucial analysis of dependencies in software development. It identifies a significant oversimplification of codebase dependency policies, implying that all dependencies are created equal and have zero downsides.

The article highlights several benefits of using dependencies:

1. **Reduced time investment**: Dependencies often require less upfront effort to learn than writing custom features.
2. **Less breaking changes risk**: Complex dependencies can minimize the need to re-write code during breaking changes.
3. **Simplified client-side deployment**: Without explicit implementation, clients don't need to worry about installing or managing the dependency itself.

However, the article also emphasizes that these benefits come with trade-offs:

1. **Lack of modularity**: Over-reliance on dependencies can lead to a loss of modularity and maintainability.
2. **Supply chain risks**: Dependencies can introduce security vulnerabilities or performance issues if not carefully managed.

The author cites an example, TigerBeetle, a financial database written in Vanilla Zig with "zero dependencies." This showcases the potential for simplified dependency management but also underscores that not all dependencies are desirable.
