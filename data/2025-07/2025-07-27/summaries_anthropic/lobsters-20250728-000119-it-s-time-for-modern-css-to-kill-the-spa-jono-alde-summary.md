# It's time for modern CSS to kill the SPA - Jono Alderson

Here's a 3-4 paragraph analysis of the article 'It's time for modern CSS to kill the SPA' from a solo developer business perspective:

The article discusses a significant problem and opportunity for solo developers - the overuse of Single Page Applications (SPAs) in building websites, when simpler Multi-Page Applications (MPAs) powered by modern CSS features can often outperform them. The author argues that the assumption that "seamless navigation requires us to build an app" is now obsolete, as native CSS features like View Transitions and Speculation Rules can deliver smooth, fast page transitions without the complexity and performance overhead of a full SPA.

This presents a clear market opportunity for solo developers. Many businesses and clients still default to building SPA-based websites, unaware of the technical feasibility and performance benefits of modern CSS approaches. The article cites specific metrics showing how an MPA with View Transitions and Speculation Rules can outperform a typical Next.js SPA in terms of JavaScript bundle size, Time to Interactive, and overall user experience. This indicates a strong willingness to pay for these solutions, as businesses seek to deliver high-performance, SEO-friendly websites.

From a solo developer perspective, this approach seems highly viable. The required skills - HTML, CSS, and some basic JavaScript for enhancements - are well within the reach of most solo developers. The article emphasizes the declarative, resilient nature of CSS, making it accessible to anyone familiar with web development. The time investment is also likely lower compared to building a full SPA, as the author notes that modern browsers are "constantly looking for ways to improve speed, responsiveness, and efficiency" in these types of declarative, multi-page architectures. Overall, this appears to be a promising business opportunity for solo developers to provide high-performance, cost-effective website solutions to their clients.
