# ongoing by Tim Bray · The Real GenAI Issue

**Analysis**

The article "The Real GenAI Issue" by Tim Bray is a scathing critique of the growing influence of General Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) in the business world. The author highlights two central issues with AI: its intended purpose and the enormous costs associated with it.

Firstly, the author notes that despite billions of dollars invested in GenAI research, there are hardly any startups or businesses that genuinely use the technology for the typical purposes it's sold to end-users (e.g., image and speech recognition). Instead, these investments can be wasted on deploying AI services that are actually replacing human workers with cheaper alternatives.

Secondly, a significant portion of GenAI investment is being directed by corporate leaders towards "disrupting" traditional industries rather than improving the lives of consumers. This misguided strategy prioritizes profit over people and environmental concerns.

**Actionable Insights**

For building a profitable solo developer business:

1. **Understand your target customers**: Recognize who would be willing and able to pay $100s/month for an AI solution, which implies a certain level of disposable income.
2. **Prioritize features over price**: Focus on delivering value rather than prioritizing the cost savings offered by larger companies.
3. **Develop partnerships with established businesses**: Develop relationships with established companies that would benefit from your expertise and can help you tap into their existing customer base.

If you identify a business opportunity in AI development, consider developing more robust products or services to address specific pain points, such as:

* Implementing new technologies (e.g., machine learning libraries)
* Creating user-friendly APIs or interfaces
* Offering premium features that aren't easily replicable at low cost

By focusing on the real problems posed by GenAI and taking a more nuanced view of the market landscape, solo developers can build successful businesses that cater to the actual needs of users.
